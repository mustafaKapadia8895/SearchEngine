<p>The <b>South Atlantic Gyre</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Ocean_gyre#Subtropical_gyres" title="Ocean gyre">subtropical gyre</a> in the south <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. In the southern portion of the gyre, northwesterly (or southeastward-flowing) winds drive eastward-flowing currents that are difficult to distinguish from the northern boundary of the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circumpolar_Current" title="Antarctic Circumpolar Current">Antarctic Circumpolar Current</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Like other oceanic gyres, it collects vast amounts of floating debris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Southern_boundary">Southern boundary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Atlantic_Gyre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Southern boundary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circumpolar_Current" title="Antarctic Circumpolar Current">Antarctic Circumpolar Current</a></div>
<p>South of this gyre is the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circumpolar_Current" title="Antarctic Circumpolar Current">Antarctic Circumpolar Current</a>. This current flows from West to East around <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>. Another name for this current is the West Wind Drift. This current allows Antarctica to maintain its huge ice sheet by keeping warm ocean waters away. At approximately 125 <a href="/wiki/Sverdrup" title="Sverdrup">Sv</a>, this current is the largest ocean current.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Brazil_Current" title="Brazil Current">Brazil Current</a> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_boundary_current" title="Western boundary current">western boundary current</a> of the gyre. It flows south along the Brazilian coast to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata" title="Rio de la Plata">Rio de la Plata</a>. The current is considerably weaker than its North Atlantic counterpart, the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
