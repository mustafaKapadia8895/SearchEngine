<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Arctogadus glacialis</i></h1>

<p><i><b>Arctogadus glacialis</b></i>, known also with ambiguous common names <b>Arctic cod</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FB_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FB-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <b>polar cod</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FB_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FB-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a> species of fish in the cod <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Gadidae" title="Gadidae">Gadidae</a>, related to the true <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a> (genus <i>Gadus</i>). <i>Arctogadus glacialis</i> is found in icy water. They grow to about 30 cm long, and are favorite food of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narwhals" title="Narwhals">narwhals</a> and other arctic whales.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_names_and_taxonomy">Common names and taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arctogadus_glacialis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Common names and taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The common names "Arctic cod" and "polar cod" can refer to either <i>Arctogadus glacialis</i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Boreogadus_saida" title="Boreogadus saida">Boreogadus saida</a></i>, and "Arctic cod" may also refer to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eleginus_nawaga" title="Eleginus nawaga">Eleginus nawaga</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another Arctic gadid, the <a href="/wiki/East_Siberian_cod" title="East Siberian cod">East Siberian cod</a> (<i>Arctogadus borisovi</i>), was until recently considered the closest relative of <i>A. glacialis</i>. It has, however, been found not to be distinct from <i>A. glacialis</i>, and should be included in this species. According to this result, <i>Arctogadus</i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotypic" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a> genus. However, <i>Arctogadus</i> is a close relative of <i><a href="/wiki/Boreogadus_saida" title="Boreogadus saida">Boreogadus</a></i>, and should perhaps be included in that genus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jordan_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jordan-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arctogadus_glacialis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arctic cod is completely white in appearance and has been cited to grow up to 32.5 cm (12.8 in) in <a href="/wiki/Fish_measurement" title="Fish measurement">total length</a>. It has been distinguished from other cod species by its lack of the chin <a href="/wiki/Barbel_(anatomy)" title="Barbel (anatomy)">barbel</a>. Populations of Arctic cod previously referred to the East Siberian cod however do have a chin barbel and grow up to 50-60 cm length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jordan_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jordan-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guelph_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-guelph-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arctogadus_glacialis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Arctic cod is widely distributed in the western part of the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic basin</a> and the northwest and northeast coasts of <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>. Its range is between 85° and 72°N <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a>. Arctic cod can be found at depths of up to 1000 m, and frequently under ice.</p>
<p>Fish earlier attributed to the East Siberian cod are found off the western half of the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> coast and the coasts of <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> and also off the northern and southern coasts of <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>. The fish lives close to the sea floor at depths of 15 to 40 m, but it sometimes enters estuaries, and may also be found under pack ice.</p>
<p>The species is of minor commercial value.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arctogadus_glacialis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>A. glacialis</i> in an ice-free area off northeastern Greenland were found to feed almost exclusively on pelagic prey (primarily <a href="/wiki/Copepod" title="Copepod">copepods</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphipod" title="Amphipod">amphipods</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mysid" title="Mysid">mysids</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
