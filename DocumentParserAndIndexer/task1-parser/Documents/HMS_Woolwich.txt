<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">HMS <i>Woolwich</i></h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>Nine ships of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> have borne the name <b>HMS <i>Woolwich</i></b>, after the port town and naval base of <a href="/wiki/Woolwich" title="Woolwich">Woolwich</a>. A tenth was planned but entered service under a different name.</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Woolwich_(1673)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Woolwich (1673) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1673)</a> was a 4-gun <a href="/wiki/Sloop-of-war" title="Sloop-of-war">sloop</a> launched in 1673 and wrecked in 1675.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/HMS_Woolwich_(1675)" title="HMS Woolwich (1675)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1675)</a> was a 54-gun <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fourth_rate" title="Fourth rate">fourth rate</a> launched in 1675, rebuilt in 1702 and 1741, and broken up in 1747.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/HMS_Woolwich_(1749)" title="HMS Woolwich (1749)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1749)</a> was a 44-gun <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fifth_rate" title="Fifth rate">fifth rate</a> launched in 1749 and sold in 1762.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Woolwich_(1755)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Woolwich (1755) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1755)</a> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storeship" title="Storeship">storeship</a> launched in 1755 and listed until 1760. She may have been a rebuild of an earlier ship named <i>Woolwich Transport</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/HMS_Woolwich_(1785)" title="HMS Woolwich (1785)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1785)</a> was a 44-gun fifth rate launched in 1785. She was used as a storeship by 1794 and was wrecked in 1813.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Woolwich_(1788)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Woolwich (1788) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1788)</a> was a 6-gun <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_tender" title="Ship's tender">tender</a> purchased in 1788 and sold in 1808.</li>
<li>HMS <i>Woolwich</i> was to have been a storeship. She was launched instead as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Portsmouth_(1811)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Portsmouth (1811) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Portsmouth</i></a> in 1811.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Woolwich_(1815)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Woolwich (1815) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1815)</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Lighter_(barge)" title="Lighter (barge)">store lighter</a> launched in 1815. She was renamed HMS <i>Port Royal</i> in 1818. Her fate is unknown.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Woolwich_(1912)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Woolwich (1912) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (1912)</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Depot_ship" title="Depot ship">depot ship</a> launched in 1912 and sold in 1926.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/HMS_Woolwich_(F80)" title="HMS Woolwich (F80)">HMS <i>Woolwich</i> (F80)</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Destroyer_tender" title="Destroyer tender">destroyer tender</a> and depot ship launched in 1934 and sold for scrapping in 1962.</li>
</ul>
