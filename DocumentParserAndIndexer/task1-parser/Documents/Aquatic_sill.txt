<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Aquatic sill</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>An <b>aquatic sill</b> (or an <b>oceanic sill</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_floor" title="Sea floor">sea floor</a> (or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_floor" title="Lake floor">lake floor</a>) barrier of relatively shallow depth restricting water movement between <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_basin" title="Oceanic basin">oceanic basins</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sills_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sills-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aquatic_sills_as_barriers">Aquatic sills as barriers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aquatic_sill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Aquatic sills as barriers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An aquatic sill can be a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biogeographic_barrier" title="Biogeographic barrier">biogeographic barrier</a> for species located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deep_basin" title="Deep basin">deep basins</a> on either side. On top of an aquatic sill, water is often warmer than deeper water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sills_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sills-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>An aquatic sill can restrict movement of water masses in the bottom, and also results in their isolation, which can be partial, and is sometimes near total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ocean_Basin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ocean_Basin-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
