<p>The <b>Yucatán Peninsula</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Península de Yucatán</i></span>), in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, separates the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>, with the northern coastline on the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Channel" title="Yucatán Channel">Yucatán Channel</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> lies east of the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Tehuantepec" title="Isthmus of Tehuantepec">Isthmus of Tehuantepec</a>, a northwestern <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geographic</a> partition separating the region of <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> from the rest of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. It is approximately 181,000 km<sup>2</sup> (70,000 sq mi) in area, and is almost entirely composed of limestone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-readersnatural_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-readersnatural-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The proper derivation of the word Yucatán is widely debated. <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s" title="Hernán Cortés">Hernán Cortés</a>, in the first of <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s#Writings_-_The_Cartas_de_Relaci.C3.B3n" title="Hernán Cortés">his letters to Charles V, the Holy Roman Emperor</a>, claimed that the name Yucatán comes from a misunderstanding. In this telling, the first <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> explorers asked what the area was called and the response they received, "Yucatan", was a <a href="/wiki/Yucatec_Maya_language" title="Yucatec Maya language">Yucatec Maya</a> word meaning "I don't understand what you're saying."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Others claim that the source of the name is the <a href="/wiki/Nahuatl" title="Nahuatl">Nahuatl</a> (Aztec) word Yocatlān, "place of richness."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-human">Pre-human</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-human">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Yucatán Peninsula is the site of the <a href="/wiki/Chicxulub_crater" title="Chicxulub crater">Chicxulub crater</a> impact, which was created 66 million years ago by an asteroid of about 10 to 15 kilometres (6 to 9 miles) in diameter, which is reportedly the asteroid strike that killed the dinosaurs during the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cretaceous_Period" title="Cretaceous Period">Cretaceous Period</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maya">Maya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Maya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya civilization</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ReliefMapMayanSites.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/ReliefMapMayanSites.JPG/220px-ReliefMapMayanSites.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/ReliefMapMayanSites.JPG/330px-ReliefMapMayanSites.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/ReliefMapMayanSites.JPG/440px-ReliefMapMayanSites.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The Yucatán Peninsula comprises a significant proportion of the ancient Maya Lowlands (although the Maya culture extended south of the Yucatán Peninsula, through present <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> and into <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> and highland <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>), and was the center of the Mayan civilization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-readersnatural_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-readersnatural-1">[1]</a></sup> There are many <a href="/wiki/List_of_Maya_sites" title="List of Maya sites">Maya archaeological sites</a> throughout the peninsula; some of the better-known are <a href="/wiki/Chichen_Itza" title="Chichen Itza">Chichen Itza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tulum" title="Tulum">Tulum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uxmal" title="Uxmal">Uxmal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">Indigenous</a> <a href="/wiki/Maya_peoples" title="Maya peoples">Maya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">Mestizos</a> of partial Maya descent make up a sizable portion of the region's population, and <a href="/wiki/Mayan_languages" title="Mayan languages">Mayan languages</a> are widely spoken there.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_conquest">Spanish conquest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spanish conquest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Spanish conquest of Yucatán">Spanish conquest of Yucatán</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_administration">Current administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Current administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The peninsula comprises the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Mexico" title="States of Mexico">Mexican states</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_(state)" title="Yucatán (state)">Yucatán</a>, <a href="/wiki/Campeche" title="Campeche">Campeche</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>; the northern part of the nation of <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>'s northern <a href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_Department" title="Petén Department">El Petén Department</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:15-07-14-Yucatan-%C3%96lfelder-RalfR-WMA_0481.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1815" data-file-width="2719" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/15-07-14-Yucatan-%C3%96lfelder-RalfR-WMA_0481.jpg/220px-15-07-14-Yucatan-%C3%96lfelder-RalfR-WMA_0481.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/15-07-14-Yucatan-%C3%96lfelder-RalfR-WMA_0481.jpg/330px-15-07-14-Yucatan-%C3%96lfelder-RalfR-WMA_0481.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/15-07-14-Yucatan-%C3%96lfelder-RalfR-WMA_0481.jpg/440px-15-07-14-Yucatan-%C3%96lfelder-RalfR-WMA_0481.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Cantarell</div>

<p>In the late historic and early modern eras, the Yucatán Peninsula was largely a cattle ranching, logging, <a href="/wiki/Chicle" title="Chicle">chicle</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henequen" title="Henequen">henequen</a> production area. Since the 1970s (and the fall of the world henequen and chicle markets due to the advent of synthetic substitutes), the Yucatán Peninsula has reoriented its economy towards <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>, especially in the Mexican state of <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>. Once a small fishing village, <a href="/wiki/Canc%C3%BAn" title="Cancún">Cancún</a> in the northeast of the peninsula has grown into a thriving city. The <a href="/wiki/Riviera_Maya" title="Riviera Maya">Riviera Maya</a>, which stretches along the east coast of the peninsula between <a href="/wiki/Canc%C3%BAn" title="Cancún">Cancún</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tulum" title="Tulum">Tulum</a>, houses over 50,000 beds. The best-known locations are the former fishing town of <a href="/wiki/Playa_del_Carmen" title="Playa del Carmen">Playa del Carmen</a>, the ecological parks <a href="/wiki/Xcaret" title="Xcaret">Xcaret</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xel-H%C3%A1" title="Xel-Há">Xel-Há</a> and the Maya ruins of <a href="/wiki/Tulum" title="Tulum">Tulum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coba" title="Coba">Coba</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="2400" height="183" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg/220px-Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg/330px-Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg/440px-Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sediment_off_the_Yucatan_Peninsula.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Sediment" title="Sediment">Sediment</a> off the Yucatán Peninsula.</div>

<p>The peninsula is the exposed portion of the larger <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Platform&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yucatán Platform (page does not exist)">Yucatán Platform</a>, all of which is composed of <a href="/wiki/Carbonate_rock" title="Carbonate rock">carbonate</a> and soluble rocks, being mostly <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> although <a href="/wiki/Dolomite" title="Dolomite">dolomite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evaporite" title="Evaporite">evaporites</a> are also present at various depths. The whole of the Yucatán Peninsula is an unconfined flat lying <a href="/wiki/Karst" title="Karst">karst</a> landscape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-readersnatural_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-readersnatural-1">[1]</a></sup> Sinkholes, known locally as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cenotes" title="Cenotes">cenotes</a>, are widespread in the northern lowlands.</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Alvarez_hypothesis" title="Alvarez hypothesis">Alvarez hypothesis</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mass_extinction" title="Mass extinction">mass extinction</a> of the dinosaurs at the transition from the Cretaceous to the Paleogene Period, the <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous%E2%80%93Paleogene_boundary" title="Cretaceous–Paleogene boundary">Cretaceous–Paleogene boundary</a> (K–T boundary), 65 million years ago was caused by an asteroid impact somewhere in the greater <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caribbean_Basin" title="Caribbean Basin">Caribbean Basin</a>. The deeply buried <a href="/wiki/Chicxulub_crater" title="Chicxulub crater">Chicxulub crater</a> is centered off the north coast of the peninsula near the town of <a href="/wiki/Chicxulub,_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Chicxulub, Yucatán">Chicxulub</a>. The now-famous "Ring of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cenotes" title="Cenotes">Cenotes</a>" (visible in <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> imagery) outlines one of the shock-waves from this impact event in the rock of ~66 million years of age, which lies more than 1 km below the modern ground surface near the centre, with the rock above the impact strata all being younger in age. The presence of the crater has been determined first on the surface from the Ring of Cenotes, but also by geophysical methods, and direct drilling with recovery of the drill cores.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Arrowsmith_Bank" title="Arrowsmith Bank">Arrowsmith Bank</a> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Submerged_bank" title="Submerged bank">submerged bank</a> located off the northeastern end of the peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_resources">Water resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Water resources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the extreme <a href="/wiki/Karst" title="Karst">karst</a> nature of the whole peninsula, the northern half is devoid of rivers. Where <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swamp" title="Swamp">swamps</a> are present, the water is marshy and generally unpotable. Due to its coastal situation, the whole of the peninsula is underlain by an extensive contiguous density stratified coastal <a href="/wiki/Aquifer" title="Aquifer">aquifer</a>, where a fresh water lens formed from <a href="/wiki/Meteoric_water" title="Meteoric water">meteoric water</a> floats on top of intruding saline water from the coastal margins. The thousands of sinkholes known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cenotes" title="Cenotes">cenotes</a> throughout the region provide access to the groundwater system. The cenotes have long been relied on by ancient and contemporary <a href="/wiki/Maya_peoples" title="Maya peoples">Maya</a> people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-readersnatural_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-readersnatural-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vegetation">Vegetation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Vegetation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Short and tall tropical jungles are the predominant natural vegetation types of the Yucatán Peninsula. The boundaries between northern Guatemala (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_(department)" title="Petén (department)">El Petén</a>), Mexico (<a href="/wiki/Campeche" title="Campeche">Campeche</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>), and western <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a> are still occupied by the largest continuous tracts of tropical <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. However, these forests are suffering extensive <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>.<a class="external autonumber" href="https://www.jstor.org/stable/10.2307/982931" rel="nofollow">[2]</a></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like much of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, the peninsula lies within the Atlantic Hurricane Belt, and with its almost uniformly flat terrain it is vulnerable to these large storms coming from the east. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_Hurricane_Season" title="2005 Atlantic Hurricane Season">2005 Atlantic Hurricane Season</a> was a particularly bad season for <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>'s tourism industry, with two forceful category 5 storms hitting, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Emily_(2005)" title="Hurricane Emily (2005)">Hurricane Emily</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Wilma" title="Hurricane Wilma">Hurricane Wilma</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_Atlantic_Hurricane_Season" title="2006 Atlantic Hurricane Season">2006 Atlantic Hurricane Season</a> was a typical year which left the Yucatán untouched, but in the 2007 Atlantic Hurricane season Yucatán was hit by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Dean" title="Hurricane Dean">Hurricane Dean</a> (also a category 5 storm), nevertheless Dean left little damage on the peninsula despite heavy localized flooding.</p>
<p>Strong storms called nortes can quickly descend on the Yucatán Peninsula any time of year. Although these storms pummel the area with heavy rains and high winds, they tend to be short-lived, clearing after about an hour. The average percentage of days with rain per month ranges from a monthly low of 7% in April to a high of 25% in October. Breezes can have a cooling effect, <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a> is generally high, particularly in the remaining rainforest areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
