<p>The <b>Government of Queensland</b>, also referred to as the <b>Queensland Government</b>, is the <a href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia" title="States and territories of Australia">Australian state</a> democratic administrative authority of <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>. The Government of Queensland, a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_system" title="Parliamentary system">parliamentary</a> <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>, was formed in 1859 as prescribed in its <a href="/wiki/History_of_Queensland#Constitution" title="History of Queensland">Constitution</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-q150_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-q150-1">[1]</a></sup> as amended from time to time. Since the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Australia" title="Federation of Australia">Federation of Australia in 1901</a>, Queensland has been a state of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Government" title="Australian Government">Commonwealth of Australia</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Australia" title="Constitution of Australia">Constitution of Australia</a> regulates its relationship with the Commonwealth. Under the Australian Constitution, Queensland ceded legislative and judicial supremacy to the Commonwealth, but retained powers in all matters not in conflict with the Commonwealth. Key state government offices are located at <a href="/wiki/1_William_Street,_Brisbane" title="1 William Street, Brisbane">1 William Street</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_central_business_district" title="Brisbane central business district">Brisbane central business district</a>.</p>

<p>The Government of Queensland operates under the <a href="/wiki/Westminster_system" title="Westminster system">Westminster system</a>, a form of parliamentary government based on the model of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Queensland" title="Governor of Queensland">Governor of Queensland</a>, as the representative of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Australia" title="Monarchy of Australia">Queen of Australia</a>, holds nominal power, although in practice only performs ceremonial duties. The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Queensland" title="Parliament of Queensland">Parliament of Queensland</a> holds legislative power, while <a href="/wiki/Executive_(government)" title="Executive (government)">executive</a> power lies with the <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Queensland" title="Premier of Queensland">Premier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Queensland" title="Cabinet of Queensland">Cabinet</a>, and judicial power is exercised by a system of courts and tribunals.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Queensland" title="Parliament of Queensland">Parliament of Queensland</a> is the state's <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislature</a>. It consists of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Her Majesty The Queen</a> (represented by the Governor), and a single chamber; the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Queensland" title="Legislative Assembly of Queensland">Legislative Assembly</a>. Queensland is the only Australian state with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unicameral" title="Unicameral">unicameral</a> <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> after a second chamber, the <a href="/wiki/Queensland_Legislative_Council" title="Queensland Legislative Council">Legislative Council</a>, was abolished in 1922.</p>
<p>The Legislative Assembly has 89 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Members_of_Parliament" title="Members of Parliament">members</a>; one representing each <a href="/wiki/Electoral_districts_of_Queensland" title="Electoral districts of Queensland">electoral district</a> in Queensland. Elections for the Legislative Assembly are held approximately every three years.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Queensland" title="Cabinet of Queensland">Cabinet of Queensland</a> is the government's chief policy-making organ, and consists of the Premier and all <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">ministers</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Queensland_government_agencies">Queensland government agencies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Government_of_Queensland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Queensland government agencies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Queensland_government_agencies" title="List of Queensland government agencies">List of Queensland government agencies</a></div>
<p>The Queensland Government delivers services, determines policy and regulations, including legal interpretation, by a number of <a href="/wiki/Government_agency" title="Government agency">agencies</a> grouped under areas of portfolio responsibility. Each portfolio is led by a <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">government minister</a> who is a member of the Parliament. As of April 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Government_of_Queensland&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> there were nineteen lead agencies, called <a href="/wiki/Ministry_(government_department)" title="Ministry (government department)">government departments</a>, that consist of:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deps_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-deps-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>

<li>Department of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Agriculture,_Fisheries_and_Forestry_(Queensland)" title="Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry (Queensland)">Department of Agriculture and Fisheries</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Communities,_Child_Safety_and_Disability_Services_(Queensland)" title="Department of Communities, Child Safety and Disability Services (Queensland)">Department of Communities, Child Safety and Disability Services</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Department_of_Education_and_Training_(Queensland)" title="Department of Education and Training (Queensland)">Department of Education and Training</a></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judiciary">Judiciary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Government_of_Queensland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Judiciary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Queensland_courts_and_tribunals" title="List of Queensland courts and tribunals">List of Queensland courts and tribunals</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law,_Brisbane_03.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="2448" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law%2C_Brisbane_03.jpg/170px-Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law%2C_Brisbane_03.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law%2C_Brisbane_03.jpg/255px-Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law%2C_Brisbane_03.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law%2C_Brisbane_03.jpg/340px-Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law%2C_Brisbane_03.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law,_Brisbane_03.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II_Courts_of_Law,_Brisbane" title="Queen Elizabeth II Courts of Law, Brisbane">Queen Elizabeth II Courts of Law</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a> in which the Supreme Court of Queensland and District Court of Queensland sit</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The judiciary of Queensland consists of the <a href="/wiki/Magistrates_Court_of_Queensland" title="Magistrates Court of Queensland">Magistrates Court</a>, the <a href="/wiki/District_Court_of_Queensland" title="District Court of Queensland">District Court</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Queensland" title="Supreme Court of Queensland">Supreme Court</a>, as well as a number of smaller courts and tribunals. The <a href="/wiki/Chief_Justice_of_Queensland" title="Chief Justice of Queensland">Chief Justice of Queensland</a> is the state's most senior judicial officer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Magistrates_Court">Magistrates Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Government_of_Queensland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Magistrates Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Magistrates_Court_of_Queensland" title="Magistrates Court of Queensland">Magistrates Court of Queensland</a></div>
<p>The Magistrates Court is the lowest tier of the judicial hierarchy of Queensland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magistrates_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Magistrates-3">[3]</a></sup> The court's <a href="/wiki/Criminal_jurisdiction" title="Criminal jurisdiction">criminal jurisdiction</a> covers <a href="/wiki/Summary_offence" title="Summary offence">summary offences</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Indictable_offence" title="Indictable offence">indictable offences</a> which may be heard summarily, but all criminal proceedings in Queensland begin in the Magistrates Court, even if they are not within this jurisdiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magistrates-Factsheet_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Magistrates-Factsheet-4">[4]</a></sup> For charges beyond its jurisdiction, the court conducts <a href="/wiki/Committal_procedure" title="Committal procedure">committal hearings</a> in which the presiding magistrate decides, based on the strength of the evidence, whether to refer the matter to a higher court or dismiss it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magistrates-Factsheet_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Magistrates-Factsheet-4">[4]</a></sup> The court's civil jurisdiction covers matters in which the amount in dispute is less than or equal to $150,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magistrates-Factsheet_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Magistrates-Factsheet-4">[4]</a></sup> Appeals against decisions by the Magistrates Court are heard by the District Court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magistrates-Factsheet_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Magistrates-Factsheet-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="District_Court">District Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Government_of_Queensland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: District Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/District_Court_of_Queensland" title="District Court of Queensland">District Court of Queensland</a></div>
<p>The District Court is the middle tier of the judicial hierarchy of Queensland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-District-Factsheet_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-District-Factsheet-5">[5]</a></sup> The court has jurisdiction to hear all appeals from decisions made in the Magistrates Court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-District-Factsheet_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-District-Factsheet-5">[5]</a></sup> Its criminal jurisdiction covers serious indictable offences (such as armed robbery, rape, and dangerous driving).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-District-Factsheet_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-District-Factsheet-5">[5]</a></sup> The court's civil jurisdiction covers matters in which the amount in dispute is more than $150,000 but less than or equal to $750,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-District-Factsheet_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-District-Factsheet-5">[5]</a></sup> Appeals against decisions by the District Court are heard by the Court of Appeal, a division of the Supreme Court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-District-Factsheet_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-District-Factsheet-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Supreme_Court">Supreme Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Government_of_Queensland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Supreme Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Queensland" title="Supreme Court of Queensland">Supreme Court of Queensland</a></div>
<p>The Supreme Court is the highest tier of the judicial hierarchy Queensland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Supreme_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Supreme-6">[6]</a></sup> The court has two divisions; the Trial Division and the Court of Appeal. The Trial Division's jurisdiction covers serious criminal offences (including <a href="/wiki/Murder" title="Murder">murder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manslaughter" title="Manslaughter">manslaughter</a>), and civil matters involving claims of more than $750,000. The Court of Appeal's jurisdiction allows it to hear cases on appeal from the Trial Division, the District Court, and a number of other judicial tribunals in Queensland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Supreme_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Supreme-6">[6]</a></sup> Appeals against decisions by the Court of Appeal are heard by the <a href="/wiki/High_Court_of_Australia" title="High Court of Australia">High Court of Australia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Idiosyncrasies">Idiosyncrasies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Government_of_Queensland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Idiosyncrasies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are several factors that differentiate Queensland's government from other Australian states. The legislature has no <a href="/wiki/Upper_house" title="Upper house">upper house</a>. For a large portion of its history, the state was under a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gerrymander" title="Gerrymander">gerrymander</a> that heavily favoured rural electorates. This, combined with the already decentralised nature of Queensland, meant that politics has been dominated by regional interests. Queensland, along with <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, formerly operated a balloting system known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optional_Preferential_Voting" title="Optional Preferential Voting">Optional Preferential Voting</a> for state elections. This is different from the predominant Australian electoral system, the <a href="/wiki/Instant-runoff_voting" title="Instant-runoff voting">instant-runoff voting</a> system, and in practice is closer to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_past_the_post" title="First past the post">first past the post</a> ballot (similar to the ballot used in the UK), which some say is to the detriment of minor parties. The next Queensland election will use instant-runoff voting.</p>
<p>These conditions have had notable practical ramifications for politics in Queensland. The lack of an <a href="/wiki/Upper_house" title="Upper house">upper house</a> for substantial legislative review has meant that Queensland has had a tradition of domination by strong-willed, <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">populist</a> premiers, often with arguably <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Authoritarian" title="Authoritarian">authoritarian</a> tendencies, holding office for long periods.</p>
<p>While most Australian states have their populations heavily concentrated in the capital cities, Brisbane accounts for only about 45 percent of Queensland's population. The strong decentralisation of the population made the state a stronghold for the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_Australia" title="National Party of Australia">National Party</a>. Before the merger of the Queensland branches of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Australia" title="Liberal Party of Australia">Liberals</a> and Nationals as the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_National_Party_of_Queensland" title="Liberal National Party of Queensland">Liberal National Party</a>, the National Party had been the senior partner in the non-<a href="/wiki/Australian_Labor_Party" title="Australian Labor Party">Labor</a> <a href="/wiki/Coalition_(Australia)" title="Coalition (Australia)">Coalition</a> since 1924. In other states and federally, the long-standing coalition between the Nationals and Liberals typically has the National Party as the junior partner.</p>
