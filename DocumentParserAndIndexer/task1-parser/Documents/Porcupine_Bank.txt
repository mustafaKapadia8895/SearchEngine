<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Porcupine Bank</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p><b>Porcupine Bank</b> is an area of the Irish shelf, on the fringes of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> approximately 200 kilometres (120Â mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The relatively raised area of seabed, 200 m below sea level at its highest,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> lies between the deep-water <a href="/wiki/Porcupine_Seabight" title="Porcupine Seabight">Porcupine Seabight</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rockall_Trough" title="Rockall Trough">Rockall Trough</a>.</p>
<p>The name comes from the bank's discovery in 1862 by HMS <i>Porcupine</i>, a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> sail and <a href="/wiki/Paddle_steamer" title="Paddle steamer">paddle-wheel ship</a> used mainly for surveying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The northern and western slopes of the bank feature species of <a href="/wiki/Deep-water_coral" title="Deep-water coral">cold-water corals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Dr. Anthony Grehan of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_University_of_Ireland,_Galway" title="National University of Ireland, Galway">National University of Ireland, Galway</a>, the virtually untouched site could benefit dwindling fish stocks and possibly aid medical research.</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>"These are by far the most pristine, thriving and hence spectacular examples of cold-water coral <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reefs</a> that Iâve encountered in almost ten years of study in Irish waters. There is also evidence of recent recruitment of corals and many other reef animals in the area suggesting this area is an important source of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Larvae" title="Larvae">larvae</a> supply to other areas further along the Porcupine Bank."</p>
<div class="templatequotecite">ââ<cite>Dr. Anthony Grehan, <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>In an 1870 paper presented to the Geological Society of Ireland, Mr W Fraser suggested that these reefs mark the site of the sunken island of <a href="/wiki/Brasil_(mythical_island)" title="Brasil (mythical island)">Hy-Brasil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Hy-Brasil was recorded on maps of the 15th and 16th centuries and is sometimes referred to as the other <a href="/wiki/Atlantis" title="Atlantis">Atlantis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
