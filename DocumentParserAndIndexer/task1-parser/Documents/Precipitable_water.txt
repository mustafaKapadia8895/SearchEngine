<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Precipitable water</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Precipitable water</b> is the depth of water in a column of the atmosphere, if all the water in that column were precipitated as rain. As a depth, the precipitable water is measured in millimeters or inches. Often abbreviated as "TPW" = <b>t</b>otal <b>p</b>recipitable <b>w</b>ater.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Measurement">Measurement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Precipitable_water&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Measurement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are different measurement techniques:</p>
<ul>
<li>One type of measurement is based on the measurement of the solar <a href="/wiki/Irradiance" title="Irradiance">irradiance</a> on two wavelengths, one in a water absorption band, and the other not. The precipitable water column is determined using the irradiances in these bands and the <a href="/wiki/Beer%E2%80%93Lambert_law" title="Beer–Lambert law">Beer–Lambert law</a>.</li>
<li>The precipitable water can also be calculated by integration of <a href="/wiki/Radiosonde" title="Radiosonde">radiosonde</a> data (<a href="/wiki/Relative_humidity" title="Relative humidity">relative humidity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pressure" title="Pressure">pressure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a>) over the whole <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">atmosphere</a>.</li>
<li>Data can be viewed on a Lifted-K index. The numbers represent inches of water as mentioned above for a geographical location.</li>
<li>Recently, methods using the <a href="/wiki/Global_Positioning_System" title="Global Positioning System">Global Positioning System</a> have been developed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>Some work has been performed to create empirical relationships between surface <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Specific_humidity" title="Specific humidity">specific humidity</a> and precipitable water based on localized measurements (generally a 2nd to 5th order polynomial).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> However, this method has not received widespread use in part because humidity is a local measurement and precipitable water is a total column measurement.</li>
</ul>
