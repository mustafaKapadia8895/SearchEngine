<p><b>Saithe</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'">eɪ</span><span title="/ð/: 'th' in 'thy'">ð</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'">eɪ</span><span title="/θ/: 'th' in 'thigh'">θ</span></span>/</a></span></span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Pollachius virens</i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">marine</a> <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Pollock" title="Pollock">Pollachius</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a>. Together with <i><a href="/wiki/Pollachius_pollachius" title="Pollachius pollachius">Pollachius pollachius</a></i> it is generally referred to in the United States as <b><a href="/wiki/Pollock" title="Pollock">pollock</a></b>. Other names include the <b>Boston blue</b> (separate from <a href="/wiki/Bluefish" title="Bluefish">bluefish</a>), <b>coalfish</b>/<b>coley</b>, and <b>saithe</b> in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pollachius_virens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species can be separated from <i>P. pollachius</i> by looking at the relative lengths of the upper and lower jaws. <i>P. pollachius</i> has a longer underslung lower jaw while <i>P. virens</i> has approximately equal upper and lower jaw lengths. This gives a very different profile to the head. In general, <i>P. pollachius</i> is a brown or golden colour with a dark back while <i>P. virens</i> is bright silver with a very dark green back. <i>P. virens</i> generally appears to have relatively smaller eyes. The lateral line of <i>P. pollachius</i> has a noticeable kink over the pectoral fins while that of <i>P. virens</i> is straighter.</p>
<p>The flesh of coalfish (<i>P. virens</i>) is darkly coloured (hence the common name) while that of <i>P. pollachius</i> is similar to other members of the cod family. This dark colour in the fresh uncooked flesh may have led to the undeserved reputation of this fish as poor for eating.</p>
<p>It is common in the northern parts of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Northern Atlantic</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Biscay" title="Bay of Biscay">Bay of Biscay</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Palmas_Altas_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Palmas Altas Campus (page does not exist)">Palmas Altas Campus</a>. Adults can grow up to 130 cm (51 in) and weigh up to 32 kg (71 lb); the species is of great commercial value to <a href="/wiki/Fishery" title="Fishery">fisheries</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-5">[5]</a></sup> The fish can be found close to the shore, particularly in rocky areas, but larger examples tend to be found around off-shore wrecks and reefs. The largest coalfish ever caught was 50 lb (23 kg) at <a href="/wiki/Saltstraumen" title="Saltstraumen">Saltstraumen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="As_food">As food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pollachius_virens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As food">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coalfish is edible and has commercial value, although it is considerably less valuable than premium <a href="/wiki/Whitefish_(fisheries_term)" title="Whitefish (fisheries term)">whitefish</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haddock" title="Haddock">haddock</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> To achieve a <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">salmon</a>-like orange color, it can be salted and smoked. In <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the fish is commonly sold as <i>Seelachs</i> (literally 'sea salmon'), although it is not closely related to any salmon.</p>
