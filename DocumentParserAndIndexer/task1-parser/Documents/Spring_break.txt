<p><b>Spring break</b> is a vacational period in early <a href="/wiki/Spring_(season)" title="Spring (season)">Spring</a> at universities and schools which started during the 1930s in the United States and is observed in some other mainly Western countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Spring break is also a phenomenon dominated by extensive gatherings and riotous partying in warm climate locations such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach" title="Daytona Beach">Daytona Beach</a>, Florida and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cancun" title="Cancun">Cancun</a>, Mexico, attended regardless of participants' educational standings.</p>
<p>As a holiday it is variously known as <b>Easter vacation</b>, <b>Easter holiday</b>, <b>April break</b>, <b>Spring vacation</b>, <b>mid-term break</b>, <b>study week</b>, <b>reading week</b>, <b>reading period</b>, or <b>Easter week</b>, depending on regional conventions.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spring break is an academic tradition in various mostly western countries that is scheduled for different periods depending on the state and sometimes the region.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, the Spring break starts with the end of the academic year in March and ends on April 1 with the beginning of a new academic year.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Kuwait">Kuwait</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kuwait">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, the Spring break is between the two academic or school semesters, usually in December or January, but could last until early February. It is usually a 2 or 3-week break, however, there is no fixed date for it, as the date is adjusted in accordance with the Lunar or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hijri_calendar" title="Hijri calendar">Hijri calendar</a>, as it is the case for almost all Arab and Muslim nations.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Korea">South Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: South Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, the Spring break starts in mid-February (the end of the academic year) and ends on March 1 (a national holiday) with the beginning of a new academic year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Czech_Republic">Czech Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Czech Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, only primary and secondary school students have a Spring break. The break is one week long and the date of the break differs from county to county to avoid overcrowding of the break destinations in the Czech Republic (Czechs usually travel to the mountains to ski there). The counties are divided into six groups, each group containing counties evenly distributed across the country. The first group starts the holiday on the first Monday of February, the last group starts the holiday five weeks later (usually in early March). The last group of counties becomes the first one to have the Spring break the next year.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Georgia">Georgia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Georgia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Before 2017, the Spring break in the country of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> was typically an Easter holiday, lasting from Thursday to Tuesday in the Holy Week. In 2017, the new Minister of Education and Science of Georgia Aleksandre Jejelava made a new reform by which, students of preschools, elementary &amp; high schools as well as colleges and universities get six days of holiday in March, lasting from March 8 to March 15, for people to go on winter vacations or do other activities.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, universities typically schedule a semester break of five to eight weeks around March. The <a href="/wiki/Whitsun" title="Whitsun">Whitsun</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a>) holidays around late May or early June are also considered a Spring break.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, Spring break takes place during the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week" title="Holy Week">Holy Week</a> and the one after it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lithuania">Lithuania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Lithuania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, Spring break (called Easter holidays or Spring holidays) takes place one week before Easter and one day after it (as it is the second day of Easter), all school students have this vacation. Primary school students have another week of holidays after Easter.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Portugal">Portugal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Portugal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, Spring break is mostly known as "Easter Holidays" and it gives two weeks to all students around the country.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Russia">Russia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Russia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Before 1917 there was an Easter Break in schools. In the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, Spring break was always from 24 to 31 of March. Now, many schools in Russia still have the Spring break, but the exact date is decided by the school itself. In the majority of cases it is set in the middle of April. Also, the public holidays in May, connected with Labour day, can be an accurate equivatent of the spring break.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, primary school students typically have winter sports holiday for one week in February as well as Easter holidays for one week in April, during Easter.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, there is not a Spring break proper. Instead, the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week" title="Holy Week">Holy Week</a> is celebrated and students usually have holidays during these days.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Easter break in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> is from one to two and a half weeks (depending on the local council and school policy) and fits around Easter.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> gives a week-long break to its <a href="/wiki/Primary_education" title="Primary education">elementary school</a> and <a href="/wiki/Secondary_education" title="Secondary education">secondary school</a> students in the month of March, with the time varying from province to province; <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, for example, place their March breaks during the first week of March; <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> schedule theirs during the second or third week, and is usually a week long; the break in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> usually occurs in the last week of March. Post-secondary students in Ontario and Alberta usually get a week off in mid-February.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Jamaica">Jamaica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Jamaica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, the Spring break starts in the first week of March. The break may range from three days to one week.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, Spring break takes place during the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week" title="Holy Week">Holy Week</a> and the one after it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Spring break at the college and university level can occur from March to April, depending on term dates and when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Easter_Sunday" title="Easter Sunday">Easter holiday</a> falls. Usually, Spring break is about one week long, but many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%E2%80%9312_(education)" title="K–12 (education)">K–12</a> institutions in the United States schedule a two-week-long break known as "Easter Break," "Easter Holidays", or "Easter Vacation", as they generally take place in the weeks before or after Easter.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> However, in the states of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, schools typically schedule Spring break for the week of the third Monday in April to coincide with <a href="/wiki/Patriots%27_Day" title="Patriots' Day">Patriots' Day</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_America">Central America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Central America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="Guatemala,_El_Salvador_and_Honduras"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Guatemala.2C_El_Salvador_and_Honduras">Guatemala, El Salvador and Honduras</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Guatemala, El Salvador and Honduras">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, it takes place during Easter; schools give students a whole week to rest while the staff workforce rests approximately three days.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_America">South America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: South America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Colombia">Colombia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Colombia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, Spring break takes place the first week of April, during the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week" title="Holy Week">Holy Week</a> until the second week.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Chile">Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, many schools and universities take vacations in the middle of September coinciding with the celebration of the <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_Patrias_(Chile)" title="Fiestas Patrias (Chile)">Fiestas Patrias</a> (Celebration of that country).Being located in the Southern Hemisphere, Spring begins approximately at the end of this holiday week so it acts similarly to the American Spring break.</p>

<h5><span id="Panama_City_Beach,_Florida"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Panama_City_Beach.2C_Florida">Panama City Beach, Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: Panama City Beach, Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Starting in the late 90's, <a href="/wiki/Panama_City_Beach,_Florida" title="Panama City Beach, Florida">Panama City Beach</a> began advertising the destination hoping to attract crowds that had formerly gone to Fort Lauderdale and then Daytona before those communities enacted restrictions. From 2010-2016 an estimated 300,000 students traveled to the destination. The spawn of social media and digital marketing helped propel the beach town into a student mecca during March. Following well publicized shootings and a gang rape in 2015, several new ordinances were put into effect prohibiting drinking on the beach and establishing a bar closing time of 2 a.m. Central Time. Reports show a drop in Panama City Beach's Spring break turnout in March 2016<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> followed by increased family tourism in April 2016. Both are credited/blamed on the new ordinances by the <a href="/wiki/Bay_County,_Florida" title="Bay County, Florida">Bay County</a> Community Development Corporation (CDC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span id="Fort_Lauderdale,_Florida"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Fort_Lauderdale.2C_Florida">Fort Lauderdale, Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: Fort Lauderdale, Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale" title="Fort Lauderdale">Fort Lauderdale</a>'s reputation as a Spring break destination for college students started when the <a href="/wiki/Colgate_University" title="Colgate University">Colgate University</a> men's swim team arrived to practice there over Christmas break in 1934.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Attracting approximately 20,000 college students in the 1950s, Spring break was still known as 'Spring vacation' and was a relatively low key affair. This began to change when <a href="/wiki/Glendon_Swarthout" title="Glendon Swarthout">Glendon Swarthout</a>’s novel, <i>Where the Boys Are</i> was published in 1958, effectively ushering in modern Spring break.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Swarthout’s 1958 novel was quickly made into a movie of the same title in 1960 <i><a href="/wiki/Where_the_Boys_Are" title="Where the Boys Are">Where the Boys Are</a></i>, in which college girls met boys while on Spring break there. The number of visiting college students immediately jumped to over 50,000. By the early 1980s, Ft. Lauderdale was attracting between 250,000-350,000 college students per year during Spring break. Residents of the Fort Lauderdale area became so upset at the damage done by college students that the local government passed laws restricting parties in 1985. At the same time, the <a href="/wiki/National_Minimum_Drinking_Age_Act" title="National Minimum Drinking Age Act">National Minimum Drinking Age Act</a> was enacted in the United States, requiring that Florida raise the minimum drinking age to 21 and inspiring many underage college vacationers to travel to other locations in the United States for Spring break. By 1989, the number of college students traveling to Fort Lauderdale fell to 20,000, a far cry from the 350,000 who went four years prior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span id="South_Padre_Island,_Texas"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Padre_Island.2C_Texas">South Padre Island, Texas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: South Padre Island, Texas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>In the early 1980s, <a href="/wiki/South_Padre_Island" title="South Padre Island">South Padre Island</a> became the first location outside of Florida to draw a large number of college students for Spring break. With only a few thousand residents, South Padre Island has consistently drawn between 80,000 and 120,000 Spring breakers for the last 30 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_marketing">Corporate marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=45" title="Edit section: Corporate marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is common for major brands that cater to the youth market (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gillette_(brand)" title="Gillette (brand)">Gillette</a>, <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, and branches of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a>) to market at Spring break destinations.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon.
