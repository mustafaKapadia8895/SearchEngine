<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Azores Triple Junction</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

The Azores Triple Junction</div>

<p><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Azores_Triple_Junction&amp;params=39.44_N_-29.83_E_"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">39°26′N</span> <span class="longitude">29°50′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">39.44°N 29.83°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">39.44; -29.83</span></span></span></a></span></span></span> The <b>Azores Triple Junction</b> (<b>ATJ</b>) is a geologic <a href="/wiki/Triple_junction" title="Triple junction">triple junction</a> where the boundaries of three tectonic plates intersect: the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Plate" title="North American Plate">North American Plate</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Plate" title="Eurasian Plate">Eurasian Plate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/African_Plate" title="African Plate">African Plate</a>. This triple junction is located along the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_Ridge" title="Mid-Atlantic Ridge">Mid-Atlantic Ridge</a> (MAR) amidst the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> islands, nearly due west of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straits_of_Gibraltar" title="Straits of Gibraltar">Straits of Gibraltar</a>. It is classed as a R-R-R triple junction, T type (for its shape), as it is an intersection of the Mid-Atlantic Ridge running north-south and the <a href="/wiki/Terceira_Rift" title="Terceira Rift">Terceira Rift</a> which runs east-southeast.</p>
<p>The spreading rate along the MAR does not change abruptly at the ATJ, instead decreasing from 22.9±0.1 mm/yr at 40°N to 19.8±0.2 mm/yr at 38°N. This means the ATJ is not a simple triple junction where three tectonic plates meet at a point. The transitional range of spreading rates instead indicates the presence of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microcontinent" title="Microcontinent">microplate</a>, commonly referred to as the Azores microplate. Its northern boundary intersects the MAR between 39.4°N and 40.0°N and its southern between 38.2°N and 38.5°N. The microplate moves about 2 mm/yr east-northeast along its Nubian boundary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
