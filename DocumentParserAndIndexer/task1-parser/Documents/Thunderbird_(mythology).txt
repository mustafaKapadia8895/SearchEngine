<p>The <b>thunderbird</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Legendary_creature" title="Legendary creature">legendary creature</a> in certain <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">North American indigenous peoples'</a> history and culture. It is considered a supernatural being of power and strength. It is especially important, and frequently depicted, in the art, songs and oral histories of many <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Pacific_Northwest_Coast" title="Indigenous peoples of the Pacific Northwest Coast">Pacific Northwest Coast</a> cultures, but is also found in various forms among some peoples of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Southwest" title="American Southwest">American Southwest</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Plains_Indians" title="Plains Indians">Great Plains</a>. It is also the symbol of the Canadian Military Police.</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Algonquian_peoples" title="Algonquian peoples">Algonquian</a> mythology, the thunderbird controls the upper world while the underworld is controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Underwater_panther" title="Underwater panther">underwater panther</a> or <a href="/wiki/Horned_Serpent#In_Native_American_cultures" title="Horned Serpent">Great Horned Serpent</a>. The thunderbird throws lightning at the underwater creatures and creates thunder by flapping its wings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alg-1">[1]</a></sup> Thunderbirds in this tradition are commonly depicted as having an X-shaped appearance. This varies between a simple X to recognizable birds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alg_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Alg-1">[1]</a></sup> The X-shaped thunderbird is often used to depict the thunderbird with its wings alongside its body and the head facing forwards instead of in profile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NE_Art_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NE_Art-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Menominee">Menominee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thunderbird_(mythology)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Menominee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Menominee" title="Menominee">Menominee</a> of Northern Wisconsin tell of a great mountain that floats in the western sky on which dwell the thunderbirds. They control the rain and hail and delight in fighting and deeds of greatness. They are the enemies of the great horned snakes - the Misikinubik - and have prevented these from overrunning the earth and devouring mankind. They are messengers of the Great Sun himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ojibwe" title="Ojibwe">Ojibwe</a> version of the myth states that the thunderbirds were created by <a href="/wiki/Nanabozho" title="Nanabozho">Nanabozho</a> for the purpose of fighting the underwater spirits. They were also used to punish humans who broke moral rules. The thunderbirds lived in the <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_direction#Americas" title="Cardinal direction">four directions</a> and arrived with the other birds in the springtime. In the fall they migrated south after the ending of the underwater spirits' most dangerous season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ojibwa_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ojibwa-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Winnebago">Winnebago</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thunderbird_(mythology)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Winnebago">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winnebago_(tribe)" title="Winnebago (tribe)">Winnebago</a> tradition states that a man who has a vision of a thunderbird during a solitary fast will become a war chief.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
