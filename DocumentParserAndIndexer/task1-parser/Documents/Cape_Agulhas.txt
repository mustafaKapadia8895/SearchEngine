<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cape Agulhas</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p><b>Cape Agulhas</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɡ/: 'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/ʌ/: 'u' in 'cut'">ʌ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Cabo das Agulhas</i></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[ˈkaβu ðɐz ɐˈɣuʎɐʃ]</a></span>, "Cape of the Needles") is a rocky <a href="/wiki/Headland" title="Headland">headland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Cape" title="Western Cape">Western Cape</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.</p>
<p>It is the geographic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extreme_points_of_Africa" title="Extreme points of Africa">southern tip</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African continent</a> and the beginning of the dividing line between the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a> Oceans according to the <a href="/wiki/International_Hydrographic_Organization" title="International Hydrographic Organization">International Hydrographic Organization</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Historically, the cape has been known to sailors as a major hazard on the traditional <a href="/wiki/Clipper_route" title="Clipper route">clipper route</a>. It is sometimes regarded as one of the <a href="/wiki/Great_capes" title="Great capes">great capes</a>. It was most commonly known in English as <b>Cape L'Agulhas</b> until the 20th century. The town of <a href="/wiki/L%27Agulhas" title="L'Agulhas">L'Agulhas</a> is located near to the cape.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Agulhas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CapeHopeDetail.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="487" data-file-width="906" height="118" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/CapeHopeDetail.png/220px-CapeHopeDetail.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/CapeHopeDetail.png/330px-CapeHopeDetail.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/CapeHopeDetail.png/440px-CapeHopeDetail.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CapeHopeDetail.png" title="Ingrandisci"></a></div>
Map showing the location of Cape Agulhas relative to the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Cape Agulhas is located in the <a href="/wiki/Overberg" title="Overberg">Overberg</a> region, 170 kilometres (105 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>. The cape was named by <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_discoveries" title="Portuguese discoveries">Portuguese navigators</a>, who called it <i>Cabo das Agulhas</i>—<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> for "Cape of Needles"—after noticing that around the year 1500 the direction of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic_north" title="Magnetic north">magnetic north</a> (and therefore the compass needle) coincided with <a href="/wiki/True_north" title="True north">true north</a> in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The cape is within the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Agulhas_Local_Municipality" title="Cape Agulhas Local Municipality">Cape Agulhas Local Municipality</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Overberg_District_Municipality" title="Overberg District Municipality">Overberg District</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Cape" title="Western Cape">Western Cape</a> province of South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capemuni_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-capemuni-3">[3]</a></sup> The nearby small airport of Andrew's Field services Agulhas.</p>
<p>South of Cape Agulhas the warm <a href="/wiki/Agulhas_Current" title="Agulhas Current">Agulhas Current</a> that flows south along the east coast of Africa retroflects back into the Indian Ocean. While retroflecting, it pinches off large ocean eddies (Agulhas rings) that drift into the South Atlantic Ocean and take enormous amounts of heat and salt into the neighbouring ocean. This mechanism constitutes one of the key elements in the global <a href="/wiki/Thermohaline_circulation" title="Thermohaline circulation">conveyor belt</a> circulation of heat and salt.</p>
<p>Unlike its better-known relative, the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>, Cape Agulhas is relatively unspectacular, consisting of a gradually curving coastline with a rocky beach. A survey marker indicates the location of the cape, which would otherwise be difficult to identify. The waters of the <a href="/wiki/Agulhas_Bank" title="Agulhas Bank">Agulhas Bank</a> off the coast are quite shallow and are renowned as one of the best fishing grounds in South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rocks that form Cape Agulhas belong to the <a href="/wiki/Table_Mountain" title="Table Mountain">Table Mountain</a> Group, often loosely termed the Table Mountain sandstone. They are closely linked to the geological formations that are exposed in the spectacular cliffs of Table Mountain, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Point" title="Cape Point">Cape Point</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Agulhas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cape Agulhas a warm <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>:<i>Csb</i> ). The climate is consistently mild, with no temperature or rainfall extremes. According to <a href="/wiki/South_African_National_Parks" title="South African National Parks">South African National Parks</a>, who administer the nature reserve, the average rainfall is 400–600 mm per annum, mostly received in winter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Temperature climate data is available for Cape Agulhas, averages are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Jan max: 23.8 °C (min: 17.7 °C); Jul max: 16.5 °C (min: 10.8 °C)</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shipping_hazards">Shipping hazards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Agulhas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shipping hazards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AgulhasLighthouse.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/AgulhasLighthouse.jpg/220px-AgulhasLighthouse.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/AgulhasLighthouse.jpg/330px-AgulhasLighthouse.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/AgulhasLighthouse.jpg/440px-AgulhasLighthouse.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AgulhasLighthouse.jpg" title="Ingrandisci"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Cape_Agulhas_Lighthouse" title="Cape Agulhas Lighthouse">lighthouse at Cape Agulhas</a> has guided many ships around the cape over the years.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The sea off Cape Agulhas is notorious for winter storms and mammoth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freak_wave" title="Freak wave">rogue waves</a>, which can range up to 30 metres (100 ft) high and can sink even large ships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Econ1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Econ1-9">[9]</a></sup> Over the past few hundred years it has been believed that around 150 ships have sunk around Agulhas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> These conditions are caused by a number of factors. The naturally strong winds of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roaring_forties" title="Roaring forties">roaring forties</a>, which blow from west to east, and the cold <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circumpolar_Current" title="Antarctic Circumpolar Current">Antarctic Circumpolar Current</a> flowing in the same direction, come up against the warmer <a href="/wiki/Agulhas_Current" title="Agulhas Current">Agulhas Current</a> in the region of the cape. These conflicting currents of water of different densities, and the west winds blowing against the Agulhas Current, can create extremely hazardous wave conditions; these are further exacerbated by the shallow waters of the <a href="/wiki/Agulhas_Bank" title="Agulhas Bank">Agulhas Bank</a>, a broad, shallow part of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelf</a> which juts 250 kilometres (155 mi) south from the cape, after which it falls steeply away to the <a href="/wiki/Abyssal_plain" title="Abyssal plain">abyssal plain</a>.</p>
<p>These hazards have combined to make the cape notorious among sailors. The coast here is littered with wrecks: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arniston_(ship)" title="Arniston (ship)">Arniston</a></i> (1815), <i>Cooranga</i> (1964), <i>Elise</i> (1879), <i>Federal Lakes</i> (1975), <i>Geortyrder</i> (1849), <i>Gouritz</i> (1981), and <i>Gwendola</i> (1968) are just a few of the vessels lost in the proximity of the "Cape of Needles".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wreck_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-wreck-11">[11]</a></sup> Owing to the hazards and following the loss of several vessels, notably the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arniston_(ship)" title="Arniston (ship)"><i>Arniston</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Agulhas_Lighthouse" title="Cape Agulhas Lighthouse">a lighthouse</a> was built in 1848.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The lighthouse now plays host to a museum and a small rustic restaurant.</p>
