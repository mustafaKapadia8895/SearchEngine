<p><b>Hurricane Alex</b> was the first <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic hurricane</a> in January since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Alice_(December_1954)" title="Hurricane Alice (December 1954)">Hurricane Alice</a> in <a href="/wiki/1955_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1955 Atlantic hurricane season">1955</a>, and the first to form in the month since <a href="/wiki/1938_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_One" title="1938 Atlantic hurricane season">1938</a>. The first <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2016 Atlantic hurricane season">2016 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Alex originated as an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on January 7, 2016. The system initially traveled northeast, passing <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on January 8, before turning southeast. It subsequently deepened and acquired hurricane-force winds by January 10. Slight weakening took place thereafter, and the system eventually turned east and northeast as it acquired <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical characteristics</a>. On January 12, it developed into a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclone</a> well south of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>, becoming the first tropical or subtropical system during January in the North Atlantic since an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1978_AHS#Subtropical_Storm_One" title="1978 AHS">unnamed storm in 1978</a>. As it turned north-northeast, Alex transitioned into a full-fledged tropical cyclone on January 14 and became a hurricane. The storm peaked as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_1" title="SSHS">Category 1</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> of 85 mph (140 km/h) and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 981 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>; 28.97 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_Mercury" title="Inches of Mercury">inHg</a>). After weakening slightly, Alex made <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> on <a href="/wiki/Terceira_Island" title="Terceira Island">Terceira Island</a> as a tropical storm the next day. Concurrently, Alex began transitioning back into an extratropical cyclone; it completed this cycle hours after moving away from the Azores. The system ultimately merged with another extratropical cyclone over the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Sea" title="Labrador Sea">Labrador Sea</a> on January 17.</p>
<p>The hurricane prompted the issuance of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane and tropical storm warnings</a> for the Azores and the closure of schools and businesses. Alex ultimately brought gusty winds and heavy rain to the archipelago, causing generally minor damage. One person died from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_attack" title="Heart attack">heart attack</a> when the inclement weather prevented a helicopter from transporting them to a hospital.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alex_(2016)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As currently defined, the Atlantic hurricane season lasts from June 1 to November 30, the period in which tropical cyclones are most likely to develop across the basin. Occasionally, systems <a href="/wiki/List_of_off-season_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of off-season Atlantic hurricanes">develop outside the season</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_G1-1">[1]</a></sup> most frequently in May or December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup> Activity in January is considered extremely rare, with only five other known instances since 1851: an <a href="/wiki/1938_January_hurricane" title="1938 January hurricane">unnamed hurricane in 1938</a>, an <a href="/wiki/1951_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_One" title="1951 Atlantic hurricane season">unnamed tropical storm in 1951</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Alice_(December_1954)" title="Hurricane Alice (December 1954)">Hurricane Alice in 1954–55</a>, an <a href="/wiki/1978_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Subtropical_Storm_One" title="1978 Atlantic hurricane season">unnamed storm in 1978</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Zeta" title="Tropical Storm Zeta">Tropical Storm Zeta in 2005–06</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU3_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WU3-3">[3]</a></sup> Since Alice originated in December 1954, Alex is only the second hurricane to develop solely within January, after the 1938 storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU3_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-WU3-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D4_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-D4-4">[4]</a></sup> When Alex made landfall on <a href="/wiki/Terceira_Island" title="Terceira Island">Terceira</a> as a strong tropical storm this marked only the second time that an Atlantic tropical cyclone has made landfall in January, with the other being Hurricane Alice of 1955 which made landfall on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">Saint Martin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">Saba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WU-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition to forming well outside of hurricane season, Alex developed unusually far north and east, becoming only the second hurricane to form north of <a href="/wiki/30th_parallel_north" title="30th parallel north">30°N</a> and east of <a href="/wiki/30th_meridian_west" title="30th meridian west">30°W</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unrelated to Alex, <a href="/wiki/2016_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Pali" title="2016 Pacific hurricane season">Hurricane Pali</a> developed over the Central Pacific in early January, and persisted through the formation of Alex. This marked the first known occurrence of simultaneous January tropical cyclones between the two basins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU3_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-WU3-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alex_(2016)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alex_2016_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1945" data-file-width="2700" height="158" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Alex_2016_track.png/220px-Alex_2016_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Alex_2016_track.png/330px-Alex_2016_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Alex_2016_track.png/440px-Alex_2016_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alex_2016_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In early January 2016, a <a href="/wiki/Stationary_front" title="Stationary front">stationary front</a> was draped across the western <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>; a <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">non-tropical</a> low developed along this boundary over northwestern <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> by January 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIMSS_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIMSS-8">[8]</a></sup> Moving northeast ahead of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical_jet_stream" title="Subtropical jet stream">subtropical jet stream</a>, the system acquired a surface vortex the following day to the northeast of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-WU1-9">[9]</a></sup> Initial conditions, including strong <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, low <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">sea surface temperatures</a>, and dry air,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWO1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-TWO1-10">[10]</a></sup> inhibited formation of a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> as the storm moved northeast toward <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-WU1-9">[9]</a></sup> The large system featured <a href="/wiki/Gale" title="Gale">gale</a>-force winds, with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> of 60–65 mph (95–100 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWO1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-TWO1-10">[10]</a></sup> On January 8, the system passed about 75 mi (120 km) north of Bermuda, bringing strong winds and heavy rain to the islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TRG1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-TRG1-11">[11]</a></sup> On January 9, an anomalous <a href="/wiki/Block_(meteorology)" title="Block (meteorology)">blocking pattern</a> prevented the cyclone from continuing along a seasonal northeasterly track. Instead, the storm turned east-southeast into a region slightly more favorable for <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical development</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWO3_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-TWO3-12">[12]</a></sup> Hurricane-force winds developed around the core of the cyclone on January 10 as it deepened to 979 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>; 28.91 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_Mercury" title="Inches of Mercury">inHg</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> though <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">convective activity</a> near the center remained sparse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWO4_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-TWO4-13">[13]</a></sup> Separated from the <a href="/wiki/Jet_stream" title="Jet stream">jet stream</a>, the cyclone dove south-southeast over the central Atlantic around a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">mid-latitude trough</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU3_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-WU3-3">[3]</a></sup> Slight weakening took place on January 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWO5_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-TWO5-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Substantial structural changes took place on January 12: <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">frontal boundaries</a> separated from the core of the cyclone; convection developed atop the circulation; and it acquired a weak warm-core. All these factors, alongside the system being co-located with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper-level_low" title="Upper-level low">upper-level low</a>, indicated its transition into a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclone</a> by 18:00 UTC. Upon doing so, the system <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">received the name</a> <i>Alex</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> Though relatively shallow, owing to the seasonally low level of the <a href="/wiki/Tropopause" title="Tropopause">tropopause</a>, convection was deemed deep enough for classification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-D1-15">[15]</a></sup> At this time, the subtropical storm was situated 1,150 mi (1,850 km) west-southwest of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> The trough that previously enabled the sharp southward turn later steered Alex to the east-northeast and later north-northeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D1_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-D1-15">[15]</a></sup> Operationally, Alex was not classified as a subtropical storm until late on January 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D1_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-D1-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye feature</a> soon appeared within a complex of several banding features, marking intensification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-D2-16">[16]</a></sup> The 20 mi (25 km) wide feature cleared out early on January 14 and was surrounded by a ring of −75 °F (−60 °C) cloud tops. As it moved away from the upper-level low it was previously situated under, Alex acquired a deeper warm core and upper-level <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> became established, indicating the system was becoming more tropical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D3_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-D3-17">[17]</a></sup> Despite moving over 72 °F (22 °C) waters, Alex continued to deepen and transitioned into a full-fledged tropical cyclone by 06:00 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> The transition was enabled by colder-than-average <a href="/wiki/Troposphere#Temperature" title="Troposphere">upper-tropospheric temperatures</a> which created greater <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_instability" title="Atmospheric instability">instability</a> than would otherwise be expected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D4_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-D4-4">[4]</a></sup> Upon transitioning into a tropical cyclone, satellite intensity estimates—using the <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak technique</a>—indicated Alex achieved hurricane strength. Soon thereafter, Alex achieved its peak intensity as a tropical cyclone with winds of 85 mph (140 km/h) and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 981 mbar (hPa; 28.97 inHg); this made it a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_1" title="SSHS">Category 1</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-11"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>As Alex moved north toward the Azores, decreasing sea surface temperatures and increasing wind shear caused it to weaken late on January 14 and into January 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D5_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-D5-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D6_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-D6-19">[19]</a></sup> The hurricane's eyewall opened up and its convective structure decayed, indicating the start of its transition back into an extratropical cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D6_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-D6-19">[19]</a></sup> Becoming increasingly tilted with height due to shear,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D8_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-D8-20">[20]</a></sup> Alex weakened to a tropical storm before making <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> over <a href="/wiki/Terceira_Island" title="Terceira Island">Terceira Island</a> at 13:15 UTC with winds of 65 mph (100 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-12"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> Less than five hours later, the system completed its transition into an extratropical cyclone, with its circulation becoming more elongated and the <a href="/wiki/Radius_of_maximum_wind" title="Radius of maximum wind">radius of maximum winds</a> expanding significantly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-13"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> Furthermore, the overall structure became more "comma shaped", a feature of frontal systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D9_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-D9-21">[21]</a></sup> Turning northwest, the system deepened slightly to 978 mbar (hPa; 28.88 inHg) as it approached <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>. Hurricane-force winds developed along the southeastern coast of Greenland due to interaction with the island's mountains, rather than the result of the storm intensifying. By 06:00 UTC on January 17, the remnants of Alex were absorbed by a larger extratropical low over the North Atlantic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-14"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alex_(2016)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bermuda">Bermuda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alex_(2016)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bermuda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The precursor to Alex brought <a href="/wiki/Gale" title="Gale">gale</a>-force winds and heavy rain to Bermuda on January 8. Gusts to 60 mph (97 km/h) disrupted air travel, downed trees, and left sporadic power outages, while waves as high as 20 ft (6 m) necessitated <a href="/wiki/Small_craft_advisory" title="Small craft advisory">small craft advisories</a> for the islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TRG1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-TRG1-11">[11]</a></sup> Ferry services to and from Cavello Bay, Dockyard, Belmont, Hodsdon’s Ferry, and Lower Ferry were suspended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> During the three days in which the system impacted the territory, 1.33 in (34 mm) of rain fell at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bermuda_International_Airport" title="Bermuda International Airport">Bermuda International Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Azores">Azores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alex_(2016)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Azores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3508" data-file-width="4258" height="181" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg/220px-Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg/330px-Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg/440px-Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alex%27s_final_waves_over_Azores.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rough surf from Hurricane Alex on São Miguel Island, in the Azores</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Upon Alex's classification as a hurricane on January 14, the Azores Meteorological Service issued a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">hurricane warning</a> for the islands of <a href="/wiki/Faial_Island" title="Faial Island">Faial</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pico_Island" title="Pico Island">Pico</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Jorge_Island" title="São Jorge Island">São Jorge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Graciosa" title="Graciosa">Graciosa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Terceira_Island" title="Terceira Island">Terceira</a>. They also issued a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">tropical storm warning</a> for <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Miguel_Island" title="São Miguel Island">São Miguel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Maria_Island" title="Santa Maria Island">Santa Maria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PA4_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-PA4-24">[24]</a></sup> A red alert—the highest stage for meteorological warnings—was declared for central and eastern islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Publico_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Publico-25">[25]</a></sup> Homeowners protected doors and windows with plywood and sandbags in anticipation of damaging winds and flooding. Officials closed schools and administrative buildings for the duration of the hurricane on January 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/SATA_Air_A%C3%A7ores" title="SATA Air Açores">SATA Air Açores</a> cancelled 33 domestic and international flights for the morning of January 15, stranding more than 700 passengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The hurricane and tropical storm warnings were discontinued after Alex's passage on January 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PA8_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-PA8-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first hurricane to impact the Azores since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_AHS#Hurricane_Gordon" title="2012 AHS">Hurricane Gordon</a> in 2012, Alex brought heavy rain and gusty winds to the archipelago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-26">[26]</a></sup> It is the only known hurricane to track within 230 mi (370 km) of the Azores outside of August and September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWC_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-TWC-30">[30]</a></sup> Rainfall totals reached 4.04 in (103 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Lagoa,_Azores" title="Lagoa, Azores">Lagoa</a>, São Miguel,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and 3.71 in (94 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Angra_do_Hero%C3%ADsmo" title="Angra do Heroísmo">Angra do Heroísmo</a>, Terceira.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Wind gusts exceeded 50 mph (80 km/h) on Santa Maria Island and peaked at 57 mph (92 km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Ponta_Delgada" title="Ponta Delgada">Ponta Delgada</a>, São Miguel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIMSS_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-CIMSS-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWC_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-TWC-30">[30]</a></sup> The strong winds brought down trees, inflicted damage on some roofs, and triggered scattered power outages. Additionally, minor flooding was observed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Six homes in Ponta Delgada sustained flood damage and another had its roof destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Landslide" title="Landslide">Landslides</a> occurred across parts of the central islands, though they caused only limited damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Publico_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Publico-25">[25]</a></sup> The overall effects of the storm were less than initially feared,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_26-3"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-26">[26]</a></sup> possibly due to the maximum winds being located far from the center as Alex underwent extratropical transition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_7-15"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> The hurricane indirectly led to one death—the person suffered a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_attack" title="Heart attack">heart attack</a> unrelated to the storm—when an Island Health Unit helicopter was unable to take off due to turbulent conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alex_(2016)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
