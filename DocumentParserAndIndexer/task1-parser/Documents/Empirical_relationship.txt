<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Empirical relationship</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>, an <b>empirical relationship</b> or <b>phenomenological relationship</b> is a relationship or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Correlation" title="Correlation">correlation</a> that is supported by experiment and observation but not necessarily supported by <a href="/wiki/Theory" title="Theory">theory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hall_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hall-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Analytical_solutions_without_a_theory">Analytical solutions without a theory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Empirical_relationship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Analytical solutions without a theory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An empirical relationship is supported by confirmatory data irrespective of theoretical basis such as <a href="/wiki/First_principle#In_physics" title="First principle">first principles</a>. Sometimes theoretical explanations for what were initially empirical relationships are found, in which case the relationships are no longer considered empirical. An example was the <a href="/wiki/Rydberg_formula" title="Rydberg formula">Rydberg formula</a> to predict the <a href="/wiki/Wavelength" title="Wavelength">wavelengths</a> of hydrogen <a href="/wiki/Spectral_line" title="Spectral line">spectral lines</a>. Proposed in 1876, it perfectly predicted the wavelengths of the <a href="/wiki/Lyman_series" title="Lyman series">Lyman series</a>, but lacked a theoretical basis until <a href="/wiki/Niels_Bohr" title="Niels Bohr">Niels Bohr</a> produced his <a href="/wiki/Bohr_model" title="Bohr model">Bohr model</a> of the atom in 1925.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McMullin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-McMullin-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On occasion, what was thought to be an empirical factor is later deemed to be a <a href="/wiki/Dimensionless_physical_constant" title="Dimensionless physical constant">fundamental physical constant</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Approximations">Approximations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Empirical_relationship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Approximations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some empirical relationships are merely approximations, often equivalent to the first few terms of the <a href="/wiki/Taylor_series" title="Taylor series">Taylor series</a> of the analytical solution describing the phenomenon.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Other relationships only hold under certain specific conditions, reducing them to special cases of more general relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McMullin_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-McMullin-2">[2]</a></sup> Some approximations, in particular <a href="/wiki/Phenomenological_model" title="Phenomenological model">phenomenological models</a>, may even contradict theory; they are employed because they are more mathematically tractable than some theories, and are able to yield results.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
