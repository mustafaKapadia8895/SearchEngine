<p>The <b>Straits of Florida</b>, <b>Florida Straits</b>, or <b>Florida Strait</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> located south-southeast of the North American <a href="/wiki/Mainland" title="Mainland">mainland</a>, generally accepted to be between the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, and between the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a> (U.S.) and <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. It is 150 km (93 mi) wide at the narrowest point between <a href="/wiki/Key_West" title="Key West">Key West</a> and the Cuban shore, and has been sounded to a depth of 6,000 feet (1,800 m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The strait carries the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Current" title="Florida Current">Florida Current</a>, the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>, from the Gulf of Mexico.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oil_and_gas">Oil and gas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Straits_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Oil and gas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Five wells were drilled in state waters south of the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a> from 1947 to 1962. <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Oil" title="Gulf Oil">Gulf Oil</a> drilled three wells in federal waters south of the Florida Keys in 1960 and 1961. All the wells were dry holes.</p>
<p>The boundary between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zones</a> of the US and Cuba is halfway between Cuba and Florida, as determined by a 1977 treaty between the US and Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Offshore_Cuba">Offshore Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Straits_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Offshore Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cuba has three producing <a href="/wiki/Offshore_drilling" title="Offshore drilling">offshore oil</a> fields within 5 km of its north coast opposite Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Geological_Survey" title="US Geological Survey">US Geological Survey</a> estimates that the North Cuba Basin contains 5,500,000,000 barrels (870,000,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of undiscovered petroleum liquids and 9.8 trillion cubic feet of natural gas, almost all in the offshore part of the basin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The issue of allowing oil and gas exploration offshore Florida became a hotly contested topic in the 2008 US elections. In a column published 5 June 2008, syndicated columnist <a href="/wiki/George_Will" title="George Will">George Will</a> wrote that a Chinese oil company was then drilling in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuban</a> waters 60 miles (97 km) from the Florida coast, a claim that was repeated by candidates in favor of offshore drilling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In fact, no drilling was then taking place in that part of Cuban waters.</p>
<p>In 2004 the Spanish oil company <a href="/wiki/Repsol" title="Repsol">Repsol-YPF</a> drilled in deep Cuban waters between Cuba and the Florida Keys, and found an oil deposit; the deposit was judged noncommercial, and the hole was plugged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In October 2008, Cuba signed an agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> state oil company <a href="/wiki/Petrobras" title="Petrobras">Petrobras</a>, which provides for Petrobras to drill for oil and gas in deep waters off the north shore of Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In July 2009, Cuba signed an agreement with the Russian government giving the Russian oil company <a href="/wiki/Zarubezhneft" title="Zarubezhneft">Zarubezhneft</a> oil exploration rights off the north shore of Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> By May 2011 Petrobas had withdrawn from the 2008 agreement due to poor prospects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Offshore_Bahamas">Offshore Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Straits_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Offshore Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009 the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands" title="Falkland Islands">Falkland Islands</a>-registered company Bahamas Petroleum Company Ltd. and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> company <a href="/wiki/Statoil" title="Statoil">Statoil</a> announced a joint venture to drill for oil in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamian</a> waters north of Cuba and southeast of Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The government of the Bahamas has indicated that applications for offshore drilling are on hold pending negotiations with Cuba, the United States, and the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a> on the exact boundaries between their respective <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
