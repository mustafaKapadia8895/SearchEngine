<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hurricane Able (1951)</h1>

<p><b>Hurricane Able</b> was a rare hurricane that formed outside the typical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricane</a> season. The first <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> in <a href="/wiki/1951_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1951 Atlantic hurricane season">1951</a>, Able developed from a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> of low pressure on May 15 about 300 miles (480 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. Initially <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical</a> in nature, Able acquired <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical characteristics</a> as it moved over the warm waters of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a> and attained hurricane status on May 17 off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. This made Able one of only four May Atlantic hurricanes on record. On May 22 Able reached peak winds of 90 mph (150 km/h) about 70 miles (115 km) off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras,_North_Carolina" title="Cape Hatteras, North Carolina">Cape Hatteras, North Carolina</a>. The hurricane weakened as it turned eastward, and became an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> on May 23.</p>
<p>Hurricane Able did not affect land significantly. In <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, the storm dropped light precipitation, while in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> it produced winds of up to 95 mph (152 km/h). From <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> through <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, Able produced higher than normal tides. No casualties were reported.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Able_(1951)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Able_1951_track.png"><img alt="The track of a major hurricane starts south of Bermuda, loops north near the Bahamas, and reaches peak intensity near the Carolinas before moving out to sea." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1669" data-file-width="2700" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Able_1951_track.png/220px-Able_1951_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Able_1951_track.png/330px-Able_1951_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Able_1951_track.png/440px-Able_1951_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Able_1951_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>An active <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> of low pressure exited the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a> on May 12, and by the following day it passed near Bermuda. Initially the trough was located only near the surface, though as cold air from behind it advanced eastward, the trough extended into the mid- to upper-levels of the atmosphere. By May 14 a closed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> developed in seclusion from the <a href="/wiki/Westerlies" title="Westerlies">westerlies</a>. Continued cold air from behind the trough, about 12° <a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit" title="Fahrenheit">F</a> (7° <a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">C</a>) colder than usual for the time of year, extended across the low in conjunction with warm upper levels and warm <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a>, resulting in large amounts of <a href="/wiki/Instability" title="Instability">instability</a>. The polar trough gradually weakened as the low organized, and on May 15 it developed into a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical depression</a> while located about 300 miles (480 km) south of Bermuda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Located under an upper-level low, the depression initially tracked briskly to the northwest, followed by a turn to the west-southwest on May 16 as the upper-level low turned southwestward. Later on the 16th, the depression approached the warmest waters of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>. Strong <a href="/wiki/Divergence" title="Divergence">divergence</a> from the upper-level low and a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">high pressure system</a> to its northeast provided favorable conditions towards <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">transitioning</a> into a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> and late on the 16th the depression attained winds of 40 mph (65 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup> The storm continued to intensify as it turned to the southwest, and early on May 17 a ship near the center reported about 55 mph (88 km/h) winds and up to 30 foot (9 m) waves while located about 125 miles (200 km) east of <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach,_Florida" title="Daytona Beach, Florida">Daytona Beach, Florida</a>, indicating the system transitioned into a tropical storm. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">Navy</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">reconnaissance squadron</a> flew into the system later that day, and reported a storm of full hurricane strength moving southward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Receiving the name <i>Able</i>, the small hurricane turned to the southeast and to the east while passing near or over the <a href="/wiki/Bahama_Banks" title="Bahama Banks">Bahama Banks</a>. Able subsequently turned to the northeast and later to the north, and finished executing its cyclonic loop on May 20. The hurricane continued to intensify with an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> 20 miles (32 km) in diameter, and on May 21 Reconnaissance Aircraft estimated the hurricane attained peak winds of 90 mph (150 km/h). Shortly thereafter Able passed about 70 miles (110 km) east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras,_North_Carolina" title="Cape Hatteras, North Carolina">Cape Hatteras, North Carolina</a>, and early on May 22 Able weakened from its peak intensity after turning to the east. On May 23 it deteriorated into a tropical storm while passing over cooler waters, and that night Able became an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> while located about 520 miles (840 km) south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Halifax" title="City of Halifax">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a>. The extratropical remnant turned to the northeast before losing its identity late on May 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_records">Impact and records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Able_(1951)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact and records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Storm warnings</a> were issued from <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Pierce,_Florida" title="Fort Pierce, Florida">Fort Pierce, Florida</a> subsequent to the discovery of the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap517_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap517-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NOAA" title="NOAA">National Weather Bureau</a> advised all small craft to remain at port<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi518_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi518-5">[5]</a></sup> and recommended residents in the northern Bahamas to take immediate precautions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap518_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap518-6">[6]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Grand_Bahama" title="Grand Bahama">Grand Bahama</a>, the threat of Hurricane Able resulted in a construction crew to evacuate further inland; the crew was constructing an observation post for guiding long-range missiles from <a href="/wiki/Cape_Canaveral" title="Cape Canaveral">Cape Canaveral</a>. Fishermen on dozens of boats left the open ocean for safety on two Bahamian islands, while two airplanes safely rode out the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap519_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap519-7">[7]</a></sup> Small craft warnings and storm warnings were later issued from <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> through <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap521_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap521-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The outer <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">rainbands</a> of Able produced light rainfall and high seas along the Florida coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-up518_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-up518-9">[9]</a></sup> While moving slowly near the Bahamas, Able produced strong winds reaching 90 to 95 mph (145 to 152 km/h) at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Walker_Cay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Walker Cay (page does not exist)">Walker Cay</a>, and minimal hurricane force on <a href="/wiki/Grand_Bahama" title="Grand Bahama">Grand Bahama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abaco_Islands" title="Abaco Islands">Little Abaco Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-3">[3]</a></sup> The hurricane produced high surf in <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington, North Carolina</a> and abnormally high tides northward through <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, though no damage was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap521_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap521-8">[8]</a></sup> A ship called "City of Eastbourne" sailed through the storm and reported hurricane-force winds, along with "mountainous, confused seas and driving rain."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Able is one of only four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricanes</a> on record to exist during the month of May, the others occurring in <a href="/wiki/1889_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1889 Atlantic hurricane season">1889</a>, <a href="/wiki/1908_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1908 Atlantic hurricane season">1908</a>, and <a href="/wiki/1970_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Alma" title="1970 Atlantic hurricane season">1970</a>. None of the others reached winds of more than 80 mph (130 km/h). It was described as "wrong way, wrong time", referring to its unusual motion to the southwest near the Bahamas, as well as its presence outside the normal hurricane season. Additionally, the Weather Bureau described it as a "freak of freaks".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
