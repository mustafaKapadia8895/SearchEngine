<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mafic</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p><b>Mafic</b> is an adjective describing a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silicate_mineral" title="Silicate mineral">silicate mineral</a> or <a href="/wiki/Igneous_rock" title="Igneous rock">igneous rock</a> that is rich in <a href="/wiki/Magnesium" title="Magnesium">magnesium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a>, and is thus a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of <b>ma</b>gnesium and <a href="/wiki/Ferric" title="Ferric"><b>f</b>err<b>ic</b></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Most mafic minerals are dark in color, and common rock-forming mafic minerals include <a href="/wiki/Olivine" title="Olivine">olivine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pyroxene" title="Pyroxene">pyroxene</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amphibole" title="Amphibole">amphibole</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Biotite" title="Biotite">biotite</a>. Common mafic rocks include <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">basalt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diabase" title="Diabase">diabase</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabbro" title="Gabbro">gabbro</a>. Mafic rocks often also contain calcium-rich varieties of <a href="/wiki/Plagioclase" title="Plagioclase">plagioclase</a> feldspar.</p>
<p>Chemically, mafic rocks are on the other side of the rock spectrum from the <a href="/wiki/Felsic" title="Felsic">felsic</a> rocks. The term roughly corresponds to the older <b>basic rock</b> class.</p>
<p>Mafic <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">lava</a>, before cooling, has a low <a href="/wiki/Viscosity" title="Viscosity">viscosity</a>, in comparison with felsic lava, due to the lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silica" title="Silica">silica</a> content in mafic <a href="/wiki/Magma" title="Magma">magma</a>. Water and other volatiles can more easily and gradually escape from mafic lava. As a result, eruptions of volcanoes made of mafic lavas are less explosively violent than felsic-lava eruptions. Most mafic-lava volcanoes are <a href="/wiki/Shield_volcano" title="Shield volcano">shield volcanoes</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> like those in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>.</p>
