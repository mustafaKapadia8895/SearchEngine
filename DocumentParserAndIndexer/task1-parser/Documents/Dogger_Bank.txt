<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Dogger Bank</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<p><b>Dogger Bank</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>Doggersbank</i>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i>Doggerbank</i>, <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>: <i>Doggerbanke</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Shoal" title="Shoal">sandbank</a> in a shallow area of the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> about 100 kilometres (62Â mi) off the east coast of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.</p>
<p>During the last ice age the bank was part of a large landmass connecting Europe and the British Isles, now known as <a href="/wiki/Doggerland" title="Doggerland">Doggerland</a>. It has long been known by fishermen to be a productive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fishing_bank" title="Fishing bank">fishing bank</a>; it was named after the <i><a href="/wiki/Dogger_(boat)" title="Dogger (boat)">doggers</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Old_Dutch" title="Old Dutch">Old Dutch</a> fishing boats especially used for catching <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a>.</p>
<p>At the beginning of the 21st century the area was identified as a potential site for a UK <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Round_3_wind_farm" title="Round 3 wind farm">round 3 wind farm</a>, being developed as <a href="/wiki/Dogger_Bank_Wind_Farm" title="Dogger Bank Wind Farm">Dogger Bank Wind Farm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dogger_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:North_Sea_map-en.png"><img alt="North Sea map-en.png" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3100" data-file-width="2475" height="276" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/North_Sea_map-en.png/220px-North_Sea_map-en.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/North_Sea_map-en.png/330px-North_Sea_map-en.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/North_Sea_map-en.png/440px-North_Sea_map-en.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:North_Sea_map-en.png" title="Ingrandisci"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The bank extends over about 17,600 square kilometres (6,800Â sqÂ mi), and is about 260Â km long by 97Â km broad (160 by 60 mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stride_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stride-2">[2]</a></sup> The water depth ranges from 15 to 36 metres (49 to 118Â ft), about 20 metres (66Â ft) shallower than the surrounding sea.</p>
<p>The bank is an important <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a> area, with <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">herring</a> being caught in large numbers. It gives its name to the Dogger sea area used in the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a> <a href="/wiki/Shipping_Forecast" title="Shipping Forecast">Shipping Forecast</a>. Several <a href="/wiki/Shipwreck" title="Shipwreck">shipwrecks</a> lie on the bank.</p>
<p>Dogger Bank has been identified as an oceanic environment that exhibits high primary productivity throughout the year in the form of <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">phytoplankton</a>. As such, it has been proposed by various groups to designate the area a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_Nature_Reserve" title="Marine Nature Reserve">Marine Nature Reserve</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dogger_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Geologically, the feature is most likely a <a href="/wiki/Moraine" title="Moraine">moraine</a>, formed during the <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stride_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stride-2">[2]</a></sup> At differing times during the <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">last glacial period</a> it was either joined to the mainland or an island. The bank was part of a large landmass, now known as <a href="/wiki/Doggerland" title="Doggerland">Doggerland</a>, which connected Britain to the European mainland until it was flooded some time after the end of the last glacial period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-spin-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">Fishing trawlers</a> working the area have dredged up large amounts of moor <a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">peat</a>, remains of <a href="/wiki/Mammoth" title="Mammoth">mammoth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rhinoceros" title="Rhinoceros">rhinoceros</a>, and occasionally <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">Paleolithic</a> hunting artefacts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/1931_Dogger_Bank_earthquake" title="1931 Dogger Bank earthquake">1931 Dogger Bank earthquake</a> took place below the bank, measuring 6.1 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richter_scale" title="Richter scale">Richter scale</a> and was the largest earthquake ever recorded in the United Kingdom. Its <a href="/wiki/Hypocenter" title="Hypocenter">hypocentre</a> was 23 kilometres (14Â mi) beneath the bank, and the quake was felt in countries all around the North Sea, causing damage across eastern England.</p>
<p>South of Dogger Bank is the <a href="/wiki/Cleaver_Bank" title="Cleaver Bank">Cleaver Bank</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naval_battles_and_incidents">Naval battles and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dogger_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Naval battles and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dogger_Bank_(1696)" title="Battle of Dogger Bank (1696)">Battle of Dogger Bank (1696)</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Years%27_War" title="Nine Years' War">Nine Years' War</a> a French fleet under the command of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Bart" title="Jean Bart">Jean Bart</a> was victorious over the ships of a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> force of five ships and the <a href="/wiki/Convoy" title="Convoy">convoy</a> it was escorting.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dogger_Bank_(1781)" title="Battle of Dogger Bank (1781)">Battle of Dogger Bank (1781)</a>, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_of_American_Independence" title="War of American Independence">War of American Independence</a>, a Royal Navy squadron fought a Dutch squadron on 5 August 1781.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dogger_Bank_incident" title="Dogger Bank incident">Dogger Bank incident</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a>, Russian naval ships opened fire on British fishing boats in the on 21 October 1904, mistaking them for Japanese torpedo boats.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dogger_Bank_(1915)" title="Battle of Dogger Bank (1915)">Battle of Dogger Bank (1915)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dogger_Bank_(1916)" title="Battle of Dogger Bank (1916)">Battle of Dogger Bank (1916)</a>, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>, saw battles between the Royal Navy and the German High Seas Fleet.</li>
<li>In 1966, the German submarine <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_submarine_U-2365#Post_war_service" title="German submarine U-2365">U-Hai</a></i>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Type_XXIII_submarine" title="German Type XXIII submarine">German Type XXIII submarine</a> sank during a gale. 19 of 20 men died, one of the worst peacetime naval disasters in German history.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wind_farm_and_wind_power_hub">Wind farm and wind power hub</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dogger_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wind farm and wind power hub">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dogger_Bank_Wind_Farm" title="Dogger Bank Wind Farm">Dogger Bank Wind Farm</a></div>
<p>The Dogger Bank is an attractive location for offshore wind farms because it is far away from shore, avoiding complaints about the visual impact of wind turbines, yet the water is shallow enough for traditional fixed foundation wind turbine designs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Fixed-foundation wind turbines are economically limited to maximum water depths of 40m to 50m<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>, at greater water depths new <a href="/wiki/Floating_wind_turbine" title="Floating wind turbine">floating wind turbine</a> designs are required, which currently cost significantly more to build.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2010, a licence to develop a <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farm</a> on Dogger Bank was granted to <a href="/wiki/Forewind" title="Forewind">Forewind</a> Ltd, a consortium of developers. Originally projected to develop up to 9 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gigawatt" title="Gigawatt">gigawatts</a> of power as part of a planned nine zone project of 32 gigawatts, the plan was later scaled down to a 7.2-gigawatt installation in agreement with the area's owner <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_Estates" title="Crown Estates">Crown Estates</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Construction was scheduled to start around 2014 at the earliest, but has been repeatedly postponed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dutch, German, and Danish electrical grid operators are cooperating in a project to build a <a href="/wiki/North_Sea_Wind_Power_Hub" title="North Sea Wind Power Hub">North Sea Wind Power Hub</a> complex on one or more artificial islands to be constructed on Dogger Bank as part of a European system for sustainable electricity. At the North Seas Energy Forum in Brussels on 23 March 2017, <a href="/wiki/Energinet.dk" title="Energinet.dk">Energinet.dk</a> will sign a contract to work with the German and Dutch branches of <a href="/wiki/TenneT" title="TenneT">TenneT</a>; thereafter a feasibility study will be produced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
