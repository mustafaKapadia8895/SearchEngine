<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gale warning</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>Weather services issue a <b>gale warning</b> for maritime locations currently or imminently experiencing winds of <a href="/wiki/Gale" title="Gale">Gale</a> Force on the <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">Beaufort scale</a>. Gale warnings (and gale watches) allow mariners to take precautionary actions to ensure their safety at sea or to seek safe anchorage and ride out the storm on land. Though usually associated with deep <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">low-pressure areas</a>, winds strong enough to catalyze a gale warning can occur in other conditions too, including from <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclones</a>, or high-pressure systems, in the continental interior.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._Usage">U.S. Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gale_warning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: U.S. Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maritime">Maritime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gale_warning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Maritime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> issues gale warnings for marine areas (oceans, sounds, estuaries, and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a>) experiencing, or about to experience, winds within the range of 34 knots (63 km/h; 39 mph) to 47 knots (87 km/h; 54 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In United States maritime warning flag systems, two red pennants indicate a gale warning; the use of one such flag denotes a <a href="/wiki/Small_craft_advisory" title="Small craft advisory">small craft advisory</a>.</p>
<p>The National Weather Service issues a <b><a href="/wiki/Storm_warning" title="Storm warning">storm warning</a></b> for higher winds of 48 knots (89 km/h; 55 mph) to 63 knots (117 km/h; 72 mph) at sea. In the event of a tropical cyclone, however, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">tropical storm warning</a> replaces the gale warning or storm warning. The storm-warning maritime flag also replaces the gale-warning flag, regardless of the strength or weakness of the tropical storm.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Land">Land</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gale_warning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Land">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> issues a similar <b>high wind warning</b> (<a href="/wiki/Specific_Area_Message_Encoding" title="Specific Area Message Encoding">Specific Area Message Encoding</a> code: HWW) for high winds on land. The criteria vary from place to place; however, in most cases, the warning applies to winds of 40 miles per hour (64 km/h) to 57 miles per hour (92 km/h) for at least 1 hour; <i>or</i> any gusts of 58 miles per hour (93 km/h) to 73 miles per hour (117 km/h) on land unless a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">tropical storm warning</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blizzard_warning" title="Blizzard warning">blizzard warning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Winter_storm_warning" title="Winter storm warning">winter storm warning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Severe_thunderstorm_warning" title="Severe thunderstorm warning">severe thunderstorm warning</a>, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dust_storm_warning" title="Dust storm warning">dust storm warning</a> covers the phenomenon.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="U.K._usage">U.K. usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gale_warning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: U.K. usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorological_Office" title="Meteorological Office">Meteorological Office</a> issues gale warnings, and radio broadcasts them four times a day at fixed times on 198 kHz in the <a href="/wiki/Shipping_Forecast" title="Shipping Forecast">Shipping Forecast</a>, part of the broadcast output of <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>. If a considerable time will intervene before the next Shipping Forecast, forecasters may issue an extra gale warning, read between programmes. The Meteorological Office issues warnings for sea areas surrounding the United Kingdom for all predictions of winds of <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">Beaufort scale</a> Force 8 or greater, the forecasts extending as far north as <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> and as far south as southern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Met_%C3%89ireann" title="Met Éireann">Met Éireann</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> meteorological office, issues an area forecast for the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Sea" title="Irish Sea">Irish Sea</a> and warnings for sea areas around Ireland with headlands of Ireland (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Fair_Head" title="Fair Head">Fair Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malin_Head" title="Malin Head">Malin Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mizen_Head" title="Mizen Head">Mizen Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carnsore_Point" title="Carnsore Point">Carnsore Point</a>) defining stretches of coast.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_Canada" title="Meteorological Service of Canada">Meteorological Service of Canada</a> issues a similar gale warning for lakes, oceans, and other marine areas within jurisdiction of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Example">Example</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gale_warning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Example">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following is an example of a Gale Warning issued by the National Weather Service office in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Webster" title="North Webster">North Webster, Indiana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<pre>
URGENT - MARINE WEATHER MESSAGE
NATIONAL WEATHER SERVICE NORTHERN INDIANA
901 PM EDT WED OCT 29 2014

LMZ043-046-300915-
/O.CON.KIWX.GL.W.0014.141031T1200Z-141101T0900Z/
NEW BUFFALO MI TO ST JOSEPH MI-MICHIGAN CITY IN TO NEW BUFFALO MI-
901 PM EDT WED OCT 29 2014

...GALE WARNING REMAINS IN EFFECT FROM 8 AM FRIDAY TO 5 AM EDT
SATURDAY...

* WINDS...NORTHWEST 15 TO 25 KNOTS WITH GUSTS TO 30 KNOTS BY
  DAYBREAK FRIDAY...VEERING NORTH TO 35 KNOT GALES WITH GUSTS TO
  45 KNOT GALES FRIDAY. NORTH WINDS DIMINISHING TO 20 TO 30
  KNOTS WITH GUSTS TO 35 KNOT GALES LATE FRIDAY NIGHT.

* WAVES..5 TO 8 FEET LATE THURSDAY NIGHT...BUILDING TO 14 TO 20
  FEET FRIDAY...SUBSIDING TO 8 TO 12 FEET LATE FRIDAY NIGHT.

PRECAUTIONARY/PREPAREDNESS ACTIONS...

A GALE WARNING MEANS WINDS OF 34 TO 47 KNOTS ARE IMMINENT OR
OCCURRING. OPERATING A VESSEL IN GALE CONDITIONS REQUIRES
EXPERIENCE AND PROPERLY EQUIPPED VESSELS. IT IS HIGHLY
RECOMMENDED THAT MARINERS WITHOUT THE PROPER EXPERIENCE SEEK SAFE
HARBOR PRIOR TO THE ONSET OF GALE CONDITIONS.

&amp;&amp;

$$

STAY TUNED TO NOAA WEATHER RADIO FOR FURTHER DETAILS OR
UPDATES...OR CHECK OUR WEB SITE AT WEATHER.GOV/IWX/?N=MARINE


</pre>
