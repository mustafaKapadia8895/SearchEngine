<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Banqiao Dam</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Banqiao Reservoir Dam</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%9D%BF" title="wikt:板">板</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%A1%A5" title="wikt:桥">桥</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B0%B4" title="wikt:水">水</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%BA%93" title="wikt:库">库</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%A4%A7" title="wikt:大">大</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%9D%9D" title="wikt:坝">坝</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">板橋水庫大壩</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Bǎnqiáo Shuǐkù Dàbà</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Dam" title="Dam">dam</a> on the <b>River Ru</b> in <a href="/wiki/Zhumadian" title="Zhumadian">Zhumadian</a> City, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> province, China. Its <a href="/wiki/Dam_failure" title="Dam failure">failure</a> in 1975 caused more casualties than any other dam failure in history at an estimated 171,000 deaths and 11 million displaced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The dam was subsequently rebuilt.</p>
<p>The Banqiao dam and <b>Shimantan Reservoir Dam</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%9F%B3" title="wikt:石">石</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%BC%AB" title="wikt:漫">漫</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%BB%A9" title="wikt:滩">滩</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B0%B4" title="wikt:水">水</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%BA%93" title="wikt:库">库</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%A4%A7" title="wikt:大">大</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%9D%9D" title="wikt:坝">坝</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">石漫灘水庫大壩</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Shímàntān Shuǐkù Dàbà</span></i>) are among 62 <a href="/wiki/Dam" title="Dam">dams</a> in <a href="/wiki/Zhumadian" title="Zhumadian">Zhumadian</a> that <a href="/wiki/Catastrophic_failure" title="Catastrophic failure">failed catastrophically</a> or were intentionally destroyed in 1975 during <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Nina_(1975)" title="Typhoon Nina (1975)">Typhoon Nina</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Banqiao_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Banqiaomap.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1125" data-file-width="1078" height="313" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Banqiaomap.png/300px-Banqiaomap.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Banqiaomap.png/450px-Banqiaomap.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Banqiaomap.png/600px-Banqiaomap.png 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Banqiaomap.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Approximate location of Banqiao Dam</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Construction of the Banqiao dam began in April 1951 on the Ru River with the help of Soviet consultants as part of a project to control <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> and provide <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_power" title="Hydroelectric power">electrical power generation</a>. The construction was a response to severe flooding in the <a href="/wiki/Huai_River" title="Huai River">Huai River</a> Basin in 1949 and 1950.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The dam was completed in June 1952. Because of the absence of hydrology data, the design standard was lower than usual. After the 1954 <a href="/wiki/Huai_River" title="Huai River">Huai River</a> great flood, the upstream reservoirs including Banqiao were extended, constructed, and consolidated. Banqiao Dam was increased in height by three meters. The dam crest level was 116.34 meters above sea level and the crest level of the wave protection wall was 117.64 meters above sea level. The total capacity of the reservoir was 492 million m<sup>3</sup> (398,000 acre feet), with 375 million m<sup>3</sup> (304,000 acre feet) reserved for <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flood</a> storage. The dam was made of <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a> and was 24.5 meters high. The maximum discharge of the reservoir was 1742 m<sup>3</sup>/s.</p>
<p>Cracks in the <a href="/wiki/Dam" title="Dam">dam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sluice" title="Sluice">sluice gates</a> appeared after completion due to construction and engineering errors. They were repaired with the advice from <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> engineers and the new design, dubbed <i>the iron dam</i>, was considered unbreakable.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chen_Xing_(hydrologist)" title="Chen Xing (hydrologist)">Chen Xing</a> (陈惺), one of China's foremost <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrologist" title="Hydrologist">hydrologists</a>, was involved in the design of the dam. He was also a vocal critic of the government's dam building policy, which involved many dams in the basin. He had recommended 12 sluice gates for the Banqiao Dam, but this was criticized as being too conservative, and the number was reduced to five. Other dams in the project, including the Shimantan Dam, had a similar reduction of safety features and Chen was removed from the project. In 1961, after problems with the water system were revealed, he was brought back to help. Chen continued to be an outspoken critic of the system and was again removed from the project.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1975_Banqiao_Dam_Flood">1975 Banqiao Dam Flood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Banqiao_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1975 Banqiao Dam Flood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="472" data-file-width="700" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png/220px-Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png/330px-Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png/440px-Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Banqiao_Dam_Failture_Waterflow.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rough diagram of waterflow during the Banqiao Dam failure</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Officially, the dam failure was a natural as opposed to man-made disaster, with government sources placing an emphasis on the amount of rainfall as opposed to poor engineering and construction. The <i><a href="/wiki/People%27s_Daily" title="People's Daily">People's Daily</a></i> has maintained that the dam was designed to survive a once-in-1000-years flood (300 mm of rainfall per day) but a once-in-2000-years flood occurred in August 1975, following the collision of <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Nina_(1975)" title="Typhoon Nina (1975)">Typhoon Nina</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold front</a>. The typhoon was blocked for two days before its direction ultimately changed from northeastward to westward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As a result of this near stationary thunderstorm system, more than a year's worth of rain fell within 24 hours (new records were set, at 189.5 mm (7.46 inches) rainfall per hour and 1060 mm (41.73 inches) per day, exceeding the average annual precipitation of about 800 mm (31.5 inches)),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface-4">[4]</a></sup> which weather forecasts failed to predict.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/China_Central_Television" title="China Central Television">China Central Television</a> reported that the typhoon disappeared from radar as it degraded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xinhua" title="Xinhua">Xinhua</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> the forecast was for rainfall of 100 mm by the Beijing-based Central Meteorological Observatory.</p>
<p>Communication with the dam was largely lost due to wire failures. On August 6, a request to open the dam was rejected because of the existing flooding in downstream areas. On August 7 the request was accepted, but the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telegram" title="Telegram">telegrams</a> failed to reach the dam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The sluice gates were not able to handle the overflow of water partially due to <a href="/wiki/Sedimentation" title="Sedimentation">sedimentation</a> blockage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On August 7 at 21:30, the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> Unit 34450 (by name the 2nd Artillery Division in residence at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queshan" title="Queshan">Queshan</a> county), which was deployed on the Banqiao Dam, sent the first dam failure warning via telegraph. On August 8, at 1:00, water at the Banqiao crested at the 117.94 m level above sea level, or 0.3 meter higher than the wave protection wall on the dam, and it failed. The same storm caused the failure of 62 dams in total. The runoff of Banqiao Dam was 13,000 m<sup>3</sup> per second in vs. 78,800 m<sup>3</sup> per second out, and as a result 701 million m<sup>3</sup> of water were released in 6 hours,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface-4">[4]</a></sup> while 1.67 billion m<sup>3</sup> of water were released in 5.5 hours at an upriver Shimantan Dam, and 15.738 billion m<sup>3</sup> of water were released in total.</p>
<p>The resulting flood waters caused a wave 10 kilometers (6.2 mi) wide and 3–7 meters (9.8–23.0 ft) high in <a href="/wiki/Suiping_County" title="Suiping County">Suiping</a> (遂平) that rushed onto the plains below at nearly 50 kilometers per hour (31 mph), almost wiping out an area 55 kilometers (34 mi) long and 15 kilometers (9.3 mi) wide, and creating temporary lakes as large as 12,000 square kilometers (4,600 sq mi). Seven county seats, Suiping, <a href="/wiki/Xiping_County" title="Xiping County">Xiping</a> (西平), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ru%27nan_County" title="Ru'nan County">Ru'nan</a> (汝南), <a href="/wiki/Pingyu_County" title="Pingyu County">Pingyu</a> (平舆), <a href="/wiki/Xincai_County" title="Xincai County">Xincai</a> (新蔡), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luohe_County" title="Luohe County">Luohe</a> (漯河), and <a href="/wiki/Linquan_County" title="Linquan County">Linquan</a> (临泉) were inundated, as were thousands of square kilometers of countryside and countless communities. Evacuation orders had not been fully delivered due to weather conditions and poor communications. Telegraphs failed, signal flares fired by Unit 34450 were misunderstood, telephones were rare, and some messengers were caught by the flood. While only 827 out of 6,000 people died in the evacuated community of Shahedian just below Banqiao Dam, half of a total of 36,000 people died in the unevacuated Wencheng commune of Suipin County next to Shahedian, and the Daowencheng Commune was wiped from the map, killing all 9,600 citizens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface-4">[4]</a></sup> Although a large number of people were reported as lost at first, many of them later returned home. A 2005 book compiled by the Archives Bureau of Suiping county reports that more than 230,000 were carried away by water, in which 18,869 died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It has been reported that 90,000 - 230,000 people were killed as a result of the dam breaking.</p>
<p>To protect other dams from failure, several <a href="/wiki/Flood_control" title="Flood control">flood diversion areas</a> were evacuated and inundated, and several dams were deliberately destroyed by air strikes to release water in desired directions. The Nihewa and Laowangpo flood diversion areas downstream of the dams soon exceeded their capacity and gave up part of their storage on August 8, forcing more flood diversion areas to begin to evacuate. The dikes on the <a href="/wiki/Quan_River" title="Quan River">Quan River</a> collapsed in the evening of August 9, and the entire Linquan county in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuyang,_Anhui" title="Fuyang, Anhui">Fuyang, Anhui</a> was inundated. As the Boshan Dam, with a capacity of 400 million m<sup>3</sup>, crested and the water released from the failures of Banqiao and Shimantan was rushing downstream, air strikes were made against several other dams to protect the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Suya_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Suya Lake (page does not exist)">Suya Lake</a> dam, already holding 1.2 billion m<sup>3</sup> of water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Suya Lake won only a temporary reprieve, as both it and Boshan became eventual targets. Finally, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bantai_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bantai Dam (page does not exist)">Bantai Dam</a>, holding 5.7 billion m<sup>3</sup> of water, was bombed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jingguang_Railway" title="Jingguang Railway">Jingguang Railway</a>, a major artery from <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> to <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, was cut for 18 days, as were other crucial communications lines. Although 42,618 <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> troops were deployed for disaster relief, all communication to and from the cities was cut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface-4">[4]</a></sup> Nine days later there were still over a million people trapped by the waters, who relied on <a href="/wiki/Airdrop" title="Airdrop">airdrops</a> of food and were unreachable by disaster relief workers. Epidemics and famine devastated the trapped survivors. The damage of the <a href="/wiki/Zhumadian" title="Zhumadian">Zhumadian</a> area was estimated to be about CN¥3.5 billion (<span style="white-space: nowrap"><a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>513 million</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Zhumadian government appealed to the whole nation for help, and received more than CN¥300 million (<span style="white-space: nowrap">US$44,000,000</span>) in donations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_30_Year_Anniversary_of_the_August.2C_1975_flood_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_30_Year_Anniversary_of_the_August.2C_1975_flood-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the flood, a summit of National Flood Prevention and Reservoir Security at Zhengzhou, Henan was held by the Department of Water Conservancy and Electricity, and a nationwide reservoir security examination was performed. Chen Xing was again brought back to the project.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casualties">Casualties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Banqiao_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Casualties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the Hydrology Department of Henan Province, approximately 26,000 people died in the province<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> from <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> and another 145,000 died during subsequent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epidemics" title="Epidemics">epidemics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">famine</a>. In addition, about 5,960,000 buildings collapsed, and 11 million residents were affected. Unofficial estimates of the number of people killed by the disaster have run as high as 230,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The death toll of this disaster was <a href="/wiki/Declassification" title="Declassification">declassified</a> in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-After_30_years.2C_secrets.2C_lessons_of_China.27s_worst_dams_burst_accident_surface-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reconstruction">Reconstruction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Banqiao_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reconstruction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Within eleven years of the dam failure, the lower reach of the River Ru, esp. <a href="/wiki/Zhumadian" title="Zhumadian">Zhumadian</a> City, experienced several more disastrous floods. After many feasibility studies, the new Banqiao Reservoir reconstruction was listed as a key national project of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Five-Year_Plans_of_China#The_Seventh_Five-Year_Plan.2C_1986-1990" title="Five-Year Plans of China">The Seventh Five-Year Plan of China</a>. The project owner was <a href="/wiki/Huai_River_Water_Resources_Commission" title="Huai River Water Resources Commission">Huai River Water Resources Commission</a>. The construction contractor was <a href="/wiki/Gezhouba_Group" title="Gezhouba Group">Changjiang Gezhouba Engineering Bureau</a>. By the end of 1986, the rebuilding project commenced. On June 5, 1993, the project was certified by the Chinese government.</p>
<p>The reconstructed Banqiao Reservoir controls a catchment area of 768 km<sup>2</sup> (297 sq mi). The maximum reserve capacity is 675 million m<sup>3</sup> (178 billion gallons), a capacity increase of 34% above the capacity of the failed dam. The effective storage is 256 million m<sup>3</sup> (67.6 billion gallons) and the corresponding normal high water level is 111.5 m (366 ft) above sea level. The flood control storage is 457 million m<sup>3</sup> (121 billion gallons). The dam is made of <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a> and is 3,720 m (12,200 ft) long and 50.5 m (166 ft) high. The dam crest level is 120 m (390 ft) above sea level. The maximum discharge of the reservoir is 15000 m<sup>3</sup>/s (about 3.96 million gallons/s).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Banqiao_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the disaster of the Banqiao dam failure, the Chinese government became very focused on surveillance, repair, and consolidation of reservoir dams. China has 87,000 reservoirs across the country; most of which were built in the 1950s–1970s using low construction standards. Most of these reservoirs are in serious disrepair, posing challenges to the prevention and control of flood-triggered geological disasters in areas with a population of 130 million or more. China's medium and small rivers are considered to be the <a href="/wiki/Achilles%27_heel" title="Achilles' heel">Achilles' heel</a> in the country's river control systems. According to statistics from the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Water_Resources_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Water Resources of the People's Republic of China">Ministry of Water Resources</a>, China has invested CN¥64.9 billion (<span style="white-space: nowrap">US$9.72 billion</span>) since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1998_Yangtze_River_floods" title="1998 Yangtze River floods">1998 Yangtze River floods</a> in repairing and consolidating the country's 9,197 degraded reservoirs, of which 2,397 are large or medium-sized, and 6,800 are key small reservoirs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
