<p>An <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanographic</a> <b>water mass</b> is identifiable body of <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> with a common formation history which has physical properties distinct from surrounding water. Properties include <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salinity" title="Salinity">salinity</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical" title="Chemical">chemical</a> - <a href="/wiki/Isotope" title="Isotope">isotopic</a> ratios, and other physical quantities.</p>
<p>Water masses are generally distinguished not only by their respective <a href="/wiki/Flow_tracer" title="Flow tracer">tracers</a> (see above) but also by their location in the Worlds' oceans. Water masses are also distinguished by their vertical position, so that there are surface water masses, intermediate water masses and deep water masses. Common water masses in the world ocean are: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_Bottom_Water" title="Antarctic Bottom Water">Antarctic Bottom Water</a> (AABW), <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Deep_Water" title="North Atlantic Deep Water">North Atlantic Deep Water</a> (NADW), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circumpolar_Deep_Water" title="Circumpolar Deep Water">Circumpolar Deep Water</a> (CDW), <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Intermediate_Water" title="Antarctic Intermediate Water">Antarctic Intermediate Water</a> (AAIW), <a href="/wiki/Subantarctic_Mode_Water" title="Subantarctic Mode Water">Subantarctic Mode Water</a> (SAMW), <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Intermediate_Water" title="Arctic Intermediate Water">Arctic Intermediate Water</a> (AIW), the central waters of various <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_basin" title="Oceanic basin">oceanic basins</a>, and various ocean surface waters.</p>
