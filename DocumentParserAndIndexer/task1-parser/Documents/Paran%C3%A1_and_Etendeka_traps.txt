<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Paraná and Etendeka traps</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<p>The <b>Paraná-Etendeka traps</b> (or <b>Paraná and Etendeka Plateau</b>; or <b>Paraná and Etendeka Province</b>) comprise a <a href="/wiki/Large_igneous_province" title="Large igneous province">large igneous province</a> which includes both the main Paraná traps (in <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_Basin" title="Paraná Basin">Paraná Basin</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_American" title="South American">South American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geological_basin" title="Geological basin">geological basin</a>) as well as the smaller severed portions of the <a href="/wiki/Flood_basalt" title="Flood basalt">flood basalts</a> at the Etendeka traps (in northwest <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> and southwest <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>). The original basalt flows occurred 128 to 138 million years ago. The province had a post-flow surface area of 1.5 x 10<sup>6</sup> km² (580,000 miles²) and an original volume projected to be in excess of 2.3 x 10<sup>6</sup> km³.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The basalt samples at Paraná and Etendeka have an age of about 132 Ma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ar_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ar-3">[3]</a></sup> Indirectly, the rifting and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extension_(geology)" title="Extension (geology)">extension</a> are probably the origin of the Paraná and Etendeka traps and it could be the origin of the <a href="/wiki/Gough_Island" title="Gough Island">Gough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tristan_da_Cunha" title="Tristan da Cunha">Tristan da Cunha</a> Islands as well, as they are connected by the <a href="/wiki/Walvis_Ridge" title="Walvis Ridge">Walvis Ridge</a> (Gough/<a href="/wiki/Tristan_hotspot" title="Tristan hotspot">Tristan hotspot</a>). The seamounts of the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_Rise" title="Rio Grande Rise">Rio Grande Rise</a> (25°S to 35°S) that go eastwards from the Paraná side<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> are part of this traps system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Interpretations of geochemistry, including isotopes, have led geologists to conclude that the magmas forming the traps and associated <a href="/wiki/Igneous_rock" title="Igneous rock">igneous rocks</a> formed by melting of <a href="/wiki/Asthenosphere" title="Asthenosphere">asthenosphic mantle</a> due to the arrival of a <a href="/wiki/Mantle_plume" title="Mantle plume">mantle plume</a> to the base of Earth's <a href="/wiki/Lithosphere" title="Lithosphere">lithosphere</a>. Then much of the magma was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Crustal_contamination&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Crustal contamination (page does not exist)">contaminated</a> with <a href="/wiki/Continental_crust" title="Continental crust">crustal materials</a> prior to their eruption. Some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plutonic_rock" title="Plutonic rock">plutonic rocks</a> related to the traps escaped crustal contamination reflecting more directly the source of the magmas in the mantle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>A type of rock called <a href="/wiki/Ignimbrite" title="Ignimbrite">ignimbrite</a> is found in some parts of the traps indicating <a href="/wiki/Explosive_eruption" title="Explosive eruption">explosive</a> volcanic activity. The Paraná Traps possibly contains the site of the single <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_volcanic_eruptions" title="List of largest volcanic eruptions">largest explosive volcanic eruption</a> known in Earth's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bryan_et_al_2010_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bryan_et_al_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
