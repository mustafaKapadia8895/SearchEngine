<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Scotian Shelf</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Canadian_field-naturalist_(1967)_(20493806286).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1558" data-file-width="1916" height="285" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/The_Canadian_field-naturalist_%281967%29_%2820493806286%29.jpg/350px-The_Canadian_field-naturalist_%281967%29_%2820493806286%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/The_Canadian_field-naturalist_%281967%29_%2820493806286%29.jpg/525px-The_Canadian_field-naturalist_%281967%29_%2820493806286%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/The_Canadian_field-naturalist_%281967%29_%2820493806286%29.jpg/700px-The_Canadian_field-naturalist_%281967%29_%2820493806286%29.jpg 2x" width="350"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Canadian_field-naturalist_(1967)_(20493806286).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Scotian Shelf</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <b>Scotian Shelf</b> is a geological formation, part of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">Continental shelf</a>, located southwest of <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It covers an area of 120,000 km²,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup> is 700 km long and ranges in width from 120 to 240 km. It has an average depth of 90 metres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover-2">[2]</a></sup> The Scotian Shelf contains the ecologically important Scotian Shelf Large Marine Ecosystem (LME) and the Scotian Shelf Waters (SSW).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The northeastern boundary is defined by the <a href="/wiki/Laurentian_Channel" title="Laurentian Channel">Laurentian Channel</a>, where it drops off to 400 m. Further south is the continental slope, which sharply drops off to a depth of more than 3,000 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup> The southwestern boundary ends at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Northeast_Channel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Northeast Channel (page does not exist)">Northeast Channel</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Scotian Shelf is characterized by shallow, offshore banks 25 m to 100 m under the ocean surface, with deep basins and troughs between that vary in depth from 160 m to 300 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover-2">[2]</a></sup> These culminate at <a href="/wiki/Sable_Island" title="Sable Island">Sable Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>A southwesterly ocean current, (occasionally containing runoff from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_of_St_Lawrence" title="Gulf of St Lawrence">Gulf of St Lawrence</a>) flows over the inner shelf. The water flow over the banks is weaker and tends have greater variation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-P.C._Smith_And_R.J._Conover-2">[2]</a></sup> The Scotian Shelf contains a canyon called the "Gully", which is more than 1000 m deep. Currents flow through this canyon southward, mixing offshore waters with the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nova_Scotia_Current&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nova Scotia Current (page does not exist)">Nova Scotia Current</a>. This causes an increase in biological productivity toward the east, across the Continental Shelf.</p>
<p>The Scotian Shelf is heavily influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>, resulting in a variety of marine species being present which are normally found further south. These appear at regular intervals due to the main current spinning off cores of warm water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marine_life">Marine life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scotian_Shelf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Marine life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="505" data-file-width="820" height="135" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg/220px-StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg/330px-StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg/440px-StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:StLawrence_Gulf_bathymetry.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Bathymetry" title="Bathymetry">Bathymetry</a> near the Gulf of Saint Lawrence, including the Scotian Shelf.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Scotian Shelf Large Marine Ecosystem (LME) contains numerous species, including a broad range of shellfish and fishes that use the area as spawning and nursery grounds. This abundance is the reason that the Scotian Shelf is one of the Atlantic Ocean's most fished areas.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Right_whale" title="Right whale">right whale</a> has a critical habitat in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roseway_Basin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roseway Basin (page does not exist)">Roseway Basin</a>, the northeastern part of the Scotian Shelf. Approximately 30 percent of the known population uses this habitat throughout the course of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Northern_bottlenose_whale" title="Northern bottlenose whale">northern bottlenose whale</a> also lives in the Scotian Shelf Waters area, in particular, the Gully. About 230 individual specimens have been recorded there. Other species, such as the <a href="/wiki/Sperm_whale" title="Sperm whale">sperm whale</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harbour_seal" title="Harbour seal">harbour seal</a>, are also found in this region, including the <a href="/wiki/Grey_seal" title="Grey seal">grey seal</a>, which is common on Sable Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Various waterfowl use the coastal areas as a migratory staging area. Offshore areas are used by such birds as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dovekies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dovekies (page does not exist)">dovekies</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murres" title="Murres">murres</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shearwaters" title="Shearwaters">shearwaters</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_ducks" title="Sea ducks">sea ducks</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Common_eider" title="Common eider">common eider</a>, and alcids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
