<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">German Meteor expedition</h1>

<p>The <b>German Meteor expedition</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i>Deutsche Atlantik Expedition</i>) was an <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanographic</a> expedition that explored the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Atlantic" title="South Atlantic">South Atlantic</a> ocean from the equatorial region to <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> in 1925–1927. Depth soundings, water temperature studies, water samples, studies of marine life and atmospheric observations were conducted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stein_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stein-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expedition">Expedition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=German_Meteor_expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Expedition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The survey vessel <a href="/wiki/German_survey_ship_Meteor" title="German survey ship Meteor"><i>Meteor</i></a> left <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmshaven" title="Wilhelmshaven">Wilhelmshaven</a> on 16 April 1925 with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oceanographer" title="Oceanographer">oceanographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Merz" title="Alfred Merz">Alfred Merz</a> in charge of the expedition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DHI_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-DHI-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1703" data-file-width="1265" height="296" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg/220px-DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg/330px-DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg/440px-DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DAE_Reiseplan_orig.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Planned routeing of the expedition<br/>
<small>Alfred Merz, 1925</small></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The ship zigzaged between <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> and took cross sections of the South Atlantic between 20° North and 60° South. In January 1926 the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a> was transited; in March the same year a <a href="/wiki/Seamount" title="Seamount">seamount</a> was found and named <i>Meteor Bank</i> (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=German_Meteor_expedition&amp;params=48_16_S_08_16_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">48°16′S</span> <span class="longitude">08°16′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">48.267°S 8.267°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">-48.267; 8.267</span></span></span></a></span>).</p>
<p>In June 1926 Merz, who already had health problems before the start of the expedition, was hospitalised at the German Hospital in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>. He died of <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> on 25 August 1926. The overall lead of the expedition was assumed by the ship's <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">captain</a> Fritz Spieß, while <a href="/wiki/Georg_W%C3%BCst" title="Georg Wüst">Georg Wüst</a> became chief oceanographer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DHI_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-DHI-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The expedition returned to Wilhelmshaven on 2 June 1927.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DHI_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-DHI-2">[2]</a></sup> In the course of the venture 67,000 depth soundings were made, more than 67,000 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (124,000 km; 77,000 mi) were sailed and more than 800 <a href="/wiki/Weather_balloon" title="Weather balloon">weather balloons</a> were launched.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stein_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stein-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=German_Meteor_expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1372" data-file-width="1684" height="179" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg/220px-The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg/330px-The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg/440px-The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Meteor_of_the_German_Meteor_expedition.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Meteor</i> at sea</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Meteor</i> was equipped with early <a href="/wiki/Sonar" title="Sonar">sonar</a> equipment with which it produced the first detailed survey of the south Atlantic Ocean floor. The survey established that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_ridge" title="Mid-Atlantic ridge">mid-Atlantic ridge</a> was continuous through the South Atlantic and continued into the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> beyond the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stein_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stein-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
