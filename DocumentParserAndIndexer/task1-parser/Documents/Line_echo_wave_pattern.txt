<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Line echo wave pattern</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Line_echo_wave_pattern.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="491" data-file-width="366" height="295" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Line_echo_wave_pattern.gif/220px-Line_echo_wave_pattern.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Line_echo_wave_pattern.gif/330px-Line_echo_wave_pattern.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2b/Line_echo_wave_pattern.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Line_echo_wave_pattern.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Radar schematic of a LEWP. This shape is a single wave along the pattern, for real LEWPs this shape is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tessellate" title="Tessellate">tessellated</a> repeatedly in a linear fashion</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A <b>line echo wave pattern</b> (<b>LEWP</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">weather radar</a> formation in which a single line of thunderstorms presenting multiple <a href="/wiki/Bow_echo" title="Bow echo">bow echoes</a> forms south (or equatorward) of a <a href="/wiki/Mesoscale_meteorology" title="Mesoscale meteorology">mesoscale</a> <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">low-pressure area</a> with a rotating "head".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> LEWP often are associated with a multiple-bow serial <a href="/wiki/Derecho" title="Derecho">derecho</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and often produce tornadoes, some of which can be strong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The existence of a LEWP on radar means that a serial <a href="/wiki/Derecho" title="Derecho">derecho</a> has developed or is likely to develop soon, much as a <a href="/wiki/Hook_echo" title="Hook echo">hook echo</a> indicates the same for a <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a>.</p>
<p>A LEWP, according to the NWAS, is defined as "a <a href="/wiki/Squall_line" title="Squall line">squall line</a> that has developed into a wave-like pattern due to acceleration at one end of the line and deceleration along the portion immediately adjacent."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Illinois-LEWP.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="502" data-file-width="512" height="216" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Illinois-LEWP.jpg/220px-Illinois-LEWP.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Illinois-LEWP.jpg/330px-Illinois-LEWP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Illinois-LEWP.jpg/440px-Illinois-LEWP.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Illinois-LEWP.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A LEWP in Illinois/Indiana and Arkansas, associated with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/April_19%E2%80%9324,_2011_tornado_outbreak_sequence" title="April 19–24, 2011 tornado outbreak sequence">very large derecho</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A LEWP is an extension of the concept of the bow echo, which usually indicates a powerful convective windstorm. Areas hit by the apices of bows often see the worst weather, with the highest winds and very heavy rain. However, if the sides of the bows reach enough of an orientation parallel to the derecho's movement, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storm_train" title="Storm train">a very long-duration</a> heavy rain event can result, leading to <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">flash flooding</a>. A serial derecho can be in the form of a LEWP or a single, very large <a href="/wiki/Bow_echo" title="Bow echo">bow echo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
