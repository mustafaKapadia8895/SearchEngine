<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Seascape</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>A <b>seascape</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Photograph" title="Photograph">photograph</a>, <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painting</a>, or other <a href="/wiki/Work_of_art" title="Work of art">work of art</a> which depicts the <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">sea</a>, in other words an example of <a href="/wiki/Marine_art" title="Marine art">marine art</a>. By a backwards development, the word has also come to mean the view of the sea itself, and be applied in planning contexts to geographical locations possessing a good view of the sea.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Planning_use">Planning use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seascape&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Planning use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Clifton_Beach_5.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1262" data-file-width="1919" height="92" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Clifton_Beach_5.jpg/140px-Clifton_Beach_5.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Clifton_Beach_5.jpg/210px-Clifton_Beach_5.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Clifton_Beach_5.jpg/280px-Clifton_Beach_5.jpg 2x" width="140"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Clifton_Beach_5.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A seascape photograph at <a href="/wiki/Clifton_Beach,_Tasmania" title="Clifton Beach, Tasmania">Clifton Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Arm,_Tasmania" title="South Arm, Tasmania">South Arm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>, Australia</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the UK a seascape is defined in planning and land use contexts as a combination of adjacent land, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coastline" title="Coastline">coastline</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">sea</a> within an area, defined by a mix of land-sea inter-visibility and coastal landscape character assessment, with major headlands forming division points between one seascape area and the next. This approach to coastal <a href="/wiki/Landscape_planning" title="Landscape planning">landscape planning</a> was developed jointly by Government environmental bodies in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> (UK) and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> in 2000 to assist spatial planning for (at that time new) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Offshore_wind_farm" title="Offshore wind farm">offshore wind farm</a> developments. The resulting "Guide to best practice in seascape assessment <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>", (Marine Institute, Ireland, 2001), have since been adapted and applied in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> for guidance to offshore wind farm developers and for carrying out spatial planning assessments.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the word has also been adopted in England <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> referring to the historic and archaeological character areas of the sea - a different but complementary methodological approach encompassing what lies beneath the sea surface. This use of the word departs from the focus on scenery and visual perception, relying instead just on cognitive perception (what lies beneath the sea surface is out of sight to most of us).</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh language</a> already distinguishes between 'Morluniau' (seascape in the traditional sense of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Picture" title="Picture">picture</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perspective_(visual)" title="Perspective (visual)">view</a> or <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painting</a>) and 'Morweddau' (seascape as a distinct, geographical area exhibiting particular characteristics and qualities). There is no such distinction in the English language.</p>
