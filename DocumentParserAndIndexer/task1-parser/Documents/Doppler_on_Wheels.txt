<p><b>Doppler on Wheels</b> (or <b>DOW</b>) is a fleet of X-band <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">radar</a> trucks maintained by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Center_for_Severe_Weather_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Center for Severe Weather Research (page does not exist)">Center for Severe Weather Research</a> (CSWR) in <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">Boulder, Colorado</a> led by principal investigator <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Wurman" title="Joshua Wurman">Joshua Wurman</a>, with the funding largely provided by the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a> (NSF). The DOW fleet and its associated Mobile Mesonet and Pod deployable weather stations are Lower Atmospheric Observing Facilities (LAOF) "National Facilities" supporting a wide variety NSF-sponsored research.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_deployment">History and deployment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doppler_on_Wheels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and deployment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2017, there are three operational DOWs of a total of eight constructed since 1995. All three are dual-polarization dual-frequency quick-scanning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doppler_weather_radar" title="Doppler weather radar">Doppler weather radars</a>. Several instrumented mobile <a href="/wiki/Mesonet" title="Mesonet">mesonet</a> pickup trucks host <a href="/wiki/In_situ" title="In situ">in situ</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_instrument" title="Weather instrument">weather instrumentation</a> on 3.5 metres (11 ft) masts to complement the <a href="/wiki/Remote_sensing" title="Remote sensing">remote sensing</a> radars. These mobile mesonets also carry approximately 20 instrumented "PODS", which are ruggedized quickly deployable <a href="/wiki/Weather_station" title="Weather station">weather stations</a> designed to survive inside <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornadoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a>, and other adverse environments. The DOW fleet is sometimes accompanied by a Mobile Operations and Repair Center (MORC), a large van containing workstations for in-field coordination, data management, and equipment repair.</p>
<p>The DOW fleet has collected data in 200 tornadoes and inside the cores of 13 hurricanes. DOWs have been deployed to Europe twice,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> for the MAP and COPS field programs, and to Alaska twice for the JAWS-Juneau projects. DOWs have operated as high as 12,700 feet (3,900 m) on Bristol Head and at 10,000 feet (3,000 m) for the ASCII project at Battle Pass. Three DOWs, Mobile Mesonets and PODS were deployed for the <a href="/wiki/OWLeS" title="OWLeS">OWLeS</a> <a href="/wiki/Lake-effect_snow" title="Lake-effect snow">lake-effect snow</a> study. The DOWs have participated in many field programs including: <a href="/wiki/VORTEX_projects#VORTEX1" title="VORTEX projects">VORTEX</a>, <a href="/wiki/VORTEX_projects#VORTEX2" title="VORTEX projects">VORTEX2</a>, COPS, MAP, ASCII, IHOP, SCMS, CASES, ROTATE, PAMREX, SNOWD-UNDER, FLATLAND, HERO, UIDOW, UNDEO.</p>
<p>The DOW fleet was deployed to the nocturnal convection study, PECAN, in June–July 2015.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Findings">Findings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doppler_on_Wheels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Findings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DOW data led to the discovery of sub-kilometer hurricane boundary layer rolls, which likely modulate wind damage and may play a key role in <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">hurricane intensification</a>. DOW data revealed the most intense winds ever recorded (<a href="/wiki/Bridge_Creek,_Oklahoma" title="Bridge Creek, Oklahoma">Bridge Creek</a>, 3 May 1999),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and the largest tornadic circulation ever documented (also 3 May 1999 in Mulhall, OK),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and made the first 3D maps of tornado winds and sub-tornadic vortex winds, and documented intense vortices within lake-effect snow bands. About 70 peer reviewed scientific publications have used DOW data.</p>
<p>The DOW fleet, PODS, and mobile mesonets have been featured on TV, including <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a>'s reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Storm_Chasers_(TV_series)" title="Storm Chasers (TV series)">Storm Chasers</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Channel" title="National Geographic Channel">National Geographic Channel</a>'s specials <i>Tornado Intercept</i> and <i>The True Face of Hurricanes</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Public_Broadcast_Service" title="Public Broadcast Service">PBS</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PBS_Nova" title="PBS Nova"><i>Nova</i></a> episode "The Hunt for the Supertwister," and others.</p>
