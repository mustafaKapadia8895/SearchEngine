 icing  nautical  -  wikipediaicing on ships is a serious hazard where cold temperatures  below about −10  °c or 14  °f  combined with high wind speed  typically force 8  or above on the beaufort scale  result in spray blown off the sea freezing immediately on contact with the ship  if not frequently knocked off  the ice can soon build up on the ship s superstructure to a sufficient weight to cause the ship to capsize it is typically a problem in arctic and antarctic seas  but can also occur in other seas such as the gulf of saint lawrence  the sea of japan  the baltic sea  and very rarely  the north sea
