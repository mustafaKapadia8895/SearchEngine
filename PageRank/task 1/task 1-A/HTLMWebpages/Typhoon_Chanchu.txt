<p><b>Typhoon Chanchu</b>, known in the Philippines as <b>Typhoon Caloy</b>, was the most intense <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">typhoon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> in the month of May according to the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Observatory" title="Hong Kong Observatory">Hong Kong Observatory</a> (HKO). The first <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2006 Pacific typhoon season">2006 Pacific typhoon season</a>, Chanchu formed on May 8 in the vicinity of the <a href="/wiki/Federated_States_of_Micronesia" title="Federated States of Micronesia">Federated States of Micronesia</a> and progressed westward. It gradually intensified into a tropical storm and later <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Western_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone scales">severe tropical storm</a> before moving through the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. On May 13, Chanchu entered the South China Sea and became a typhoon, according to the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> (JMA). Warm waters and favorable <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> allowed the storm to quickly intensify to peak <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> of 175 km/h (110 mph) on May 15. Around that time, the typhoon turned sharply to the north toward southeastern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. Chanchu weakened as it curved to the northeast, making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Shantou" title="Shantou">Shantou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> on May 17 as a severe tropical storm. The government of China considered Chanchu the earliest typhoon to make landfall in the province. On the next day, the storm emerged into the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a>, becoming <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> on May 19 before dissipating west of <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a>.</p>
<p>Early in its duration, Chanchu moved through the Philippines, causing power outages and landslides in several islands. Despite a general warning against small boats sailing, a ferry departed <a href="/wiki/Masbate" title="Masbate">Masbate</a> and capsized due to the storm, killing 28 people. Throughout the country, 41 people died, and damage reached ₱117.57 million (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_peso" title="Philippine peso">PHP</a>, $2.15 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Dollar" title="United States Dollar">USD</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[nb 1]</a></sup> While in the South China Sea, Chanchu caught many <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnamese</a> fisherman off guard, causing 17 ships to sink and damaging several others. Chinese ships assisted in the search-and-rescue mission, ultimately rescuing 330 fishermen from 22 boats; however, 21 bodies were found, and the remaining 220 missing were presumed killed. In southern China, flooding and strong winds from Chanchu wrecked about 14,000 houses and damaged over 190,000 ha (470,000 acres) of crop fields. Damage was heaviest in Shantou where it moved ashore, with flooding covering roads and entering hundreds of homes. Damage in China totaled ¥7 billion yuan (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_yuan" title="Chinese yuan">RMB</a>, $872 million USD), and there were 23 deaths. Rains from the typhoon killed two people in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> after sweeping them up in a river, and crop damage there reached NT$158.88 million (<a href="/wiki/New_Taiwan_dollar" title="New Taiwan dollar">NTD</a>, $5 million USD). Later, high waves killed one person in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a> and left another person missing, while rains extended into <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chanchu_2006_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1547" data-file-width="2000" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Chanchu_2006_track.png/220px-Chanchu_2006_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Chanchu_2006_track.png/330px-Chanchu_2006_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Chanchu_2006_track.png/440px-Chanchu_2006_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chanchu_2006_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>An area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a>, or thunderstorms, persisted on May 5 southeast of <a href="/wiki/Yap_State" title="Yap State">Yap State</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Federated_States_of_Micronesia" title="Federated States of Micronesia">Federated States of Micronesia</a> (FSM). Initially it remained disorganized while tracking to the west, although a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Primary_circulation:_rotating_winds" title="Tropical cyclone">circulation</a> became more distinct on May 7, indicative of gradual organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup> At 06:00 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on May 8, the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> (JMA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[nb 2]</a></sup> declared that a tropical depression had developed about 175 km (110 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Palau" title="Palau">Palau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-jma-3">[2]</a></sup> Five hours later, the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[nb 3]</a></sup> issued a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_formation_alert" title="Tropical cyclone formation alert">tropical cyclone formation alert</a>, and at 18:00 UTC they classified the system as Tropical Depression 02W. The system moved to the west-southwest, influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> to the north. Early on May 9, the JTWC upgraded the depression to tropical storm status, and at 12:00 UTC the JMA followed suit by upgrading the system to Tropical Storm Chanchu. Also on that day, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_Atmospheric,_Geophysical_and_Astronomical_Services_Administration" title="Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration">Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration</a> (PAGASA) began issuing warnings on the storm as Tropical Storm Caloy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chanchu06.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Chanchu06.jpg/220px-Chanchu06.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Chanchu06.jpg/330px-Chanchu06.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Chanchu06.jpg/440px-Chanchu06.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chanchu06.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Severe Tropical Storm Chanchu near <a href="/wiki/Samar" title="Samar">Samar</a> on May 11</div>

</div>
<p>In its formative stages, Chanchu was located in an environment generally favorable for intensification. Its tracked shifted more to the west-northwest due to a building ridge to the south.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[4]</a></sup> Late on May 10, the JTWC upgraded Chanchu to typhoon status, estimating <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">1 minute sustained winds</a> of 140 km/h (85 mph). By contrast, the JMA estimated it intensified only into a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Western_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone scales">severe tropical storm</a> with winds of 95 km/h (60 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-jma-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bt-8">[5]</a></sup> Turning more to the west, Chanchu made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on <a href="/wiki/Samar" title="Samar">Samar</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> on May 11. Despite moving through the archipelago, Chanchu intensified slightly within the <a href="/wiki/Sibuyan_Sea" title="Sibuyan Sea">Sibuyan Sea</a>, striking <a href="/wiki/Mindoro" title="Mindoro">Mindoro</a> on May 12 with 1 minute winds of 160 km/h (100 mph), according to the JTWC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-bt-8">[5]</a></sup> On May 13, Chanchu emerged into the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>, and later that day the JMA upgraded it to typhoon status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-jma-3">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon reaching the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>, Chanchu encountered an area of warm <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> and low <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[6]</a></sup> After an <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">upper-level low</a> to the east provided favorable <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> to the south and the east,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pr22_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-pr22-10">[7]</a></sup> Chanchu <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensified</a> on May 14. While the storm was active, the JTWC upgraded Chanchu to a <a href="/wiki/Typhoon#Intensity_classifications" title="Typhoon">super typhoon</a> with peak 1 minute winds of 250 km/h (155 mph),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup> although the agency later downgraded the peak winds to 230 km/h (145 mph). By contrast, the JMA estimated peak 10 minute winds of 175 km/h (110 mph) at 00:00 UTC on May 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-bt-8">[5]</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Observatory" title="Hong Kong Observatory">Hong Kong Observatory</a>, the 10 minute winds reached 185 km/h (115 mph),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-bt-8">[5]</a></sup> which made Chanchu the strongest typhoon in the South China Sea in the month of May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hko_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-hko-11">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the time Chanchu attained peak winds, an eastward-moving <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> over China broke up the ridge to the north,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pr22_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-pr22-10">[7]</a></sup> causing the typhoon to turn sharply to the north into a less favorable environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup> With decreased outflow and stronger wind shear, Chanchu began slowly weakening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> initially remained small, but the outer eyewall deteriorated on May 16 as the convection decreased in the northern periphery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[10]</a></sup> The trough that previously weakened the ridge steered Chanchu to the north-northeast and forced an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> transition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[11]</a></sup> Late on May 17, the JMA downgraded the typhoon to a severe tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-jma-3">[2]</a></sup> Around that time, Chanchu made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Shantou" title="Shantou">Shantou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> in southeastern China,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup> about 315 km (195 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[12]</a></sup> the JTWC estimated landfall winds of 130 km/h (85 mph),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup> while the JMA estimated them at 110 km/h (70 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-bt-8">[5]</a></sup> Early on May 18, the JTWC discontinued advisories, although the JMA continued tracking Chanchu over southeastern China through eastern <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a> province. Later on May 18, the storm emerged into the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a>, becoming fully extratropical at 00:00 UTC on May 19. The remnants continued toward Japan before dissipating at 18:00 UTC that day off the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a> in southern Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-jma-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-bt-8">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3120" data-file-width="2000" height="343" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG/220px-Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG/330px-Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG/440px-Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Affected_Philippine_provinces_by_typhoon_Chanchu_2006.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Philippine provinces where public storm signals</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Officials in southern <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">Leyte</a> recommended that residents evacuate to prevent a repeat of a <a href="/wiki/2006_Southern_Leyte_mudslide" title="2006 Southern Leyte mudslide">deadly landslide</a> in February 2006. Officials canceled several flights and ferry lines,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dpa512_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-dpa512-16">[13]</a></sup> stranding 10,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps514_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ps514-17">[14]</a></sup> In all, 2,144 people evacuated in the Philippines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caloy_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-caloy-18">[15]</a></sup> PAGASA issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Philippines" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">storm signal number 2</a> for several provinces along Chanchu's path, as well as storm signal number 1 for other areas, largely forecasting for rainfall and gusty winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vietnamese fishermen in the South China Sea received 24 hours of warning from the National Hydrometerological Forecast Center before Chanchu approached the area, less time than other agencies in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp63_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp63-20">[17]</a></sup> At one point, Chanchu was forecast to become a strong typhoon and make landfall near <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>. In response to the threat, officials at the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Observatory" title="Hong Kong Observatory">Hong Kong Observatory</a>, as well as in Macau, issued a standby signal to inform the public of the approaching typhoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HK_issues_warning_as_Typhoon_Chanchu_getting_closer_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-HK_issues_warning_as_Typhoon_Chanchu_getting_closer-21">[18]</a></sup> The HKO issued a <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_tropical_cyclone_warning_signals" title="Hong Kong tropical cyclone warning signals">warning signal number 3</a> on May 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hko_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-hko-11">[8]</a></sup> In the territory, 60 flights were canceled with another 14 delayed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hko_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-hko-11">[8]</a></sup> beaches were closed, and ferry service was disrupted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp517_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp517-22">[19]</a></sup> Ahead of the storm, about 1 million people evacuated from coastal Guangdong and Fujian provinces to government warehouses, schools, tents, or the houses of relatives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc519_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc519-23">[20]</a></sup> In the former province, 62,000 fishermen were ordered to return to port, while four flights were canceled at <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Baiyun_International_Airport" title="Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport">Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport</a>. Residents were advised to remain indoors, and workers reinforced billboards in anticipation of the strong winds. Rail and boat transport was stopped between Guangdong and <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Qiongzhou_Strait" title="Qiongzhou Strait">Qiongzhou Strait</a> due to the typhoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp517_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp517-22">[19]</a></sup> Schools were closed in Guangdong during the storm's passage, although they remained open in Fujian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scmp_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-scmp-24">[21]</a></sup> In Shanghai, the speed limit of <a href="/wiki/Donghai_Bridge" title="Donghai Bridge">Donghai Bridge</a> was halved because of strong winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ahead of the storm, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Weather_Bureau" title="Central Weather Bureau">Central Weather Bureau</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> issued land and sea warnings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-epoch_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-epoch-26">[23]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tainan_City_Government" title="Tainan City Government">Tainan City Government</a> and three county governments closed for one day. All domestic flights to offshore islands were canceled, and rail service was interrupted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cp518_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-cp518-27">[24]</a></sup> Later, airlines canceled 12 flights in Japan due to the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dt_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-dt-28">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Philippines_and_Malaysia">The Philippines and Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Philippines and Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="488" data-file-width="680" height="158" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png/220px-Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png/330px-Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png/440px-Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chanchu2006rain_-_2.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rainfall from Chanchu up to May 12</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>While moving through the Philippines, Chanchu affected several islands with strong winds and heavy rainfall. In <a href="/wiki/Legazpi,_Albay" title="Legazpi, Albay">Legazpi, Albay</a>, strong waves wrecked 100 homes and left 1,500 people homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dpa512_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-dpa512-16">[13]</a></sup> High winds left widespread power outages, particularly in Mindoro, <a href="/wiki/Batangas" title="Batangas">Batangas</a>, and across the <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Region" title="Bicol Region">Bicol Region</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps516_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ps516-29">[26]</a></sup> The storm severely damaged the banana industry and affected various other fruit crops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tzu_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tzu-30">[27]</a></sup> Near <a href="/wiki/Metro_Manila" title="Metro Manila">Metro Manila</a>, the winds damaged billboards, knocked over trees, and caused isolated power outages. Rough seas sank a ferry off <a href="/wiki/Masbate" title="Masbate">Masbate</a>, despite a warning against the operation of small craft, having left at sunrise to avoid the police. The Coast Guard rescued 18 passengers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps514_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-ps514-17">[14]</a></sup> but 28 people died in the wreck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps516_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-ps516-29">[26]</a></sup> An empty ferry sank at port in <a href="/wiki/Tabaco" title="Tabaco">Tabaco</a>. A ferry with 700 people aboard went missing, but the Coast Guard found it washed ashore with everyone safe on board. Similarly, an oil tanker washed ashore at <a href="/wiki/Pinamalayan,_Oriental_Mindoro" title="Pinamalayan, Oriental Mindoro">Pinamalayan, Oriental Mindoro</a>, and its crew of 13 was rescued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps514_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-ps514-17">[14]</a></sup> Throughout the country, Chanchu killed 41 people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp517_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-afp517-22">[19]</a></sup> mostly from the Masabate ferry wreck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps516_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-ps516-29">[26]</a></sup> The storm damaged 5,630 homes, and destroyed 1,013 others,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caloy_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-caloy-18">[15]</a></sup> forcing 53,307 people to leave their homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps516_29-3"><a href="#cite_note-ps516-29">[26]</a></sup> Agricultural losses totaled ₱71.57 million (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_peso" title="Philippine peso">PHP</a>, $1.3 million USD),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-php_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-php-32">[nb 4]</a></sup> chiefly to the corn harvest,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps516_29-4"><a href="#cite_note-ps516-29">[26]</a></sup> with an additional ₱46 million (PHP, $850,000 USD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-php_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-php-32">[nb 4]</a></sup> in infrastructure damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caloy_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-caloy-18">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>While stalled over the South China Sea, Chanchu's large circulation caused an increase in rainfall over <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-33">[29]</a></sup> The typhoon brought the onset of the summer <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> in the South China Sea after shifting the prevailing winds over the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vietnam">Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vietnam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While moving slowly through the South China Sea, Chanchu produced strong waves that struck the east coast of <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>. The associated flooding washed away many shrimp from coastal ponds and also entered <a href="/wiki/Thu_B%E1%BB%93n_River" title="Thu Bồn River">Thu Bồn River</a>, thus preventing its use as a source for irrigation for about 1,000 ha (2,500 acres) of rice paddy fields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc519_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc519-23">[20]</a></sup> Due to its unexpected change in course and ferocity, Chanchu caught dozens of ships off guard and damaged communications, sinking 17 ships and damaging several others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp521_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp521-35">[31]</a></sup> Initially, there were 400 fishermen missing,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reut520_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-reut520-36">[32]</a></sup> although there was conflicting information with regard to the number of ships and people affected,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp521_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp521-35">[31]</a></sup> particularly with ships near Hainan or Taiwan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp523_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp523-37">[33]</a></sup> Following a request from the Vietnamese government, the Chinese government deployed rescue ships on May 19, a day after the storm made its final landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp521_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-afp521-35">[31]</a></sup> Offshore <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_Ng%C3%A3i_Province" title="Quảng Ngãi Province">Quảng Ngãi Province</a>, 94 fishermen sought refuge on a Chinese island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chi520_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-chi520-38">[34]</a></sup> and 22 boats were found in the <a href="/wiki/Pratas_Islands" title="Pratas Islands">Dongsha Islands</a> offshore China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp521_35-3"><a href="#cite_note-afp521-35">[31]</a></sup> One Chinese ship rescued 97 fishermen, but also found 18 people killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reut520_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-reut520-36">[32]</a></sup> Chinese ships ultimately rescued 330 fishermen from 22 boats and provided them with food and water; this was the country's largest oceanic rescue at the time. Two Vietnamese boats departed from Quảng Ngãi to assist crews on damaged boats attempting to return to port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp521_35-4"><a href="#cite_note-afp521-35">[31]</a></sup> Medical teams greeted the ships returning to harbor, while an altar was set up for the deceased.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp523_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp523-37">[33]</a></sup> After two weeks, the government of Vietnam ended the search, with 21 bodies found, and the remaining 220 missing fishermen presumed killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp63_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp63-20">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="5600" data-file-width="5600" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg/220px-Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg/330px-Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg/440px-Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Super_Typhoon_Chanchu.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Typhoon Chanchu approaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southeastern_China" title="Southeastern China">Southeastern China</a> on May 17</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Typhoon Chanchu was the earliest on record to strike Guangdong at the time, having struck the country 44 days earlier than the average date for the first strike. Rainfall in the country spread across Guangdong, Fujian, <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-escap_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-escap-39">[35]</a></sup> and Chanchu became the earliest typhoon to affect <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> in 80 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chi520_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-chi520-38">[34]</a></sup> Rainfall totaled over 250 mm (9.8 in) in southeastern Guangdong and southwestern Fujian. In the former province, wind gusts peaked at 170 km/h (106 mph) in <a href="/wiki/Huilai_County" title="Huilai County">Huilai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-escap_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-escap-39">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon striking China, Chanchu produced deadly flooding and landslides along its path,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc519_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc519-23">[20]</a></sup> with flooding spreading as far northeast as Zhejiang province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[36]</a></sup> One landslide in Fujian killed eight people and wrecked two houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xin518_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-xin518-41">[37]</a></sup> In Guangdong and Fujian, Chanchu wrecked 14,000 houses and damaged over 190,000 hectares (470,000 acres) of crop fields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hko_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-hko-11">[8]</a></sup> Damage was particularly heavy in Shantou, Guangdong, where Chanchu moved ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chi520_38-2"><a href="#cite_note-chi520-38">[34]</a></sup> There were about 200 flooded houses,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xin518_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-xin518-41">[37]</a></sup> and many roads covered, after rivers flooded from the heavy rainfall. The storm also caused power outages in Shantou,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt518_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt518-42">[38]</a></sup> and damage there totaled ¥2.56 billion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_yuan" title="Chinese yuan">yuan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">RMB</a>, $320 million <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chi520_38-3"><a href="#cite_note-chi520-38">[34]</a></sup> In nearby <a href="/wiki/Xiamen" title="Xiamen">Xiamen</a>, the typhoon forced 43 factories to temporarily close, resulting in a loss of ¥62.2 million yuan (RMB, $7.8 million USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cny_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-cny-43">[nb 5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scmp_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-scmp-24">[21]</a></sup> The storm killed eight people in Guangdong, five of them due to traffic accidents, and a further 15 in Fujian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chi520_38-4"><a href="#cite_note-chi520-38">[34]</a></sup> Overall damage was estimated at ¥7 billion yuan (RMB, $872 million USD),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cny_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-cny-43">[nb 5]</a></sup> roughly evenly split between Guangdong and Fujian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chi520_38-5"><a href="#cite_note-chi520-38">[34]</a></sup> This was less than expected given the winds at landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scmp_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-scmp-24">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>While passing east of Hong Kong, the outer rainbands of Chanchu dropped 43.5 mm (1.71 in) of rainfall at <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin" title="Sha Tin">Sha Tin</a>. Sustained winds in the territory reached 96 km/h (60 mph), while gusts reached 146 km/h (91 mph), both recorded at <a href="/wiki/Tate%27s_Cairn" title="Tate's Cairn">Tate's Cairn</a>. Chanchu produced a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> of 0.77 m (2.5 ft), causing minor flooding, sinking a yacht, and injuring one person who was swept into the sea. The storm downed several trees and damaged some scaffolding. Six people were injured in the territory, including three on a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_929" title="Boeing 929">jetfoil</a> bound for <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hko_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-hko-11">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Taiwan,_Japan,_and_South_Korea"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Taiwan.2C_Japan.2C_and_South_Korea">Taiwan, Japan, and South Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Taiwan, Japan, and South Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>High waves in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> washed an oil tanker ashore in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_City" title="Kaohsiung City">Kaohsiung City</a>; all 13 crew members were rescued with helicopters. In <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_County" title="Kaohsiung County">Kaohsiung County</a>, the typhoon wrecked several dikes in coastal cities. Chanchu also produced heavy rainfall on the island, causing flooding and landslides, the latter of which covered a highway. Swollen rivers swept away three farmers in <a href="/wiki/Hualien_County" title="Hualien County">Hualien County</a>, who were later rescued, and killed two sisters in <a href="/wiki/Pingtung_County" title="Pingtung County">Pingtung County</a> underneath the Sandimen Bridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cp518_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-cp518-27">[24]</a></sup> Crop damage totaled NT$158.88 million (<a href="/wiki/New_Taiwan_dollar" title="New Taiwan dollar">NTD</a>, $5 million USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-epoch_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-epoch-26">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Nishihara,_Okinawa" title="Nishihara, Okinawa">Nishihara, Okinawa</a>, high waves caused by Chanchu swept away three bathers. The <a href="/wiki/Japan_Coast_Guard" title="Japan Coast Guard">Japan Coast Guard</a> rescued one, another was killed, and the third remained missing as of May 23.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[40]</a></sup> The remnants of Chanchu produced 121 mm (4.8 in) of rainfall in <a href="/wiki/Gifu_Prefecture" title="Gifu Prefecture">Gifu Prefecture</a> in combination with a nearby cold front, causing one landslide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[41]</a></sup> A fallen tree in <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki_Prefecture" title="Nagasaki Prefecture">Nagasaki Prefecture</a> caused a small power outage, and nearby there was a damaged home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dt_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-dt-28">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The trough that engulfed Chanchu drew moisture from the typhoon, leading to heavy rainfall in portions of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> that reached 144 mm (5.7 in) on <a href="/wiki/Jeju_Province" title="Jeju Province">Jeju Island</a>. Along with strong winds, the rains caused ferry and flight cancelations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Chanchu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the days after Chanchu moved through the Philippines, then-President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal-Arroyo" title="Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo">Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo</a> ordered that the country's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Disaster_Coordinating_Council" title="National Disaster Coordinating Council">National Disaster Coordinating Council</a> help all towns affected by the storm. The agency helped coordinate search and rescue missions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[43]</a></sup> Several areas were declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of calamity</a>, mostly on Mindoro, Samar, and Batangas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[44]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tzu_Chi" title="Tzu Chi">Tzu Chi</a> Foundation visited islands in eastern Samar, providing money to the families whose houses were destroyed. Towns in the region also assisted by supplying <a href="/wiki/Thatching" title="Thatching">thatch</a> to rebuild homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tzu_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tzu-30">[27]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Oriental_Mindoro" title="Oriental Mindoro">Oriental Mindoro</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Red_Cross" title="Philippine Red Cross">Philippine Red Cross</a> provided food and relief items to families in <a href="/wiki/Calapan" title="Calapan">Calapan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc519_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc519-23">[20]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Adventist_Development_and_Relief_Agency" title="Adventist Development and Relief Agency">Adventist Development and Relief Agency</a> also provided building materials for 200 families in Mindoro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[45]</a></sup> Ultimately, the government provided storm victims with ₱415.1 million (PHP, $7.6 million USD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-php_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-php-32">[nb 4]</a></sup> worth of relief supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps516_29-5"><a href="#cite_note-ps516-29">[26]</a></sup> Rainfall from the storm caused a <a href="/wiki/Red_tide" title="Red tide">red tide</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taal_Lake" title="Taal Lake">Taal Lake</a>, after dispersing a <a href="/wiki/Ceratium" title="Ceratium">Ceratium</a> bloom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Immediate after Chanchu's China landfall, officials began distributing tents, quilts, <a href="/wiki/Portable_water_purification" title="Portable water purification">water purification tablets</a>, and disinfectant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc519_23-4"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc519-23">[20]</a></sup> The government of Fujian set up a ¥8.5 million yuan (RMB, $1.06 million) relief fund.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chi520_38-6"><a href="#cite_note-chi520-38">[34]</a></sup> In the months after Chanchu, China suffered from several other damaging tropical cyclones, including <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Bilis" title="Tropical Storm Bilis">Tropical Storm Bilis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Saomai" title="Typhoon Saomai">Typhoon Saomai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[47]</a></sup> Damage from Chanchu forced the <a href="/wiki/China_National_Offshore_Oil_Corporation" title="China National Offshore Oil Corporation">China National Offshore Oil Corporation</a> to shut down for a time, which contributed to an annual decrease in its oil output.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[48]</a></sup> The Chinese government recognized 50 people who assisted in the South China Sea search and rescue mission, and two vessels were declared "hero ships".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/President_of_Vietnam" title="President of Vietnam">Vietnamese president</a> <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n_%C4%90%E1%BB%A9c_L%C6%B0%C6%A1ng" title="Trần Đức Lương">Trần Đức Lương</a> expressed his thanks to the Chinese government on May 22 for rescuing the Vietnamese on the imperiled ships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[50]</a></sup> Residents and industries in Vietnam raised ₫360 million (<a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_%C4%91%E1%BB%93ng" title="Vietnamese đồng">VND</a>, $36,000 USD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vnd_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-vnd-57">[nb 6]</a></sup> for the families of the deceased fishermen, as well as providing 1 ton of rice. Trade unions encouraged workers to donate one day's salary to help storm victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[52]</a></sup> The Vietnamese embassy in India raised about $1,000 (USD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[53]</a></sup> and Vietnamese people living in Greece raised ₫26 million (VND, €1,300 <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euros</a>) for storm victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[54]</a></sup> Ultimately, 43 different organizations and people donated $29,000 (USD) to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Cross_of_Viet_Nam" title="Red Cross of Viet Nam">Vietnam Red Cross</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[55]</a></sup> The country's <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Labour,_Invalids_and_Social_Affairs_(Vietnam)" title="Ministry of Labour, Invalids and Social Affairs (Vietnam)">Ministry of Labour, Invalids and Social Affairs</a> presented an award to the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_News_Agency" title="Vietnam News Agency">Vietnam News Agency</a> in June 2006 for its charitable donations, which included the distribution of ₫112 million (VND, $11,200 USD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vnd_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-vnd-57">[nb 6]</a></sup> to storm victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[56]</a></sup> One fisherman claimed to survive for two weeks in the open seas before being rescued, although he later confessed that he was safely on another boat, and wanted his family to retain the disaster compensation; after the man revealed that he had lied, his family was able to retain the relief funds due to their poverty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[57]</a></sup> The head of the Vietnam Institute of Meteorology, Hydrology and Environment resigned two weeks after Chanchu killed many fishermen because of inadequate warnings. Then-<a href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister_of_Vietnam" title="Deputy Prime Minister of Vietnam">Deputy Prime Minister</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nguyen_Tan_Dung" title="Nguyen Tan Dung">Nguyen Tan Dung</a> ordered a review of the meteorological agency as a result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp63_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-afp63-20">[17]</a></sup> Within a few years after the typhoon, the meteorological agency began issuing more accurate and timely forecasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[58]</a></sup> After the many deaths of fishermen from Chanchu, the Vietnam government prevented any fishermen from leaving harbor during the passage of <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Durian" title="Typhoon Durian">Typhoon Durian</a> in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the season ended, members of the 39th meeting of the Typhoon Committee of the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> met in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a> in December 2006. They discussed retiring the name "Chanchu", along with four other names from the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-33">[29]</a></sup> During the 40th meeting in November 2007, the Typhoon Committee approved the retirement, announcing that the name "Sanba" would replace Chanchu on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names#Western_North_Pacific" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">basin name lists</a> beginning in <a href="/wiki/2008_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2008 Pacific typhoon season">2008</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[60]</a></sup></p>
