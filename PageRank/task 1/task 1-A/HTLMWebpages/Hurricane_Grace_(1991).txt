<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hurricane Grace</h1>

<p><b>Hurricane Grace</b> was a short-lived <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_2_hurricane" title="Category 2 hurricane">Category 2 hurricane</a> that contributed to the formation of the powerful <a href="/wiki/1991_Perfect_Storm" title="1991 Perfect Storm">1991 Perfect Storm</a>. Forming on October 26, Grace initially had <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical origins</a>, meaning it was partially <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical</a> and partially <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> in nature. It became a tropical cyclone on October 27, and ultimately peaked with winds of 90 knots (105 mph; 165 km/h). The storm had minor effects on the island of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> as it passed to the south. A developing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical_storm" title="Extratropical storm">extratropical storm</a> to the north turned Grace eastward; the hurricane was eventually absorbed into the large circulation of the larger low pressure system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Fed by the contrast between cold air to the northwest and warm air from the remnants of Hurricane Grace, this storm became a large and powerful <a href="/wiki/Nor%27easter" title="Nor'easter">nor'easter</a> that caused extremely high waves and resulted in severe coastal damage along the East Coast of the United States.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Grace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Grace_1991_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Grace_1991_track.png/220px-Grace_1991_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Grace_1991_track.png/330px-Grace_1991_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Grace_1991_track.png/440px-Grace_1991_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Grace_1991_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The origins of Grace go back to a <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">mid-level area of low pressure</a> that formed on October 23 to the south of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. Reports from a nearby ship indicated that the low had become a <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">surface feature</a> by October 25. The storm system initially contained <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical characteristics</a>, and the center of circulation lacked deep <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a> for several days. The system was designated a subtropical storm on October 26. An area of clouds near Bermuda became increasingly convective, and gradually became entrained into the expanding and developing circulation of the subtropical storm. Thunderstorm activity persisted near the center, and on October 27, the storm attained <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical storm status</a> and was named Grace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Grace continued to organize and intensify; based on satellite intensity estimates and <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance" title="Reconnaissance">reconnaissance</a> reports, the storm was upgraded to a Category 1 hurricane, the lowest level on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-3">[3]</a></sup> Grace reached its peak intensity with winds of 90 knots (105 mph; 165 km/h) and a minimum central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 980 mbar (hPa; 28.94 inHg), ranking as a Category 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-4">[4]</a></sup> Operationally, however, the peak intensity was thought to have been 65 knots (75 mph; 120 km/h), which would have made it a mere Category 1 at its peak strength.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The hurricane tracked generally northwestward until October 28, when an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> formed along an approaching <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">cold front</a> off the <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> coast. This storm rapidly intensified and influenced Grace's steering currents, turning the hurricane sharply east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">[6]</a></sup> At around the same time, an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye feature</a> associated with Hurricane Grace became apparent on satellite imagery, despite a lack of strong convective activity around the storm's center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Grace accelerated as it continued eastward, and reached its peak intensity of Category 2 status on October 29. However, the storm's rapid forward movement led to an asymmetrical circulation. The center passed approximately 50 mi (80 km) south of Bermuda without significantly affecting the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hurricane Grace turned northeast later that day, as the rapidly approaching cold front undermined the storm's lower-level center. The system became overtaken by the front and subsequently lost its status as a tropical system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-8">[8]</a></sup> It moved north along the front and merged with the large cyclone to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">[6]</a></sup> The remnants of Grace became completely indistinguishable by the next day as it was completely absorbed by the passing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical_storm" title="Extratropical storm">extratropical storm</a> on October 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-history2-9">[9]</a></sup> The nor'easter significantly strengthened as a result of the temperature contrast between the cold air to the northwest and the warmth and humidity associated with the remnants of Hurricane Grace. The low-pressure system continued deepening as it drifted southeastward and then southwestward towards the United States. The cyclone attained its peak intensity 390 miles (630 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_Nova_Scotia" title="Halifax, Nova Scotia">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a>, with winds of up to 60 knots (69 mph; 110 km/h). The storm became known as the <a href="/wiki/1991_Perfect_Storm" title="1991 Perfect Storm">1991 Perfect Storm</a>, or more commonly, the "Perfect Storm".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history2_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-history2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Grace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In advance of Hurricane Grace, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">tropical storm warning</a> was issued for Bermuda on October 27. The following day, about 10 hours before the storm's closest approach to the island, the tropical storm warning was upgraded to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">hurricane warning</a>. On October 29, the hurricane warning was lowered to a tropical storm warning, which was amended to a <a href="/wiki/Gale_warning" title="Gale warning">gale warning</a> shortly thereafter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR7_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR7-10">[10]</a></sup> The island experienced <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">bands</a> of squally weather in association with the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Precipitation peaked at 3.21 in (82 mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-12">[12]</a></sup> However, no significant damage was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-8">[8]</a></sup> A yacht traveling from Bermuda to New York encountered strong winds and 25 ft (7.6 m) seas well off the <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> coast; its nine occupants were rescued by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">Coast Guard</a> helicopters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because of its large size, Grace generated large swells along the East Coast of the United States,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-8">[8]</a></sup> combined with abnormally high tides; these waves reached at least 15 ft (4.6 m). Despite minor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">beach erosion</a>, no substantial property damage occurred, although <a href="/wiki/Carolina_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="Carolina Beach, North Carolina">Carolina Beach, North Carolina</a>, lost about 1 ft (0.30 m) of sand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite the light impacts from Hurricane Grace, the resultant nor'easter caused extensive coastal damage, high seas, and powerful winds. Hurricane-force wind gusts were reported in New England. The storm churned the ocean for several days; a wave 101 ft (31 m) in height was reported by an offshore buoy. Extensive coastal flooding occurred along the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_states" title="Mid-Atlantic states">Mid-Atlantic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northeast_U.S." title="Northeast U.S.">Northeastern</a> U.S., with effects as far north as <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> and as far south as <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>. The nor'easter eventually transitioned into <a href="/wiki/1991_Perfect_Storm" title="1991 Perfect Storm">another hurricane</a> that made landfall in <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">[6]</a></sup> A ship known as the <i><a href="/wiki/Andrea_Gail" title="Andrea Gail">Andrea Gail</a></i> was lost, along with her six crew members, during the storm. The story of the <i>Andrea Gail</i> inspired <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Junger" title="Sebastian Junger">Sebastian Junger</a>'s 1997 book, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Perfect_Storm_(book)" title="The Perfect Storm (book)">The Perfect Storm</a></i>, and a <a href="/wiki/The_Perfect_Storm_(film)" title="The Perfect Storm (film)">2000 motion picture film</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history2_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-history2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
