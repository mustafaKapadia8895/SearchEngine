<p>The <b>American plaice</b> or sole (<i>Hippoglossoides platessoides</i>) is a North Atlantic <a href="/wiki/Flatfish" title="Flatfish">flatfish</a> that belongs, along with other right-eyed <a href="/wiki/Flounder" title="Flounder">flounders</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Pleuronectidae" title="Pleuronectidae">Pleuronectidae</a> family. In the northwest Atlantic (<i>H. p. platessoides</i>) it ranges from <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a> to <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, and in the northeast Atlantic (<i>H. p. limandoides</i>) it ranges from <a href="/wiki/Murmansk_Oblast" title="Murmansk Oblast">Murmansk</a> to the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fishbase_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-fishbase-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Muus_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Muus-2">[2]</a></sup> They live on soft bottoms at depths of 10 to 3,000 m (33–9,843 ft), but mainly between 90 and 250 m (300–820 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fishbase_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-fishbase-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a> spawning peaks in April and May. They grow to a maximum length of 70 centimetres (28 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The species is considered by the Northwest Atlantic Fisheries Organization to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overfished" title="Overfished">overfished</a>, with no signs of recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On the other hand, the Canadian government believes the species is abundant, and counts it as the second most caught flatfish, totalling 50% of the flatfish caught by Canadian fishermen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> A 1997 study reports that plaice are endangered in Canada due to overfishing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In its European range the species is generally common and not actively sought by fishers, but it is often part of the <a href="/wiki/Bycatch" title="Bycatch">bycatch</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Muus_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Muus-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>American plaice may be an <a href="/wiki/Intermediate_host" title="Intermediate host">intermediate host</a> for the nematode parasite <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Otostrongylus_circumlitis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Otostrongylus circumlitis (page does not exist)">Otostrongylus circumlitis</a></i>, which is a <a href="/wiki/Lungworm" title="Lungworm">lungworm</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">seals</a>, primarily affecting animals less than 1 yr of age.</p>
