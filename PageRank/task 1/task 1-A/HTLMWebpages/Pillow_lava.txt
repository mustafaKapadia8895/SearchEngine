<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Pillow lava</h1>

<p><b>Pillow lavas</b> are <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">lavas</a> that contain characteristic pillow-shaped structures that are attributed to the extrusion of the lava under water, or <i>subaqueous extrusion</i>. Pillow lavas in <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_rock" title="Volcanic rock">volcanic rock</a> are characterized by thick sequences of discontinuous pillow-shaped masses, commonly up to one metre in diameter. They form the upper part of <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_crust#Composition" title="Oceanic crust">Layer 2 of normal oceanic crust</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pillow_lava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pillow lavas are commonly of <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">basaltic</a> composition, although pillows formed of <a href="/wiki/Komatiite" title="Komatiite">komatiite</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Picrite" title="Picrite">picrite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boninite" title="Boninite">boninite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basaltic_andesite" title="Basaltic andesite">basaltic andesite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andesite" title="Andesite">andesite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dacite" title="Dacite">dacite</a> or even <a href="/wiki/Rhyolite" title="Rhyolite">rhyolite</a> are known.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FangNiu2003_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FangNiu2003-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In general the more <a href="/wiki/Intermediate_composition" title="Intermediate composition">intermediate</a> (less rich in silica) the composition, the larger the pillows, due to the increase in <a href="/wiki/Viscosity" title="Viscosity">viscosity</a> of the erupting lava.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Occurrence">Occurrence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pillow_lava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Occurrence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>They occur wherever lava is extruded under water, such as along marine <a href="/wiki/Hotspot_(geology)" title="Hotspot (geology)">hotspot</a> <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> chains and the <a href="/wiki/Divergent_boundary" title="Divergent boundary">constructive plate boundaries</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mid-ocean_ridge" title="Mid-ocean ridge">mid-ocean ridges</a>. As new oceanic crust is formed, thick sequences of pillow lavas are erupted at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spreading_center" title="Spreading center">spreading center</a> fed by <a href="/wiki/Dike_(geology)" title="Dike (geology)">dykes</a> from the underlying <a href="/wiki/Magma_chamber" title="Magma chamber">magma chamber</a>. Pillow lavas and the related <a href="/wiki/Sheeted_dyke_complex" title="Sheeted dyke complex">sheeted dyke complexes</a> form part of a classic <a href="/wiki/Ophiolite" title="Ophiolite">ophiolite</a> sequence (when a segment of oceanic crust is <a href="/wiki/Obduction" title="Obduction">thrust over</a> the <a href="/wiki/Continental_crust" title="Continental crust">continental crust</a>, thus exposing the oceanic segment above sea level).</p>
<p>The presence of pillow lavas in the oldest preserved volcanic sequences on the planet, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isua_greenstone_belt" title="Isua greenstone belt">Isua</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barberton_belt" title="Barberton belt">Barberton</a> <a href="/wiki/Greenstone_belt" title="Greenstone belt">greenstone belts</a>, confirms the presence of large bodies of water on the Earth's surface early in the <a href="/wiki/Archean" title="Archean">Archean</a> Eon. Pillow lavas are used generally to confirm subaqueous volcanism in <a href="/wiki/Metamorphism" title="Metamorphism">metamorphic</a> belts.</p>
<p>Pillow lavas are also found associated with some <a href="/wiki/Subglacial_volcano" title="Subglacial volcano">subglacial volcanoes</a> at an early stage of an eruption.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pillow_lava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pillows.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3024" data-file-width="3024" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Pillows.jpg/150px-Pillows.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Pillows.jpg/225px-Pillows.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Pillows.jpg/300px-Pillows.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pillows.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Household pillows, showing their characteristic shape, 2017</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>They are created when <a href="/wiki/Magma" title="Magma">magma</a> reaches the surface but, as there is a large difference in temperature between the lava and the water, the surface of the emergent tongue cools very quickly, forming a skin. The tongue continues to lengthen and inflate with more lava, forming a lobe, until the pressure of the magma becomes sufficient to rupture the skin and start the formation of a new eruption point nearer the vent. This process produces a series of interconnecting lobate shapes that are pillow-like in cross-section.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The skin cools much faster than the inside of the pillow, so it is very fine-grained, with a glassy texture. The magma inside the pillow cools slowly, so is slightly coarser grained than the skin, but nevertheless it is still classified as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fine_grained" title="Fine grained">fine grained</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_as_a_way-up_criterion">Use as a way-up criterion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pillow_lava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Use as a way-up criterion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pillow lavas can be used as a <a href="/wiki/Way_up_structure" title="Way up structure">way-up</a> indicator in geology;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> that is, study of their shape reveals the attitude, or position, in which they were originally formed. Pillow lava shows it is still in its original orientation when:</p>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vesicular_texture" title="Vesicular texture">Vesicles</a> are found towards the top of a pillow (because the gas trapped as part of the rock is less dense than its solid surroundings).</li>
<li>The pillow structures show a convex (rounded) upper surface.</li>
<li>The pillows might have a tapered base downwards, as they may have moulded themselves to any underlying pillows during their formation.</li>
</ol>
