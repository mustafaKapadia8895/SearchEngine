<p>The <b>round sardinella</b> (<i>Sardinella aurita</i>) is a species of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ray-finned_fish" title="Ray-finned fish">ray-finned fish</a> in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Sardinella" title="Sardinella">Sardinella</a></i> found in both sides of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whitehead_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Whitehead-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>S. aurita</i> went through a large boom in catch population around 1990. However, its numbers have been very stable through the last several years. <i>S. aurita</i> inhabits warm waters. It is a small, pelagic species that lives in tropical and subtropical waters of the western and eastern Atlantic Ocean, the Paciﬁc Ocean, the Mediterranean, and occasionally, the Black Sea. The gonads start to develop in April and are fully mature one month later. Plankton in spawning regions are full of eggs and larvae from the end of June into September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ana_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ana-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diagnostic_features">Diagnostic features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Round_sardinella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Diagnostic features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sardinella aurita</i> has a particularly elongated body, a relatively rounded belly, and a large number of fine gill rakers (up to 160). This is one of the largest <i>Sardinella</i> species, averaging 23 to 28cm. It has eight pelvic fin rays. It has frontoparietal stripes on the top of its head, a faint golden midlateral line, and a distinctive black spot on the hind border of the gill cover. It is often caught along with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sardinella_longiceps" title="Sardinella longiceps">Sardinella longiceps</a></i>, and the two are not easily distinguished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whitehead_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Whitehead-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Round_sardinella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fish prefer shallower waters around 24°C. It reaches depths of 350 m. It is a <a href="/wiki/Shoaling_and_schooling" title="Shoaling and schooling">schooling</a> fish that feeds on <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">phytoplankton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zooplankton" title="Zooplankton">zooplankton</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Copepod" title="Copepod">copepods</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whitehead_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Whitehead-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fisheries">Fisheries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Round_sardinella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fisheries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fisheries for this species exist off the West African coast, in the Mediterranean Sea, and along the coasts of Venezuela and Brazil. Fishery numbers in 1983 totaled 1,983,000 tons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whitehead_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Whitehead-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
