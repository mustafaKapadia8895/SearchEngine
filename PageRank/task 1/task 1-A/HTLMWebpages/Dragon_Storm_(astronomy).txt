<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Dragon Storm (astronomy)</h1>

<p><b>Dragon Storm</b> (dubbed so in September 2004 because of its unusual shape) is a large, bright and complex <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convective" title="Convective">convective</a> <a href="/wiki/Storm" title="Storm">storm</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a>'s southern <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">hemisphere</a>. It appears to be long-lived and periodically flares up to produce dramatic white plumes that then subside. The storm is a strong source of radio emissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nasa-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
