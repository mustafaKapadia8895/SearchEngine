<p><b>Coffeyville</b> is a city in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County,_Kansas" title="Montgomery County, Kansas">Montgomery County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, located along the <a href="/wiki/Verdigris_River" title="Verdigris River">Verdigris River</a> in the state's <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Kansas" title="Southeast Kansas">southeastern region</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GNIS_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-GNIS-1">[1]</a></sup> As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the city population was 10,295.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-6">[6]</a></sup> It is the most populous city of Montgomery County and with its southeast Kansas location is located in the <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a> media market. The town of <a href="/wiki/South_Coffeyville,_Oklahoma" title="South Coffeyville, Oklahoma">South Coffeyville, Oklahoma</a> is located approximately 1 mile south of the city, existing as a separate political entity immediately south of the state line.</p>

<p>This settlement was founded in 1869 as an Indian trading post by Col. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=James_A._Coffey&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="James A. Coffey (page does not exist)">James A. Coffey</a>, serving the population across the border in what was then the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Territory" title="Indian Territory">Indian Territory</a>. The town was stimulated in 1871 by being made a stop on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Leavenworth,_Lawrence_%26_Galveston_Railroad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Leavenworth, Lawrence &amp; Galveston Railroad (page does not exist)">Leavenworth, Lawrence &amp; Galveston Railroad</a>, which connected it to other markets and developments. With the arrival of the railroad, a young surveyor, Napoleon B. Blanton, was dispatched to lay out the town. The naming of the town was left to the toss of a coin between Col. Coffey and U.S. Army Captain Blanton. Coffey won the toss and the town was officially named Coffeyville.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The city was first incorporated in 1872, but the charter was voided as illegal, and the city was re-incorporated in March 1873.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-hist-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Federal_Writers.27_Project_1939_174_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Federal_Writers.27_Project_1939_174-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a frontier settlement, Coffeyville had its share of violence. On October 5, 1892, four of the <a href="/wiki/Dalton_Gang" title="Dalton Gang">Dalton Gang</a> were killed in a shootout during an attempted bank robbery; <a href="/wiki/Emmett_Dalton" title="Emmett Dalton">Emmett Dalton</a> survived with 23 gunshot wounds and convicted at trial for his crimes. He served 14 years before being pardoned. The gang had been trying to rob the First National and Condon banks, located across the street from each other. Residents recognized them under their disguises of fake beards and attacked the gang members as they fled one of the banks. Four citizens, including a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._marshal" title="U.S. marshal">U.S. marshal</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.odmp.org/officer/3343-marshal-charles-t-connelly" rel="nofollow">Marshal Charles T. Connelly</a>, died defending the town. The town holds an annual celebration each October to commemorate the Dalton Raid and the citizens who were lost.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>After the discovery of its resources of plentiful natural gas and abundant clay, Coffeyville enjoyed rapid growth from 1890 to 1910, as its population expanded sixfold. From the turn of the 20th century to the 1930s, it was one of the largest glass and brick manufacturing centers in the nation. During this same period, the development of oil production attracted the founding of several oil field equipment manufacturers, and more workers and residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Federal_Writers.27_Project_1939_174_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Federal_Writers.27_Project_1939_174-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Coffeyville industrialist Douglas Brown founded Coffeyville Multiscope, which produced components of the <a href="/wiki/Norden_bombsight" title="Norden bombsight">Norden bombsight</a>. This played a determining role in the perfection of precision daylight bombing during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> as a result of the bombsight's advanced accuracy and drift correction capability.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1930 residents in Coffeyville organized a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Church_of_Latter_Day_Saints" title="Church of Latter Day Saints">Church of Latter Day Saints</a> (LDS) Sunday School; it was one of only 11 places in Kansas to have such a facility then.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_flood">2007 flood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2007 flood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 1, 2007, Coffeyville suffered a major flood when the <a href="/wiki/Verdigris_River" title="Verdigris River">Verdigris River</a> crested at 10 feet above flood stage and flooded approximately a third of the city. The flood topped the local refinery (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coffeyville_Resources_LLC" title="Coffeyville Resources LLC">Coffeyville Resources LLC</a>) levees by 4 feet, allowing oil to pollute the water. Approximately 1700 barrels (71,000 gallons) of crude oil mingled with the already contaminated flood waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPA" title="EPA">EPA</a> worked to prevent the oil and flood water mix from continuing downriver, where it could damage the water in Oologah Lake near <a href="/wiki/Oologah,_Oklahoma" title="Oologah, Oklahoma">Oologah, Oklahoma</a>. Many residential water supplies are drawn from that lake. A minimal amount of oil reached Oolagah Lake, and it did not pose a threat to the water supplies of other cities along the Verdigris River or from the lake. A number of animals were found dead or injured in flood areas, covered with oil.</p>
<p>By July 2, areas east of Patterson Street were off limits, and a curfew was enacted in other areas of the city. On July 3, the city lost its supply of potable water, but the water service was restored and the order to boil water rescinded on July 7. The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> (FEMA) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> came to aid residents, and Kansas Governor <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Sebelius" title="Kathleen Sebelius">Kathleen Sebelius</a> and President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> declared the city a federal disaster area. Most displaced residents found shelter with family and friends, but many were sheltered in two area churches and a senior citizens' apartment complex. Some pets were rescued to a temporary animal shelter built for them at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=LeClere_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="LeClere Park (page does not exist)">LeClere Park</a>.</p>
<p>The flooded area on the city's east side was reopened on July 11 for residents and business owners to begin assessing damage and to retrieve salvageable items.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>In order to focus on the post-flood recovery and clean-up, the city and state cancelled the 2007 Inter-State Fair &amp; Rodeo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The ongoing flood recovery included a wholesale environmental remediation of the flood-affected eastern portion of the city, which continued through late 2008 to early 2009. Many of the flood-damaged homes were purchased by Coffeyville Resources LLC as a part of its effort to compensate the homeowners affected by the oil spill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coffeyville is located in the southeast corner of Kansas, about 75 miles (121 km) north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tulsa" title="Tulsa">Tulsa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>, and 60 miles (97 km) west of <a href="/wiki/Joplin,_Missouri" title="Joplin, Missouri">Joplin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>. The city is situated about one-half mile north of the Oklahoma state line at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;params=37_2_16_N_95_37_35_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">37°2′16″N</span> <span class="longitude">95°37′35″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">37.03778°N 95.62639°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">37.03778; -95.62639</span></span></span></a></span> (37.037708, -95.626438),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GNIS_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-GNIS-1">[1]</a></sup> along the west bank of the <a href="/wiki/Verdigris_River" title="Verdigris River">Verdigris River</a>. The city is the location of the lowest point in the state of Kansas at 679 feet (207 m) above sea level. <a href="/wiki/Coffeyville_Municipal_Airport" title="Coffeyville Municipal Airport">Coffeyville Municipal Airport</a> is a few miles northeast of the city along US-169. Though Coffeyville is the largest city in Montgomery County, the county seat is <a href="/wiki/Independence,_Kansas" title="Independence, Kansas">Independence</a>, 16 miles (26 km) northwest of the city.</p>
<p>Coffeyville, specifically a spot just north of Coffeyville Country Club, is the default center starting point of <a href="/wiki/Google_Maps" title="Google Maps">Google Maps</a>, being the accidental center point of the default starting map being displayed, which shows the 48 <a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">contiguous United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Kansas" title="Lawrence, Kansas">Lawrence, Kansas</a> also claims to be the Google center). Other locations that are considered to be the <a href="/wiki/Geographic_center_of_the_contiguous_United_States" title="Geographic center of the contiguous United States">geographic center of the contiguous United States</a> are also in Kansas.</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 7.43 square miles (19.24 km<sup>2</sup>), all land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Coffeyville has experienced a slow and steady population decline since around 1960, when its population peaked at more than 17,000. Changes in industry and oil production have caused a loss of jobs in the area, and residents have moved to find work. As of 2006 the population was estimated to be 10,387 in the year 2006, a decrease of 645, or -5.8%, over the previous six years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.7B.7BLookupUSEstPop.7C2014600.7CEYR.7D.7DSubEst_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-.7B.7BLookupUSEstPop.7C2014600.7CEYR.7D.7DSubEst-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FactFinder_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-4">[4]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 10,295 people, 4,226 households, and 2,456 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,385.6 inhabitants per square mile (535.0/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 5,021 housing units at an average density of 675.8 per square mile (260.9/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 72.3% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 11.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 5.0% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 3.3% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 6.8% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 7.4% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 4,226 households of which 28.2% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 39.9% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 13.5% had a female householder with no husband present, 4.8% had a male householder with no wife present, and 41.9% were non-families. 35.7% of all households were made up of individuals and 16.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.29 and the average family size was 2.98.</p>
<p>The median age in the city was 37.1 years. 22.6% of residents were under the age of 18; 13.3% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 22.4% were from 25 to 44; 23.6% were from 45 to 64; and 18.1% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 47.5% male and 52.5% female.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the U.S. Census in 2000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-15">[15]</a></sup> there were 11,021 people, 4,691 households, and 2,847 families residing in the city. The population density was 1,562.1 people per square mile (602.7/km²). There were 5,550 housing units at an average density of 786.6 per square mile (303.5/km²). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">racial makeup</a> of the city was 75.76% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_American" title="White American">White</a>, 12.12% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_American" title="Black American">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a>, 4.97% <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a>, 0.60% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_American" title="Asian American">Asian</a>, 0.04% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_American" title="Pacific Islander American">Pacific Islander</a>, 1.63% from other races, and 4.87% from <a href="/wiki/Multiracial" title="Multiracial">two or more races</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic" title="Hispanic">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino" title="Latino">Latino</a> of any race were 3.82% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 4,691 households out of which 26.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 45.0% were married couples living together, 11.9% had a female householder with no husband present, and 39.3% were non-families. 35.4% of all households were made up of individuals and 18.6% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.25 and the average family size was 2.90.</p>
<p>In the city the population was spread out with 24.0% under the age of 18, 10.0% from 18 to 24, 23.3% from 25 to 44, 21.3% from 45 to 64, and 21.4% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 39 years. For every 100 females there were 87.2 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 82.1 males.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Median_household_income" title="Median household income">median income for a household</a> in the city was $25,542, and the median income for a family was $33,180. Males had a median income of $29,199 versus $17,940 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $15,182. About 10.9% of families and 15.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 18.6% of those under age 18 and 10.9% of those age 65 or over.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coffeyville has a long history as a center of industry and manufacturing.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Coffeyville_Resources" title="Coffeyville Resources">Coffeyville Resources</a> operates a 100,000 barrels per day refinery and a large nitrogen fertilizer plant, using a unique Texaco process of ammonia extraction from coke byproducts produced in the refinery. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sherwin_Williams" title="Sherwin Williams">Sherwin Williams</a> Chemical Co. has operated a smelting facility in the community since 1909.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Coffeyville is also home to <a href="/wiki/John_Deere" title="John Deere">John Deere</a> Corporation's Coffeyville Works, which is a major manufacturer of off-road equipment automatic transmissions for the construction, agriculture and mining industries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Acme Foundry is a foundry that has been in operation since 1905 and employs more than 300 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Taylor Crane &amp; Rigging is a regional hauling operation, full-service industrial mover and craning services company. Taylor also maintains a facility in <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Other nearby in-county employers include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cessna_Aircraft" title="Cessna Aircraft">Cessna Aircraft</a> Division of <a href="/wiki/Textron" title="Textron">Textron</a> and Spears Manufacturing, a large producer of extruded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PVC" title="PVC">PVC</a> pipe products.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Historic</dt>
</dl>
<p>Southwire Corp is a maker of stranded and solid core wire and acquired the Leviton Industries facility of American Insulated Wire in 2010. The plant was closed in 2014.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Coffeyville is home to a fulfillment center leased since 1999 by online retailer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> It was opened in a facility previously operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Books" title="Golden Books">Golden Books</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After more than a decade of operations here, Amazon announced that it would close this warehouse in February 2015, because it was shifting to warehouses located closer to large cities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>Public</dt>
</dl>
<p>Public education is provided by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coffeyville_School_District" title="Coffeyville School District">Coffeyville School District</a> (USD 445). The district educates almost 2,000 students in three facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The district has one early learning center, one large consolidated elementary school with four pods and an Age-to-Age kindergarten, one middle school and one high school.</p>
<ul>
<li>Field Kindley High School, grades 9–12, named after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Field_Kindley" title="Field Kindley">Field Kindley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li>Roosevelt Middle School, grades 7–8.</li>
<li>Community Elementary School, grades K–6. Age-to-Age Kindergarten</li>
</ul>
<p>Five-year-old kindergarten children spend their days learning beside community members who have 80, 90, and even 100 years of life skills and experiences. Now, as we end our seventh year of intergenerational learning, it is evident that both age groups benefit from what the other generation has to offer. The needs of the elderly are met through reasons most people understand: the children offer them purpose and fill their days with energy and productive activities. The children’s needs are met at a different but equal level. The grandmas and grandpas have time to listen to their stories about family and pets and about what makes them happy and what scares them.</p>
<p>Age-to-Age Kindergarten offers a unique experience for students on a daily basis. Age-to-Age days are spent with intergenerational exercise, small group table times, individual sign-in times with a grandma, one-on-one reading, and regular social studies and science experiences. This is in partnership with Windsor Place.</p>
<ul>
<li>Dr. Jerry Hamm Early Learning Center, grades preschool. The educational opportunities provided by the teachers and staff at the early learning center are designed to allow young children experiences to increase their social and emotional skills as well as their cognitive, literacy and numeracy skills. The children who attend the ELC are entering the kindergarten classroom better prepared to thrive in the classroom environment. The goal is to create a foundation which will impact students’ learning for a lifetime. This is in partnership with Kansas Head Start Association.</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dt>Private</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>Holy Name School, grades PK-6, parochial private school operated by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Wichita" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Wichita">Roman Catholic Diocese of Wichita</a>.</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dt>College</dt>
</dl>
<p>Advanced education is provided by <a href="/wiki/Coffeyville_Community_College" title="Coffeyville Community College">Coffeyville Community College</a> at three campuses. The main campus and technical trades campus are each located in Coffeyville, while a third campus is located in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Kansas" title="Columbus, Kansas">Columbus, Kansas</a>. Four-year college degrees are offered by <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_Wesleyan_University" title="Oklahoma Wesleyan University">Oklahoma Wesleyan University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Friends_University" title="Friends University">Friends University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sterling_College_(Kansas)" title="Sterling College (Kansas)">Sterling College</a> at the main Coffeyville Community College campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Coffeyville Community College has a long history of academic and athletic success.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coffeyville,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bus service is provided northward towards <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a> and southward towards <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a> by Jefferson Lines (subcontractor of <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound Lines</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Coffeyville and surrounding communities are also served by Connections Transportation which has its headquarters in nearby Independence, Kansas. Coffeyville has two railroads that serve the community, <a class="external text" href="http://www.up.com/index.htm" rel="nofollow">Union Pacific</a> and a short line railroad SKO and <a href="/wiki/Watco_Companies" title="Watco Companies">WATCO</a>. Coffeyville is served by two US highways, US 166 which runs east-west from where the highway enters from the east at the <a class="external text" href="https://www.google.com/maps/place/37%C2%B002'30.8%22N+95%C2%B035'30.6%22W/@37.041893,-95.5923842,175m/data=!3m2!1e3!4b1!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x0" rel="nofollow">Verdigris River bridge</a> on Northeast Street to Eleventh Street, and exits to the west at the <a class="external text" href="https://www.google.com/maps/place/37%C2%B002'11.3%22N+95%C2%B040'25.2%22W/@37.036469,-95.6742202,175m/data=!3m2!1e3!4b1!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x0" rel="nofollow">Union Pacific Railroad viaduct</a> on Eighth Street. US 169 which is a north-south highway, enters from the east at the Verdigris River bridge on Northeast Street to Eleventh Street, then turns left just past the <a class="external text" href="https://www.google.com/maps/place/37%C2%B001'58.7%22N+95%C2%B036'52.8%22W/@37.03296,-95.6152082,175m/data=!3m2!1e3!4b1!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x0" rel="nofollow">SKO-WATCO railroad overpass</a> onto <a class="external text" href="https://www.google.com/maps/@37.0329574,-95.6152779,3a,75y,274.53h,81.94t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1slmcFxewgbgU8L6725NU5hw!2e0!5s20140401T000000!7i13312!8i6656" rel="nofollow">Walnut Street</a> and continues south on <a class="external text" href="https://www.google.com/maps/@37.0079562,-95.6160008,3a,65y,189.91h,91.05t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sMcHrTigyhtVpTP4kHjitvA!2e0!7i13312!8i6656" rel="nofollow">Walnut Street</a> and exits the city and continues south towards <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a>.</p>
