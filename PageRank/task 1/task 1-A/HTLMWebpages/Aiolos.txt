<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Aeolus</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Aiolos&amp;redirect=no" title="Aiolos">Aiolos</a>)</span></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For other uses, see <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Aeolus_(disambiguation)" title="Aeolus (disambiguation)">Aeolus (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>, <b>Aeolus</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>: <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">Αἴολος</span> <i>Aiolos</i> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Greek" title="Help:IPA/Greek">[a͜ɪ́olos]</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Modern Greek</a>: <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Greek" title="Help:IPA/Greek">[ˈe.o.los]</a></span><small class="nowrap"> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Ell-Aiolos.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9c/Ell-Aiolos.ogg" title="Ell-Aiolos.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small> "quick-moving, nimble") is a name shared by three mythical characters. These three personages are often difficult to tell apart, and even the ancient mythographers appear to have been perplexed about which Aeolus was which. <a href="/wiki/Diodorus_Siculus" title="Diodorus Siculus">Diodorus Siculus</a> made an attempt to define each of these three (although it is clear that he also became muddled), and his opinion is followed here.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dgrbm_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dgrbm-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>The first <b><a href="/wiki/Aeolus_(son_of_Hellen)" title="Aeolus (son of Hellen)">Aeolus</a></b> was a son of <a href="/wiki/Hellen" title="Hellen">Hellen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eponym" title="Eponym">eponymous</a> founder of the Aeolian race.</li>
<li>The second <b><a href="/wiki/Aeolus_(son_of_Poseidon)" title="Aeolus (son of Poseidon)">Aeolus</a></b> was a son of <a href="/wiki/Poseidon" title="Poseidon">Poseidon</a>, who led a colony to islands in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrhenian_Sea" title="Tyrrhenian Sea">Tyrrhenian Sea</a>.</li>
<li>The third <b><a href="/wiki/Aeolus_(son_of_Hippotes)" title="Aeolus (son of Hippotes)">Aeolus</a></b> was a son of <a href="/wiki/Hippotes" title="Hippotes">Hippotes</a> who is mentioned in <i><a href="/wiki/Odyssey" title="Odyssey">Odyssey</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Aeneid" title="Aeneid">Aeneid</a></i> as the Keeper of the <a href="/wiki/Anemoi" title="Anemoi">Winds</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>All three men named Aeolus appear to be connected genealogically, although the precise relationship, especially regarding the second and third Aeolus, is often ambiguous as their identities seem to have been merged by many ancient writers.</p>
<p>Aeolus was also the name of the following minor characters:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Aeolus</b> was a defender of <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Greece" title="Thebes, Greece">Thebes</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Against_Thebes" title="Seven Against Thebes">Seven Against Thebes</a>. He was killed by <a href="/wiki/Parthenopeus" title="Parthenopeus">Parthenopaeus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Aeolus</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Troy" title="Troy">Trojan</a>, companion of <a href="/wiki/Aeneas" title="Aeneas">Aeneas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, where he was killed by <a href="/wiki/Turnus" title="Turnus">Turnus</a>, King of the <a href="/wiki/Rutuli" title="Rutuli">Rutulians</a>. Aeolus was father of <a href="/wiki/Clytius" title="Clytius">Clytius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Misenus" title="Misenus">Misenus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
