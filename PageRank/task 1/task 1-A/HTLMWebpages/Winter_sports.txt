<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Winter sport</h1>

<p>A <b>winter sport</b> or <b>winter activity</b> is a recreational activity or <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sport</a> which is played on snow or ice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Most such sports are variations of <a href="/wiki/Skiing" title="Skiing">skiing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_skating" title="Ice skating">ice skating</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sledding" title="Sledding">sledding</a>. Traditionally such sports were only played in cold areas during <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a>, but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artificial_snow" title="Artificial snow">artificial snow</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artificial_ice" title="Artificial ice">artificial ice</a> allow more flexibility. Artificial ice can be used to provide <a href="/wiki/Ice_rink" title="Ice rink">ice rinks</a> for <a href="/wiki/Ice_skating" title="Ice skating">ice skating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bandy" title="Bandy">bandy</a> in a milder climate.</p>
<p>Common individual sports include <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing" title="Alpine skiing">Alpine skiing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snowboarding" title="Snowboarding">snowboarding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ski_jumping" title="Ski jumping">ski jumping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating" title="Speed skating">speed skating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating" title="Figure skating">figure skating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luge" title="Luge">luge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skeleton_(sport)" title="Skeleton (sport)">skeleton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobsleigh" title="Bobsleigh">bobsleigh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snowmobile" title="Snowmobile">snowmobiling</a>. Common team sports include <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Curling" title="Curling">curling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bandy" title="Bandy">bandy</a>. Based on the number of participants, ice hockey is the world's most popular winter sport, followed by bandy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Winter sports often have their own multi-sport tournaments, such as the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympic_Games" title="Winter Olympic Games">Winter Olympic Games</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_sport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Snow and ice during the wintertime has led to other means of transportation, such as sledges, skis and skates. This also led to different pastimes and sports being developed in the winter season as compared to other times of the year. Naturally, winter sports are more popular in countries with longer winter seasons.</p>
<p>While most winter sports are played outside, ice hockey, speed skating and to some extent bandy have moved indoors starting in the mid-20th century. Indoor <a href="/wiki/Ice_rink" title="Ice rink">ice rinks</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artificial_ice" title="Artificial ice">artificial ice</a> allow <a href="/wiki/Ice_skating" title="Ice skating">ice skating</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hockey" title="Hockey">hockey</a> to be played in hot climates.</p>

<p><i><b>Note</b></i>: the Olympic rings next to a sport indicates that this particular sport is included in the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympic_Games" title="Winter Olympic Games">Winter Olympic Games</a>, as of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympics" title="2014 Winter Olympics">2014 Winter Olympics</a> in Sochi. The Paralympic logo indicates the same for a sport not in the Olympics but in the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Paralympic_Games" title="Winter Paralympic Games">Winter Paralympic Games</a>.</p>
