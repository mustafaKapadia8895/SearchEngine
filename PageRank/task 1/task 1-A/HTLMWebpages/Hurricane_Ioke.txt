<p><b>Hurricane Ioke</b>, also referred to as <b>Typhoon Ioke</b>, was the most intense <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a> ever recorded in the Central <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>. The first storm to form in the Central Pacific in the <a href="/wiki/2006_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2006 Pacific hurricane season">2006 Pacific hurricane season</a>, Ioke was a record breaking, long-lived and extremely powerful storm that traversed the Pacific for 17 days, reaching the equivalent of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Pacific_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Pacific hurricanes">Category 5</a> status on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale</a> on three different occasions.</p>
<p>The cyclone developed in the <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> on August 20 far to the south of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>. Encountering warm waters, little <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, and well-defined <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, Ioke intensified from a tropical depression to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 4</a> status within 48 hours. Late on August 22 it rapidly weakened to Category 2 status before crossing over <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Atoll" title="Johnston Atoll">Johnston Atoll</a>. Two days later favorable conditions again allowed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid strengthening</a>, and Ioke attained Category 5 status on August 25 before crossing the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a>. As it continued westward its intensity fluctuated, and on August 31 it passed near <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a> with winds of 155 mph (250 km/h). Ioke gradually weakened as it turned northwestward and northward, and by September 6 it had transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>. The remnants of Ioke accelerated northeastward and ultimately crossed into <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>.</p>
<p>Ioke did not affect any permanently populated areas in the Central Pacific or Western Pacific basins as a hurricane or a typhoon. A crew of 12 people rode out the hurricane in a hurricane-proof bunker on <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Atoll" title="Johnston Atoll">Johnston Atoll</a>; the crew estimated winds reached over 100 mph (160 km/h), which damaged trees on the island but did not impact the island's bird population. The typhoon left moderate damage on <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a> totaling $88 million (2006 USD, equivalent to $110 million in 2016), including blown off roofs and damaged buildings, though the infrastructure of the island was left intact; all military personnel were evacuated from the island. Later, the extratropical remnants of Ioke produced a severe storm surge along the <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaskan</a> coastline, causing beach erosion.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ioke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ioke_2006_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1476" data-file-width="2000" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ioke_2006_track.png/220px-Ioke_2006_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ioke_2006_track.png/330px-Ioke_2006_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ioke_2006_track.png/440px-Ioke_2006_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ioke_2006_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> (ITCZ) spawned a tropical disturbance with a low-level circulation far to the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawai<span class="unicode">ʻ</span>i</a> in the middle of August 2006. Under the influence of a strong westward-moving <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> to its north, the disturbance tracked nearly due westward, with deep convection in the region increasing and decreasing on a daily basis. It slowly became better organized, and early on August 20 the disturbance developed into Tropical Depression One-C while located about 775 mi (1,245 km) south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honolulu,_Hawaii" title="Honolulu, Hawaii">Honolulu, Hawaii</a>. At the time, there was no convection associated with the ITCZ within 10° <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">Longitude</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> With <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> practically non-existent and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">sea surface temperatures</a> of around 82 °F (28 °C), conditions favored strengthening,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> and operationally the cyclone was forecast to reach minimal hurricane status within four days before beginning to weaken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The depression attained tropical storm status within six hours of developing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Central Pacific Hurricane Center">Central Pacific Hurricane Center</a> designated the system with the name <i>Ioke</i> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_language" title="Hawaiian language">Hawaiian</a> for the name <a href="/wiki/Joyce_(name)" title="Joyce (name)">Joyce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ioke_name_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ioke_name-3">[3]</a></sup> Subsequently, Ioke quickly strengthened, and by late on August 20 the storm developed a <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">central dense overcast</a> and the beginnings of an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eyewall</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> early on August 21 the storm intensified into a hurricane, just 24 hours after first developing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hurricane Ioke steadily deepened as it continued west-northwestward, with better definition of the eye and deepening of the eyewall convection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Near the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a> a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">frontal trough</a> turned the hurricane to the northwest, and after a period of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a> Ioke attained winds of 135 mph (215 km/h) early on August 22 while located about 280 mi (450 km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Atoll" title="Johnston Atoll">Johnston Atoll</a>. After maintaining Category 4 status on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a> for about 18 hours,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> southwesterly wind shear slightly disrupted the inner core of the hurricane,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and Ioke quickly weakened to winds of about 105 mph (169 km/h). Late on August 22, the hurricane passed about 30 mi (48 km) south of Johnston Atoll,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> with the northeastern portion of the eyewall crossing the atoll early on August 23.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After turning westward later in the day, wind shear began to decrease, allowing a second period of rapid deepening. By August 24 the hurricane maintained a 23 mi (37 km) closed eyewall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and on August 25 Ioke attained <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Pacific_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Pacific hurricanes">Category 5</a> status on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale while located about 970 mi (1,560 km) west-southwest of the Hawaiian Island of <a href="/wiki/Kauai" title="Kauai">Kaua<span class="unicode">ʻ</span>i</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Typhoon_Ioke_28_aug_2006_0130Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="7200" data-file-width="7200" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Typhoon_Ioke_28_aug_2006_0130Z.jpg/220px-Typhoon_Ioke_28_aug_2006_0130Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Typhoon_Ioke_28_aug_2006_0130Z.jpg/330px-Typhoon_Ioke_28_aug_2006_0130Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Typhoon_Ioke_28_aug_2006_0130Z.jpg/440px-Typhoon_Ioke_28_aug_2006_0130Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Typhoon Ioke gaining strength after crossing the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a> on August 28</div>

<p>After maintaining Category 5 status for about 18 hours,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Hurricane Ioke weakened slightly due to an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)#Eyewall_replacement_cycles" title="Eye (cyclone)">eyewall replacement cycle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Completing the cycle on August 26,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc26_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc26-10">[10]</a></sup> the hurricane restrengthened to Category 5 status. The trough to its west tracked further away from the hurricane, allowing the subtropical ridge to build ahead of the hurricane which turned Ioke to the southwest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The overall environment remained very favorable for sustainment of the powerful cyclone. Strong upper-level cyclones far to its northwest provided outflow channels and light wind shear, with warm water temperatures along its path.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc26_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc26-10">[10]</a></sup> With the conditions, the <a href="/wiki/Geophysical_Fluid_Dynamics_Laboratory" title="Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory">Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory</a> hurricane model predicted Ioke to reach winds of 220 mph (350 km/h), with a predicted minimum pressure of 860 mbar (25 inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Early on August 27, the pressure dropped to 915 mbar (27.0 inHg), and shortly thereafter Ioke crossed the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a>, becoming a 160 mph (255 km/h) typhoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unofficially referred as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">super typhoon</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC), Ioke remained at the equivalence of a Category 5 hurricane for about 12 hours after crossing the Date Line. It then began a slight weakening trend on August 28,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iokeatcr_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-iokeatcr-12">[12]</a></sup> due to increased <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">inflow</a> from the ridge to its north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prog33_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-prog33-13">[13]</a></sup> On August 29, the cyclone turned to the west and west-northwest while tracking around the periphery of the subtropical ridge, and Ioke again reached the equivalence of Category 5 status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iokeatcr_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-iokeatcr-12">[12]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> (JMA) assessed Ioke as attaining peak 10‑minute sustained winds of 120 mph (195 km/h) on August 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-jma-14">[14]</a></sup> Later that day, the typhoon weakened to the equivalent of a Category 4 hurricane for the final time, and on August 31 Ioke passed very near <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a> with winds of about 155 mph (250 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iokeatcr_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-iokeatcr-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>By September 1, increased wind shear and drier air caused the eye of Ioke to become cloud-filled and elongated,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prog51_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-prog51-15">[15]</a></sup> and by September 2 Ioke was undergoing another eyewall replacement cycle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prog55_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-prog55-16">[16]</a></sup> On September 2, Ioke passed about 50 mi (80 km) north of <a href="/wiki/Minami-Tori-shima" title="Minami-Tori-shima">Minami-Tori-shima</a> with winds of about 125 mph (200 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-17">[17]</a></sup> Gradual weakening continued, and the typhoon steadily shifted its track to the northwest around the subtropical ridge. A deepening <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> turned Ioke to the north-northwest and north,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prog61_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-prog61-18">[18]</a></sup> and the cyclone weakened to a tropical storm a few hundred miles east of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. After accelerating northeastward, the cyclone began losing tropical characteristics, and the JTWC declared Ioke as an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> on September 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iokeatcr_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-iokeatcr-12">[12]</a></sup> The JMA maintained Ioke as a typhoon until a day later, and maintained Ioke as a tropical cyclone until it was declared extratropical midday on September 6. The extratropical remnants of Ioke were tracked by the JMA until September 7, when it was located near the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-jma-14">[14]</a></sup> The storm deepened as it approached the Aleutians, and re-developed winds of hurricane-force. The storm entered the <a href="/wiki/Bering_Sea" title="Bering Sea">Bering Sea</a> on September 8, and after turning eastward, crossed the Aleutian Islands and entered the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Alaska" title="Gulf of Alaska">Gulf of Alaska</a>. The extratropical remnants of Ioke dissipated near southeastern <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> on September 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwl_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwl-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Johnston_Atoll">Johnston Atoll</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ioke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Johnston Atoll">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Late on August 21, about 24 hours prior to its closest approach, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Central Pacific Hurricane Center">Central Pacific Hurricane Center</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane warning</a> for uninhabited territory of <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Atoll" title="Johnston Atoll">Johnston Island</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa8_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa8-20">[20]</a></sup> due to the uncertainty of whether anyone was on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> vessel and a 12 person crew were on the island, and after securing their ship the crew took shelter in a hurricane-proof concrete bunker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> There were no meteorological observations on the island, but the crew estimated tropical storm force winds lasted for about 27 hours with hurricane-force winds lasting six to eight hours; peak wind gusts were estimated at 110 to 130 mph (175 to 210 km/h). The crew sustained no injuries, and their ship received only minor damage. Hurricane Ioke, with a portion of its eye crossing the atoll, left an estimated 15% of the palm trees on the island with their tops blown off, with some ironwood trees blown over; the island bird population was unaffected. The hurricane produced rough surf which washed away a portion of a sea wall and an adjacent road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wake_Island">Wake Island</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ioke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wake Island">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="9600" data-file-width="8000" height="264" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg/220px-Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg/330px-Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg/440px-Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Typhoon_Ioke_Over_Wake_2006.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Typhoon Ioke near Wake Island on August 31</div>
</div>

<p>Under the threat of the typhoon for several days, two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-17_Globemaster_III" title="C-17 Globemaster III">C-17 Globemaster III</a> <a href="/wiki/Airlift" title="Airlift">airlifters</a> evacuated between 188–200 military personnel from <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a> to Hawaii, the first full-scale evacuation of the island since Typhoon Sarah in <a href="/wiki/1967_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1967 Pacific typhoon season">1967</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-829afpn_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-829afpn-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> A buoy just east of the island recorded a pressure of 921.5 millibars (27.21 inHg) as Ioke crossed directly over it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-gp-25">[25]</a></sup> Before the typhoon passed just north of the island, an anemometer recorded hurricane-force winds with a peak wind gust of 100 mph (160 km/h) before the instrument stopped reporting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wimo_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-wimo-26">[26]</a></sup> Sustained winds were estimated to have reached 155 mph (250 km/h), with gusts to 190 mph (310 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The minimum central pressure recorded on the island was 934 mbar at 0906 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on August 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wimo_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-wimo-26">[26]</a></sup> The typhoon was expected to produce a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> of 18 ft (5.5 m) and wave heights of 40 ft (12 m) along Wake Island, where the highest point is 20 ft (6.1 m). Additionally, heavy rainfall from the typhoon left buildings flooded, with 2 ft (0.61 m) of standing water found several days after its passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afd_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-afd-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The powerful winds of Typhoon Ioke caused extensive damage to the island's power grid, leaving most power lines to buildings and backup generators damaged. The combination of the winds and storm surge flooding damaged 70% of the buildings in the territory, many of which with moderate roof damage. All low-lying areas were described as being covered with sea water or sand, and the territory was left without running water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afd_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-afd-28">[28]</a></sup> Communications were downed on the island, with satellite dishes and cables destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afpn917_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-afpn917-29">[29]</a></sup> Damage to the infrastructure was extensive, though repairable and less than expected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afd2_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-afd2-30">[30]</a></sup> Damage on the island was estimated at $88 million (2006 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan_and_Alaska">Japan and Alaska</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ioke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Japan and Alaska">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<div class="thumbcaption">

The extratropical remnants of Ioke over the <a href="/wiki/Bering_Sea" title="Bering Sea">Bering Sea</a>, west of Alaska on September 7</div>

<p>On September 1, the Japan Meteorological Agency ordered the temporary evacuation of its staff on <a href="/wiki/Minami-Tori-shima" title="Minami-Tori-shima">Minami-Tori-shima</a>, under threat of the typhoon. The agency expected high waves and winds on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Facilities on the island were damaged, although it was repaired and fully operational within three weeks after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical remnant</a> of Ioke produced a storm surge and high surf in excess of 30 ft (9.1 m) along the southwestern coastline of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>, which coincided with the <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">astronomical high tide</a>; the combination led to minor flooding along <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Bay" title="Bristol Bay">Bristol Bay</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yukon-Kuskokwim_Delta" title="Yukon-Kuskokwim Delta">Yukon-Kuskokwim Delta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-34">[34]</a></sup> Wind gusts peaked at 84 mph (135 km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Unalaska,_Alaska" title="Unalaska, Alaska">Unalaska</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc2_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc2-35">[35]</a></sup> The system produced moderate to heavy rainfall across the western portion of Alaska, including daily rainfall records of 1.15 inches (29 mm) at <a href="/wiki/Bethel,_Alaska" title="Bethel, Alaska">Bethel</a> and 0.67 in (17 mm) at <a href="/wiki/Kotzebue,_Alaska" title="Kotzebue, Alaska">Kotzebue</a>. Rainfall continued into the southeastern portion of the state, contributing to above-normal rainfall totals near <a href="/wiki/Juneau,_Alaska" title="Juneau, Alaska">Juneau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cattlenet_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-cattlenet-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records_and_aftermath">Records and aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ioke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Records and aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3000" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg/250px-Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg/375px-Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg/500px-Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ioke-wake-damage003.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Damage on Wake Island after Ioke</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Hurricane Ioke became one of five hurricanes to reach <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Pacific_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Pacific hurricanes">Category 5</a> status on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a> in the Central North Pacific Ocean, and the only one originating in the basin. With an estimated minimum central pressure of 915 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (27.019 <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>), the cyclone attained the lowest estimated surface pressure for any hurricane within the basin,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> surpassing the previous minimum set by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gilma_(1994)" title="Hurricane Gilma (1994)">Hurricane Gilma</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1994 Pacific hurricane season">1994</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Ioke maintained at least Category 4 status for 198 consecutive hours, which was the longest continuous time period at that intensity ever observed for any tropical cyclone anywhere on Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Additionally, the cyclone remained at the equivalence of a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">super typhoon</a> for 174 consecutive hours, which was also a record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-gp-25">[25]</a></sup> As a result of its extended duration and intensity, Hurricane/Typhoon Ioke accrued an <a href="/wiki/Accumulated_cyclone_energy" title="Accumulated cyclone energy">Accumulated cyclone energy</a> index of 82, which set a new worldwide record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> first performed an aerial assessment of damage on <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a> on September 2, three days after the typhoon struck. The flight indicated an overall damage smaller than expected, and reported a lack of oil spill or hazardous material leak. The U.S. Coast Guard arrived by boat with a team on September 7, with a preliminary damage assessment completed four days later; the team repaired a generator to provide power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afd_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-afd-28">[28]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>'s combat stores ship, the <a href="/wiki/USS_San_Jose_(AFS-7)" title="USS San Jose (AFS-7)">USNS San Jose (T-AFS-7)</a>, and sixteen members <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a>'s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=36th_Contingency_Response_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="36th Contingency Response Group (page does not exist)">36th Contingency Response Group</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andersen_AFB" title="Andersen AFB">Andersen AFB</a>, Guam arrived on September 8 and analyzed the stability of the airfield along with assisting in clean-up efforts, and after core tests workers cleared the runway to allow flights onto the territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On September 13, a group of engineers restored power on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> About two weeks after the cyclone, several buildings were operational.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afd2_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-afd2-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Central Pacific Hurricane Center requested the retirement of the name, and in April 2007, the name <i>Ioke</i> was retired, and replaced with Iopa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
