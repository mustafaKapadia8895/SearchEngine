<p>A <b>disaster</b> is a serious disruption, occurring over a relatively short time, of the functioning of a community or a society involving widespread human, material, economic or environmental loss and impacts, which exceeds the ability of the affected community or society to cope using its own resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In contemporary academia, disasters are seen as the consequence of inappropriately <a href="/wiki/Risk_management" title="Risk management">managed risk</a>. These risks are the product of a combination of both hazards and vulnerability. Hazards that strike in areas with low vulnerability will never become disasters, as in the case of uninhabited regions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quarantelli_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-quarantelli-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Developing countries suffer the greatest costs when a disaster hits – more than 95 percent of all deaths caused by hazards occur in developing countries, and losses due to natural hazards are 20 times greater (as a percentage of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GDP" title="GDP">GDP</a>) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developing_countries" title="Developing countries">developing countries</a> than in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrialized_countries" title="Industrialized countries">industrialized countries</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Disaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The word <i>disaster</i> is derived from Middle <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> <i>désastre</i> and that from Old <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> <i>disastro</i>, which in turn comes from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Pejorative" title="Pejorative">pejorative</a> prefix δυσ-, (<i>dus-</i>) "bad"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and ἀστήρ (<i>aster</i>), "star".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The root of the word <i>disaster</i> ("bad star" in Greek) comes from an <a href="/wiki/Astrology" title="Astrology">astrological</a> sense of a calamity blamed on the position of planets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classifications">Classifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Disaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Classifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Researchers have been studying disasters for more than a century, and for more than forty years <a href="/wiki/Disaster_research" title="Disaster research">disaster research</a>. The studies reflect a common opinion when they argue that all disasters can be seen as being human-made, their reasoning being that human actions before the strike of the hazard can prevent it developing into a disaster. All disasters are hence the result of human failure to introduce appropriate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disaster_management" title="Disaster management">disaster management</a> measures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atrisk_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-atrisk-8">[8]</a></sup> Hazards are routinely divided into natural or human-made, although complex disasters, where there is no single root cause, are more common in <a href="/wiki/Developing_country" title="Developing country">developing countries</a>. A specific disaster may spawn a secondary disaster that increases the impact. A classic example is an <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> that causes a <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a>, resulting in coastal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flooding" title="Flooding">flooding</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_disaster">Natural disaster</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Disaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Natural disaster">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">Natural disaster</a></div>
<p>A natural disaster is a natural process or phenomenon that may cause loss of life, injury or other health impacts, property damage, loss of livelihoods and services, social and economic disruption, or environmental damage.</p>
<p>Various phenomena like earthquakes, landslides, volcanic eruptions, floods, hurricanes, tornadoes, blizzards, tsunamis, and cyclones are all natural hazards that kill thousands of people and destroy billions of dollars of habitat and property each year. However, the rapid growth of the world's population and its increased concentration often in hazardous environments has escalated both the frequency and severity of disasters. With the tropical climate and unstable land forms, coupled with deforestation, unplanned growth proliferation, non-engineered constructions which make the disaster-prone areas more vulnerable, tardy communication, and poor or no budgetary allocation for disaster prevention, developing countries suffer more or less chronically from natural disasters. <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> tops the list of casualties caused by natural hazards.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Human-instigated">Human-instigated</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Disaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Human-instigated">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Man-made_disasters" title="Man-made disasters">Man-made disasters</a></div>
<p>Human-instigated disasters are the consequence of technological hazards. Examples include <a href="/wiki/Stampede" title="Stampede">stampedes</a>, fires, transport accidents, industrial accidents, oil spills and nuclear explosions/radiation. War and deliberate attacks may also be put in this category. As with natural hazards, man-made hazards are events that have not happened—for instance, terrorism. Man-made disasters are examples of specific cases where man-made hazards have become reality in an event.</p>
