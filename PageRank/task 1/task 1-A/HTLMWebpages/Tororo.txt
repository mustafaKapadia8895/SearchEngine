<p><b>Tororo</b> is a town in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Region,_Uganda" title="Eastern Region, Uganda">Eastern Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>. It is the main municipal, administrative, and commercial center of <a href="/wiki/Tororo_District" title="Tororo District">Tororo District</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tororo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is approximately 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) west of the town of <a href="/wiki/Malaba,_Uganda" title="Malaba, Uganda">Malaba</a> at the border between Uganda and <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>. This is approximately 230 kilometres (140 mi), by road, east of <a href="/wiki/Kampala" title="Kampala">Kampala</a>, Uganda's capital and largest city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The coordinates of the town are 0°41'34.0"N, 34°10'54.0"E (Latitude:0.692780; Longitude:34.181655).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tororo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2002, the national census put the population of the town of Tororo at about 34,800. In 2010, the Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS) estimated the population at 42,500. In 2011, UBOS estimated the mid-year population at 43,700.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The 2014 national population census put the population at 41,906.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Border_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Border-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tororo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tororo has a major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cement_in_Africa" title="Cement in Africa">cement works</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tororo_Cement_Limited" title="Tororo Cement Limited">Tororo Cement Limited</a>, that in 2007, because of power shortages, could produce only 19,000 metric tonnes of cement daily against an installed capacity of 35,000 metric tonnes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is also home to SEBA Foods, a food factory that was officially opened by the president of Uganda on 25 June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Electromaxx Limited constructed a 20 megawatt thermal plant, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tororo_Power_Station" title="Tororo Power Station">Tororo Power Station</a>, that was commissioned in June 2010.</p>
<p>Tororo is also the location of Nilefos Minerals Limited, a subsidiary of the <a href="/wiki/Madhvani_Group" title="Madhvani Group">Madhvani Group</a> of companies. Nilefos mines and processes phosphates for use in fertilizer and related industries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail_transport">Rail transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tororo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rail transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="1350" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg/220px-Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg/330px-Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg/440px-Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Uganda_railways_assessment_2010-14.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tororo railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_Railway" title="Uganda Railway">Uganda Railway</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Tororo marks the junction of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_line" title="Railway line">railway lines</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pakwach" title="Pakwach">Pakwach</a>, via <a href="/wiki/Soroti" title="Soroti">Soroti</a>, and to <a href="/wiki/Kasese" title="Kasese">Kasese</a>, via Kampala. Since 1993, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metre_gauge" title="Metre gauge">metre gauge</a> line from Tororo Junction to <a href="/wiki/Gulu" title="Gulu">Gulu</a> and Pakwach has been out of use.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rift_Valley_Railways_Consortium" title="Rift Valley Railways Consortium">Rift Valley Railways</a> funded the clearing of the line east to Gulu of vegetation and repairing track and bridges, thus allowing the first commercial train for 20 years to run through on the metre gauge track from the <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenyan</a> port of <a href="/wiki/Mombasa" title="Mombasa">Mombasa</a> through <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eldoret" title="Eldoret">Eldoret</a> to the Kenyan frontier in Tororo and onwards to Gulu on the line to Pakwach on 14 September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_and_weather">Climate and weather</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tororo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate and weather">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tororo has a tropical monsoon climate with a short dry season<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The average annual temperature is 22.4 °C (72.3 °F) (average high 28.7 °C (83.7 °F) and average low 16.2 °C (61.2 °F)). Average annual <a href="/wiki/Precipitation" title="Precipitation">precipitation</a> is 1,494 millimetres (58.8 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Tororo has many <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a> and may be the place where thunder occurs on more days than any other place on Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Points_of_interest">Points of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tororo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Points of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following additional points of interest lie within the town limits or near its edges:</p>
<ul>
<li>offices of Tororo Town Council</li>
<li>Tororo central market</li>
<li>Tororo Hospital,a 200-bed public hospital administered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uganda_Ministry_of_Health" title="Uganda Ministry of Health">Uganda Ministry of Health</a></li>
<li>Uganda Martyrs' Cathedral, the seat of the archbishop of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Tororo" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Tororo">Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Tororo</a></li>
<li>St. Anthony's Hospital, a 150-bed mission hospital affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Tororo" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Tororo">Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Tororo</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tororo_Priory" title="Tororo Priory">Christ the King Priory</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Benedict" title="Order of Saint Benedict">Benedictine</a> <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottilien_Congregation" title="Ottilien Congregation">Congregation of Missionary Benedictines of Saint Ottilien</a></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Tororo_Airport" title="Tororo Airport">Tororo Airport</a>, a public airport administered by the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_of_Uganda" title="Civil Aviation Authority of Uganda">Civil Aviation Authority of Uganda</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tororo_Solar_Power_Station" title="Tororo Solar Power Station">Tororo Solar Power Station</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Busitema_University" title="Busitema University">Busitema University</a>, one of the six public universities in Uganda, located 28 kilometres (17 mi), by road, southwest of the central business district of Tororo town.</li>
<li>mobile branch of <a href="/wiki/PostBank_Uganda" title="PostBank Uganda">PostBank Uganda</a></li>
<li>branch of the <a href="/wiki/National_Social_Security_Fund_(Uganda)" title="National Social Security Fund (Uganda)">National Social Security Fund</a></li>
