<p><b>Hurricane Bertha</b> was the longest-lived Atlantic <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> on record during July, and it also attained tropical storm intensity farther east than any other system on record during the month. The second named storm, first hurricane, and first major hurricane of the active <a href="/wiki/2008_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2008 Atlantic hurricane season">2008 season</a>, Bertha developed into a tropical depression on July 3 from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> that departed western Africa two days prior. On a west-northwest to northwest track, the depression intensified into Tropical Storm Bertha six hours formation and ultimately attained hurricane intensity on July 7. A period of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a> brought the cyclone to its peak as a <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Category 3 hurricane</a> with winds of 125 mph (205 km/h) late that afternoon. Changes in <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> and the storm's internal structure allowed Bertha to fluctuate in intensity over the next week as it passed very near Bermuda, but a track over cooler waters eventually caused the system to become an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> by July 20. It continued northeast and was absorbed by another low near <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> the following day. Rough surf propelled by Bertha killed three people along the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>; hundreds of other swimmers were injured and/or required rescuing. In Bermuda, some streets were flooded and strong gusts cut power to approximately 7,500 homes; only minor damage was observed.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bertha_(2008)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bertha_2008_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="1024" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Bertha_2008_track.png/220px-Bertha_2008_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Bertha_2008_track.png/330px-Bertha_2008_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Bertha_2008_track.png/440px-Bertha_2008_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bertha_2008_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On July 1, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) began monitoring a vigorous <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a>, accompanied by both a weak <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">surface low</a> and a large area of shower and thunderstorm activity, just off the western coast of Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The disturbance began to show signs of organization early on July 2;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> satellite imagery the next morning showcased deep <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">convection</a> over the apparent center and increasingly pronounced <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">banding features</a> in the northwestern quadrant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Disc1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Disc1-3">[3]</a></sup> Thus, in conjunction with satellite wind data, the storm was classified as a tropical depression by 06:00 UTC on July 3, located about 255 miles (410 km) south-southeast of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabo_Verde" title="Cabo Verde">Cabo Verde</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup> The NHC noted that <a href="/wiki/Numerical_weather_prediction" title="Numerical weather prediction">global modelling</a> suggested the possibility of tropical development over a week prior, a "remarkable achievement" at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Disc1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Disc1-3">[3]</a></sup> The storm's cloud pattern continued to coalesce, and it was upgraded to Tropical Storm Bertha six hours later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the southern periphery of a mid-level <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> over the open Atlantic, Bertha moved swiftly west-northwest to northwest for several days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Disc1_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Disc1-3">[3]</a></sup> Despite being embedded within an environment of low vertical wind shear, the storm passed over ocean temperatures near 77°F (25°C), and its overall appearance was characterized by intermittent bursts of deep convection near the center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Eventually, by late on July 5, Bertha began to enter warmer waters and gain organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Convection coalesced throughout July 6, and a series of microwave passes later that day indicated the formation of an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>-like feature, despite some tilt to Bertha's low- and mid-level circulations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Rapid deepening ensued as the aforementioned eye became readily apparent and convection in the eyewall cooled;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Bertha became the season's first hurricane around 06:00 UTC on July 7, and by 21:00 UTC that afternoon, attained its peak as a <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Category 3 major hurricane</a> with winds of 125 mph (205 km/h) and a minimum barometric pressure of 952 mbar (hPa; 28.12 inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The cyclone's rapid intensification phase was equaled by a rapid weakening phase almost immediately after peak intensity,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and Bertha fell to minimal hurricane intensity by 06:00 UTC on July 9 under the influence of strong wind shear. These unfavorable winds quickly slackened, allowing Bertha to regain Category 2 intensity by 18:00 UTC that day, before the hurricane began an <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">eyewall replacement cycle</a>. A weak steering regime directed the storm just east of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> as it weakened to a tropical storm on July 13, and a large <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">upper-level low</a> over the central Atlantic steered it east and southeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup> After generally maintaining its strength for several days, an eye redeveloped and convection became more symmetric about the feature on July 18,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> prompting the NHC to upgrade Bertha to a hurricane around 18:00 UTC that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup> The storm rapidly accelerated ahead of a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> off the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>, forcing Bertha into rapidly cooler waters. Its wind field became extremely asymmetric and deep convection near the center dissipated,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> indicative of an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone#Extratropical_transition" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical transition</a> that Bertha completed by 12:00 UTC on July 20. The post-tropical cyclone continued northeast and merged with a larger mid-latitude low near Iceland the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span id="Preparations,_impact,_and_records"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations.2C_impact.2C_and_records">Preparations, impact, and records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bertha_(2008)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations, impact, and records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="7200" data-file-width="5600" height="283" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg/220px-Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg/330px-Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg/440px-Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bertha_14_july_2008_1505Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tropical Storm Bertha near <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on July 14</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>As a tropical storm, Bertha dropped rainfall over the southern Cape Verde islands. No damages or deaths were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 7, Bermuda residents began purchasing lamps, tarpaulins and flashlights in anticipation of Hurricane Bertha. In the sudden rush, some stores sold out of batteries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The Emergency Measures Organisation urged the public to monitor forecasts and remain away from beaches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Weather_Service" title="Bermuda Weather Service">Bermuda Weather Service</a>, meanwhile, hoisted <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical cyclone warnings and watches</a> for the island upon its approach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup> On July 10, the Department of Parks placed high surf warning signs along the South Shore beaches as Bertha sent strong waves ahead of its path. Barricades were erected at all of the island's beaches, which were closed to swimming and watersports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dale14_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dale14-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>All flights into and out of Bermuda were disrupted on July 14 as the storm made its final approach to the island. <a href="/wiki/JetBlue" title="JetBlue">JetBlue</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> canceled their flights while <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a> executed its flights to Miami and New York a day early, escaping before the tropical storm arrived. <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a> delayed its flight to the afternoon, hoping that the storm would have passed by the time its plane arrives. Ferry service and traffic to <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s,_Bermuda" title="St. George's, Bermuda">St. George's</a> was canceled for the whole day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dale14_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dale14-15">[15]</a></sup> Some roads were flooded, and tree branches were broken by wind gusts that reached 91 mph (146 km/h) at an elevated weather station on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Downed power lines cut electricity to approximately 7,500 homes, but engineers from the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Electric_Light_Company_Limited" title="Bermuda Electric Light Company Limited">Bermuda Electric Light Company Limited</a> worked quickly to restore service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> A total of 4.77 in (121 mm) of rainfall fell at the <a href="/wiki/L.F._Wade_International_Airport" title="L.F. Wade International Airport">L.F. Wade International Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hurricane produced strong waves and rip currents along the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>, which caused three deaths along the coastline of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>. On July 12, a 51-year-old man died after suddenly losing consciousness during his rescue. On July 13, three men swam out to a buoy about 300 ft (91.4 m) off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Wildwood,_New_Jersey" title="Wildwood, New Jersey">Wildwood Beach</a> when they were overcome by the rough surf. One swimmer was found unconscious in the water and pronounced dead at the scene, the second was never found and presumed dead, and the third was rescued. During the event, a total of 57 people had to be rescued along the coast of New Jersey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Three rip currents also caused 55 injuries throughout beaches in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>. The injuries ranged from minor scrapes to broken bones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Four people were also injured in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, one of which nearly drowned as he had aspirated water shortly before a lifeguard rescued him. At least 60 people had to be rescued from the rough seas over a two-day span.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lasting 17 days, Hurricane Bertha holds the records for the longest-lived July Atlantic tropical cyclone on record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Btcr_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-Btcr-4">[4]</a></sup> It also set the record for the easternmost-forming tropical storm during the month, at 24.7°W.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
