<p>The <b>Central Mexican Plateau</b>, also known as the <b>Mexican Altiplano</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Altiplanicie Mexicana</i>), is a large arid-to-semiarid <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> that occupies much of northern and central <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. Averaging 1,825 m (5,988 ft) above sea level, it extends from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US-Mexico_border" title="US-Mexico border">United States border</a> in the north to the <a href="/wiki/Trans-Mexican_Volcanic_Belt" title="Trans-Mexican Volcanic Belt">Trans-Mexican Volcanic Belt</a> in the south, and is bounded by the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Sierra Madre Occidental</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental" title="Sierra Madre Oriental">Sierra Madre Oriental</a> to the west and east, respectively.</p>
<p>A low east-west mountain range in the state of <a href="/wiki/Zacatecas" title="Zacatecas">Zacatecas</a> divides the plateau into northern and southern sections. These two sections, called the Northern Plateau (Spanish: <i>Mesa del Norte</i>) and Central Plateau (Spanish: <i>Mesa Central</i>), are now generally regarded by geographers as sections of one plateau.</p>
<p>The Mexican Plateau is mostly covered by <a href="/wiki/Deserts_and_xeric_shrublands" title="Deserts and xeric shrublands">deserts and xeric shrublands</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Madrean_pine-oak_woodlands" title="Madrean pine-oak woodlands">pine-oak forests</a> covering the surrounding mountain ranges and forming <a href="/wiki/Sky_island" title="Sky island">sky islands</a> on some of the interior ranges. The Mexican Altiplano is one of six distinct physiographic sections of the <a href="/wiki/Basin_and_Range_Province" title="Basin and Range Province">Basin and Range Province</a>, which in turn is part of the <a href="/wiki/Intermontane_Plateaus" title="Intermontane Plateaus">Intermontane Plateaus</a> physiographic division.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Phytogeography" title="Phytogeography">phytogeography</a>, the Sonoran Desert is within the Sonoran <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Floristic_province" title="Floristic province">Floristic Province</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Madrean_Region" title="Madrean Region">Madrean Region</a> in southwestern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Holarctic" title="Holarctic">Holarctic Kingdom</a> of the northern <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>.</p>
<p>While the plateau stretches from north to south, the southern east-west arc of the Central Mexican Plateau from Jalisco to Veracruz states historically as well as today has served as the population nexus of the Mexican nation, it is home to its biggest metro areas of Guadalajara, Leon, Querétaro, Morelia, Mexico City, Toluca, Cuernavaca, and Puebla.</p>

<p>The Mesa del Norte or northern plateau averages 1,100 metres (3,600 ft) in elevation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">above mean sea level</a> and extends south from the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande" title="Rio Grande">Rio Grande</a> (Río Bravo del Norte) through the states of <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">Chihuahua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">Coahuila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Durango" title="Durango">Durango</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zacatecas" title="Zacatecas">Zacatecas</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Luis_Potos%C3%AD" title="San Luis Potosí">San Luis Potosí</a>. Various narrow, isolated ridges cross the Mesa del Norte and numerous depressions also dot the region, the largest of which is the <a href="/wiki/Bols%C3%B3n_de_Mapim%C3%AD" title="Bolsón de Mapimí">Bolsón de Mapimí</a>. The Río Bravo del Norte and its tributary, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Conchos" title="Río Conchos">Río Conchos</a>, drain portions of the northern plateau, and the <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1nuco_River" title="Pánuco River">Río Pánuco</a> and its tributaries drain the southeastern corner. Both drain to the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. Much of the northern plateau comprises internal drainage basins that do not drain to the sea. The <a href="/wiki/Chihuahuan_Desert" title="Chihuahuan Desert">Chihuahuan Desert</a> extends across the northern portion of the northern plateau, while the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Meseta_Central_matorral&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Meseta Central matorral (page does not exist)">Meseta Central matorral</a> covers the central portion, and the <a href="/wiki/Central_Mexican_matorral" title="Central Mexican matorral">Central Mexican matorral</a> extends from the southern portion of the northern plateau across the southern plateau.</p>
<p>The Mesa Central or southern plateau is higher than its northern counterpart, averaging 2,000 m (6,600 ft) in elevation. The southern plateau contains numerous valleys originally formed by ancient lakes. It extends across the states of <a href="/wiki/Aguascalientes" title="Aguascalientes">Aguascalientes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zacatecas" title="Zacatecas">Zacatecas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guanajuato" title="Guanajuato">Guanajuato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quer%C3%A9taro" title="Querétaro">Querétaro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoacán">Michoacán</a>, <a href="/wiki/State_of_Mexico" title="State of Mexico">México</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(state)" title="Hidalgo (state)">Hidalgo</a> plus the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_District_(Mexico)" title="Federal District (Mexico)">Federal District</a>. Several of Mexico's most prominent cities, including <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guadalajara,_Jalisco" title="Guadalajara, Jalisco">Guadalajara</a>, are located in the valleys of the southern plateau. Much of the southern plateau is drained by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Santiago" title="Río Grande de Santiago">Río Grande de Santiago</a> and its tributaries, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Lerma" title="Río Lerma">Río Lerma</a>, which drain west into the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. Tributaries of the Río Pánuco drain the eastern portion of the southern plateau. The Central Mexican matorral covers much of the southern plateau, with the subtropical <a href="/wiki/Baj%C3%ADo_dry_forests" title="Bajío dry forests">Bajío dry forests</a> occupying the lower portions of the Lerma–Río Grande de Santiago basin. Higher altitudes are covered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mixed_Forest" title="Mixed Forest">Mixed Forests</a>, then Temperate <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Coniferous_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Coniferous Forest (page does not exist)">Coniferous Forests</a>, up to the snow line in the top of the volcanoes that surround the southern and western edges.</p>
