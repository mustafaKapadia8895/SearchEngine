<p>The <b>Rocas Atoll</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Atol das Rocas</i></span>) is the only <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atoll</a> in the South <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amado-Filho_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Amado-Filho-1">[1]</a></sup> belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">State</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Norte" title="Rio Grande do Norte">Rio Grande do Norte</a>. It is located approximately 260 km (160 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Natal,_Rio_Grande_do_Norte" title="Natal, Rio Grande do Norte">Natal</a> and 145 km (90 mi) west of the <a href="/wiki/Fernando_de_Noronha" title="Fernando de Noronha">Fernando de Noronha</a> <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>. The atoll is of volcanic origin and coralline formation.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rocas_Atoll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>The oval atoll is 3.7 kilometres (2.3 mi) long and 2.5 kilometres (1.6 mi) wide. The <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoon</a> is up to 6 metres (20 ft) deep and has an area of 7.1 square kilometres (2.7 sq mi). The land area of the two islets (Cemitério Island, southwest and Farol Cay, northwest) is 0.36 square kilometres (89 acres). Farol Cay accounts for almost two thirds of the aggregate area. The highest point is a sand <a href="/wiki/Dune" title="Dune">dune</a> in the south of larger Farol Cay, with a height of 6 metres (20 ft). Both islets are overgrown with grasses, bushes and a few <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palm_trees" title="Palm trees">palm trees</a>. The population consists of crabs, spiders, scorpions, sand fleas, beetles, large roaches, and many species of birds.</p>
<p>There is a lighthouse of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Navy" title="Brazilian Navy">Brazilian Navy</a> that has been in operation and maintained since the 1960s, at the Northern end of Farol Bay. In its vicinity is a derelict lighthouse from 1933.</p>
<p>The atoll is a wildlife sanctuary, and in 2001 was designated by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> as a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>. Numerous <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shark" title="Shark">sharks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolphin" title="Dolphin">dolphins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> live in the area. The atoll consists mainly of <a href="/wiki/Coral" title="Coral">coral</a> and red <a href="/wiki/Algae" title="Algae">algae</a>. The coral ring is almost closed, with a 200 metres (660 ft) wide channel on the North side and a much narrower channel on the West side.</p>
<p>The atoll and surrounding waters are contained in the <a href="/wiki/Atol_das_Rocas_Biological_Reserve" title="Atol das Rocas Biological Reserve">Atol das Rocas Biological Reserve</a>. The reserve it is currently used solely for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific" title="Scientific">scientific</a> research. Due to their remote location, the islands remain largely undisturbed by human activities. On the other hand, the remoteness also limits researchers' access to the islands and few studies have been developed on this <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atoll</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entomological" title="Entomological">entomological</a> <a href="/wiki/Fauna" title="Fauna">fauna</a> from Atol das Rocas was already recorded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
