<p><b>Georges Bank</b> (formerly known as <b>St. Georges Bank</b>) is a large elevated area of the sea floor between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Cod,_Massachusetts" title="Cape Cod, Massachusetts">Cape Cod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> (United States), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Sable_Island,_Nova_Scotia" title="Cape Sable Island, Nova Scotia">Cape Sable Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> (Canada). It separates the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.</p>
<p>The origin of its name is obscure. The 1610 Velasco map, prepared for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_I_of_England" title="James I of England">King James I of England</a>, used the name "S. Georges Banck", a common practice when the name of the English patron saint, <a href="/wiki/Saint_George" title="Saint George">St. George</a>, was sprinkled around the English-colonized world. By the 1850s, it was known simply as Georges Bank.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_environment">Physical environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georges_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Physical environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Georges Bank is the most westward of the great Atlantic fishing banks, the now-submerged portions of the North American mainland which now comprise the continental shelf running from the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Banks_of_Newfoundland" title="Grand Banks of Newfoundland">Grand Banks of Newfoundland</a> to Georges. Georges Bank was part of the North American mainland as recently as 12,000 years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Roughly oval in shape, Georges Bank measures about 149 miles (240 kilometres) in length by 75 miles (120 kilometres) in width, making it larger than <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. Located 62 miles (100 kilometres) offshore, Georges Bank is part of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelf</a>. It is submerged to a depth of several metres to several dozen metres; almost the entire bank is at least 330 feet (100 m) shallower than the Gulf of Maine to the north.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Gulf of Maine shelf waters are the Bank's primary source. They enter the northern flank, move clockwise around the eastern end, and then westward along the southern flank, mostly emptying into the Mid-Atlantic Bight (the continental shelf ocean between <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">Cape Hatteras</a> and Georges Bank).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_fishing">Commercial fishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georges_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Commercial fishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Georges Bank, while not having the most productive <a href="/wiki/Fishery" title="Fishery">fishery</a> in the world (the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Banks_of_Newfoundland" title="Grand Banks of Newfoundland">Grand Banks</a> takes this claim<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>), has great prominence in that it is probably the most geographically accessible of all the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fishing_bank" title="Fishing bank">fishing banks</a> in the North Atlantic. Lying adjacent to New England's famous seaports, Georges Bank is singlehandedly responsible for the development of coastal fisheries in towns such as <a href="/wiki/Gloucester,_Massachusetts" title="Gloucester, Massachusetts">Gloucester</a>, Massachusetts, and <a href="/wiki/Yarmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Yarmouth, Nova Scotia">Yarmouth</a>, Nova Scotia.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>For over 400 years, Georges Bank supported lucrative fisheries for <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_cod" title="Atlantic cod">Atlantic cod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halibut" title="Halibut">halibut</a>. Over time, <a href="/wiki/Bottom_trawling" title="Bottom trawling">bottom trawlers</a> became very efficient, some catching as much cod in an hour as traditional boats caught in a season. Bottom trawlers, however, damaged the sea floor <a href="/wiki/Coral" title="Coral">coral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sponge" title="Sponge">sponge</a> habitats, and federal fisheries regulations aim to control this large scale <a href="/wiki/Overfishing" title="Overfishing">overfishing</a> to establish future sustainability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1976 to 1982, oil companies drilled ten exploratory wells in the U.S. part of the Georges Bank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> None were successful, however, and both <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> have since imposed moratoriums on oil exploration and production on the Georges Bank, to ensure fisheries conservation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The decision by Canada and the United States to declare an <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone" title="Exclusive economic zone">exclusive economic zone</a> (EEZ) of 200 nautical miles (370 km) in the late 1970s led to overlapping EEZ claims on Georges Bank, and resulted in quickly deteriorating relations between fishers from both countries, who respectively asserted claim to the fishery resources. Both nations agreed in 1979 to refer the question of maritime <a href="/wiki/Boundary_delimitation" title="Boundary delimitation">boundary delimitation</a> to the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>. Following five years of hearings and consultation, the ICJ delivered its decision in 1984, which split the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_boundary" title="Maritime boundary">maritime boundary</a> in the Gulf of Maine between both nations out to the 200 nautical miles limit, giving the bulk of Georges Bank to the United States. Canada's portion of the Gulf of Maine now includes the easternmost portion of Georges Bank.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On September 15, 2016, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> proclaimed the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Canyons_and_Seamounts_Marine_National_Monument" title="Northeast Canyons and Seamounts Marine National Monument">Northeast Canyons and Seamounts Marine National Monument</a> to protect the area's marine biodiversity. This protected a portion of Georges Bank from fishing and mining. Despite the protection, commercial fishing for <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Red_crab" title="Red crab">red crabs</a><sup class="plainlinks noprint Inline-Template" style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="external text" href="http://dispenser.info.tm/~dispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py?page=Georges_Bank&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn" rel="nofollow"><span title="Link needs disambiguation. (March 2017)">disambiguation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and <a href="/wiki/American_lobster" title="American lobster">American lobsters</a> is allowed to continue within the monument for a grace period of seven years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
