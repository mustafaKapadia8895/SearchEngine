<p>The <b>Witch flounder</b> or <b>Torbay sole</b> <i>(Glyptocephalus cynoglossus)</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a right-eyed <a href="/wiki/Flatfish" title="Flatfish">flatfish</a> found in the North Atlantic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The species lives on soft bottoms between 45 and 1460 m and prefers temperatures of 2–6 °C. It eats mostly <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a>, worms and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brittlestar" title="Brittlestar">brittlestars</a>. It spawns from May to September. It grows to maturity in 3 or 4 years, and may live up to 14 years.</p>
<p>The name <i>Torbay sole</i> appears to be a mainly culinary term,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> following the habit of renaming certain fish to broaden their appeal.</p>
<p>It is also called grey flounder, witch flounder as well as other local names.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lefteye_flounder" title="Lefteye flounder">lefteye flounder</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arnoglossus_scapha" title="Arnoglossus scapha">Arnoglossus scapha</a></i> is also known as witch.</p>
