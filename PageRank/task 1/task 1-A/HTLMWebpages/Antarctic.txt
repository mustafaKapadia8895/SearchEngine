The Antarctic comprises in the strict sense the continent of <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> and the island territories located on the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Plate" title="Antarctic Plate">Antarctic Plate</a>. In a broader sense the Antarctic region include the <a href="/wiki/Ice_shelf" title="Ice shelf">ice shelves</a>, waters, and <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> territories in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">Southern Ocean</a> situated south of the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Convergence" title="Antarctic Convergence">Antarctic Convergence</a>, a zone approximately 32 to 48 km (20 to 30 mi) wide varying in latitude seasonally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup> The region covers some 20% of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a>, of which 5.5% (14 million km<sup>2</sup>) is the surface area of the Antarctic continent itself. All of the land and <a href="/wiki/Ice_shelf" title="Ice shelf">ice shelves</a> south of <a href="/wiki/60th_parallel_south" title="60th parallel south">60°S latitude</a> are administered under the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Treaty_System" title="Antarctic Treaty System">Antarctic Treaty System</a>. In a <a href="/wiki/Biogeography" title="Biogeography">biogeographic</a> sense, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_ecozone" title="Antarctic ecozone">Antarctic ecozone</a> is one of eight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecozone" title="Ecozone">ecozones</a> of the Earth's land surface.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The maritime part of the region constitutes the area of application of the international <a href="/wiki/Convention_for_the_Conservation_of_Antarctic_Marine_Living_Resources" title="Convention for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources">Convention for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources</a> (CCAMLR), where for technical reasons the Convention uses an approximation of the Convergence line by means of a line joining specified points along <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">parallels of latitude</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meridian_(geography)" title="Meridian (geography)">meridians of longitude</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> The implementation of the Convention is managed through an international Commission headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Hobart" title="Hobart">Hobart</a>, Australia, by an efficient system of annual <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a> quotas, licenses and international inspectors on the fishing vessels, as well as <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellite</a> surveillance.</p>
<p>Most of the Antarctic region is situated south of 60°S latitude parallel, and is governed in accordance with the international legal regime of the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Treaty_System" title="Antarctic Treaty System">Antarctic Treaty System</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup> The Treaty area covers the continent itself and its immediately adjacent islands, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelagos</a> of the <a href="/wiki/South_Orkney_Islands" title="South Orkney Islands">South Orkney Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands" title="South Shetland Islands">South Shetland Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_I_Island" title="Peter I Island">Peter I Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Island" title="Scott Island">Scott Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Balleny_Islands" title="Balleny Islands">Balleny Islands</a>.</p>
<p>The islands situated between 60°S latitude parallel to the south and the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Convergence" title="Antarctic Convergence">Antarctic Convergence</a> to the north, and their respective 200-nautical-mile (370 km) <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone" title="Exclusive economic zone">exclusive economic zones</a> fall under the national jurisdiction of the countries that possess them: <a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_and_the_South_Sandwich_Islands" title="South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands">South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands</a> (United Kingdom; also an <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overseas_Countries_and_Territories#Overseas_countries_and_territories" title="Overseas Countries and Territories">Overseas territory</a>), <a href="/wiki/Bouvet_Island" title="Bouvet Island">Bouvet Island</a> (Norway), and <a href="/wiki/Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands" title="Heard Island and McDonald Islands">Heard and McDonald Islands</a> (Australia).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kerguelen_Islands" title="Kerguelen Islands">Kerguelen Islands</a> (France; also an EU Overseas territory) are situated in the Antarctic Convergence area, while the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands" title="Falkland Islands">Falkland Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_los_Estados" title="Isla de los Estados">Isla de los Estados</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hornos_Island" title="Hornos Island">Hornos Island</a> with <a href="/wiki/Cape_Horn" title="Cape Horn">Cape Horn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diego_Ram%C3%ADrez_Islands" title="Diego Ramírez Islands">Diego Ramírez Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Campbell_Island,_New_Zealand" title="Campbell Island, New Zealand">Campbell Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Island" title="Macquarie Island">Macquarie Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_Amsterdam" title="Île Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_Saint-Paul" title="Île Saint-Paul">Saint Paul</a> Islands, <a href="/wiki/Crozet_Islands" title="Crozet Islands">Crozet Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Islands" title="Prince Edward Islands">Prince Edward Islands</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gough_Island" title="Gough Island">Gough Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tristan_da_Cunha" title="Tristan da Cunha">Tristan da Cunha group</a> remain north of the Convergence and thus outside the Antarctic region.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife">Wildlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wildlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A variety of animals live in Antarctica for at least some of the year, including:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">Seals</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Penguin" title="Penguin">Penguin</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_pipit" title="South Georgia pipit">South Georgia pipits</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Albatross" title="Albatross">Albatrosses</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antarctic_petrel" title="Antarctic petrel">Antarctic petrels</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Whale" title="Whale">Whales</a></li>
<li>Fish, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_icefish" title="Antarctic icefish">Antarctic icefish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_toothfish" title="Antarctic toothfish">Antarctic toothfish</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Squid" title="Squid">Squid</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Colossal_squid" title="Colossal squid">colossal squid</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antarctic_krill" title="Antarctic krill">Antarctic krill</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Most of the Antarctic continent is permanently covered by ice and snow; less than 1% of the land is exposed. There are only two species of flowering plant, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_hair_grass" title="Antarctic hair grass">Antarctic hair grass</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_pearlwort" title="Antarctic pearlwort">Antarctic pearlwort</a>, but a range of <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">mosses</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liverwort" title="Liverwort">liverworts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichens</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macrofungi" title="Macrofungi">macrofungi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: People">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first Antarctic land discovered was the island of <a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_and_the_South_Sandwich_Islands#Geography" title="South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands">South Georgia</a>, visited by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">English</a> merchant <a href="/wiki/Anthony_de_la_Roch%C3%A9" title="Anthony de la Roché">Anthony de la Roché</a> in 1675. Although myths and speculation about a <i><a href="/wiki/Terra_Australis" title="Terra Australis">Terra Australis</a></i> ("Southern Land") date back to antiquity, the first confirmed sighting of the continent of <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> is commonly accepted to have occurred in 1820 by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Russian_explorers" title="List of Russian explorers">Russian expedition</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Gottlieb_von_Bellingshausen" title="Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen">Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Lazarev" title="Mikhail Lazarev">Mikhail Lazarev</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Vostok_(sloop-of-war)" title="Vostok (sloop-of-war)">Vostok</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mirny_(sloop-of-war)" title="Mirny (sloop-of-war)">Mirny</a></i>. The first human born in the Antarctic was <a href="/wiki/Solveig_Gunbj%C3%B8rg_Jacobsen" title="Solveig Gunbjørg Jacobsen">Solveig Gunbjørg Jacobsen</a> born on 8 October 1913 in <a href="/wiki/Grytviken" title="Grytviken">Grytviken</a>, South Georgia.</p>
<p>The Antarctic region had no <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indigenous_population" title="Indigenous population">indigenous population</a> when first discovered, and its present inhabitants comprise a few thousand transient <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">scientific</a> and other personnel working on tours of duty at the several dozen <a href="/wiki/Research_stations_in_Antarctica" title="Research stations in Antarctica">research stations</a> maintained by various countries. However, the region is visited by more than 40,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> tourists annually, the most popular destinations being the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Peninsula" title="Antarctic Peninsula">Antarctic Peninsula</a> area (especially the <a href="/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands" title="South Shetland Islands">South Shetland Islands</a>) and <a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_and_the_South_Sandwich_Islands#Geography" title="South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands">South Georgia Island</a>.</p>
<p>In December 2009, the growth of <a href="/wiki/Tourism_in_Antarctica" title="Tourism in Antarctica">tourism</a>, with consequences for both the ecology and the safety of the travellers in its great and remote wilderness, was noted at a conference in New Zealand by experts from signatories to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_Treaty" title="Antarctic Treaty">Antarctic Treaty</a>. The definitive results of the conference was presented at the Antarctic Treaty states' meeting in Uruguay in May 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Antarctic hosts the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Protected_area" title="Protected area">protected area</a> comprising 1.07 million km<sup>2</sup>, the South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands Marine Protection Area created in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> The latter exceeds the surface area of another vast protected territory, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenland_National_Park" title="Greenland National Park">Greenland National Park</a>’s 972,000 km<sup>2</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup> (While the <a href="/wiki/Ross_Sea" title="Ross Sea">Ross Sea</a> Marine Protection Area established in 2016 is still larger at 1.55 million km<sup>2</sup>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup> its protection is set to expire in 35 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_Slezak_2016_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_Slezak_2016-16">[15]</a></sup>)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Time_zones">Time zones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Time zones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Time_in_Antarctica" title="Time in Antarctica">Time in Antarctica</a></div>
<p>Because Antarctica surrounds the South Pole, it is theoretically located in all <a href="/wiki/Time_zone" title="Time zone">time zones</a>. For practical purposes, time zones are usually based on <a href="/wiki/Territorial_claims_in_Antarctica" title="Territorial claims in Antarctica">territorial claims</a> or the time zone of a station's owner country or supply base.</p>
