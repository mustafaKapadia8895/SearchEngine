<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Absolute_angular_momentum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Absolute_angular_momentum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Absolute angular momentum sums the angular momentum of a particle or fluid parcel in a relative coordinate system and the angular momentum of that relative coordinate system.</p>
<p>Meteorologists typically express the three vector components of velocity <span class="texhtml"><b>v</b> = (<var>u</var>, <var>v</var>, <var>w</var>)</span> (eastward, northward, and upward). <p>where</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="texhtml"><var>M</var></span> represents absolute angular momentum per unit mass of the fluid parcel (in <span class="texhtml"><span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">m<sup>2</sup></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span>),</li>
<li><span class="texhtml"><var>r</var></span> represents distance from the center of the earth to the fluid parcel (in <span class="texhtml">m</span>),</li>
<li><span class="texhtml"><var>u</var></span> represents earth-relative eastward component of velocity of the fluid parcel (in <span class="texhtml"><span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">m</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span>),</li>
<li><span class="texhtml"><var>φ</var></span> represents <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a> (in <span class="texhtml">rad</span>), and</li>
<li><span class="texhtml"><var>Ω</var></span> represents angular rate of <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation" title="Earth's rotation">Earth's rotation</a> (in <span class="texhtml"><span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">rad</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span> , usually <span class="texhtml"><span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">2 π <a href="/wiki/Radian" title="Radian">rad</a></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sidereal_day" title="Sidereal day">sidereal day</a></span></span> ≈ 72.921150 × 10<sup>−6</sup> <span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">rad</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span> ).</li>
</ul>
<p>The first term represents the angular momentum of the parcel with respect to the surface of the earth, which depends strongly on weather. The second term represents the angular momentum of the earth itself at a particular latitude (essentially constant at least on non-geological timescales).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications">Applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Absolute_angular_momentum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><span class="texhtml"><var>M</var></span> represents absolute angular momentum per unit mass of the fluid parcel (in <span class="texhtml"><span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">m<sup>2</sup></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span>),</li>

<li><span class="texhtml"><var>φ</var></span> represents <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a> (in <span class="texhtml">rad</span>), and</li>
<li><span class="texhtml"><var>Ω</var></span> represents angular rate of <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation" title="Earth's rotation">Earth's rotation</a> (in <span class="texhtml"><span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">rad</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span> , usually <span class="texhtml"><span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">2 π <a href="/wiki/Radian" title="Radian">rad</a></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sidereal_day" title="Sidereal day">sidereal day</a></span></span> ≈ 72.921150 × 10<sup>−6</sup> <span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">rad</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span> ).</li>
</ul>
<p>At the North Pole and South Pole (latitude <span class="texhtml"><var>φ</var>=±90°=<span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">π</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">2</span></span>rad</span>), no absolute angular momentum can exist (<span class="texhtml"><var>M</var>=0 <span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">m<sup>2</sup></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span> because <span class="texhtml">cos(±90°)=0</span>). If a fluid parcel with no eastward wind speed (<span class="texhtml"><var>u<sub>0</sub></var>=0<span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">m</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span></span>) originating at the equator (<span class="texhtml"><var>φ</var>=0 rad</span> so <span class="texhtml">cos(<var>φ</var>)= cos(0 rad) = 1</span>) conserves its angular momentum (<span class="texhtml"><var>M</var><sub>0</sub> =<var>M</var></span>) as it moves poleward, then its eastward wind speed increases dramatically: <span class="texhtml"><var>u<sub>0</sub></var> <var>a</var> cos(<var>φ<sub>0</sub></var>) + <var>Ω</var> <var>a</var><sup>2</sup> cos<sup>2</sup>(<var>φ<sub>0</sub></var>) = <var>u</var> <var>a</var> cos(<var>φ</var>) + <var>Ω</var> <var>a</var><sup>2</sup> cos<sup>2</sup>(<var>φ</var>)</span>. After those substitutions, <span class="texhtml"><var>Ω</var> <var>a</var><sup>2</sup> = <var>u</var> <var>a</var> cos(<var>φ</var>) + <var>Ω</var> <var>a</var><sup>2</sup> cos<sup>2</sup>(<var>φ</var>)</span>, or after further simplification, <span class="texhtml"><var>Ω</var> <var>a</var>(1-cos<sup>2</sup>(<var>φ</var>)) = <var>u</var> cos(<var>φ</var>)</span>. Solution for <span class="texhtml"><var>u</var></span> gives <span class="texhtml"><var>Ω</var> <var>a</var>(<span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">1</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">cos(<var>φ</var>)</span></span> - cos(<var>φ</var>)) = <var>u</var></span>. If <span class="texhtml"><var>φ</var> = 15°</span> (<span class="texhtml">cos(<var>φ</var>)=<span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">1+<span class="nowrap">√<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">3</span></span></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">2<span class="nowrap">√<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">2</span></span></span></span></span>), then <span class="texhtml">72.921150 × 10<sup>−6</sup> <span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">rad</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span> × 6.371009 Mm ×(<span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">2<span class="nowrap">√<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">2</span></span></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">1+<span class="nowrap">√<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">3</span></span></span></span> - <span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">1+<span class="nowrap">√<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">3</span></span></span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">2<span class="nowrap">√<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">2</span></span></span></span>) ≈ 32.2<span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">m</span><span class="visualhide">/</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">s</span></span> ≈ <var>u</var></span>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Zonal_and_meridional" title="Zonal and meridional">zonal</a> <a href="/wiki/Pressure_gradient" title="Pressure gradient">pressure gradient</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddy_(fluid_dynamics)" title="Eddy (fluid dynamics)">eddy</a> <a href="/wiki/Stress_(mechanics)" title="Stress (mechanics)">stresses</a> cause <a href="/wiki/Torque" title="Torque">torque</a> that changes the absolute angular momentum of fluid parcels.</p>
