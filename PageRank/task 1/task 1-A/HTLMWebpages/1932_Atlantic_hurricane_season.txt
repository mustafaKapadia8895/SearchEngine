<p>The <b>1932 Atlantic hurricane season</b> was the period during 1932 in which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a> formed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_Basin" title="Atlantic Basin">Atlantic Basin</a>. It was a relatively active season, with fifteen known storms, six hurricanes, and four <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Atlantic_and_East_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone scales">major hurricanes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[nb 1]</a></sup> Two storms attained <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> intensity, the first known occurrence in which multiple Category 5 hurricanes formed in the same year. The season began with the formation of <a href="/wiki/1932_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_One" title="1932 Atlantic hurricane season">Tropical Storm One</a> on May 5, and ended with the dissipation of <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">Hurricane Fourteen</a>, also known as the 1932 Cuba hurricane, on November 14. Tropical cyclones that did not approach populated areas or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shipping_lane" title="Shipping lane">shipping lanes</a>, especially if they were relatively weak and of short duration, may have remained undetected. Because technologies such as <a href="/wiki/Satellite_imagery" title="Satellite imagery">satellite monitoring</a> were not available until the 1960s, historical data on tropical cyclones from this period are often not reliable. The <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project" title="Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project">Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project</a> discovered four new tropical cyclones, all of which were tropical storms, that occurred during the year. These storms were later added to the <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">HURDAT</a> database.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In total, the season resulted in at least 3,384 fatalities and at least $77.706 million in damages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[nb 2]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/1932_Freeport_hurricane" title="1932 Freeport hurricane">strong hurricane</a> struck <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">Freeport, Texas</a> in mid–August, severely affecting a large swath of the coast. A <a href="/wiki/1932_Bahamas_hurricane" title="1932 Bahamas hurricane">Category 5 hurricane</a>, the first of the season, devastated areas of <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a>, especially the <a href="/wiki/Abaco_Islands" title="Abaco Islands">Abaco Islands</a>. Another strong <a href="/wiki/1932_San_Ciprian_hurricane" title="1932 San Ciprian hurricane">Category 4 hurricane</a> struck <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> in late September, leaving catastrophic damage and causing $30 million in damages. A second <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">Category 5 hurricane</a> in early November became one of the deadliest hurricanes of the 20th century after making <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> and killing at least 3,103 people. <a href="/wiki/1932_Florida%E2%80%93Alabama_hurricane" title="1932 Florida–Alabama hurricane">A hurricane</a> and four other tropical storms made landfalls during the season.</p>

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Atlantic_and_East_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone scales">tropical depression</a> formed on May 5 in the south-central Caribbean. Moving slowly northeast, the system became a tropical storm the next day and gradually strengthened before making landfall on <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> with winds of 50 miles per hour (80 km/h) on May 7. The system crossed the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, emerging into the western Atlantic on May 8 as it continued to strengthen, reaching its peak intensity of 65 mph (105 km/h) on May 10 while centered south-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. The diffuse system later transitioned into an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical_storm" title="Extratropical storm">extratropical storm</a> the same day, later dissipating on May 11 well to the northwest of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> after merging with a <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">frontal boundary</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>The Freeport Hurricane of 1932</b><br/></p>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1932_Freeport_hurricane" title="1932 Freeport hurricane">1932 Freeport hurricane</a></div>
<p>A tropical storm formed on August 11 in the southern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> near the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a>. Moving northward over the southern Gulf of Mexico on the 12th, it <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensified</a> from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale">Category 1</a> to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale">Category 4</a> hurricane with winds estimated at 150 mph (240 km/h) and an estimated central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">pressure</a> of 935 mb (27.6 inHg) shortly before making landfall near <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">Freeport, Texas</a>, on August 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> of the compact storm crossed the coast about 10 p.m. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Daylight_Time" title="Central Daylight Time">CDT</a> on August 13, creating a 40–30 mi (64–48 km) wide path of heavy destruction in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazoria_County" title="Brazoria County">Brazoria County</a>, Texas. The lowest barometric pressure recorded was 27.83 inches (938 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millibars" title="Millibars">mbar</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Velasco,_Texas" title="Velasco, Texas">Velasco</a> in Brazoria County. Official warning on the storm began just four hours prior to the storm's landfall, and many people evacuating inland were forced to abandon their cars in high winds and heavy rains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TexasHurricaneHistory_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-TexasHurricaneHistory-6">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">Galveston, Texas</a>, power to electrical and phone service was cut off, temporarily reducing communications. The passage between <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">Galveston Island</a> and mainland Texas was flooded, temporarily cutting off Galveston from the mainland. Heavy rains occurred, and a foot of rain fell as the storm moved north towards the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TexasHurricaneHistory_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-TexasHurricaneHistory-6">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-7">[5]</a></sup> 40 people were killed by the hurricane. The greatest single death toll for any town was seven in <a href="/wiki/West_Columbia,_Texas" title="West Columbia, Texas">West Columbia</a>, Texas, where sustained winds over 100 mph (160 km/h) flattened several homes. Two neighborhoods that had been constructed for oil industry workers there were nearly destroyed by the high tides and <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>. <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">Freeport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angleton,_Texas" title="Angleton, Texas">Angleton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">Galveston</a> suffered extensive wind damage, and the inland towns of <a href="/wiki/Brazoria,_Texas" title="Brazoria, Texas">Brazoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Columbia,_Texas" title="West Columbia, Texas">West Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Damon,_Texas" title="Damon, Texas">Damon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Needville,_Texas" title="Needville, Texas">Needville</a>, all in the path of the eye, were also devastated. Damages associated with the hurricane were estimated to total to $7.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TexasHurricaneHistory_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-TexasHurricaneHistory-6">[4]</a></sup><br/></p>

<p>A tropical disturbance was first identified north of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> on August 26. Slowly moving towards the west-northwest, it intensified to tropical storm strength before making landfall on <a href="/wiki/South_Florida" title="South Florida">South Florida</a> early on August 30. After crossing the Florida peninsula and entering the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>, the system reached peak intensity as a Category 1 hurricane with winds of 85 mph (137 km/h), before subsequently making its final landfall near the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>–<a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a> border on September 1 at peak intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> Over land, the hurricane weakened, and after becoming an extratropical cyclone on September 2, the storm merged with another extratropical system over <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> on September 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the storm's initial landfall in southern Florida, heavy rains and strong winds caused extensive damage to <a href="/wiki/Crop" title="Crop">crops</a>, particularly to <a href="/wiki/Avocado" title="Avocado">avocado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a>. As the strengthening hurricane moved northwestwards through the Gulf of Mexico, it generated strong surf that caused severe damage to coastal areas across western Florida,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movingforcediminish_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-movingforcediminish-8">[6]</a></sup> before making its second landfall, where it produced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_force" title="Hurricane force">hurricane force</a> winds across a wide swath of the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">Agricultural</a> lands in coastal regions of Mississippi and Alabama were damaged by strong winds and heavy rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> The hurricane caused one indirect death and $226,000 in total damages across its path.</p>

<p><b>The Great Bahamas Hurricane of 1932</b> or <b>Hurricane San Ciprián of 1932</b><br/></p>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1932_Bahamas_hurricane" title="1932 Bahamas hurricane">1932 Bahamas hurricane</a></div>
<p>A tropical depression was first detected north of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a> late on August 30. Moving towards the west-northwest, the depression steadily intensified, reaching tropical storm strength and later hurricane strength on September 2, as it passed near the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> After reaching hurricane intensity, the hurricane began to rapidly intensify,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-9">[7]</a></sup> becoming a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_3_hurricane" title="Category 3 hurricane">major hurricane</a> on September 4. The intensifying hurricane moved through <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a> and reached peak intensity as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Atlantic_Category_5_hurricanes" title="List of Atlantic Category 5 hurricanes">Category 5 hurricane</a>, the first of the season, with maximum sustained winds of 160 mph (260 km/h). Afterwards, the hurricane slowly weakened as it recurved towards the north and subsequently northeast, bypassing <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> before becoming extratropical on September 9. The extratropical cyclone traversed across <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> and later dissipated on September 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hurricane caused extensive devastation across much of The Bahamas, where it killed 16 people and injured an additional 300 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-9">[7]</a></sup> Strong winds destroyed numerous homes and hampered water and food supplies. The large hurricane also generated a strong <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> which inundated the <a href="/wiki/Abaco_Islands" title="Abaco Islands">Abaco Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hurt_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hurt-10">[8]</a></sup> Effects of the hurricane in the United States were much less severe, due to the far proximity of the hurricane. However, strong gales still swept through much of New England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-9">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>On September 4, an area of <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">low pressure</a> developed from a <a href="/wiki/Stationary_front" title="Stationary front">stationary front</a> over the open Atlantic Ocean. <p>A tropical storm first formed in the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a> on September 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> Moving slowly northwards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-11">[9]</a></sup> the tropical storm reached its peak intensity of 60 mph (97 km/h) while located south of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River_Delta" title="Mississippi River Delta">Mississippi River Delta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> Beginning on September 14, the storm began to accelerate as it turned towards the northeast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-11">[9]</a></sup> and subsequently made landfall at peak intensity near <a href="/wiki/St._Marks,_Florida" title="St. Marks, Florida">St. Marks</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Big_Bend_(Florida)" title="Big Bend (Florida)">Big Bend of Florida</a> at 0400 UTC on September 15. The storm crossed the peninsula in only six hours, before becoming extratropical at 12000 UTC later that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> The extratropical cyclone quickly accelerated parallel to the United States East Coast, before being absorbed by another extratropical system over Quebec.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> Due to the tropical storm's quick passage over the Florida peninsula, damage was relatively minor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Train" title="Train">train</a> crossed over a washout on its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railroad" title="Railroad">railroad</a>, causing it to <a href="/wiki/Derail" title="Derail">derail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-galehitsflorida_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-galehitsflorida-12">[10]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Apalachicola,_Florida" title="Apalachicola, Florida">Apalachicola</a>, there was considerable damage to crops, and torrential rains caused $2,000 in damages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> Electric power in the city was also disabled, and some buildings suffered structural damage, totaling to $8,000 in damages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-severesept_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-severesept-13">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>Another tropical depression was first identified on September 18 in the Bay of Campeche. Moving quickly towards the north-northeast, the depression intensified to a tropical storm and reached peak intensity with maximum sustained winds of 65 mph (105 km/h) and a minimum pressure of 998 mbar (29.5 inHg) early the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-10"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> The storm subsequently made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Morgan_City,_Louisiana" title="Morgan City, Louisiana">Morgan City, Louisiana</a> at 1900 UTC on September 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-15"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> Weakening over land, the tropical storm degenerated to a tropical depression at 1200 UTC on September 20, before becoming extratropical the next day west of <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a> and later dissipating after it was absorbed by an approaching front.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-16"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-11"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Hurricane San Ciprián of 1932</b><br/></p>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1932_San_Ciprian_hurricane" title="1932 San Ciprian hurricane">1932 San Ciprian hurricane</a></div>
<p>On September 25, ships first noted a tropical storm east of the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a>. The tropical storm quickly intensified as it moved towards the west due to a nearby high pressure system, reaching hurricane strength at 0600 UTC that day. As it approached <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, the hurricane continued to rapidly intensify, before reaching its peak intensity as a Category 4 hurricane with winds of 145 mph (233 km/h) and a minimum pressure of 943 mbar (27.8 inHg) on September 27, before making landfall near <a href="/wiki/Ceiba,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ceiba, Puerto Rico">Ceiba, Puerto Rico</a> at 0300 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-18"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-11">[9]</a></sup> The hurricane slightly weakened over the island, and after crossing the <a href="/wiki/Mona_Passage" title="Mona Passage">Mona Passage</a>, made a second landfall in southern areas of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> as a Category 2 hurricane. Its passage of Hispaniola significantly weakened the hurricane, downgrading it to tropical strength. After brushing <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> to the south, the tropical storm struggled to organize, and did not strengthen up until landfall on <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">British Honduras</a> with winds of 45 mph (72 km/h) and an estimated minimum pressure of 1,003 mbar (29.6 inHg) October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-12"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> There, the storm quickly weakened, and dissipated over southeastern Mexico on October 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-19"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hurricane was considered the worst to affect Puerto Rico since the <a href="/wiki/1928_Okeechobee_hurricane" title="1928 Okeechobee hurricane">1928 Okeechobee hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worstpr_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-worstpr-15">[13]</a></sup> Strong storm surge and winds devastated areas of the island coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-11">[9]</a></sup> Winds also knocked out communications systems, hampering relief efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35loss_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-35loss-16">[14]</a></sup> Additional rainfall also inundated numerous homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-relief-17">[15]</a></sup> In Puerto Rico, the hurricane killed 257 hurricane, and caused $30 million in damages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pr_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-pr-18">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>A tropical depression first formed on October 7 from a broad low-pressure area in the central Caribbean Sea northeast of <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-22"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-15"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> Moving slowly to the west-northwest,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oct32mwr_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-oct32mwr-19">[17]</a></sup> the depression steadily intensified, reaching tropical storm strength at 1200 UTC the same day. The storm continued to intensify as it moved towards the Yucatán Peninsula, reaching peak intensity late on October 9, with maximum sustained wind speeds of 70 mph (110 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-16"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> The storm made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Playa_del_Carmen" title="Playa del Carmen">Playa del Carmen</a> at 0400 UTC the next day, while still at peak intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-23"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> Over land, the storm degenerated into a tropical depression on October 11 as it began to curve towards the north. The depression later reentered the Gulf of Mexico the following, where it began to restrengthen. By 1200 UTC on October 13, the elongated system regained tropical storm strength, before reaching a secondary peak intensity of 60 mph (97 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-17"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> However, baroclinic forces caused the storm to become extratropical prior to making landfall on the Louisiana coast in the afternoon of October 15. The extratropical remnants of the storm continued to trek to the northeast, before it merged with another extratropical system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-24"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The approaching storm forced <a href="/wiki/Storm_warning" title="Storm warning">storm warnings</a> to be issued for several areas of the Gulf Coast from <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">Brownsville, Texas</a> to Apalachicola, Florida. Its extratropical remnants brought gale–force winds to portions of Louisiana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oct32mwr_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-oct32mwr-19">[17]</a></sup> Offshore, a <a href="/wiki/Cargo_ship" title="Cargo ship">cargo ship</a> carrying <a href="/wiki/Asphalt" title="Asphalt">asphalt</a> sunk due to strong waves, causing $30,000 in losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-severeoct_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-severeoct-20">[18]</a></sup> In addition heavy rains caused rivers to exceed <a href="/wiki/Flood_stage" title="Flood stage">flood stage</a>, particularly east of where the storm made landfall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-7">[5]</a></sup> isolating homes and interrupting traffic. <a href="/wiki/Black_Warrior_River" title="Black Warrior River">Black Warrior River</a> exceeded its flood stage by 14 ft (4.3 m), and the <a href="/wiki/Catawba_River" title="Catawba River">Catawba River</a> also overflowed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3states_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-3states-21">[19]</a></sup> A peak rainfall of 8.5 in (220 mm) was reported during a 17‑hour period in <a href="/wiki/Tuscaloosa,_Alabama" title="Tuscaloosa, Alabama">Tuscaloosa, Alabama</a>. The storm also brought widespread rainfall across the <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_States" title="South Atlantic States">South Atlantic States</a> through <a href="/wiki/Orographic_lift" title="Orographic lift">orographic lift</a>, peaking at 9.3 in (240 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Rock_House_(Roaring_Gap,_North_Carolina)" title="Rock House (Roaring Gap, North Carolina)">Rock House, North Carolina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-7">[5]</a></sup> Washout from the rains also caused a <a href="/wiki/Train_wreck" title="Train wreck">train wreck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3states_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-3states-21">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>A tropical depression was first identified on October 8 northeast of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a> and quickly intensified to a tropical storm. Moving towards the west and then northwest, the storm intensified to its peak intensity of 60 mph (97 km/h) at 1800 UTC on October 10 as it neared <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-18"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> The lowest pressure recorded in Bermuda was 1,002 mbar (29.6 inHg), which was associated with minimal tropical storm force winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-25"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> The storm weakened as it progressed further northwards and curved towards the northeast, before transitioning into an extratropical cyclone by 0600 UTC on October 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-19"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> The system was absorbed by a strong cold frontal boundary later that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-26"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>On October 17, a <a href="/wiki/Stationary_front" title="Stationary front">stationary front</a> positioned over the eastern Atlantic began to dissipate, and a large area of low pressure began to form along the front boundary. The disturbance intensified to tropical storm strength at 0000 UTC on October 18, after losing <a href="/wiki/Baroclinity" title="Baroclinity">baroclinity</a>. As it moved towards the north,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-20"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> the system slowly intensified to an estimated peak intensity of 70 mph (110 km/h) at 0600 UTC on October 19, as suggested by a nearby ship that also reported 70 mph (110 km/h) winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-27"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> At the same time, the tropical storm also began to curve more towards the east, and subsequently began to weaken. The system degenerated to tropical depression strength on October 21, and shortly after dissipated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-21"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>The Great Cuba Hurricane of 1932</b><br/></p>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">1932 Cuba hurricane</a></div>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a> formed on October 30 in the Atlantic Ocean east of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a>. It was upgraded to hurricane strength on November 1 as it headed southwest into the Caribbean. It was upgraded to Category 2 status on November 3 with winds of 100 mph (160 km/h). The storm strengthened into a major hurricane on November 4, and continued to intensify, peaking as an intense <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale">Category 5</a> hurricane with a central pressure at or below 915 mb (27.02 inHg) on November 5 in the western Caribbean. The storm later weakened slightly and struck central Cuba on November 9 with winds of 150 mph (240 km/h) and a central pressure of 918 mb (27.11 inHg), later weakening to a Category 3 hurricane after crossing the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_3-28"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-3">[2]</a></sup> The storm continued to slowly weaken as it moved through the Bahamas the following day, later weakening to a tropical storm and becoming an extratropical storm the next day over the central North Atlantic. The storm caused severe damage and a total of 3,033 deaths in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSU_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-CSU-22">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>A tropical storm was first identified in the Central Atlantic at 0600 UTC on November 3,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-22"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> with ships reporting moderate gales in its vicinity. Throughout its existence, the storm moved in an erratic path, generally to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-novemberstorms_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-novemberstorms-24">[22]</a></sup> Steadily intensifying, the tropical storm attained hurricane strength at 1800 UTC on November 6, as it began to move towards the northeast towards the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>. The hurricane continued to intensify, attaining Category 2 intensity on November 8 and at the same time reaching its peak intensity with winds of 100 mph (160 km/h). After reaching peak intensity, a weakening trend began as the storm accelerated towards the northeast. Early on November 10, it transitioned into an extratropical cyclone, while still maintaining winds of hurricane force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAAHURDAT1_5-23"><a href="#cite_note-NOAAHURDAT1-5">[3]</a></sup> The extratropical system continued to quickly move towards the northeast, until it coalesced with another extratropical cyclone on November 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-novemberstorms_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-novemberstorms-24">[22]</a></sup></p>
