<p><b>Kampala</b> is the capital and largest city of <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>. The city is divided into five boroughs that oversee local planning: <a href="/wiki/Nakasero" title="Nakasero">Kampala Central Division</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kawempe_Division" title="Kawempe Division">Kawempe Division</a>, <a href="/wiki/Makindye_Division" title="Makindye Division">Makindye Division</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nakawa_Division" title="Nakawa Division">Nakawa Division</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rubaga_Division" title="Rubaga Division">Rubaga Division</a>. The city is <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/conterminous" title="wikt:conterminous">conterminous</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kampala_District" title="Kampala District">Kampala District</a>. Surrounding Kampala is the rapidly growing <a href="/wiki/Wakiso_District" title="Wakiso District">Wakiso District</a>, whose population more than doubled between 2002 and 2014 and now stands at over 2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kampala was named the 13th fastest growing city on the planet, with an annual population growth rate of 4.03 percent,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> by City Mayors. Kampala has been ranked the best city to live in East Africa<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kigali" title="Kigali">Kigali</a> by Mercer, a global development consulting agency based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the arrival of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> colonists, the <a href="/wiki/Kabaka_of_Buganda" title="Kabaka of Buganda">Kabaka of Buganda</a> had chosen the zone that would become Kampala as a hunting reserve. The area, composed of rolling hills with grassy wetlands in the valleys, was home to several species of antelope, particularly <a href="/wiki/Impala" title="Impala">impala</a>. When the British arrived, they called it "Hills of the Impala". The language of the <a href="/wiki/Buganda" title="Buganda">Buganda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luganda" title="Luganda">Luganda</a>, adopted many English words because of their interactions with the British. The Buganda translated "Hill of the Impala" as <i>Akasozi ke'Empala</i> – "Kasozi" meaning "hill", "ke" meaning "of", and "empala" the plural of "impala". In Luganda, the words "ka'mpala" mean "that is of the impala", in reference to a hill, and the single word "Kampala" was adopted as the name for the city that grew out of the Kabaka's hills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Kampala" title="Timeline of Kampala">Timeline of Kampala</a></div>
<p>The city grew as the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Buganda" title="Buganda">Buganda</a> kingdom, from which several buildings survive, including the <a href="/wiki/Kasubi_Tombs" title="Kasubi Tombs">Kasubi Tombs</a> (built in 1881), the <a href="/wiki/Lubiri" title="Lubiri">Lubiri</a> Palace, the <a href="/wiki/Bulange" title="Bulange">Buganda Parliament</a> and the Buganda Court of Justice. In 1890, British colonial administrator Frederick Lugard constructed a forum along <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mengo_Hill" title="Mengo Hill">Mengo Hill</a> within the city, which allowed for the British to occupy much of the territory controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Buganda" title="Buganda">Buganda</a>, including Kampala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1894, the British government officially established a protectorate within this territory, and in 1896, the protectorate expanded to cover the <a href="/wiki/Ankole" title="Ankole">Ankole</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toro_Kingdom" title="Toro Kingdom">Toro Kingdom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bunyoro" title="Bunyoro">Bunyoro</a> kingdoms as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1905, the British government formally declared the entire territory to be a British colony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> From that time until the independence of the country in 1962, the capital was relocated to <a href="/wiki/Entebbe" title="Entebbe">Entebbe</a>, although the city continued to be the primary economic and manufacturing location for Uganda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1922, the Makerere Technical Institute, now known as <a href="/wiki/Makerere_University" title="Makerere University">Makerere University</a>, started as the first collegiate institution both within Kampala, and within the British colonies on the east coast of Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-9">[9]</a></sup> Following the 1962 independence, <a href="/wiki/Milton_Obote" title="Milton Obote">Milton Obote</a> became president of Uganda, and held the position until 1971, when former sergeant <a href="/wiki/Idi_Amin" title="Idi Amin">Idi Amin</a> defeated his government in a military coup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> He proceeded to expel all Asian residents living within Kampala, and attacked the Jewish population living within the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1978, he invaded the neighboring country of <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, and in turn, the government there started the <a href="/wiki/Uganda%E2%80%93Tanzania_War" title="Uganda–Tanzania War">Uganda–Tanzania War</a>, which created severe damage to the buildings of Kampala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Since then, the city has since then been rebuilt with constructions of new construction of hotels, banks, shopping malls, educational institutions, and hospitals and the improvement of war torn buildings and infrastructure. Traditionally, Kampala was a city of seven hills, but over time it has come to have a lot more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>The main campus of <a href="/wiki/Makerere_University" title="Makerere University">Makerere University</a> is in the <a href="/wiki/Makerere" title="Makerere">Makerere</a> Hill area of the city.</p>
<p>Kampala also hosts the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/East_African_Development_Bank" title="East African Development Bank">East African Development Bank</a> on <a href="/wiki/Nakasero" title="Nakasero">Nakasero</a> Hill and the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_Local_Governments_Association" title="Uganda Local Governments Association">Uganda Local Governments Association</a> on Entebbe Road.</p>
<p>Kampala was originally <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_claimed_to_be_built_on_seven_hills" title="List of cities claimed to be built on seven hills">built on seven hills</a>, but as its size has increased, it has expanded to more hills than seven. The original seven hills are:</p>
<ol>
<li>The first hill in historical importance is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kasubi" title="Kasubi">Kasubi</a> Hill.</li>
<li>The second is <a href="/wiki/Mengo,_Uganda" title="Mengo, Uganda">Mengo Hill</a>.</li>
<li>The third is <a href="/wiki/Kibuli" title="Kibuli">Kibuli</a> Hill, which is home to the Kibuli <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">Mosque</a>.</li>
<li>The fourth is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Namirembe" title="Namirembe">Namirembe</a> Hill, home to the Namirembe <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">Cathedral</a>.</li>
<li>The fifth is <a href="/wiki/Lubaga" title="Lubaga">Lubaga</a> Hill, the site of the Rubaga Catholic Cathedral.</li>
<li>The sixth is <a href="/wiki/Nsambya" title="Nsambya">Nsambya</a> Hill.</li>
<li>The seventh is <a href="/wiki/Kampala_Hill" title="Kampala Hill">Kampala Hill</a> (Old Kampala). A mosque was built with monetary assistance from Libya on the hill in 2003, with a seating capacity of 15,000 people. The completed mosque was opened officially in June 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
</ol>

<p>The city spread to <a href="/wiki/Nakasero" title="Nakasero">Nakasero</a> Hill, where there are international hotels, including the <a href="/wiki/Kampala_Speke_Hotel" title="Kampala Speke Hotel">Kampala Speke Hotel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Imperial_Hotel" title="Grand Imperial Hotel">Grand Imperial Hotel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kampala_Intercontinental_Hotel" title="Kampala Intercontinental Hotel">Kampala Intercontinental Hotel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Royale_Hotel" title="Imperial Royale Hotel">Imperial Royale Hotel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kampala_Serena_Hotel" title="Kampala Serena Hotel">Kampala Serena Hotel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kampala_Sheraton_Hotel" title="Kampala Sheraton Hotel">Kampala Sheraton Hotel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Pearl_of_Africa_Hotel_Kampala" title="The Pearl of Africa Hotel Kampala">The Pearl of Africa Hotel Kampala</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are also <a href="/wiki/Muyenga" title="Muyenga">Tank Hill</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mulago_Hill" title="Mulago Hill">Mulago Hill</a>. The city is expanding rapidly to include <a href="/wiki/Makindye" title="Makindye">Makindye</a> Hill and Konge Hill.</p>
<p>Other features of the city include the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_Museum" title="Uganda Museum">Uganda Museum</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ugandan_National_Theatre" title="Ugandan National Theatre">Ugandan National Theatre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nakasero_Market" title="Nakasero Market">Nakasero Market</a>, and St. Balikuddembe Market (formerly Owino Market). Kampala is also known for its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nightlife_(activity)" title="Nightlife (activity)">nightlife</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> which includes several casinos, notably Casino Simba in the Garden City shopping centre, Kampala Casino, and Mayfair Casino. <a href="/wiki/Port_Bell" title="Port Bell">Port Bell</a> on the shores of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a> is 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) away.</p>
<p>Kampala hosts a <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_House_of_Worship" title="Bahá'í House of Worship">Bahá'í House of Worship</a> known as the Mother Temple of Africa and is situated on Kikaya Hill on the outskirts of the city. The temple was inaugurated in January 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Ahmadiyya Central Mosque in Kampala is the central mosque of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya_Muslim_Community" title="Ahmadiyya Muslim Community">Ahmadiyya Muslim Community</a>, which has six minarets and can hold up to 9,000 worshippers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-around_the_world_112_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-around_the_world_112-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>While more than 30% of Kampala's citizens practice <a href="/wiki/Urban_agriculture" title="Urban agriculture">urban agriculture</a>, the city of Kampala donated 32 acres to promote urban agriculture in the northeastern parish of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kyanja&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kyanja (page does not exist)">Kyanja</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KampalaRd_Uganda_house_Kampala.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/KampalaRd_Uganda_house_Kampala.JPG/220px-KampalaRd_Uganda_house_Kampala.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/KampalaRd_Uganda_house_Kampala.JPG/330px-KampalaRd_Uganda_house_Kampala.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/KampalaRd_Uganda_house_Kampala.JPG/440px-KampalaRd_Uganda_house_Kampala.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

Uganda House, Kampala Road, Kampala</div>

<p>Kampala is served by <a href="/wiki/Entebbe_International_Airport" title="Entebbe International Airport">Entebbe International Airport</a>, which is the largest airport in Uganda.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boda-boda" title="Boda-boda">Boda-bodas</a> (local motorbike transport) are a popular mode of transport that gives access to many areas within and outside the city. Standard fees for these range from USh:1,000 to 2,000 or more. Boda-bodas are useful for passing through rush-hour traffic, although many are poorly maintained and dangerous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2007, it was announced that Kampala would remove commuter taxis from its streets and replace them with a comprehensive city bus service. (In Kampala, the term "taxi" refers to a 15-seater minibus used as public transport.) The bus service was expected to cover the greater Kampala metropolitan area including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mukono" title="Mukono">Mukono</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mpigi" title="Mpigi">Mpigi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombo,_Uganda" title="Bombo, Uganda">Bombo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Entebbe" title="Entebbe">Entebbe</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakiso" title="Wakiso">Wakiso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gayaza" title="Gayaza">Gayaza</a>. As of December 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> the service had not yet started.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Having successfully completed the Northern Bypass, the government, in collaboration with its stakeholders, now plans to introduce the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bus_Rapid_Transit" title="Bus Rapid Transit">Bus Rapid Transit</a> (BRT) system in Kampala by 2014. On 12 March 2012, Pioneer Easy Bus Company, a private transport company, started public bus service in Kampala with an estimated 100 buses each with a 60-passenger capacity (30 seated and 30 standing), acquired from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Republic_of_China" title="Peoples' Republic of China">China</a>. Another 422 buses were expected in the country in 2012 to complement the current fleet. The buses operate 24 hours daily. The company has a concession to provide public transport in the city for the next five years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The buses were impounded for back taxes in December 2013. The company expected to resume operation in February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Uganda's President <a href="/wiki/Yoweri_Museveni" title="Yoweri Museveni">Yoweri Museveni</a> and a China transportation company signed a Memorandum of Understanding, that they would at some point begin embarking on building a light rail system in Kampala, similar to the one recently completed in <a href="/wiki/Addis_Ababa" title="Addis Ababa">Addis Ababa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>.</p>
<p>On 11 April 2011, the pressure group Activists for Change (A4C) held its first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walk_to_work" title="Walk to work">Walk to Work</a> protest near Kampala, in response to a comment by President <a href="/wiki/Yoweri_Museveni" title="Yoweri Museveni">Museveni</a> on the increased cost of fuel, which had risen by 50 percent between January and April 2011. He said: "What I call on the public to do is to use fuel sparingly. Don't drive to bars."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Al_Jazeera_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-24">[24]</a></sup> The protest, which called on workers to walk to work to highlight the increased cost of transport in Uganda,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Time-23">[23]</a></sup> was disrupted by police, who fired <a href="/wiki/Tear_gas" title="Tear gas">tear gas</a> and arrested three-time presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Kizza_Besigye" title="Kizza Besigye">Kizza Besigye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Uganda)" title="Democratic Party (Uganda)">Democratic Party</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Mao" title="Norbert Mao">Norbert Mao</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In the course of the protest, Besigye was shot in the right arm by a rubber bullet. The government blamed the violence on protesters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Al_Jazeera_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kampala has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest_climate" title="Tropical rainforest climate">tropical rainforest climate</a> (Af) under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen-Geiger climate classification system</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Climate-Data.org_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Climate-Data.org-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>A facet of Kampala's weather is that it features two annual <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">wetter seasons</a>. While the city does not have a true <a href="/wiki/Dry_season" title="Dry season">dry season</a> month, it experiences heavier precipitation from August to December and from February to June. However, it's between February and June that Kampala sees substantially heavier rainfall per month, with April typically seeing the heaviest amount of precipitation at an average of around 169 millimetres (6.7 in) of rain. Kampala has been frequently mentioned as a lightning-strike capital of the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Location_map_Kampala.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="955" data-file-width="887" height="237" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Location_map_Kampala.png/220px-Location_map_Kampala.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Location_map_Kampala.png/330px-Location_map_Kampala.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Location_map_Kampala.png/440px-Location_map_Kampala.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Uganda" title="Demographics of Uganda">Demographics of Uganda</a></div>
<p>The population of Kampala grew from 1,189,142 in 2002 to 1,507,080 in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kampala has a diverse ethnic population. The city's ethnic makeup has been defined by political and economic factors. A large number of western Ugandans, particularly the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banyankole" title="Banyankole">Banyankole</a>, moved to the capital in the new government of <a href="/wiki/Yoweri_Museveni" title="Yoweri Museveni">Yoweri Museveni</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Inter-tribal marriage in Uganda is still uncommon outside large urban centers. Although many Kampala residents have been born and brought up in the city, they still define themselves by their tribal roots and speak their ancestral languages. This is more evident in the suburbs, where tribal languages are spoken widely alongside <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swahili_language" title="Swahili language">Swahili</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luganda" title="Luganda">Luganda</a>. In addition to the <a href="/wiki/Baganda" title="Baganda">Baganda</a> and Banyankole, other large ethnic groups include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basoga" title="Basoga">Basoga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kisoro" title="Kisoro">Bafumbira</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toro_Kingdom" title="Toro Kingdom">Batoro</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bakiga" title="Bakiga">Bakiga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alur_people" title="Alur people">Alur</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bagisu" title="Bagisu">Bagisu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banyoro" title="Banyoro">Banyoro</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iteso" title="Iteso">Iteso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lango_people" title="Lango people">Langi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Acholi_people" title="Acholi people">Acholi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Efforts are underway to relocate heavy industry to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kampala_Business_and_Industrial_Park" title="Kampala Business and Industrial Park">Kampala Business and Industrial Park</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Namanve" title="Namanve">Namanve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mukono_District" title="Mukono District">Mukono District</a>, approximately 14 kilometres (8.7 mi) east of the city's central business district,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> thereby cutting down on city traffic congestion. Some of the businesses that maintain their headquarters in the city center include all of the 25 <a href="/wiki/Commercial_bank" title="Commercial bank">commercial banks</a> licensed in Uganda; the <a href="/wiki/New_Vision_Group" title="New Vision Group">New Vision Group</a>, the leading news media conglomerate and majority owned by the government; and the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Monitor" title="Daily Monitor">Daily Monitor</a></i> publication, a member of the Kenya-based <a href="/wiki/Nation_Media_Group" title="Nation Media Group">Nation Media Group</a>. <a href="/wiki/Air_Uganda" title="Air Uganda">Air Uganda</a> maintained its headquarters in an office complex on <a href="/wiki/Kololo" title="Kololo">Kololo</a> Hill in Kampala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Crown Beverages Limited, the sole <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pepsi-Cola" title="Pepsi-Cola">Pepsi-Cola</a> franchise bottler in the country, is situated in <a href="/wiki/Nakawa" title="Nakawa">Nakawa</a>, a division of Kampala, about 5 kilometres (3 mi), east of the city centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>The informal sector is a large contributor to Kampala's GDP. Citizens who work in the formal sector also participate in informal activities to earn more income for their families. A public servant in Kampala, for example, may engage in aviculture in addition to working in the formal sector. Other informal fields include owning taxis and urban agriculture. The use of Kampala's wetlands for urban farming has increased over the past few decades. It connects the informal rural settlements with the more industrialized parts of the city. The produce grown in the wetlands is sold in markets in the urban areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2015, Google launched its first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wi-fi" title="Wi-fi">wi-fi</a> network in Kampala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kampala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kampala is home to the <a href="/wiki/City_Oilers" title="City Oilers">City Oilers</a>, one of East Africa's top basketball club teams. It is the only East African team that competes in the <a href="/wiki/FIBA_Africa_Clubs_Champions_Cup" title="FIBA Africa Clubs Champions Cup">FIBA Africa Clubs Champions Cup</a>. The Oilers play their home games in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MTN_Arena&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MTN Arena (page does not exist)">MTN Arena</a>, which is based in Kampala's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lugogo_Area" title="Lugogo Area">Lugogo Area</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
