<p>A <b>coast guard</b> or <b>coastguard</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Security_Regimes" title="Maritime Security Regimes">maritime security</a> organization of a particular country. The term implies widely different responsibilities in different countries, from being a heavily armed <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> force with <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a> and security duties to being a volunteer organization tasked with <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a> functions and lacking any law enforcement powers. However, a typical coast guard's functions are distinct from typical functions of both the <i><a href="/wiki/Navy" title="Navy">navy</a></i> (a pure military force) and a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transportation_police" title="Transportation police">transportation police</a></i> (a civilian <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement" title="Law enforcement">law enforcement</a> agency).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1712" data-file-width="2288" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG/200px-Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG/300px-Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG/400px-Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Girvan_Coastguard_station.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The HM Coastguard station at Girvan, Ayrshire, Scotland.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The predecessor of the modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HM_Coastguard" title="HM Coastguard">Her Majesty's Coastguard</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> was established in 1809 as the <a href="/wiki/Waterguard" title="Waterguard">Waterguard</a>, which was originally devoted to the prevention of <a href="/wiki/Smuggling" title="Smuggling">smuggling</a> as a department of the <a href="/wiki/HM_Customs_and_Excise" title="HM Customs and Excise">HM Customs and Excise</a> authority. At the time, due to high UK taxation on liquors such as <a href="/wiki/Brandy" title="Brandy">brandy</a>, and on <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, etc., smuggling in cargoes of these from places such as France, Belgium, and Holland, was an attractive proposition for many, the barrels of brandy and other <a href="/wiki/Contraband" title="Contraband">contraband</a> being landed from the ships on England's beaches at night from small boats and later sold-on for profit, as later depicted in the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Syn" title="Doctor Syn">Doctor Syn</a> series of books by <a href="/wiki/Russell_Thorndike" title="Russell Thorndike">Russell Thorndike</a>. The Coastguard was also responsible for giving assistance to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shipwrecks" title="Shipwrecks">shipwrecks</a>.</p>
<p>Each Waterguard station was issued with a <a href="/wiki/Manby_mortar" title="Manby mortar">Manby mortar</a>, which had been invented by Captain <a href="/wiki/George_William_Manby" title="George William Manby">George William Manby</a> in 1808. The mortar fired a shot with a line attached from the shore to the wrecked ship and was used for many years. This began the process through which the Coastguard assumed a life saving role. In 1821 a committee of inquiry recommended that responsibility for the Preventative Waterguard be transferred to the Board of Customs. The <a href="/wiki/Treasury" title="Treasury">Treasury</a> agreed and (in a memorandum dated 15 January 1822) directed that the preventative services, which consisted of the <a href="/wiki/Waterguard" title="Waterguard">Preventative Water Guard</a>, cruisers, and <a href="/wiki/Riding_officer" title="Riding officer">riding officers</a> should be placed under the authority of the Board of Customs and in future should be named the "Coastguard". In 1845 the Coastguard was subordinated to the <a href="/wiki/Admiralty" title="Admiralty">Admiralty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cape_Hatteras_life_saving_station.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="218" data-file-width="360" height="133" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Cape_Hatteras_life_saving_station.jpg/220px-Cape_Hatteras_life_saving_station.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Cape_Hatteras_life_saving_station.jpg/330px-Cape_Hatteras_life_saving_station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/98/Cape_Hatteras_life_saving_station.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cape_Hatteras_life_saving_station.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Cape_Lookout_Coast_Guard_Station" title="Cape Lookout Coast Guard Station">Cape Hatteras Life-Saving Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, in use from the mid-19th century.</div>
</div>

<p>In 1829 the first UK Coastguard instructions were published and dealt with discipline and directions for carrying out preventative duties. They also stipulated that, when a wreck took place, the Coastguard was responsible for taking all possible action to save lives, to take charge of the vessel and to protect property.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United States, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> was created in 1915 by the merger of two other federal agencies. The first, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Revenue_Cutter_Service" title="United States Revenue Cutter Service">United States Revenue Cutter Service</a>, was a maritime customs enforcement agency that also assumed a supporting role to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> in wartime. The second, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Life-Saving_Service" title="United States Life-Saving Service">United States Life-Saving Service</a>, was formed in 1848 and consisted of life saving crews stationed at points along the eastern seaboard. The Coast Guard later absorbed the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Lighthouse_Service" title="United States Lighthouse Service">United States Lighthouse Service</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Navigation_and_Steamboat_Inspection" title="Bureau of Navigation and Steamboat Inspection">Bureau of Navigation and Steamboat Inspection</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among the responsibilities that may be entrusted to a coast guard service are:</p>
<ul>
<li>search and rescue,</li>
<li>enforcement of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_law" title="Maritime law">maritime law</a>,</li>
<li>safety of vessels,</li>
<li>maintenance of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seamark" title="Seamark">seamarks</a>, and</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Border_control" title="Border control">border control</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>During wartime, some national Coast Guard organisations might have a role as a naval reserve force with responsibilities in harbor defenses, <a href="/wiki/Port_security" title="Port security">port security</a>, naval <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counter-intelligence" title="Counter-intelligence">counter-intelligence</a> and coastal patrols.</p>
<p>The Coast Guard may, varying by jurisdiction, be part of a country's <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a>, a law enforcement agency, or a search and rescue body. For example, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" title="U.S. Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> is a military branch with a law enforcement capacity, whereas the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Coastguard" title="Her Majesty's Coastguard">Her Majesty's Coastguard</a> (HMCG) is a civilian organisation whose only role is search and rescue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Most coast guards operate <a href="/wiki/Ship" title="Ship">ships</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> including <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seaplane" title="Seaplane">seaplanes</a> that are either owned or leased by the agency in order to fulfil their respective roles.</p>
<p>Some coast guards, such as the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Coast_Guard" title="Irish Coast Guard">Irish Coast Guard</a>, have only a very limited law enforcement role, usually in enforcing maritime safety law, such as by inspecting ships docked in their jurisdiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In cases where the Coast Guard is primarily concerned with <i>coordinating</i> rather than executing rescue operations, <a href="/wiki/Lifeboat_(rescue)" title="Lifeboat (rescue)">lifeboats</a> are often provided by civilian voluntary organisations, such as the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Lifeboat_Institution" title="Royal National Lifeboat Institution">Royal National Lifeboat Institution</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, whilst aircraft may be provided by the countries' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armed_forces" title="Armed forces">armed forces</a>, such as the search and rescue <a href="/wiki/Westland_Sea_King" title="Westland Sea King">Sea Kings</a> operated by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>, in addition to any of the HMCG's own helicopters.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Type_and_role_by_country">Type and role by country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Type and role by country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following lists a select number of Coast Guards around the world, illustrating the varied roles they play in the respective governments and the countries they operate in:</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Argentina">Argentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Argentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Naval_Prefecture" title="Argentine Naval Prefecture">Argentine Naval Prefecture</a>, in Spanish <i>Prefectura Naval Argentina</i> or PNA, is a service of the Argentine Republic's Security Ministry charged with protecting the country's rivers, lakes and maritime territory. It therefore fulfills the functions of other countries' coast guards, and furthermore acts as a <a href="/wiki/Gendarmerie" title="Gendarmerie">gendarmerie</a> force policing navigable rivers and lakes. They belonged to the Ministry of Defence until the 1980s, and the corps´ highest official was a Navy rear-admiral. They have since been transferred to the Ministry of Interior and, more recently, to the newly created Ministry of Security. However, in case of armed conflict, they can be put under Navy´s command.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Coast_guards_in_Australia" title="Coast guards in Australia">Coast guards in Australia</a></div>
<p>While having 19,650 kilometres of coastline, the Commonwealth of Australia does not have a force purely to defend its coast. The duty of patrolling its coastline falls to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Australian_Navy" title="Royal Australian Navy">Royal Australian Navy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Border_Force" title="Australian Border Force">Australian Border Force</a> (through its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Customs_Marine_Unit" title="Customs Marine Unit">Marine Unit</a> division), and the Police services of the several states and territories.</p>
<p>In addition, there are several private volunteer coast guard organizations, the two largest organizations being the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Volunteer_Coastal_Patrol" title="Royal Volunteer Coastal Patrol">Royal Volunteer Coastal Patrol</a> (established in 1937) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Volunteer_Coast_Guard" title="Australian Volunteer Coast Guard">Australian Volunteer Coast Guard</a> (established in 1961). These volunteer organizations have no law enforcement powers, and are essentially auxiliary Search and Rescue services. In NSW these two organisations have joined to become Marine Rescue in 2009.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Coast_Guard" title="Bangladesh Coast Guard">Bangladesh Coast Guard</a></div>
<p>The <b>Bangladesh Coast Guard</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali</a> transliteration: বাংলাদেশ কোস্ট গার্ড; translated from <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <i>বাংলাদেশ উপকূল রক্ষক</i>); <b>BCG</b> is the maritime law enforcement force of <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>. It is a paramilitary force which is under the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Home_Affairs_(Bangladesh)" title="Ministry of Home Affairs (Bangladesh)">Ministry of Home Affairs</a>. Its officers are transferred from the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Navy" title="Bangladesh Navy">Bangladesh Navy</a>. The Bangladesh Coast Guard also performs the duty of maritime border security of Bangladesh. The headquarters is located in <a href="/wiki/Dhaka" title="Dhaka">Dhaka</a>, Bangladesh. Currently the coast guard has 3,339 personnel<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cga_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-cga-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Barbados_Coast_Guard" title="Barbados Coast Guard">Barbados Coast Guard</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bosnia_and_Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bosnia and Herzegovina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Having 18 km (~11 mi) of coastline only, Bosnia Herzegovina does not have a force dedicated to defend its coast. The duty of patrolling its coastline falls to the Granična policija (Border Police).</p>

<p>In addition to the roles of a traditional navy, the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Navy" title="Brazilian Navy">Brazilian Navy</a> also carries out the role of organizing the merchant navy and other operational safety missions traditionally conducted by a coast guard. Other roles include: Conducting national maritime policy, and implementing and enforcing laws and regulations with respect to the sea and inland waters.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Coast_Guard" title="Canadian Coast Guard">Canadian Coast Guard</a> (CCG) is a civilian service under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Fisheries_and_Oceans_(Canada)" title="Department of Fisheries and Oceans (Canada)">Department of Fisheries and Oceans</a> responsible for patrolling the world's longest coastline of 243,042 km (~151,000 mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The CCG holds responsibility for all marine search and rescue throughout Canada. The CCG coordinates search and rescue operations with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces" title="Canadian Forces">Canadian Armed Forces</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">Royal Canadian Mounted Police</a>, and other organizations. The CCG maintains and operates seamarks, coastal light stations, vessel traffic services, marine pollution response services, marine communications systems, and provides icebreaking services. The CCG also operates all Federal scientific research and hydrographic survey vessels. To accomplish these tasks, the CCG has a sizable fleet of vessels and aircraft, all serviced from various bases and smaller stations located on three coasts (<a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Coast_Guard_College" title="Canadian Coast Guard College">Canadian Coast Guard College</a> is located near <a href="/wiki/Sydney,_Nova_Scotia" title="Sydney, Nova Scotia">Sydney, Nova Scotia</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_Coast_Guard" title="Chinese Coast Guard">Chinese Coast Guard</a></div>
<p>The <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peoples_Republic_of_China" title="Peoples Republic of China">Peoples Republic of China</a></b> used to operate several <b>coast guard agencies</b>. In March 2013, China announced it would combine the assets of four separate maritime law enforcement agencies to form a unified Coast Guard, administrated by the <a href="/wiki/State_Oceanic_Administration" title="State Oceanic Administration">State Oceanic Administration</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The new Coast Guard has been in operation since July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The current <a href="/wiki/China_Coast_Guard" title="China Coast Guard">China Coast Guard</a> (Simplified Chinese: 中国海警) serves as a coordinating body for maritime search and rescue in the territorial waters of the People's Republic of China. The China Coast Guard was previously a maritime branch of the Public Security Border Troops, a paramilitary police force under the leadership of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Public_Security_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Public Security of the People's Republic of China">Ministry of Public Security</a> (MPS). Since 2013, it is administered by the State Oceanic Administration.</p>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/China_Maritime_Safety_Administration" title="China Maritime Safety Administration">China Maritime Safety Administration</a> (Chinese: 中国海事局) was a government agency which coordinated maritime search and rescue in the territorial waters of the People's Republic of China (PRC). The CMSA was part of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Transport of the People's Republic of China">Ministry of Transport</a>.</p>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/China_Marine_Surveillance" title="China Marine Surveillance">China Marine Surveillance</a> (CMS), created on 19 October 1998, was a paramilitary maritime law enforcement agency of the <a href="/wiki/State_Oceanic_Administration" title="State Oceanic Administration">State Oceanic Administration</a> of the People's Republic of China (PRC). The force was responsible for enforcing laws and order within China's territorial waters, exclusive economic zones (EEZ), and shores; protecting the maritime environment, natural resources, navigation aids and other facilities; and carrying out maritime surveys. In emergencies, these forces could also be deployed for other missions such as search and rescue.</p>
<p>The former <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=China_Fisheries_Law_Enforcement_Command&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="China Fisheries Law Enforcement Command (page does not exist)">China Fisheries Law Enforcement Command</a> (FLEC)(Chinese: 中国渔政), an organ of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fisheries_Management_Bureau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fisheries Management Bureau (page does not exist)">Fisheries Management Bureau</a> under the PRC Ministry of Agriculture, was responsible for the enforcement of laws concerning fishing and maritime resources within PRC's territorial waters and exclusive economic zones (EEZ). FLEC's responsibilities included protecting Chinese fishing vessels and personnel, resolving disputes in fishing activities, preventing illegal fishing, and protecting maritime resources.</p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Port_and_Marine_Police" title="Cyprus Port and Marine Police">Cyprus Port and Marine Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Police_Aviation_Unit" title="Cyprus Police Aviation Unit">Cyprus Police Aviation Unit</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Port_and_Marine_Police" title="Cyprus Port and Marine Police">Cyprus Port and Marine Police</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>Λιμενική και Ναυτική Αστυνομία</i> - <i>Limeniki kai Nautiki Astinomia</i>) fulfills the functions of other countries' coast guards for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Cyprus" title="Republic of Cyprus">Republic of Cyprus</a>. Cyprus is an <a href="/wiki/Island_country" title="Island country">island country</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Mediterranean" title="Eastern Mediterranean">Eastern Mediterranean</a> with almost 40% of its territory under occupation after a <a href="/wiki/Turkish_invasion_of_Cyprus" title="Turkish invasion of Cyprus">Turkish military Invasion</a>. Due to the country's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geopolitical" title="Geopolitical">geopolitical</a> situation, size, the recent discovery in 2011 of the <a href="/wiki/Aphrodite_gas_field" title="Aphrodite gas field">Aphrodite gas field</a> in its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Police" title="Cyprus Police">Cyprus Police</a> being the main national <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">Law enforcement agency</a>, the duties and responsibilities of the Cyprus Port and Marine Police are many and sometimes complex. It is a unit of the Cyprus Police, which resides under the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Justice_and_Public_Order_(Cyprus)" title="Ministry of Justice and Public Order (Cyprus)">Ministry of Justice and Public Order (Cyprus)</a>. However it can support the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Navy" title="Cyprus Navy">Cyprus Navy</a> in wartime which resides under the Ministry of Defence. It is staffed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Police_Officer" title="Police Officer">Police Officers</a> which can be transferred to and from other units and agencies of the Cyprus Police and are tasked with the primary mission of policing the country's sea borders and the <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement" title="Law enforcement">law enforcement</a> of the waters around it. The unit is equipped with patrol boats and radars but it does not operate its own helicopters. Instead, it operates in combination with the aerial unit of the Cyprus Police, the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Police_Aviation_Unit" title="Cyprus Police Aviation Unit">Cyprus Police Aviation Unit</a>. Main roles include law enforcement against illicit activities such as <a href="/wiki/Smuggling" title="Smuggling">smuggling</a> (due to the fact that although the <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">Customs</a> and Excise Department is a separate agency under the Ministry of Finance, it does not have an operational or tactical team of its own), <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a>, illegal <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">Illegal drug trade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illegal_immigration" title="Illegal immigration">illegal immigration</a> and is also assigned with <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">Search and Rescue</a> (SAR) duties. For <a href="/wiki/Counter-terrorism" title="Counter-terrorism">counter-terrorism</a> and anti-piracy operations, the units operate in combination with the Special Anti-Terrorist Squad which is part of the <a href="/wiki/Emergency_Response_Unit_(Cyprus)" title="Emergency Response Unit (Cyprus)">Emergency Response Unit</a> of the Cyprus Police. Although the prominent agencies responsible for all Search and Rescue operations are conducted by Cyprus Police Units, the agency responsible to organize the SAR system, to co-ordinate, to control and direct SAR operations in the region that the Republic of Cyprus is responsible for (which coincides with the Nicosia <a href="/wiki/Flight_information_region" title="Flight information region">FIR</a>) is the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Joint_Rescue_Coordination_Center" title="Cyprus Joint Rescue Coordination Center">Cyprus Joint Rescue Coordination Center</a> or JRCC Larnaca, which is an independent agency of the Ministry of Defence.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Gendarmerie" title="Maritime Gendarmerie">Maritime Gendarmerie</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French Republic</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Affaires_maritimes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Affaires maritimes (page does not exist)">Affaires maritimes</a> is the closest organization to Coast Guards. In each region, a Naval Admiral, called Préfet Maritime, is in charge of coordination of all state services for action at sea (<a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">Navy</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Affaires_maritimes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Affaires maritimes (page does not exist)">Affaires maritimes</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Gendarmerie" title="National Gendarmerie">gendarmerie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a>, fishery survey ...). The charity, <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_Nationale_de_Sauvetage_en_Mer" title="Société Nationale de Sauvetage en Mer">Société Nationale de Sauvetage en Mer</a>, provides most life saving duties at the local level.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/German_Federal_Coast_Guard" title="German Federal Coast Guard">German Federal Coast Guard</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/German_Federal_Coast_Guard" title="German Federal Coast Guard">German Federal Coast Guard</a>, known as the <i>Küstenwache,</i> is both a civilian service and a law enforcement organisation, staffed with both police officers and certain civilians from the various German federal agencies associated with maritime administration with responsibility for the coordination of all law enforcement activities within its jurisdiction in the Federal Republic of Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Coast_Guard" title="Hellenic Coast Guard">Hellenic Coast Guard</a></div>
<p>The <i>Hellenic Coast Guard</i> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>Λιμενικό Σώμα-Ελληνική Ακτοφυλακή</i> - <i>Limeniko Soma-Elliniki Aktofylaki</i> - lit. "Harbor Corps-Hellenic Coast Guard") is the national coast guard of the <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Republic of Greece</a>. It is a paramilitary organization that can support the <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Navy" title="Hellenic Navy">Hellenic Navy</a> in wartime, but resides under separate civilian control in times of peace. It was founded in 1919 by an Act of Parliament and the legal framework for its function was reformed in 1927.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Haiti">Haiti</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Haiti">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Coast_Guard" title="Haitian Coast Guard">Haitian Coast Guard</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Coast_Guard" title="Haitian Coast Guard">Haitian Coast Guard</a> is an operational unit of the <a href="/wiki/Haitian_National_Police" title="Haitian National Police">Haitian National Police</a>. It is one of the few law enforcement organisations in the world to combine water policing and coast guard duties while remaining as a policing unit. It operates primarily as a law enforcement agency, with secondary responsibilities in search and rescue.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong">Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Hong Kong, law enforcement duties are carried out by the <a href="/wiki/Marine_Region" title="Marine Region">Marine Region</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Police_Force" title="Hong Kong Police Force">Hong Kong Police Force</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Customs_and_Excise_Department_(Hong_Kong)" title="Customs and Excise Department (Hong Kong)">Customs and Excise Department</a> (Ports and Maritime Command of the Boundary and Ports Branch). The <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Maritime_Rescue_Co-ordination_Centre" title="Hong Kong Maritime Rescue Co-ordination Centre">Hong Kong Maritime Rescue Co-ordination Centre</a> (HKMRCC) co-ordinates search and rescue vessels, aircraft and other resources of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_Services_Department" title="Fire Services Department">Fire Services Department</a>, <a href="/wiki/Government_Flying_Service" title="Government Flying Service">Government Flying Service</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marine_Department_(Hong_Kong)" title="Marine Department (Hong Kong)">Marine Department</a> and the Marine Police.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Irish_Coast_Guard" title="Irish Coast Guard">Irish Coast Guard</a></div>
<p>The Irish Coast Guard (IRCG) makes up one arm of the Maritime Safety Services, the other being the Maritime Safety Directorate. Both arms are due to merge into a new "one stop shop" agency for all maritime safety matters.</p>
<p>The purpose of the Irish Coast Guard is:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>To reduce the loss of life within the Irish Search and Rescue Region and on rivers, lakes and waterways and to protect the quality of the marine environment within the Irish Pollution Responsibility Zone, Harbours and Maritime Local Authority areas and to preserve property. To promote safety standards, and by doing so, prevent, as far as possible, the loss of life at sea and on inland waters and other areas, and to provide an effective emergency response service.</p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Isle_of_Man">Isle of Man</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Isle of Man">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man_Coastguard" title="Isle of Man Coastguard">Isle of Man Coastguard</a></div>
<p>The Isle of Man is a <a href="/wiki/Crown_dependencies" title="Crown dependencies">Crown dependency</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Sea" title="Irish Sea">Irish Sea</a> between Ireland and the United Kingdom. It is not part of the United Kingdom, but historically relied upon the U.K. Coastguard. However, the U.K. Coastguard withdrew in 1988, and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man_Government" title="Isle of Man Government">Isle of Man Government</a> formed its own Coastguard in 1989. Its key functions are coastal patrol, pollution control, and shore-based search and rescue. It also co-operates with other agencies as part of the Isle of Man Inland Search and Rescue Group. It maintains the Isle of Man's Marine Operations Centre (control room), but it has no aircraft, and contracts air-sea rescue to the U.K. Coastguard.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jersey">Jersey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Jersey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The Island of Jersey Coastguard is the coastguard service of the Government of Jersey, an independent Crown dependency located near to northern France. "Channel Islands AirSearch" works with the Coastguard service on search and rescue operations, and therefore the Coastguard does not maintain its own aircraft.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Korea_Coast_Guard" title="Korea Coast Guard">Korea Coast Guard</a> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">해양경찰청</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: 海洋警察廳, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization" title="Revised Romanization">Revised Romanization</a>: Haeyang-gyeongchal-cheong, Maritime Police Agency) is responsible for maritime safety and control off the coast of the Republic of Korea/South Korea. The KCG is an external branch of the R.O.K.'s Ministry of Maritime Affairs and Fisheries during peacetime.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Malaysia">Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysian_Maritime_Enforcement_Agency_(MMEA)" title="Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency (MMEA)">Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency (MMEA)</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysia_Coast_Guard" title="Malaysia Coast Guard">Malaysia Coast Guard</a> is part of the Malaysian Civil Service and is under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister%27s_Department" title="Prime Minister's Department">Prime Minister's Department</a>. The Agency is headed by a[Director General] who is appointed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yang_Di_Pertuan_Agong" title="Yang Di Pertuan Agong">Yang Di Pertuan Agong</a> (King) on the advice of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> while other personnel are appointed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Service_Commission_(Malaysia)" title="Public Service Commission (Malaysia)">Public Service Commission</a>. In times of <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">war</a>, crisis or emergency, the Agency may be placed under the command of the Royal Malaysian Armed Forces. It was formed to combat the rise of piracy in the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Malacca" title="Strait of Malacca">Strait of Malacca</a> ("The Straits"). Personnel often work very closely with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Malaysian_Navy" title="Royal Malaysian Navy">Royal Malaysian Navy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Malaysian_Air_Force" title="Royal Malaysian Air Force">Royal Malaysian Air Force</a>. The Coast Guard operates a Special Forces Commando or STAR (Special Task and Rescue) unit, which was absorbed from the Navy's <a href="/wiki/PASKAL" title="PASKAL">PASKAL</a> and Air Force's <a href="/wiki/PASKAU" title="PASKAU">PASKAU</a>. The agency utilizes its resources in a maritime law enforcement and search and rescue capacity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistan">Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pakistan_Coast_Guard" title="Pakistan Coast Guard">Pakistan Coast Guard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Security_Agency" title="Maritime Security Agency">Maritime Security Agency</a></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Islamic Republic of Pakistan</a>, there are two units operated as Coast Guards—the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Security_Agency" title="Maritime Security Agency">Maritime Security Agency</a> (MSA) under the <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Navy" title="Pakistan Navy">Pakistan Navy</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pakistan_Coast_Guard" title="Pakistan Coast Guard">Pakistan Coast Guard</a>. The MSA is a military force operated by the Navy under the direction of Ministry of Defence in its right. The Pakistani Coast Guard are placed under the Pakistan Army and has responsibilities for protecting coastal and shores of Karachi while the MSA has entire area of responsibility of the Republic of Pakistan's coastlines in terms of strategic security, as well as law enforcement within the country's <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone" title="Exclusive economic zone">exclusive economic zone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Republic of the Philippines</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Coast_Guard" title="Philippine Coast Guard">Philippine Coast Guard</a> (PCG) is a maritime law enforcement agency operating under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Transportation_and_Communications" title="Department of Transportation and Communications">Department of Transportation</a> of the Republic of the Philippines government. It is tasked with the broader enforcement of maritime laws, especially against <a href="/wiki/Smuggling" title="Smuggling">smuggling</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Illegal_fishing" title="Illegal fishing">illegal fishing</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drug_trafficking" title="Drug trafficking">drug trafficking</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a>. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portugal">Portugal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Portugal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, the coast guard role is performed by several government agencies that, together, form the <i>Sistema de Autoridade Marítima</i> (Maritime Authority System) or SAM. The SAM includes the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Navy" title="Portuguese Navy">Portuguese Navy</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_National_Republican_Guard" title="Portuguese National Republican Guard">National Republican Guard (GNR)</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Air_Force" title="Portuguese Air Force">Portuguese Air Force</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Servi%C3%A7o_de_Estrangeiros_e_Fronteiras" title="Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras">Border and Immigration Service (SEF)</a>, the Civil Protection Authority, the National Medical Emergency Institute and the <a href="/wiki/Pol%C3%ADcia_Judici%C3%A1ria" title="Polícia Judiciária">Criminal Investigation Police (PJ)</a>.</p>
<p>The <i>Autoridade Marítima Nacional</i> (National Maritime Authority) or AMN is the branch of the Portuguese Navy responsible for its coast guard role. The function of AMN is performed by the Chief of Staff of the Navy himself, supported in this role by the Directorate of the Maritime Authority, which includes the Maritime Police, the Lifeguard Institute, the Lighthouse Department and the several <a href="/wiki/Harbourmaster" title="Harbourmaster">harbourmasters</a>. Besides the specific assets of the Directorate of Maritime Authority entirely dedicated to the coast guard role, the AMN also has at its disposal the other Portuguese Navy's assets that can be used both for military and public service missions.</p>
<p>The vessels operated within the SAM include the Maritime Police patrol boats, the Lifeguard Institute lifeboats, the harbourmasters harbour boats, the GNR Coastal Control Unit' surveillance boats and the Portuguese Navy's naval ships. The aircraft operated within the SAM include fixed-wing aircraft from the Portuguese Air Force and helicopters from the Navy, the Air Force and the Civil Protection Authority.</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Police_Coast_Guard" title="Police Coast Guard">Police Coast Guard</a> (PCG) is an operational department of the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Police_Force" title="Singapore Police Force">Singapore Police Force</a>. Functions of the coast guard were transferred from the Republic of Singapore Navy to what was then the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Police_Coast_Guard" title="Police Coast Guard">Marine Police</a> in February 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The Marine Police was thus restructured and renamed as the Police Coast Guard, one of the few <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">law enforcement</a> organizations in the world to combine water policing and coast guard duties while remaining as a policing unit. It operates primarily as a law enforcement agency, with secondary responsibilities in search and rescue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=40" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The Kingdom of Spain operates several coast guard agencies.</p>
<p>The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sociedad_de_Salvamento_y_Seguridad_Mar%C3%ADtima" title="Sociedad de Salvamento y Seguridad Marítima">Sociedad de Salvamento y Seguridad Marítima</a></i>, also known as Salvamento Marítimo, or by its acronym SASEMAR, is the body in charge of maritime traffic control, safety and rescue operations, as well as protection of the maritime environment, but lacks any law enforcement responsibilities. The agency runs 20 rescue coordination centres (RCC), employs a staff of 1500 and operates a fleet of 19 vessels, 54 boats, 8 helicopters and 4 aircraft.</p>
<p>Border protection functions in the Kingdom of Spain are carried out by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Servicio_Mar%C3%ADtimo_de_la_Guardia_Civil" title="Servicio Marítimo de la Guardia Civil">Servicio Marítimo de la Guardia Civil</a></i>, with a staff of 1100 and a fleet of 67 patrol vessels and speedboats. Maritime customs functions are the responsibility of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Servicio_de_Vigilancia_Aduanera" title="Servicio de Vigilancia Aduanera">Servicio de Vigilancia Aduanera</a></i> with 13 aircraft and a surface fleet of 2 high seas cutters; 18 high speed patrol vessels; and 21 medium-sized and smaller patrol vessels.</p>
<p><i>Salvamento Marítimo</i> operates in the four international SAR areas assigned to Spain: Atlántico, Canarias, Estrecho and Mediterráneo, covering a combined extension of 1.5 million square kilometers. During a maritime emergency, Salvamento Marítimo is responsible for the coordination of other possible responders, like the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Navy" title="Spanish Navy">Spanish Navy</a> or the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Servicio_A%C3%A9reo_de_Rescate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Servicio Aéreo de Rescate (page does not exist)">Servicio Aéreo de Rescate</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> that comprises squadrons 801, 802 and 803 of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Air_Force" title="Spanish Air Force">Spanish Air Force</a>. Coordination on land, sea and air are made with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Treaty_Organization" title="North Atlantic Treaty Organization">North Atlantic Treaty Organization</a> (NATO), and cooperative <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Galicia">Galicia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: Galicia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> of <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a> has its own separate coast guard service, the <i>Servizo de Gardacostas de Galicia</i> or simply <i>Gardacostas de Galicia</i>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Xunta_de_Galicia" title="Xunta de Galicia">Galician Government</a> agency responsible for coastal and fisheries surveillance; maritime search and rescue; and protection of the sea environment. The <i>Gardacostas de Galicia</i> operates a fleet of more than 20 vessels and three helicopters, from the bases of <a href="/wiki/Viveiro" title="Viveiro">Viveiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferrol,_Galicia" title="Ferrol, Galicia">Ferrol</a>, <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coruña">A Coruña</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mux%C3%ADa" title="Muxía">Muxía</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porto_do_Son" title="Porto do Son">Porto do Son</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ribeira,_Galicia" title="Ribeira, Galicia">Ribeira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vilagarc%C3%ADa_de_Arousa" title="Vilagarcía de Arousa">Vilagarcía de Arousa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontevedra" title="Pontevedra">Pontevedra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vigo" title="Vigo">Vigo</a>.</p>
<p>The <i>Gardacostas de Galicia</i> was created in 2004, by the amalgamation of the former <i>Servizo de Vixilancia Pesqueira</i> (Galician Fisheries Surveillance Service) and the <i>Servizo de Busca e Salvamento</i> (Galician Search and Rescue Service).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka_Coast_Guard" title="Sri Lanka Coast Guard">Sri Lanka Coast Guard</a></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, until 2009 a Coast Guard Unit existed under the Ministry of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources, responsible for fishery and natural resource protection coastal areas. A new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Coast_Guard" title="Department of Coast Guard">Department of Coast Guard</a> was created under the <i>Department of Coast Guard Bill</i> presented to parliament in 2009. The department would be under the purview of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defense_(Sri_Lanka)" title="Ministry of Defense (Sri Lanka)">Ministry of Defense</a> and be staffed by civilian personal. The department's responsibilities include <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">law enforcement</a> within the country's <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone" title="Exclusive economic zone">Exclusive economic zone</a>, conducting anti-smuggling and anti-immigration operations, suppression of terrorist activities in territorial waters of Sri Lanka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coast_Guard_(Sweden)" title="Coast Guard (Sweden)">Royal Swedish Coast Guard</a> (Swedish: Kustbevakningen) is a Swedish civilian government agency with combination of police tasks, border control, maritime rescue and environmental control. Coast Guard officers carries the same equipment and similar uniforms to regular Swedish police officers. Tasks:</p>
<ul>
<li>maritime surveillance and other control and inspection tasks as well as environmental cleanup after oil spills at sea</li>
<li>co-ordinate the civilian needs for maritime surveillance and maritime information</li>
<li>follow international development within the field and take part in international efforts to establish border controls, law enforcement at sea, environmental protection at sea and other maritime surveillance tasks.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Royal Swedish Coast Guard carries out some of its surveillance by air (from its base in Skavsta near Stockholm), and some on ice and snow (from its Luleå station). It also has marine duties in Lake Vänern, Europe's third largest lake, operating out of Vänersborg.</p>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coast_Guard_Administration_(Republic_of_China)" title="Coast Guard Administration (Republic of China)">Coast Guard Administration</a> is both a <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">law enforcement</a> organization. The CGA is considered a civilian law enforcement agency under the administration of the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Yuan" title="Executive Yuan">Executive Yuan</a>, though during wartime it may be incorporated as part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ROC_military" title="ROC military">ROC military</a>. Its primary roles are ensuring the safety and security of territorial waters and coordinating search and rescue efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The CGA includes a Maritime Patrol Directorate General and a Coast Patrol Directorate General. Officers of the Maritime Patrol Directorate General are law enforcement officials, but officers of the Coast Patrol Directorate General are considered soldiers who have partial law-enforcement power.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trinidad_and_Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=45" title="Edit section: Trinidad and Tobago">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Trinidad and Tobago Coast Guard (TTCG) is the maritime division of the <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_Defence_Force" title="Trinidad and Tobago Defence Force">Trinidad and Tobago Defence Force</a>. It is within the government portfolio of the Ministry of National Security. The Commanding Officer of the Coast Guard reports to the Chief of Defence Staff. The TTCG is responsible surveillance, search and rescue, fisheries enforcement, pollution enforcement, and counter-narcotics operations for maritime jurisdiction of Trinidad and Tobago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Turkey">Turkey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=46" title="Edit section: Turkey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Coast_Guard" title="Turkish Coast Guard">Turkish Coast Guard</a></div>

<p>The Turkish Coast Guard of the Republic of Turkey (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Sahil Güvenlik Komutanlığı</i></span>) is a branch of the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Armed_Forces" title="Turkish Armed Forces">Turkish Armed Forces</a> and was initially founded in 1859. During peace time, it is under the command of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Turkey)" title="Ministry of the Interior (Turkey)">Ministry of Interior</a>. However, during war or emergency time, it falls under the command of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Navy" title="Turkish Navy">Turkish Navy</a>. On July 13, 1982, it was assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Gendarmerie" title="Turkish Gendarmerie">Turkish Gendarmerie</a> becoming both a military and a law enforcement service. Finally, the organization obtained its current form on January 1, 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sgk_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-sgk-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Coast Guard is responsible for enforcement of Turkish maritime law and controlling of the Republic of Turkey's coasts in the responsibility area. It is also the main search and rescue coordination authority for the Turkish coastal zone. The Coast Guard consists of four area commands, as the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara" title="Sea of Marmara">Sea of Marmara</a> and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Straits" title="Turkish Straits">straits</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sgk_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-sgk-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Turkish Coast Guard maintains a fleet of coastal patrol ships and small craft, as well as helicopters and fixed-wing aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Sea_Guard" title="Ukrainian Sea Guard">Ukrainian Sea Guard</a></div>
<p>Ukrainian Sea Guard is the coast guard service of Ukraine, subordinated to its Border Guard Service, the local successor of the Soviet Border Troops Naval Units that have been similarly responsible for coast guard tasks. However, there were some interchanges in units, ships and personnel between Sea Guard and the Ukrainian Navy. Operates four sea guard detachment; a sea guard cutters division in Mariupol; a special-purpose sea guard cutters division in Yalta; and a riverine Dnieper sea guard cutters division in Kiev. Sea guard administration is split between the Azov-Black seas regional administration in Simferopol and the Southern regional administration in Odessa. Service persons of the Sea Guard wear either the black uniform similar to Ukrainian Navy, but decorated with some green elements (traditional for border guard), or a common uniform of the Border Guard.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=48" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Coastguard" title="Her Majesty's Coastguard">Her Majesty's Coastguard</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Coastguard" title="Her Majesty's Coastguard">Her Majesty's Coastguard</a> is purely concerned with <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a>. It has no role in the maintenance of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seamark" title="Seamark">seamarks</a> which is instead the responsibility of <a href="/wiki/Trinity_House" title="Trinity House">Trinity House</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Lighthouse_Board" title="Northern Lighthouse Board">Northern Lighthouse Board</a> (in Scotland) and the <a href="/wiki/Commissioners_of_Irish_Lights" title="Commissioners of Irish Lights">Commissioners of Irish Lights</a> (in Northern Ireland), nor has it any concern with customs enforcement, which is the responsibility of the UK Border Force</p>
<p>HM Coastguard does not possess many of its own lifeboats but does have several MCA Falcons which are a type of lifeboat that are used in areas that might not necessarily have a lifeboat provided by the volunteer <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Lifeboat_Institution" title="Royal National Lifeboat Institution">Royal National Lifeboat Institution</a> or other Independent Units, although it often <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wet_lease" title="Wet lease">wet leases</a> commercial helicopters—mainly <a href="/wiki/Sikorsky_S-92" title="Sikorsky S-92">Sikorsky S-92s</a> and <a href="/wiki/AgustaWestland_AW139" title="AgustaWestland AW139">AgustaWestland AW139s</a>—and tugs to provide search and rescue cover in certain areas. It does, however, maintain a number of search, cliff and mud rescue teams as well as some inshore rescue boats and is a coordinating body and public face for the maritime search and rescue services. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_and_Coastguard_Agency" title="Maritime and Coastguard Agency">Maritime and Coastguard Agency</a>, which in itself is an <a href="/wiki/Executive_agency" title="Executive agency">executive agency</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_for_transport" title="Department for transport">department for transport</a>. Cooperation is made with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> and the all volunteer National Coastwatch Institution, which mans many of the vacated former Coastguard Watch-houses along the coast.</p>

<p>Formed in 1915 by the merger of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revenue_Cutter_Service" title="Revenue Cutter Service">Revenue Cutter Service</a> (est. 1790) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifesaving_Service" title="Lifesaving Service">Lifesaving Service</a> (est. 1848), and later absorbing other federal agencies, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a> (USCG) is a <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> service, a <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">law enforcement</a> agency, and regulatory agency. It is one of the seven <a href="/wiki/Uniformed_services_of_the_United_States" title="Uniformed services of the United States">uniformed services of the United States</a> and one of the five services of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a>. Its role includes enforcement of U.S. maritime law, coastal defense, search and rescue, environmental protection, and regulation of maritime industries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snapshot_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-snapshot-28">[28]</a></sup> In terms of size, the U.S. Coast Guard by itself is the world's 12th largest naval force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>During <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peacetime" title="Peacetime">peacetime</a> the USCG falls under the administration of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Homeland_Security" title="United States Department of Homeland Security">United States Department of Homeland Security</a> (previously the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Transportation" title="United States Department of Transportation">U.S. Department of Transportation</a>, 1967–2003, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Department of the Treasury">United States Department of the Treasury</a>, 1915–1967). During <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">wartime</a>, the USCG may, at the direction of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, report to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Secretary_of_the_Navy" title="U.S. Secretary of the Navy">Secretary of the Navy</a>; its resources, however, are integrated into U.S. military operations in wartime (see <a href="/wiki/Title_14_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 14 of the United States Code">14 U.S.C.</a> <a class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/14/3" rel="nofollow">§§ 3</a>–<a class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/14/4" rel="nofollow">4</a>).</p>
<p>The USCG maintains an extensive fleet of coastal and ocean-going patrol ships, called <a href="/wiki/Cutter_(boat)" title="Cutter (boat)">cutters</a> by tradition, and small craft, as well as an extensive aviation division consisting of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HH-65_Dolphin" title="HH-65 Dolphin">HH-65 Dolphin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MH-60_Jayhawk" title="MH-60 Jayhawk">MH-60 Jayhawk</a> helicopters and fixed-wing aircraft such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-130_Hercules" title="C-130 Hercules">C-130 Hercules</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HU-25_Falcon" title="HU-25 Falcon">HU-25 Guardian</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HC-144_Ocean_Sentry" title="HC-144 Ocean Sentry">HC-144 Ocean Sentry</a>. USCG helicopters are equipped with hoists to rescue survivors and also play a major role in law enforcement. The helicopters are able to land and take off from USCG cutters, making them an indispensable tool in fighting illegal drug traffic and the influx of illegal immigrants. The fixed-wing aircraft are used for long range search and rescue and law enforcement patrols. A construction and repair shipyard has been maintained since 1899 in the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a> Harbor area at <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Bay,_Baltimore" title="Curtis Bay, Baltimore">Curtis Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard_Yard" title="United States Coast Guard Yard">United States Coast Guard Yard at Curtis Bay, Maryland</a></p>
<p>Today's lighthouses on the American coasts are all maintained by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" title="U.S. Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a>, from the previous old U.S. Lighthouse Service. The list of active light houses, lighted beacons, etc. that provide detailed information on aids to navigation with their locations and characteristic signals is currently maintained by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" title="U.S. Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a> in its <b><a href="/wiki/Light_List" title="Light List">Light List</a></b> issued each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uruguay">Uruguay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coast_guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=51" title="Edit section: Uruguay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Republic of Uruguay</a>, the Coast Guard (Prefectura Nacional Naval-PRENA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> is a part of the Navy. It is tasked with the broader enforcement of maritime laws, especially against smuggling, illegal fishing, drug trafficking and piracy. It patrols the country's coastline, and is also involved in maritime search and rescue (SAR) missions, as well as the protection of the marine environment. The Uruguayan Coast Guard also frequently patrols coast areas via-foot and on patrol vehicles which they are assigned jurisdictions which are called Subprefecturas. Prefectura is also responsible for the International Port of Montevideo operability. Even though the Uruguayan Coast guard is a Military Organization they frequently help out law enforcement agencies.</p>
<p>It must also maintain the safety of navigation and be a maritime authority on rivers, the Atlantic Ocean, The River Plate and lakes in the jurisdiction of the Navy and intervene in the flagging of vessels.</p>
<p>In 2015, there are plans for the Uruguayan Coast Guard to pass and depend entirely on the Ministry of Interior, meaning it would not be a military unit anymore.</p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_Coast_Guard" title="Vietnam Coast Guard">Vietnam Coast Guard</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vietnam_Coast_Guard" title="Vietnam Coast Guard">Vietnam Coast Guard</a> (Bộ Tư Lệnh Cảnh Sát Biển Việt Nam) is part of <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_(Vietnam)" title="Ministry of Defence (Vietnam)">Ministry of Defence (Vietnam)</a>, and it patrols and controls in accordance with the laws of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam and international treaties concerned such as the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea</a> (UNCLOS). Besides maritime search and rescue (SAR) missions, Vietnam Coast Guard missions include protection the sovereignty and jurisdiction of the waters of Vietnam; protection of natural resources; prevention of environmental pollution; detection and prevention of acts of smuggling, piracy and illegal transportation and trafficking of illegal narcotics or precursors.</p>
