<p><b>Wolfgang Petersen</b> (born 14 March 1941) is a German <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>. He was nominated for two <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a> for the 1981 <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a> warfare film <i><a href="/wiki/Das_Boot" title="Das Boot">Das Boot</a></i> (1981). His other films include <i><a href="/wiki/The_NeverEnding_Story_(film)" title="The NeverEnding Story (film)">The NeverEnding Story</a></i> (1984), <i><a href="/wiki/Enemy_Mine_(film)" title="Enemy Mine (film)">Enemy Mine</a></i> (1985), <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Line_of_Fire" title="In the Line of Fire">In the Line of Fire</a></i> (1993), <i><a href="/wiki/Outbreak_(film)" title="Outbreak (film)">Outbreak</a></i> (1995), <i><a href="/wiki/Air_Force_One_(film)" title="Air Force One (film)">Air Force One</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Perfect_Storm_(film)" title="The Perfect Storm (film)">The Perfect Storm</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/Troy_(film)" title="Troy (film)">Troy</a></i> (2004), and <i><a href="/wiki/Poseidon_(film)" title="Poseidon (film)">Poseidon</a></i> (2006).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfgang_Petersen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Petersen was born on 14 March 1941 in <a href="/wiki/Emden" title="Emden">Emden</a>. Emden is a north German community in <a href="/wiki/East_Frisia" title="East Frisia">East Frisia</a> near the Dutch border, where the <a href="/wiki/Ems_(river)" title="Ems (river)">Ems</a> River flows into the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> From 1953 to 1960, Petersen attended the <a href="/wiki/Gelehrtenschule_des_Johanneums" title="Gelehrtenschule des Johanneums">Gelehrtenschule des Johanneums</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>. In the 1960s he was directing plays at Hamburg's <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Deutsch_Theater" title="Ernst Deutsch Theater">Ernst Deutsch Theater</a>. After studying theater in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and Hamburg, Petersen attended the Film and Television Academy in Berlin (1966–1970). His first film productions were for German television, and it was during his work on the popular German <i><a href="/wiki/Tatort" title="Tatort">Tatort</a></i> (<i>Crime Scene</i>) TV series that he first met and worked with the actor <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Prochnow" title="Jürgen Prochnow">Jürgen Prochnow</a> — who would later appear as the <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">U-boat</a> captain in Petersen's famous <i><a href="/wiki/Das_Boot" title="Das Boot">Das Boot</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfgang_Petersen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Petersen made his first theatrical feature film in 1974, the <a href="/wiki/Psychological_thriller" title="Psychological thriller">psychological thriller</a> <i><a href="/wiki/One_or_the_Other_of_Us" title="One or the Other of Us">One or the Other of Us</a></i>, based on the novel <i>Einer von uns beiden</i> by Horst Bosetzky and published anonymously under his pseudonym and starring <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Prochnow" title="Jürgen Prochnow">Jürgen Prochnow</a>. He next directed the 1977 film <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Konsequenz" title="Die Konsequenz">Die Konsequenz</a></i>, a b/w 16 mm adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Ziegler" title="Alexander Ziegler">Alexander Ziegler's</a> autobiographical novel of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homosexual" title="Homosexual">homosexual love</a>. In its time, the film was considered so radical that when first broadcast in Germany, the <a href="/wiki/Bayerischer_Rundfunk" title="Bayerischer Rundfunk">Bavarian network</a> turned off the transmitters rather than broadcast it.</p>
<p>His next feature was the World War II epic <i><a href="/wiki/Das_Boot" title="Das Boot">Das Boot</a></i>, released in early 1982. The film chronicles the experiences of a German submarine crew engaged in the "Battle of the Atlantic". Though not an immediate financial success, the film received highly positive reviews and was nominated for six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Awards</a>, two of which (for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Director" title="Academy Award for Best Director">Best Director</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Writing_(Adapted_Screenplay)" title="Academy Award for Best Writing (Adapted Screenplay)">Best Adapted Screenplay</a>) went to Petersen himself; he was also nominated for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award" title="BAFTA Award">BAFTA Award</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DGA_Award" title="DGA Award">DGA Award</a>. The film starred <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Prochnow" title="Jürgen Prochnow">Jürgen Prochnow</a> as the U-boat Captain, who became a good example of Peterson's action characters, a man at war and who dares danger and fate at sea.</p>
<p>After <i><a href="/wiki/The_NeverEnding_Story_(film)" title="The NeverEnding Story (film)">The NeverEnding Story</a></i> (1984), Petersen's first full-blown <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinema_in_the_United_States" title="Cinema in the United States">Hollywood</a> effort (although filmed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bavaria_Film_Studios" title="Bavaria Film Studios">Bavaria Film Studios</a> complex in Germany), <i><a href="/wiki/Enemy_Mine_(film)" title="Enemy Mine (film)">Enemy Mine</a></i> (1985), was not a critical and box office success. He finally hit his stride in 1993 with the assassination thriller <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Line_of_Fire" title="In the Line of Fire">In the Line of Fire</a></i>. Starring <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a> as an angst-ridden presidential <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secret_Service" title="United States Secret Service">Secret Service</a> guard, <i>In the Line of Fire</i> gave Petersen the box office clout he needed to direct another suspense thriller, <i><a href="/wiki/Outbreak_(film)" title="Outbreak (film)">Outbreak</a></i> (1995), starring <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Hoffman" title="Dustin Hoffman">Dustin Hoffman</a>. The 1997 Petersen blockbuster <i><a href="/wiki/Air_Force_One_(film)" title="Air Force One (film)">Air Force One</a></i> did very well at the box office, with generally positive critical reviews from movie critics. For both <i>Air Force One</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Outbreak_(film)" title="Outbreak (film)">Outbreak</a></i> (but not for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Perfect_Storm_(film)" title="The Perfect Storm (film)">The Perfect Storm</a></i>), Petersen teamed up with the German cinematographer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ballhaus" title="Michael Ballhaus">Michael Ballhaus</a>, who has also worked frequently with director <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a>.</p>
<p>By 1998, Petersen was an established Hollywood director, with the power to both re-release his classic <i>Das Boot</i> in a new director's cut and to helm star-studded action-thrillers. As such, he was originally considered for the first movie in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> film series</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (film)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>. Petersen pulled out of the running in March 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Petersen's $160 million <a href="/wiki/Epic_film" title="Epic film">epic film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Poseidon_(film)" title="Poseidon (film)">Poseidon</a></i>, a re-telling of the 1969 <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gallico" title="Paul Gallico">Paul Gallico</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poseidon_Adventure_(novel)" title="The Poseidon Adventure (novel)">The Poseidon Adventure</a></i> (previously adapted for the <a href="/wiki/The_Poseidon_Adventure_(1972_film)" title="The Poseidon Adventure (1972 film)">1972 disaster film</a>), was released by Warner Bros. in May 2006. The film performed poorly in the US, barely cracking $60 million in domestic box office receipts by early August, although international sales surpassed $121 million.</p>
<p>Although hired to direct the <a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game_(film)" title="Ender's Game (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game" title="Ender's Game">Ender's Game</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Orson_Scott_Card" title="Orson Scott Card">Orson Scott Card</a> that was scheduled for release in 2008, he later "moved on"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> from the project. His potential projects include a live-action adaptation of the 2006 anime film <i><a href="/wiki/Paprika_(2006_film)" title="Paprika (2006 film)">Paprika</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and a film adaptation of the science fiction novel <i><a href="/wiki/Old_Man%27s_War" title="Old Man's War">Old Man's War</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a ten-year-hiatus, Petersen returned in 2016 as director of the heist comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Vier_gegen_die_Bank_(2016_film)" title="Vier gegen die Bank (2016 film)">Vier gegen die Bank</a></i>, his first German-language film since <i><a href="/wiki/Das_Boot" title="Das Boot">Das Boot</a></i> in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
