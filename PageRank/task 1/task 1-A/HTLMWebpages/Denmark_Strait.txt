<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Denmark Strait</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Denmark Strait</b> (Danish: <span lang="da" xml:lang="da"><i>Danmarksstrædet</i></span>) or <b>Greenland Strait</b> (Icelandic: <span lang="is" xml:lang="is"><i>Grænlandssund</i></span>, the latter meaning '<a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> <a href="/wiki/Sound_(geography)" title="Sound (geography)">Sound</a>') is an oceanic <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> between <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> (to its southeast) and Greenland (to its northwest). The Norwegian island of <a href="/wiki/Jan_Mayen" title="Jan Mayen">Jan Mayen</a> lies northeast of the strait.</p>
<p>It connects the <a href="/wiki/Greenland_Sea" title="Greenland Sea">Greenland Sea</a>, an extension of the Arctic Ocean, to the <a href="/wiki/Irminger_Sea" title="Irminger Sea">Irminger Sea</a>, a part of the Atlantic Ocean. It stretches 300 miles (480 km) long and 180 miles (290 km) wide at its narrowest, between Straumnes, the northwestern <a href="/wiki/Headland" title="Headland">headland</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Westfjords" title="Westfjords">Westfjords</a> <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hornstrandir" title="Hornstrandir">Hornstrandir</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Tupinier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cape Tupinier (page does not exist)">Cape Tupinier</a> on <i>Blosseville Coast</i> in <a href="/wiki/Tunu" title="Tunu">East Greenland</a>). The official <a href="/wiki/International_Hydrographic_Organization" title="International Hydrographic Organization">International Hydrographic Organization</a> (IHO) delineation between the Arctic and the North Atlantic Oceans runs from Straumnes to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Nansen" title="Cape Nansen">Cape Nansen</a>, 132 km (82 miles) southwest of Cape Tunipier. From Straumnes to Cape Nansen the distance is 336 km (209 miles).</p>
<p>The narrow depth, where the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Greenland%E2%80%93Iceland_Rise&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Greenland–Iceland Rise (page does not exist)">Greenland–Iceland Rise</a> runs along the bottom of the sea, is 625 feet (191 m). The cold <a href="/wiki/East_Greenland_Current" title="East Greenland Current">East Greenland Current</a> passes through the strait and carries <a href="/wiki/Iceberg" title="Iceberg">icebergs</a> south into the North Atlantic. It hosts important <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fisheries" title="Fisheries">fisheries</a>.</p>
<p>The world's largest known underwater waterfall, known as the <a href="/wiki/Denmark_Strait_cataract" title="Denmark Strait cataract">Denmark Strait cataract</a>, flows down the western side of the Denmark Strait.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>During World War II, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Denmark_Strait" title="Battle of the Denmark Strait">Battle of the Denmark Strait</a> took place on 24 May 1941. The <a href="/wiki/German_battleship_Bismarck" title="German battleship Bismarck">German battleship <i>Bismarck</i></a> sank the British battlecruiser <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HMS_Hood_(51)" title="HMS Hood (51)">HMS <i>Hood</i></a>, which exploded with the loss of all but three of its 1,418 crew; <a href="/wiki/HMS_Prince_of_Wales_(53)" title="HMS Prince of Wales (53)">HMS <i>Prince of Wales</i></a> was seriously damaged in the engagement. <i>Bismarck</i> entered the Atlantic through the Strait, but damage sustained in the battle—combined with British aircraft search-and-destroy missions—led to its sinking three days later.</p>
