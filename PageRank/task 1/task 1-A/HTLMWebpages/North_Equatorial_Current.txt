<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">North Equatorial Current</h1>

<p>The <b>North Equatorial Current</b> is a significant <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> current that flows east-to-west between about <a href="/wiki/10th_parallel_north" title="10th parallel north">10° north</a> and <a href="/wiki/20th_parallel_north" title="20th parallel north">20° north</a>. It is the southern side of a clockwise subtropical gyre. Despite its name, the North Equatorial Current is not connected to the equator. In both oceans, it is separated from the equatorial circulation by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Equatorial_Countercurrent" title="Equatorial Countercurrent">Equatorial Countercurrent</a> (also known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Equatorial_Countercurrent" title="North Equatorial Countercurrent">North Equatorial Countercurrent</a>), which flows eastward. The westward surface flow at the equator in both oceans is part of the <a href="/wiki/South_Equatorial_Current" title="South Equatorial Current">South Equatorial Current</a>.</p>
