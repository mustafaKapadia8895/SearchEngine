<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Shortwave radiation</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This article is about electromagnetic radiation. For radio frequency radiation, see <a href="/wiki/Shortwave_radio" title="Shortwave radio">shortwave radio</a>. For other uses, see <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Shortwave_(disambiguation)" title="Shortwave (disambiguation)">shortwave (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Shortwave radiation</b> (SW) is <a href="/wiki/Radiant_energy" title="Radiant energy">radiant energy</a> with wavelengths in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optical_spectrum" title="Optical spectrum">visible</a> (VIS), near-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultraviolet_radiation" title="Ultraviolet radiation">ultraviolet</a> (UV), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Near-infrared" title="Near-infrared">near-infrared</a> (NIR) spectra.</p>
<p>There is no standard cut-off for the near-infrared range; therefore, the shortwave radiation range is also variously defined. It may be broadly defined to include all radiation with a wavelength between 0.1<a href="/wiki/Micrometre" title="Micrometre">μm</a> and 5.0μm or narrowly defined so as to include only radiation between 0.2μm and 3.0μm.</p>
<p>There is little radiation flux (in terms of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/W/m%C2%B2" title="W/m²">W/m²</a>) to the Earth's surface below 0.2μm or above 3.0μm, although photon flux remains significant as far as 6.0μm, compared to shorter wavelength fluxes. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UV-C" title="UV-C">UV-C</a> radiation spans from 0.1μm to .28μm, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UV-B" title="UV-B">UV-B</a> from 0.28μm to 0.315μm, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UV-A" title="UV-A">UV-A</a> from 0.315μm to 0.4μm, the visible spectrum from 0.4μm to 0.7μm, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Near-infrared" title="Near-infrared">NIR</a> arguably from 0.7μm to 5.0μm, beyond which the infrared is thermal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shortwave radiation is distinguished from <a href="/wiki/Longwave" title="Longwave">longwave</a> radiation. Downward shortwave radiation is sensitive to <a href="/wiki/Solar_zenith_angle" title="Solar zenith angle">solar zenith angle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cloud_cover" title="Cloud cover">cloud cover</a> and surface <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">albedo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>
