<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Eurasian Basin</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p>The <b>Eurasian Basin</b>, is one of the two major basins into which the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Basin" title="Arctic Basin">North Polar Basin</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic Ocean</a> is split by the <a href="/wiki/Lomonosov_Ridge" title="Lomonosov Ridge">Lomonosov Ridge</a>, the other big basin is the <a href="/wiki/Amerasian_Basin" title="Amerasian Basin">Amerasian Basin</a>. The Eurasian Basin may be seen as an extension of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_basin#Ocean_bottom" title="Atlantic basin">North Atlantic Basin</a> through <a href="/wiki/Fram_Strait" title="Fram Strait">Fram Strait</a>. It is further split by the <a href="/wiki/Mid-ocean_ridge" title="Mid-ocean ridge">mid-ocean</a> <a href="/wiki/Gakkel_Ridge" title="Gakkel Ridge">Gakkel Ridge</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Nansen_Basin" title="Nansen Basin">Nansen Basin</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Amundsen_Basin" title="Amundsen Basin">Amundsen Basin</a>. The latter basin is the deepest one of the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic Ocean</a> and the geographic <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">North Pole</a> located there.</p>
<p>The Eurasian Basin is bounded from the South by <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Svalbard" title="Svalbard">Svalbard</a> archipelago, the <a href="/wiki/Lomonosov_Ridge" title="Lomonosov Ridge">Lomonosov Ridge</a>, and the Siberian <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">shelves</a> of <a href="/wiki/Laptev_Sea" title="Laptev Sea">Laptev Sea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kara_Sea" title="Kara Sea">Kara Sea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barents_Sea" title="Barents Sea">Barents Sea</a>. The maximum depth is reached at the <a href="/wiki/Litke_Deep" title="Litke Deep">Litke Deep</a> with 5449 <a href="/wiki/M" title="M">m</a> depth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is believed to be of <a href="/wiki/Cenozoic" title="Cenozoic">Cenozoic</a> origin: it was created about 63 m.y. ago by the spreading of the sea floor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
