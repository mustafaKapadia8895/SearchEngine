<p><b>Hurricane Kenna</b> is tied as the third-most intense <a href="/wiki/Pacific_hurricane" title="Pacific hurricane">Pacific hurricane</a> to strike the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> in <a href="/wiki/Recorded_history" title="Recorded history">recorded history</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Kenna was the sixteenth tropical depression, thirteenth tropical storm, seventh hurricane, sixth major hurricane, and third <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Pacific_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Pacific hurricanes">Category 5</a> hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2002 Pacific hurricane season">2002 Pacific hurricane season</a>. After forming on October 22 to the south of Mexico from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a>, forecasters consistently predicted the storm to strengthen much less than it actually did. Moving into an area of favorable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warm_core" title="Warm core">upper-level conditions</a> and warm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">sea surface temperatures</a>, Kenna quickly strengthened to reach peak winds of 165 mph (265 km/h) on October 25 while located about 255 mi (410 km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Vallarta" title="Puerto Vallarta">Puerto Vallarta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>. Weakening as it turned to the northeast, the hurricane struck near <a href="/wiki/San_Blas,_Nayarit" title="San Blas, Nayarit">San Blas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nayarit" title="Nayarit">Nayarit</a>, with winds of 140 mph (225 km/h) before dissipating on October 26 over the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Sierra Madre Occidental</a> mountains.</p>
<p>The name "Kenna" was retired from the list of Pacific hurricane names due to its effects on Mexico, which included US$101 million in damage and four deaths. The worst of the hurricane's effects occurred between San Blas in Nayarit and Puerto Vallarta in Jalisco, where over 100 people were injured and thousands of homes and businesses were damaged or destroyed. 95% of the buildings in San Blas were damaged, and hundreds of buildings were destroyed along coastal areas of Puerto Vallarta.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Kenna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kenna_2002_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Kenna_2002_track.png/220px-Kenna_2002_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Kenna_2002_track.png/330px-Kenna_2002_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Kenna_2002_track.png/440px-Kenna_2002_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kenna_2002_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>

<p>The origin of Hurricane Kenna can be traced to a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moving westward through the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on October 16, possibly the same wave that passed near <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> two days earlier. The wave entered the eastern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> on October 19, and a tropical disturbance along the wave axis gradually became better organized. Conditions favored continued development, and <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak classifications</a> began late on October 20. Early on October 22, the system developed into Tropical Depression Fourteen-E while located about 375 mi (605 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colima" title="Colima">Colima</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Initially the depression was disorganized, with little inner convective structure and sporadic deep convection. Computer models predicted an increase in <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> by 60 hours; as such the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> forecast the depression to strengthen to a peak strength of 45 mph (70 km/h) before weakening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lawrence1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-lawrence1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The depression quickly became better organized with a great increase in convection near the center, and six hours after forming it strengthened into Tropical Storm Kenna. Upper-level outflow and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbands" title="Rainbands">banding features</a> improved in organization, as well. With warm water temperatures of over 29 °C (84 °F) and updated model forecasts anticipating light amounts of vertical wind shear, forecasters predicted Kenna to slowly intensify to reach winds of 85 mph (135 km/h) within 72 hours of October 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stewart2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-stewart2-3">[3]</a></sup> The storm moved to the west-northwest around the periphery of a mid-level <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">high-pressure system</a>, and initially failed to strengthen further with much of its convection being associated with outer rainbands. On October 23, the outer rainbands dissipated and convection became more concentrated near the center, which coincided with a steady increase in strength. Late on October 23, Kenna intensified into a hurricane while located about 380 mi (615 km) southwest of Manzanillo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shortly after becoming a hurricane, Kenna began to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensify</a> with a 17 mi (27 km) wide <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> located within its well-defined central dense overcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beven9_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-beven9-4">[4]</a></sup> Early on October 24 Kenna became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">major hurricane</a>, and in a 24‑hour period the hurricane more than doubled its windspeed from 70 mph (115 km/h) winds to 145 mph (235 km/h). After turning to the north and northeast in response to the flow ahead of a large mid to upper-level <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a>, Kenna attained peak winds of 165 mph (265 km/h) early on October 25 while located about 255 mi (410 km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Vallarta" title="Puerto Vallarta">Puerto Vallarta</a>, the third <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Pacific_Category_5_hurricanes" title="List of Pacific Category 5 hurricanes">Category 5 hurricane</a> of the season. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Reconnaissance Aircraft</a> flight into the hurricane while it was near peak intensity recorded a pressure of 913 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> the fourth lowest recorded pressure for a <a href="/wiki/Pacific_hurricane" title="Pacific hurricane">Pacific hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Juliette_01_TCR_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Juliette_01_TCR-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Quickly after peaking, wind shear from the approaching trough weakened the hurricane, and by six hours after reaching peak intensity, the winds in Hurricane Kenna dropped to 150 mph (240 km/h) after the eye nearly dissipated. Despite a 27 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> increase in pressure in 12 hours, <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convective activity</a> increased prior to Kenna making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a>. On October 25 Hurricane Kenna struck near <a href="/wiki/San_Blas,_Nayarit" title="San Blas, Nayarit">San Blas</a> in the state of <a href="/wiki/Nayarit" title="Nayarit">Nayarit</a> with estimated winds of 140 mph (225 km/h). The hurricane rapidly weakened over the mountainous terrain of western Mexico, and the circulation dissipated on October 26 over the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Sierra Madre Occidental</a> mountains. The remnants continued northeastward into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> and southeastern United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>About 27 hours before landfall, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> officials issued a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_watches_and_warnings" title="Tropical cyclone watches and warnings">hurricane watch</a> from <a href="/wiki/Mazatl%C3%A1n" title="Mazatlán">Mazatlán</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Corrientes,_Jalisco" title="Cabo Corrientes, Jalisco">Cabo Corrientes, Jalisco</a>, with a tropical storm watch issued further south to <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a>. Six hours later when its track became more apparent, the watch was upgraded to a hurricane warning from Mazatlán to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Fortuna,_Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Fortuna, Mexico (page does not exist)">La Fortuna</a>, with a tropical storm warning southward to Manzanillo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Roughly 8,800 of the 9,000 residents in the landfall location, <a href="/wiki/San_Blas,_Nayarit" title="San Blas, Nayarit">San Blas</a>, evacuated, which ultimately contributed to a low death toll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Officials ordered for the evacuation of 50,000 residents and fishermen along the southwest coast of Mexico, including 3,000 in the <a href="/wiki/Islas_Mar%C3%ADas" title="Islas Marías">Islas Marías</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dpa_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-dpa-7">[7]</a></sup> 10,000 near Mazatlán, and 15,000 near flood-prone areas. Civil authorities closed all schools and docks in potentially affected areas. The <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Red_Cross" title="Mexican Red Cross">Mexican Red Cross</a> prepared for the storm by shipping 215 <a href="/wiki/Ton" title="Ton">tonnes</a> of relief supplies such as food, water, clothing, and medicine to the Red Cross branch in <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>. Assistance from the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a> delivered 10 <a href="/wiki/Ton" title="Ton">tonnes</a> of food and water, as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitr_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-sitr-8">[8]</a></sup> The Mexican Red Cross prepared 20 emergency shelters in the state of Nayarit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rrr_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-rrr-9">[9]</a></sup> Officials took security measures in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Cabos" title="Los Cabos">Los Cabos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Economic_Cooperation" title="Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation">Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation</a> was meeting during the passage of the hurricane. Early forecasts indicated a possible threat to the meeting, causing the government to prepare for a potential alternate site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dpa_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-dpa-7">[7]</a></sup> Officials recommended boats to stay at port due to severe conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rq_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-rq-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>Few official surface observations are available for the passage of the hurricane. Upon making landfall, Kenna was accompanied with an estimated 16 foot (4.9 m) <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Blas,_Nayarit" title="San Blas, Nayarit">San Blas</a>. The surge also affected <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Vallarta" title="Puerto Vallarta">Puerto Vallarta</a>, with reports of 10 foot (3 m) waves rushing inland from the bay. The hurricane dropped about 1.38 inches (35 mm) of precipitation while passing about 60 mi (95 km) east of the offshore archipelago, <a href="/wiki/Islas_Mar%C3%ADas" title="Islas Marías">Islas Marías</a>. There, sustained winds reached an estimated 106 mph (170 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> On land, Kenna dropped heavy rainfall peaking at 18.91 inches (480 mm) at <a href="/wiki/San_Ignacio,_Sinaloa" title="San Ignacio, Sinaloa">San Ignacio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>, and 12.89 inches (327 mm) near <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colima" title="Colima">Colima</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The highest recorded sustained wind on land was about 100 mph (161 km/h) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tepic,_Nayarit" title="Tepic, Nayarit">Tepic, Nayarit</a>, with wind gusts at Puerto Vallarta reaching 50 mph (80 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The hurricane also produced heavy rainfall in <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoacán">Michoacán</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colima" title="Colima">Colima</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>, and hit <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a> with strong winds and rough seas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rq_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-rq-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/San_Blas,_Nayarit" title="San Blas, Nayarit">San Blas</a>, strong winds from the hurricane damaged or destroyed 95% of the homes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-21">[21]</a></sup> with 1,540 houses damaged and 8,800 people affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-situation_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-situation-22">[22]</a></sup> There, large commercial shrimp boats were swept up to 900 feet (275 m) inland from their docks. An elderly woman died in the city when the wall of her house collapsed on her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Large portions of the city were covered with building debris and sand washed from the ocean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitr_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-sitr-8">[8]</a></sup> Elsewhere in <a href="/wiki/Nayarit" title="Nayarit">Nayarit</a>, flying debris killed a person in Santiago Escuintla. There, two elderly men drowned, one by falling into a river. Both were believed to have been killed during the storm as they fled their homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Ixcuintla" title="Santiago Ixcuintla">Santiago Ixcuintla</a>, the hurricane damaged 3,770 homes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-situation_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-situation-22">[22]</a></sup> and throughout Nayarit, strong winds from the hurricane destroyed the roofs of hundreds of houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prmh_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-prmh-23">[23]</a></sup> Federal authorities lost communications with at least 30 Indian villages due to the high winds of the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitr_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-sitr-8">[8]</a></sup> Kenna destroyed the entire banana, tobacco, and tomato crops in the rural areas of San Blas, <a href="/wiki/Tecuala" title="Tecuala">Tecuala</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Acaponeta" title="Acaponeta">Acaponeta</a>, leaving more than 700 subsistence farmers and their families in need of water and food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-situation_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-situation-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Vallarta" title="Puerto Vallarta">Puerto Vallarta</a>, about 100 mi (164 km) southeast of the landfall location, the storm surge resulted in an estimated damage total of US$5 million, primarily to hotels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The surge flooded the hotels and other waterfront areas, and extended up to 330 feet (100 m) inland. Waist-deep floodwaters swept away vans and cars,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dpaval_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-dpaval-24">[24]</a></sup> ruining several vehicles. The passage of the hurricane destroyed 150 stores near the ocean and extensively damaged three hotels. Damage to the city's port was minor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hurricane injured at least 52 in Puerto Vallarta<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xna_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-xna-26">[26]</a></sup> and dozens in San Blas from widespread flying glass and other forms of debris,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> with two people seriously injured due to the hurricane. Ten <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Mexico" title="Municipalities of Mexico">municipalities</a> suffered substantial damage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp-25">[25]</a></sup> with insured damage in Mexico totaling <span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7007960000000000000♠</span>US$96 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-damage_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-damage-27">[27]</a></sup> Rainfall from the hurricane spread across northern Mexico, causing minor flash floods and mudslides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-situation_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-situation-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The remnants of Kenna entered the south-central United States, resulting in enhanced rainfall in various locations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-14"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span id="Retirement,_aftermath_and_records"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement.2C_aftermath_and_records">Retirement, aftermath and records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Kenna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Retirement, aftermath and records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kenna_damage_(Vallarta).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="225" data-file-width="300" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Kenna_damage_%28Vallarta%29.jpg/220px-Kenna_damage_%28Vallarta%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/31/Kenna_damage_%28Vallarta%29.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kenna_damage_(Vallarta).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Damage in Puerto Vallarta</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After the season had ended, the World Meteorological Organization retired the name Kenna and replaced it with Karina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_government" title="Mexican government">Mexican government</a> declared the region in Nayarit near the landfall of Kenna as a disaster area, allowing for the usage of emergency funds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitr_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-sitr-8">[8]</a></sup> Immediately after the passage of the hurricane, the Mexican Red Cross prepared 180 technical staff and volunteers from seven states to deliver 125 <a href="/wiki/Ton" title="Ton">MT</a> of food, medicine, and clothes to the areas most affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prmh_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-prmh-23">[23]</a></sup> The Mexican government deployed the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Army" title="Mexican Army">Mexican Army</a> to the area to remove fallen trees and establish water treatment plants to assist the affected population. The <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Navy" title="Mexican Navy">Mexican Navy</a> was sent to assist to support medical personnel in the San Blas area, and the government Department for Family Development assisted the Mexican Red Cross in delivering food. <a href="/wiki/Grupo_Modelo" title="Grupo Modelo">Grupo Modelo</a>, brewers of <a href="/wiki/Corona_(beer)" title="Corona (beer)">Corona beer</a>, sent 6,600 <a href="/wiki/Gallon" title="Gallon">gallons</a> (25,000 liters) of drinking water and 1000 food sets for the San Blas area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-situation_22-4"><a href="#cite_note-situation-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Backhoes and dump trucks gradually removed the debris and sand from San Blas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitr_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-sitr-8">[8]</a></sup> Dozens of storeowners, municipal employees, and volunteers in Puerto Vallarta worked to clear the debris caused by the storm. The remaining stores, bars, and shops placed signs on their windows describing they were open in effort to attract the tourists still in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-afp-25">[25]</a></sup> By about two months after the hurricane, most hotels, restaurants, and shops were reopened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vallarta_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-vallarta-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hurricane Kenna is currently tied with a <a href="/wiki/1957_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Twelve" title="1957 Pacific hurricane season">hurricane in 1957</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1959_Mexico_hurricane" title="1959 Mexico hurricane">1959 Mexico hurricane</a> as the third most intense <a href="/wiki/Pacific_hurricane" title="Pacific hurricane">Pacific hurricane</a> to strike Mexico. The only Pacific hurricanes to strike Mexico at a greater intensity were <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Patricia" title="Hurricane Patricia">Patricia</a> of <a href="/wiki/2015_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2015 Pacific hurricane season">2015</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Madeline_(1976)" title="Hurricane Madeline (1976)">Madeline</a> of <a href="/wiki/1976_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1976 Pacific hurricane season">1976</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kenna_02_TCR_1-15"><a href="#cite_note-Kenna_02_TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Kenna is the third most intense October tropical cyclone to have occurred in the East Pacific, and is the fourth most intense East Pacific hurricane on record, behind <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Patricia" title="Hurricane Patricia">Hurricane Patricia</a> of 2015, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Linda_(1997)" title="Hurricane Linda (1997)">Hurricane Linda</a> of 1997 and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Rick_(2009)" title="Hurricane Rick (2009)">Hurricane Rick</a> of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
