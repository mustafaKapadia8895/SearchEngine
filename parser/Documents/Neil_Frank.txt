<p><b>Neil Laverne Frank</b> (born September 11, 1931) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorologist" title="Meteorologist">meteorologist</a> and former director of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) in Florida. He was instrumental in advancing both the scientific and informational aspects of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a> forecasting. He retired as Chief Meteorologist at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KHOU-TV" title="KHOU-TV">KHOU-TV</a> in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Dr. Frank announced his retirement effective May 2008 during his May 19 evening broadcast segment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history_and_family">Early history and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neil_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early history and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frank grew up in <a href="/wiki/Wellington,_Kansas" title="Wellington, Kansas">Wellington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>, and attended nearby <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_College_(Kansas)" title="Southwestern College (Kansas)">Southwestern College</a>. After receiving his bachelor's degree in <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a> in 1953 and doing some military work, he moved on to <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">Florida State University</a>, where he earned his Master's and Ph.D. degrees in meteorology.</p>
<p>Frank has been married over 60 years and has three grown children, 10 grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="NHC_career">NHC career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neil_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: NHC career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to his graduate studies in meteorology, Frank served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> where he received training as a weather officer. In 1961, he began working as a forecaster for the National Hurricane Center. He was appointed director of the Center in 1974. While director, Dr. Frank also served as chairman of the International Hurricane Committee, which coordinates hurricane warnings with other countries in North America. He also participated in meteorological experiments conducted off the African coast. In 1987, he was called to testify as an expert witness before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_Committee_on_Commerce,_Science_and_Transportation" title="United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation">United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation</a>. To date, Frank is the longest serving director of the NHC.</p>
<p>As NHC director, Frank was in the news frequently when hurricanes threatened, appearing in numerous interviews with then-<a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> news anchor <a href="/wiki/Dan_Rather" title="Dan Rather">Dan Rather</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> whose early career included coverage of several hurricanes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Allen" title="Hurricane Allen">Hurricane Allen</a> in 1980, Frank used an Amateur Radio Station to communicate directly to the Brownsville Weather Center in Texas after they had lost all of their conventional communications links. The only remaining communications link between the Hurricane Center and Brownsville was their amateur radio station on battery power and wire antenna. NHC and Brownsville discussed the strange behavior of the eye of Hurricane Allen while it stalled just off the Texas Coast for almost 2 hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting_career">Broadcasting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neil_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Broadcasting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 1987, Frank retired from the National Hurricane Center and joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,Texas" title="Houston,Texas">Houston's</a> <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> affiliate, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KHOU-TV" title="KHOU-TV">KHOU-TV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was already well-known to the <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> public from his reports as Director of the National Hurricane Center, particularly those during <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Alicia" title="Hurricane Alicia">Hurricane Alicia</a>, which came ashore near Houston in 1983. Frank was the chief meteorologist for the television station for over 20 years, receiving numerous awards and recognitions. In December 2007, Frank announced that he would retire the following year from broadcasting at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KHOU-TV" title="KHOU-TV">KHOU-TV</a>. Frank announced on the 10 PM news on Monday, May 19, 2008 that he would retire at the end of May. He will continue to provide the station with special weather projects and hurricane coverage.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Warming">Global Warming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neil_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Global Warming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frank is a signatory to <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Alliance" title="Cornwall Alliance">An Evangelical Declaration on Global Warming</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> which states that "Earth and its ecosystems – created by God's intelligent design and infinite power and sustained by His faithful providence – are robust, resilient, self-regulating, and self-correcting."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_memberships_and_awards">Professional memberships and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neil_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Professional memberships and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Elected to council, <a href="/wiki/American_Meteorological_Society" title="American Meteorological Society">American Meteorological Society</a>, 1989-1992 term.</li>
<li>First place in the 1989 Texas Press Awards, Best weathercast.</li>
<li>2004 recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Belo" title="Belo">Belo</a> William H. Seay Award.</li>
</ul>
