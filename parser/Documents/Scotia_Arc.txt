<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Scotia Arc</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p>The <b>Scotia Arc</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Island_arc" title="Island arc">island arc</a> system forming the north, east and south border of the <a href="/wiki/Scotia_Sea" title="Scotia Sea">Scotia Sea</a>. The northern border, the North Scotia Ridge, comprises (from west to east) <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_los_Estados" title="Isla de los Estados">Isla de los Estados</a> at the tip of <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Burdwood_Bank" title="Burdwood Bank">Burdwood</a>, Davis, and Aurora Banks; the <a href="/wiki/Shag_Rocks,_South_Georgia" title="Shag Rocks, South Georgia">Shag</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_Island" title="South Georgia Island">South Georgia Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clerke_Rocks" title="Clerke Rocks">Clerke Rocks</a>. The eastern border comprises the volcanic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Sandwich_Islands" title="South Sandwich Islands">South Sandwich Islands</a> flanked by the <a href="/wiki/South_Sandwich_Trench" title="South Sandwich Trench">South Sandwich Trench</a>. The southern border, the South Scotia Ridge, (east to west) comprises <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Herdman_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Herdman Bank (page does not exist)">Herdman</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Discovery Bank (page does not exist)">Discovery</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bruce_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bruce Bank (page does not exist)">Bruce</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pirie_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pirie Bank (page does not exist)">Pirie</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jane_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jane Bank (page does not exist)">Jane Banks</a>; the <a href="/wiki/South_Orkney_Islands" title="South Orkney Islands">South Orkney Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elephant_Island" title="Elephant Island">Elephant Island</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Bransfield_Strait" title="Bransfield Strait">Bransfield Strait</a>, finally, separates the arc from the <a href="/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands" title="South Shetland Islands">South Shetland Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Ross_Island" title="James Ross Island">James Ross Island</a> flanking the tip of the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Peninsula" title="Antarctic Peninsula">Antarctic Peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barker-2001-fig1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Barker-2001-fig1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Scotia Arc surrounds the small <a href="/wiki/Scotia_Plate" title="Scotia Plate">Scotia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Sandwich_Plate" title="South Sandwich Plate">South Sandwich Plates</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barker-2001-fig1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Barker-2001-fig1-1">[1]</a></sup> The arc is formed by <a href="/wiki/Continental_fragment" title="Continental fragment">continental fragments</a> that once formed a land bridge between South America and Antarctica, once part of the <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subduction</a> margin that still forms the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a>. An ancestral arc, which formed a separate subduction system, broke through this land bridge and started to form the Scotia Sea by eastward <a href="/wiki/Back-arc_basin" title="Back-arc basin">back-arc extension</a> driven by subduction of the <a href="/wiki/South_American_Plate" title="South American Plate">South American Plate</a> (also located east of the Scotia Arc).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barker-2001-intro_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Barker-2001-intro-2">[2]</a></sup> Today, the Scotia Arc forms a discontinuous link between South America and Antarctica and an important barrier between the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans, and therefore for the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circumpolar_Current" title="Antarctic Circumpolar Current">Antarctic Circumpolar Current</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalziel-etal-2013-signif_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dalziel-etal-2013-signif-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scotia_Arc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The formation of the Scotia Arc was initiated by an acceleration in South America's westward migration in the mid-<a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> (<span class="nourlexpansion plainlinks" style="white-space:nowrap;"><a class="external text" href="http://tools.wmflabs.org/timescale/?Ma=120–83">120 to 83</a> <a href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">million years ago</a></span>, Ma) which led to the <a href="/wiki/Tectonic_uplift" title="Tectonic uplift">uplift</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Fuegan</a> <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> and the then adjacent and embryonic North Scotia Ridge. The uplift of the ridge initiated the eastward migration of the South Georgia microcontinent from Tierra del Fuego towards its current location. In the Late Cretaceous South America started to move west relative to Antarctica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Little changed in the region between the Late Cretaceous and <a href="/wiki/Oligocene" title="Oligocene">Oligocene</a> (90-30 Ma) except for the subduction of the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Plate" title="Phoenix Plate">Phoenix Plate</a> on the Pacific margin of the land bridge that still connected South America and Antarctica — what would become the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a> was at this time a cusp on the Pacific side of the land bridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Late_Paleocene" title="Late Paleocene">Late Paleocene</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_Eocene" title="Early Eocene">Early Eocene</a> (55 Ma) saw rift basin formation in the Fuegan Andes which led to crustal extension: the first sign of separation between the two continents and the formation of the South Scotia Sea and South Scotia Ridge. Spreading in the West Scotia Sea led to the further lengthening of the North Scotia Ridge and South Georgia moving further east. Spreading in the West Scotia Sea finally led to the rifting of the South Orkney microcontinent from the tip of the Antarctic Peninsula. This event initiated the South Scotia Ridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The banks of the South Scotia Ridge are made of continental crust that rifted off the land bridge 40-30 Ma. The banks on the northern Central Scotia Sea, on the other hand, are volcanic constructs overlaying an oceanic <a href="/wiki/Basement_(geology)" title="Basement (geology)">basement</a> parts of which is the spreading centre of the separation between South America and Antarctica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Seafloor spreading in the West Scotia Sea continued until 6.6-5.9 Ma. The oldest volcanic activity in Central and Eastern Scotia Sea, i.e. the first signs of a volcanic arc, has been dated to 28.5 Ma. The South Sandwich Islands fore-arc originated in the Central Scotia Sea and was translated eastward by the <a href="/wiki/Back-arc_basin" title="Back-arc basin">back-arc</a> spreading centre in the East Scotia Sea, i.e. the East Scotia Ridge. South Georgia's eastward migration ended about 9 Ma when the microcontinent collided with the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Georgia_Rise" title="Northeast Georgia Rise">Northeast Georgia Rise</a> <a href="/wiki/Large_igneous_province" title="Large igneous province">large igneous province</a> on the South American Plate north-east of South Georgia. A submerged equivalent to the current South Sandwich Arc was relocated westward by the same spreading centre. After, and probably because of, the collision, the East Scotia Sea spreading centre split the ancestral South Sandwich Arc leaving a remnant arc under the Central Scotia Sea. The collision also produced the rugged <a href="/wiki/Allardyce_Range" title="Allardyce Range">Allardyce Range</a> on South Georgia, making the 3,000 m (9,800 ft)-high range three times higher than the conjugate rocks on <a href="/wiki/Navarino_Island" title="Navarino Island">Navarino Island</a> in Tierra del Fuego.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Dalziel-etal-2013-Dev-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The driving mechanism behind the formation of the arc, as proposed by <a href="#CITEREFAlvarez1982">Alvarez 1982</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Mantle_(geology)" title="Mantle (geology)">mantle</a> return-flow from the Pacific to the Atlantic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalziel-etal-2013-signif_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dalziel-etal-2013-signif-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
