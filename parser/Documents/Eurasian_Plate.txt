<p>The <b>Eurasian Plate</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tectonic_plate" title="Tectonic plate">tectonic plate</a> which includes most of the continent of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a> (a landmass consisting of the traditional <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continents</a> of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>), with the notable exceptions of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Plate" title="Arabian Plate">Arabian subcontinent</a>, and the area east of the <a href="/wiki/Chersky_Range" title="Chersky Range">Chersky Range</a> in East <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. It also includes <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_crust" title="Oceanic crust">oceanic crust</a> extending westward to the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_Ridge" title="Mid-Atlantic Ridge">Mid-Atlantic Ridge</a> and northward to the <a href="/wiki/Gakkel_Ridge" title="Gakkel Ridge">Gakkel Ridge</a>.</p>
<p>The eastern side is a boundary with the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Plate" title="North American Plate">North American Plate</a> to the north and a boundary with the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea_Plate" title="Philippine Sea Plate">Philippine Sea Plate</a> to the south and possibly with the <a href="/wiki/Okhotsk_Plate" title="Okhotsk Plate">Okhotsk Plate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Amurian_Plate" title="Amurian Plate">Amurian Plate</a>. The southerly side is a boundary with the <a href="/wiki/African_Plate" title="African Plate">African Plate</a> to the west, the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Plate" title="Arabian Plate">Arabian Plate</a> in the middle and the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Australian_Plate" title="Indo-Australian Plate">Indo-Australian Plate</a> to the east. The westerly side is a <a href="/wiki/Divergent_boundary" title="Divergent boundary">divergent boundary</a> with the North American Plate forming the northernmost part of the Mid-Atlantic Ridge, which is straddled by <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>. All of the volcanic eruptions in Iceland, such as the 1973 eruption of <a href="/wiki/Eldfell" title="Eldfell">Eldfell</a>, the 1783 eruption of <a href="/wiki/Laki" title="Laki">Laki</a>, and the 2010 eruption of <a href="/wiki/Eyjafjallaj%C3%B6kull" title="Eyjafjallajökull">Eyjafjallajökull</a>, are caused by the North American and the Eurasian plates moving apart, which is a result of <a href="/wiki/Divergent_boundary" title="Divergent boundary">divergent</a> plate boundary forces.</p>

<p>The geodynamics of central Asia is dominated by the interaction between the Eurasian and <a href="/wiki/Indian_Plate" title="Indian Plate">Indian Plates</a>. In this area, many subplates or crust blocks have been recognized, which form the Central Asian and the East Asian transit zones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
