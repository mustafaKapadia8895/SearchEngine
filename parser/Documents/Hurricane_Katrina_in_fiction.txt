<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theater">Theater</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Katrina_in_fiction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Theater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The Hurricane Katrina Comedy Festival, by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Florence&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rob Florence (page does not exist)">Rob Florence</a>, premiered at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/New_York_International_Fringe_Festival" title="New York International Fringe Festival">New York International Fringe Festival</a> at SoHo Playhouse. The cast, directed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dann_Fink&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dann Fink (page does not exist)">Dann Fink</a> and stage managed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Christina_Lowe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Christina Lowe (page does not exist)">Christina Lowe</a>, featured <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philip_Hoffman_(Broadway_actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philip Hoffman (Broadway actor) (page does not exist)">Philip Hoffman</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lizann_Mitchell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lizann Mitchell (page does not exist)">Lizann Mitchell</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maureen_Silliman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maureen Silliman (page does not exist)">Maureen Silliman</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Evander_Duck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Evander Duck (page does not exist)">Evander Duck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Cowling" title="Gary Cowling">Gary Cowling</a>. The play interweaves the stories of five individuals in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a> during and after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> According to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Out_(company)" title="Time Out (company)">Time Out New York</a></i>, the play focuses "on the kind of small anecdotes you might hear from a friend in your living room: politely amusing, occasionally moving, deliberately uplifting."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Feldman_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Feldman-5">[5]</a></sup> The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a>'</i> David Rooney compared the play's narrative approach to <a href="/wiki/The_Laramie_Project" title="The Laramie Project">The Laramie Project</a>, with the story told by each character "intertwined into a compelling chronicle,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rooney_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rooney-6">[6]</a></sup> The show extended, and was included in the New York International Fringe Festival's Encores Series, remounted at the Lucille Lortel Theatre with the original cast, director and designers reprising their roles.</li>
</ul>
