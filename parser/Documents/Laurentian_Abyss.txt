<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Laurentian fan</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Laurentian_Abyss&amp;redirect=no" title="Laurentian Abyss">Laurentian Abyss</a>)</span></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Laurentian fan</b> or <b>abyss</b> is an underwater depression off the eastern coast of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MarBioGeog_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MarBioGeog-1">[1]</a></sup> Not a trench, but more of an "underwater valley", it is estimated to be at most ~19,685 feet (3.7 miles; 6.0 km) in depth. The Laurentian fan is a product of glaciation and water currents from the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence_River" title="Saint Lawrence River">Saint Lawrence River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PhoneInterview_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-PhoneInterview-2">[2]</a></sup> It is part of the Laurentian cone region, bound by the <a href="/wiki/Laurentian_Channel" title="Laurentian Channel">Laurentian Channel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sohm_Abyssal_Plain" title="Sohm Abyssal Plain">Sohm Abyssal Plain</a>.</p>
<p>The fan is the site of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrothermal_vents" title="Hydrothermal vents">hydrothermal vents</a> with their own sub <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecosystems" title="Ecosystems">ecosystems</a> independent of sunlight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EcologyDover_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EcologyDover-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The approximate coordinates are <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Laurentian_fan&amp;params=43_40_N_56_10_W_scale:5000000"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">43°40′N</span> <span class="longitude">56°10′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">43.667°N 56.167°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">43.667; -56.167</span></span></span></a></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Laurentian_fan&amp;params=43_40_N_56_10_W_scale:5000000"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">43°40′N</span> <span class="longitude">56°10′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">43.667°N 56.167°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">43.667; -56.167</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laurentian_fan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The Laurentian Abyss was the covert rendezvous point for Soviet and American submarines in the 1990 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technothriller" title="Technothriller">technothriller</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunt_for_Red_October_(film)" title="The Hunt for Red October (film)">The Hunt for Red October</a></i>. The location, however, was a departure from the <a href="/wiki/The_Hunt_for_Red_October" title="The Hunt for Red October">original 1984 novel</a> by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy" title="Tom Clancy">Tom Clancy</a>.</li>
<li>The abyss appears in the <i><a href="/wiki/Transformers_(film)" title="Transformers (film)">Transformers live-action film</a></i> where <a href="/wiki/Megatron" title="Megatron">Megatron</a>'s dead body is dumped after he was killed by Sam Witwicky. The abyss is seen again in the sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Transformers:_Revenge_of_the_Fallen" title="Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen">Revenge of the Fallen</a></i> where the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constructicon" title="Constructicon">Constructicons</a> come to revive Megatron.</li>
</ul>
