<p>The <b>sargassum fish</b>, <b>anglerfish</b>, or <b>frog fish</b>, <i>Histrio histrio</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSIP_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MSIP-3">[3]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Frogfish" title="Frogfish">frogfish</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antennariidae" title="Antennariidae">Antennariidae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monotypic_taxon" title="Monotypic taxon">the only species in its genus</a>. It lives among <i><a href="/wiki/Sargassum" title="Sargassum">Sargassum</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Seaweed" title="Seaweed">seaweed</a> which floats in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The scientific name comes from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>histrio</i> meaning a stage-player or actor, and refers to the fish's feeding behaviour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sargassum_fish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2383" data-file-width="3255" height="161" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg/220px-Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg/330px-Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg/440px-Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Histrio_histrio_by_A._H._Baldwin.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Illustration of a sargassum fish.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Histrio histrio</i>, a strange-looking fish, blends well with its surroundings in its seaweed habitat. It is laterally compressed and its length can reach 20Â cm (7.9Â in). The colour of the body and the large oral cavity is very variable, but is usually mottled and spotted yellow, green, and brown on a paler background, and the fins often have several dark streaks or bands. The fish can change colour rapidly, from light to dark and back again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSIP_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MSIP-3">[3]</a></sup> The body and the fins are covered with many weed-like protrusions, but other than these, the skin is smooth without dermal spines. The dorsal fin has three spines and 11â13 soft rays. The front spine is modified into a slender growth on the upper lip known as an illicium, which is tipped by a fleshy lump, the esca. The junction between the head and body is indistinct because there are no gill slits, the gills opening as pores near the base of the pectoral fins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ichthyology_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ichthyology-6">[6]</a></sup> The anal fin has no spines and seven to 13 soft rays. The pelvic fins are large and the pectoral fins have 9-11 rays and are stalked and able to grip objects. The outer rays of the tail fin are simple, but the central rays are forked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSIP_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-MSIP-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frogfish_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frogfish-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sargassum_fish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Histrio histrio</i> has a <a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_distribution" title="Cosmopolitan distribution">cosmopolitan distribution</a> in tropical and subtropical seas down to a depth of about 10 metres (33Â ft). It is found in parts of the Atlantic Ocean and the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Pacific" title="Indo-Pacific">Indo-Pacific</a> Ocean where drifting seaweed accumulates. In the western Atlantic it ranges from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a> south to <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>. It has been reported from northern <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> but that sighting is likely to be as a result of its having been carried along by the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Current" title="North Atlantic Current">North Atlantic Current</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WoRMS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-WoRMS-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSIP_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-MSIP-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">Biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sargassum_fish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The sargassum fish is a voracious ambush <a href="/wiki/Predation" title="Predation">predator</a> that is also a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cannibal" title="Cannibal">cannibal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSIP_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-MSIP-3">[3]</a></sup> One individual was dissected and found to have 16 juveniles in its stomach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frogfish_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Frogfish-8">[8]</a></sup> It stalks its prey among the tangled weeds, relying on its <a href="/wiki/Crypsis" title="Crypsis">cryptic</a> camouflage for concealment. It can clamber through and cling to the seaweed stalks with its prehensile pectoral fins. It dangles its esca as a fishing lure to attract small fish, shrimps, and other invertebrates. It is able to dart forward to grab its prey by expelling water forcibly through its gill openings. It can expand its mouth to many times its original size in a fraction of a second, drawing prey in via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suction_feeding" title="Suction feeding">suction</a>, and can swallow prey larger than itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frogfish_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Frogfish-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dioecious" title="Dioecious">dioecious</a>. At breeding time, the male courts the female by following her around closely. When ready to spawn, the female ascends rapidly to the surface, where she lays a mass of eggs stuck together by gelatinous <a href="/wiki/Mucus" title="Mucus">mucus</a>. This egg raft adheres to the seaweed, where it is fertilised by the male. On hatching, each larva is surrounded by an integumentary envelope and has a large, rounded head, fully formed fins, and eyes with double notches. As the larva develops into a juvenile, this envelope fuses with the skin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ichthyology_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ichthyology-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>This fish is preyed on by larger fish and sea birds. To avoid underwater threats, it can leap above the surface onto mats of weed. It can survive for some time out of water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ichthyology_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ichthyology-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
