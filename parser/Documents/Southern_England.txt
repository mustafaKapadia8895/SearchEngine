<p><b>Southern England</b>, or the <b>South of England</b>, also known as <b>the South</b>, refers roughly to the southern <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_England" title="Counties of England">counties</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The extent of this area can take a number of different interpretations depending on the context, including geographical, cultural, political and economic.</p>
<p>Geographically, the extent of the south of England may vary from the southern quarter (below the M4/Northern M25), via one-third of the country (excluding central England), to the southern half, bordering <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">northern England</a>. The South is often considered a principal <a href="/wiki/Cultural_area" title="Cultural area">cultural area</a> of England, along with <a href="/wiki/The_Midlands" title="The Midlands">the Midlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>. Many consider the area to have a distinct identity from the rest of England, however without universal agreement on what cultural, political, and economic characteristics of the South are.</p>
<p>For statistical purposes, Southern England is divided into four <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_England" title="Regions of England">regions</a>: <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_East_England" title="South East England">South East England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">London</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/East_of_England" title="East of England">East of England</a>. Combined, these have a total area of 62,042 square kilometres (23,955 sq mi), and a population of 28 million.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: People">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>People often apply the terms "southern" and "south" loosely, without deeper consideration of the geographical identities of Southern England. This can cause confusion over the depth of affiliation between its areas. As in much of the rest of England, people tend to have a deeper affiliation to their county or city. Thus, residents of <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> are unlikely to feel much affinity with people in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>. Similarly, there is a strong distinction between natives of the south-west and south-east.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Rugby_League" title="History of Rugby League">History of Rugby League</a></div>
<p>The sport of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a> experienced a <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league" title="History of rugby league">schism</a> in 1895 with many teams based in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> and surrounding areas breaking from the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a> and forming their own <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rugby_League" title="Rugby League">League</a>. The disagreement that led to the split was over the issue of professional payments, and "broken time" or injury payments. There is a perception that league is the code of rugby played in the north, whilst union is the code played in the south.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Despite the general acceptance of these counties as Southern, those that comprise the <a href="/wiki/West_Country" title="West Country">West Country</a> are occasionally considered mutually exclusive to Southern England.</p>
<p>The exact northern extent varies and as with most geographical regions, people sometimes debate the boundaries. In the west, Southern England is generally taken to include <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>; in central Southern England, the counties of <a href="/wiki/Bedfordshire" title="Bedfordshire">Bedfordshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">Hertfordshire</a>; to the east, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Essex,_England" title="Essex, England">Essex</a> and the counties of <a href="/wiki/East_Anglia" title="East Anglia">East Anglia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire" title="Cambridgeshire">Cambridgeshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a>).</p>
<p>Despite these definitions, the northern boundary is generally taken to correspond to an imaginary line from the <a href="/wiki/Severn_Estuary" title="Severn Estuary">Severn Estuary</a> to <a href="/wiki/The_Wash" title="The Wash">the Wash</a> (or expressed in terms of towns, from <a href="/wiki/Gloucester" title="Gloucester">Gloucester</a> to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Lynn" title="King's Lynn">King's Lynn</a>).</p>
