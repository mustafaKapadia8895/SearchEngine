<p>The <b>Scotia Sea</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">sea</a> located at the northern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">Southern Ocean</a> at its boundary with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_Ocean" title="South Atlantic Ocean">South Atlantic Ocean</a>. It is bounded on the west by the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a> and on the north, east, and south by the <a href="/wiki/Scotia_Arc" title="Scotia Arc">Scotia Arc</a>, an undersea ridge and <a href="/wiki/Island_arc" title="Island arc">island arc</a> system supporting various islands. The sea sits atop the <a href="/wiki/Scotia_Plate" title="Scotia Plate">Scotia Plate</a>. It is named after the expedition ship <a href="/wiki/Scotia_(barque)" title="Scotia (barque)"><i>Scotia</i></a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location_and_description">Location and description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scotia_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location and description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Scotia Sea is the area of water between the Drake Passage, <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_Island" title="South Georgia Island">South Georgia</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Sandwich_Islands" title="South Sandwich Islands">South Sandwich Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/South_Orkney_Islands" title="South Orkney Islands">South Orkney Islands</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Peninsula" title="Antarctic Peninsula">Antarctic Peninsula</a>. These island groups all sit atop the Scotia Arc, which frames the sea on the north, east, and south. The Scotia Sea covers an area around 900,000 km<sup>2</sup> (347,500 sq mi). About half of the sea stands above the <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelf</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scotia_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The sea is named around 1932 after the <i><a href="/wiki/Scotia_(barque)" title="Scotia (barque)">Scotia</a></i>, the expedition ship used in these waters by the Scottish National Antarctic Expedition (1902–04) under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_S._Bruce" title="William S. Bruce">William S. Bruce</a>. The most famous traverse of this frigid sea was made in 1916 by Sir <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Shackleton" title="Ernest Shackleton">Ernest Shackleton</a> and five others in the adapted lifeboat <i>James Caird</i> when they left <a href="/wiki/Elephant_Island" title="Elephant Island">Elephant Island</a> and reached South Georgia two weeks later.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, the Scotia Sea is considered part of an area known as the <i>Mar Argentino</i>, and several territories claimed but not occupied by Argentina, such as South Georgia and the Falkland Islands, lie within this region.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scotia_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The islands bordering the Scotia Sea are rocky and partly covered in ice and snow year round; despite these harsh conditions, however, the islands do support vegetation and have been described as the Scotia Sea Islands tundra <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a>, which includes South Georgia, the volcanic South Sandwich Islands, and the South Orkneys in the Scotia Sea, as well as the remote <a href="/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands" title="South Shetland Islands">South Shetland Islands</a> near the Antarctic Peninsula and the small isolated volcano called <a href="/wiki/Bouvet_Island" title="Bouvet Island">Bouvet Island</a>. All these islands lie in the cold seas below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_convergence" title="Antarctic convergence">Antarctic convergence</a>. These areas support <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a> vegetation consisting of <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">mosses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichens</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Algae" title="Algae">algae</a>, while seabirds, penguins, and seals feed in the surrounding waters.</p>
<p>Seabirds include four species of albatross: black-browed albatross <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diomedea_melanophris" title="Diomedea melanophris">Diomedea melanophris</a>)</i>, grey-headed albatross <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thalassarche_chrysostoma" title="Thalassarche chrysostoma">Thalassarche chrysostoma</a>)</i>, light-mantled albatross <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phoebetria_palpebrata" title="Phoebetria palpebrata">Phoebetria palpebrata</a>)</i>, and wandering albatross <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diomedea_exulans" title="Diomedea exulans">Diomedea exulans</a>)</i>. Only five species of birds remain on land on the islands, and these include an endemic race of the yellow-billed pintail duck <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anas_georgica" title="Anas georgica">Anas georgica</a>)</i> and the endemic South Georgia pipit <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthus_antarcticus" title="Anthus antarcticus">Anthus antarcticus</a>)</i>. Other birds include the <a href="/wiki/Southern_giant_petrel" title="Southern giant petrel">southern giant petrel</a>, with sizeable colonies on <a href="/wiki/Bird_Island,_South_Georgia" title="Bird Island, South Georgia">Bird Island</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Penguin" title="Penguin">Penguin</a> species found here include large numbers of <a href="/wiki/King_penguin" title="King penguin">king penguins</a> on South Georgia especially, as well as <a href="/wiki/Chinstrap_penguin" title="Chinstrap penguin">chinstrap penguin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macaroni_penguin" title="Macaroni penguin">macaroni penguin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gentoo_penguin" title="Gentoo penguin">gentoo penguin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adelie_penguin" title="Adelie penguin">Adelie penguin</a>, and rockhopper penguin <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eudyptes_chrysocome" title="Eudyptes chrysocome">Eudyptes chrysocome</a>)</i>.</p>
<p>Seals include the Antarctic fur seal <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arctocephalus_gazella" title="Arctocephalus gazella">Arctocephalus gazella</a>)</i> and sub-Antarctic fur seal <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arctocephalus_tropicalis" title="Arctocephalus tropicalis">Arctocephalus tropicalis</a>)</i> in large numbers, <a href="/wiki/Leopard_seal" title="Leopard seal">leopard seal</a> <i>(Hydrurga leptonyx)</i>, <a href="/wiki/Weddell_seal" title="Weddell seal">Weddell seal</a> <i>(Leptonychotes weddellii)</i>, the huge <a href="/wiki/Southern_elephant_seal" title="Southern elephant seal">southern elephant seal</a> <i>(Mirounga leonina)</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Crabeater_seal" title="Crabeater seal">crabeater seal</a> <i>(Lobodon carcinophagus)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats_and_preservation">Threats and preservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scotia_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Threats and preservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Scotia-sea.png" title="Map of Scotia Sea. Toponyms: Undersea relief, maritime, nearby lands, countries and cities. Isobath interval: 2000 m"><img alt="Map of Scotia Sea. Toponyms: Undersea relief, maritime, nearby lands, countries and cities. Isobath interval: 2000 m" data-file-height="592" data-file-width="1119" height="212" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Scotia-sea.png/400px-Scotia-sea.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Scotia-sea.png/600px-Scotia-sea.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Scotia-sea.png/800px-Scotia-sea.png 2x" width="400"/></a></div>
<p>Although the islands have a harsh climate and have never been permanently occupied, they have long been used as a base for fishing and seal hunting. Wildlife on these remote islands is threatened by <a href="/wiki/Introduced_species" title="Introduced species">introduced species</a>, especially on South Georgia, where even large animals, including (now removed) <a href="/wiki/Reindeer" title="Reindeer">reindeer</a>, have been brought to the islands. Further damage to ecosystems results from overfishing. South Georgia, the South Sandwich Islands, and Bouvet Island are protected as nature reserves, with <a href="/wiki/Bird_Island,_South_Georgia" title="Bird Island, South Georgia">Bird Island, South Georgia</a>, being a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Site_of_special_scientific_interest" title="Site of special scientific interest">site of special scientific interest</a>. The seals are further protected by international agreements, and fur seal populations are recovering.</p>
