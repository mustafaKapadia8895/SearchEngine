<p><i><b>The World Factbook</b></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Standard_Serial_Number_(identifier)" title="International Standard Serial Number (identifier)">ISSN</a> <a class="external text" href="https://www.worldcat.org/search?fq=x0:jrnl&amp;q=n2:1553-8133" rel="nofollow">1553-8133</a>; also known as the <i><b>CIA World Factbook</b></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-featured_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-featured-2">[2]</a></sup> is a reference resource produced by the <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">Central Intelligence Agency</a> (CIA) with <a href="/wiki/Almanac" title="Almanac">almanac</a>-style information about the <a href="/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states" title="List of sovereign states">countries</a> of the <a href="/wiki/World" title="World">world</a>. The official print version is available from the <a href="/wiki/National_Technical_Information_Service" title="National Technical Information Service">National Technical Information Service</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_Printing_Office" title="Government Printing Office">Government Printing Office</a>. Other companies—such as <a href="/wiki/Skyhorse_Publishing" title="Skyhorse Publishing">Skyhorse Publishing</a>—also print a paper edition. <i>The Factbook</i> is available in the form of a website that is partially updated every week. It is also available for download for use off-line. It provides a two- to three-page summary of the <a href="/wiki/Demography" title="Demography">demographics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communications</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forms_of_government" title="Forms of government">government</a>, <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> of each of 267 international entities<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whatsnew_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-whatsnew-3">[3]</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_recognition" title="Diplomatic recognition">U.S.-recognized</a> countries, dependencies, and other areas in the world.</p>
<p><i>The World Factbook</i> is prepared by the CIA for the use of <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">U.S. government</a> officials, and its style, format, coverage, and content are primarily designed to meet their requirements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cci_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-cci-4">[4]</a></sup> However, it is frequently used as a resource for academic research papers and news articles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-press_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-press-5">[5]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Copyright_status_of_work_by_the_U.S._government" title="Copyright status of work by the U.S. government">work of the U.S. government</a>, it is in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain_in_the_United_States" title="Public domain in the United States">public domain in the United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pd_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-pd-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Because the <i>Factbook</i> is in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a>, people are free under United States law to redistribute it or parts of it in any way that they like, without permission of the CIA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cci_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-cci-4">[4]</a></sup> However, the CIA requests that it be <a href="/wiki/Citation" title="Citation">cited</a> when the <i>Factbook</i> is used.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pd_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-pd-6">[6]</a></sup> Copying the official seal of the CIA without permission is prohibited by U.S. federal law—specifically, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency_Act_of_1949" title="Central Intelligence Agency Act of 1949">Central Intelligence Agency Act of 1949</a> (<a href="/wiki/Title_50_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 50 of the United States Code">50 U.S.C.</a> <a class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/403m" rel="nofollow">§ 403m</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Frequency_of_updates_and_availability">Frequency of updates and availability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World_Factbook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Frequency of updates and availability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before November 2001 <i>The World Factbook</i> website was updated yearly;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faqs-u2d_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-faqs-u2d-7">[7]</a></sup> from 2004 to 2010 it was updated every two weeks;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faqs-u2d_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-faqs-u2d-7">[7]</a></sup> since 2010 it has been updated weekly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weekly_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-weekly-8">[8]</a></sup> Generally, information currently available as of January 1 of the current year<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> is used in preparing the <i>Factbook</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_edition_of_the_Factbook">Government edition of the <i>Factbook</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World_Factbook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Government edition of the Factbook">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first, <a href="/wiki/Classified_information" title="Classified information">classified</a>, edition of <i>Factbook</i> was published in August 1962, and the first unclassified version in June 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBFhistory_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-WBFhistory-10">[10]</a></sup> <i>The World Factbook</i> was first available to the public in print in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBFhistory_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-WBFhistory-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2008 the CIA discontinued printing the <i>Factbook</i> themselves, instead turning printing responsibilities over to the Government Printing Office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-printgpo_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-printgpo-11">[11]</a></sup> This happened due to a CIA decision to "focus Factbook resources" on the online edition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noprint_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-noprint-12">[12]</a></sup> The <i>Factbook</i> has been on the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a> since October 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The web version gets an average of 6 million visits per month;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-press_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-press-5">[5]</a></sup> it can also be downloaded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The official printed version is sold<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buy_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-buy-15">[15]</a></sup> by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Government_Publishing_Office" title="United States Government Publishing Office">Government Printing Office</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Technical_Information_Service" title="National Technical Information Service">National Technical Information Service</a>. In past years, the <i>Factbook</i> was available on <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">CD-ROM</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microfiche" title="Microfiche">microfiche</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wfbems_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-wfbems-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_tape" title="Magnetic tape">magnetic tape</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wfbems_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-wfbems-17">[17]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Floppy_disk" title="Floppy disk">floppy disk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wfbems_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-wfbems-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reprints_and_older_editions_online">Reprints and older editions online</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World_Factbook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reprints and older editions online">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many Internet sites use information and images from the CIA <i>World Factbook</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Several publishers, including Grand River Books,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Potomac Books (formerly known as Brassey's Inc.),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Skyhorse_Publishing" title="Skyhorse Publishing">Skyhorse Publishing</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> have re-published the <i>Factbook</i> in recent years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Entities_listed">Entities listed</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World_Factbook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Entities listed">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_entities_and_changes_in_The_World_Factbook" title="List of entities and changes in The World Factbook">List of entities and changes in The World Factbook</a></div>
<p>As of July 2011, <i>The World Factbook</i> consists of <b>267</b> entities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whatsnew_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-whatsnew-3">[3]</a></sup> These entities can be divided into categories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ents_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ents-22">[22]</a></sup> They are:</p>
<dl>
<dt>Independent countries</dt>
<dd>This category has independent countries, which the CIA defines as people "politically organized into a sovereign state with a definite territory".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ents_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-ents-22">[22]</a></sup> In this category, there are <b>195</b> entities.</dd>
<dt>Others</dt>
<dd>The <i>Other</i> category is a list of other places set apart from the list of independent countries. Currently there are <b>two</b>: <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.</dd>
<dt>Dependencies and Areas of Special Sovereignty</dt>
<dd>This category is a list of places affiliated with another country. They may be subdivided into categories using the country they are affiliated with:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>: six entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>: two entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>: two entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>: eight entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>: three entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>: three entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>: three entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>: seventeen entities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>: fourteen entities</li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Miscellaneous</dt>
<dd>This category is for <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> and places in dispute. There are <b>six</b> entities.</dd>
<dt>Other entities</dt>
<dd>This category is for the <a href="/wiki/World" title="World">World</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a>. There are <b>five</b> oceans and the World (the World entry is intended as a summary of the other entries).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-press_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-press-5">[5]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Territorial_issues_and_controversies">Territorial issues and controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World_Factbook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Territorial issues and controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political">Political</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World_Factbook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Political">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Areas not covered</dt>
<dd>Specific regions within a country or areas in dispute among countries, such as <a href="/wiki/Kashmir" title="Kashmir">Kashmir</a>, are not covered,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notin_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-notin-23">[23]</a></sup> but other areas of the world whose status is disputed, such as the <a href="/wiki/Spratly_Islands" title="Spratly Islands">Spratly Islands</a>, have entries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notin_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-notin-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Subnational areas of countries (such as <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_and_territories_of_Canada" title="Provinces and territories of Canada">Canadian provinces and territories</a>) are not included in the <i>Factbook</i>. Instead, users looking for information about subnational areas are referred to "a comprehensive encyclopedia" for their reference needs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-subnat_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-subnat-25">[25]</a></sup> This criterion was invoked in the 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-domrom_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-domrom-26">[26]</a></sup> and 2011<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mayotte_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-mayotte-27">[27]</a></sup> editions with the decision to drop the entries for <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayotte" title="Mayotte">Mayotte</a>, and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Reunion</a>. They were dropped because besides being <a href="/wiki/Overseas_department" title="Overseas department">overseas departments</a>, they were now <a href="/wiki/Overseas_region" title="Overseas region">overseas regions</a>, and an integral part of France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-domrom_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-domrom-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mayotte_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-mayotte-27">[27]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Kashmir</dt>
<dd>Maps depicting Kashmir have the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indo-Pakistani_border" title="Indo-Pakistani border">Indo-Pakistani border</a> drawn at the <a href="/wiki/Line_of_Control" title="Line of Control">Line of Control</a>, but the region of <a href="/wiki/Aksai_Chin" title="Aksai Chin">Kashmir administered by China</a> drawn in hash marks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chmap_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-chmap-28">[28]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Northern Cyprus</dt>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Northern_Cyprus" title="Northern Cyprus">Northern Cyprus</a>, which the U.S. considers part of the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Republic of Cyprus</a>, is not given a separate entry because "territorial occupations/annexations not recognized by the United States Government are not shown on U.S. Government maps."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Taiwan/Republic of China</dt>
<dd>The name "Republic of China" is not listed as <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>'s official name under the "Government" section,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jjwyiz_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-jjwyiz-30">[30]</a></sup> due to U.S. acknowledgement of Beijing's <a href="/wiki/One-China_policy" title="One-China policy">One-China policy</a> according to which there is one China and Taiwan is a part of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roceu_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-roceu-31">[31]</a></sup> The name "Republic of China" was briefly added on January 27, 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> but has since been changed back to "none".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jjwyiz_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-jjwyiz-30">[30]</a></sup> Of the <i>Factbook</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s two maps of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, one highlights the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Island_of_Taiwan" title="Island of Taiwan">island of Taiwan</a> highlighted as part of the country<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chmap_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-chmap-28">[28]</a></sup> while the other does not.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chinapage_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chinapage-33">[33]</a></sup> (See also: <a href="/wiki/Political_status_of_Taiwan" title="Political status of Taiwan">Political status of Taiwan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legal_status_of_Taiwan" title="Legal status of Taiwan">Legal status of Taiwan</a>)</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Disputed South China Sea Islands</dt>
<dd>The <a href="/wiki/Paracel_Islands" title="Paracel Islands">Paracel Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spratly_Islands" title="Spratly Islands">Spratly Islands</a>, subjects of territorial disputes, have entries in the <i>Factbook</i> where they are not listed as the territory of any one nation. The disputed claims to the islands are discussed in the entries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Factbook-Paracel_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Factbook-Paracel-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Factbook-Spratley_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Factbook-Spratley-35">[35]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Burma/Myanmar</dt>
<dd>The U.S. does not recognize the renaming of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a> by its <a href="/wiki/State_Peace_and_Development_Council" title="State Peace and Development Council">ruling military junta</a> to <i>Myanmar</i> and thus keeps its entry for the country under the <i>Burma</i> name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Republic of Macedonia</dt>
<dd>The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Republic of Macedonia</a> is entered as <i>Macedonia</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> the name used in its first entry in the <i>Factbook</i> upon independence in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yugo_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-yugo-38">[38]</a></sup> In the 1994 edition, the name of the entry was changed to the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Former_Yugoslav_Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia">Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia</a></i>, as it is recognised by the United Nations (pending resolution of the <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_naming_dispute" title="Macedonia naming dispute">Macedonia naming dispute</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> For the next decade, this was the name the nation was listed under. Finally, in the 2004 edition of the <i>Factbook</i>, the name of the entry was changed back to <i>Macedonia</i>, following a November 2004 U.S. decision to refer to the country using this name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>European Union</dt>
<dd>On December 16, 2004, the CIA added an entry for the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> (EU) for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noEU_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-noEU-44">[44]</a></sup>) The "What's New" section of the 2005 <i>Factbook</i> states: "The European Union continues to accrue <a href="/wiki/European_integration" title="European integration">more nation-like characteristics</a> for itself and so a separate listing was deemed appropriate."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roceu_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-roceu-31">[31]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>United States Pacific Island Wildlife Refuges and Iles Eparses</dt>
<dd>In the 2006 edition of <i>The World Factbook</i>, the entries for <a href="/wiki/Baker_Island" title="Baker Island">Baker Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howland_Island" title="Howland Island">Howland Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jarvis_Island" title="Jarvis Island">Jarvis Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingman_Reef" title="Kingman Reef">Kingman Reef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Atoll" title="Johnston Atoll">Johnston Atoll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palmyra_Atoll" title="Palmyra Atoll">Palmyra Atoll</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Midway_Atoll" title="Midway Atoll">Midway Islands</a> were merged into a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Pacific_Island_Wildlife_Refuges" title="United States Pacific Island Wildlife Refuges">United States Pacific Island Wildlife Refuges</a> entry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The old entries for each individual insular area remain as redirects on the <i>Factbook</i> website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> On September 7, 2006, the CIA also merged the entries for <a href="/wiki/Bassas_da_India" title="Bassas da India">Bassas da India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europa_Island" title="Europa Island">Europa Island</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Glorioso_Islands" title="Glorioso Islands">Glorioso Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Nova_Island" title="Juan de Nova Island">Juan de Nova Island</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tromelin_Island" title="Tromelin Island">Tromelin Island</a> into a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iles_Eparses" title="Iles Eparses">Iles Eparses</a> entry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> As with the new United States Pacific Island Wildlife Refuges entry, the old entries for these five islands remained as redirects on the website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> On July 19, 2007, the Iles Eparses entry and redirects for each island were dropped due to the group becoming a district of the <a href="/wiki/French_Southern_and_Antarctic_Lands" title="French Southern and Antarctic Lands">French Southern and Antarctic Lands</a> in February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taaf_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-taaf-49">[49]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Serbia and Montenegro/Yugoslavia</dt>
<dd>The <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a> (SFRY) broke apart in 1991. The following year, it was replaced in the <i>Factbook</i> with entries for each of its former constituent republics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yugo_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-yugo-38">[38]</a></sup> In doing this, the CIA listed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a> (FRY), proclaimed in 1992, as <i>Serbia and Montenegro</i>, as the U.S. did not recognize the union between the two republics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FRY2_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-FRY2-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> This was done in accordance with a May 21, 1992, decision<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FRY_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-FRY-52">[52]</a></sup> by the U.S. not to recognize any of the former Yugoslav republics<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-law_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-law-53">[53]</a></sup> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Successor_state" title="Successor state">successor states</a> to the recently dissolved SFRY.</dd>
</dl>

<dl>
<dd>These views were made clear in a disclaimer printed in the <i>Factbook</i>: "Serbia and Montenegro have asserted the formation of a joint independent state, but this entity has not been recognized as a state by the United States."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Montenegro and Serbia were treated separately in the <i>Factbook</i> data, as can be seen on the map.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> In October 2000, <a href="/wiki/Slobodan_Milo%C5%A1evi%C4%87" title="Slobodan Milošević">Slobodan Milošević</a> was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/5th_October_(Serbia)" title="5th October (Serbia)">forced out of office</a> after a disputed election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> This event led to democratic elections and U.S. diplomatic recognition. The 2001 edition of the <i>Factbook</i> thus referred to the state as <i>Yugoslavia</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> On March 14, 2002, an agreement was signed to transform the FRY into a loose state union called <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> it took effect on February 4, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> The name of the Yugoslavia entity was altered in the <i>Factbook</i> the month after the change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Kosovo</dt>
<dd>On February 28, 2008, the CIA added an entry for <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>, which <a href="/wiki/2008_Kosovo_declaration_of_independence" title="2008 Kosovo declaration of independence">declared independence</a> on February 17 of the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Before this, Kosovo was excluded in the <i>Factbook</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notin_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-notin-23">[23]</a></sup> Kosovo is the subject of a territorial dispute; <a href="/wiki/Kosovo%E2%80%93Serbia_relations" title="Kosovo–Serbia relations">Serbia continues to claim</a> Kosovo as part of its <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Province_of_Kosovo_and_Metohija" title="Autonomous Province of Kosovo and Metohija">own sovereign territory</a>. Kosovo's independence has been <a href="/wiki/International_recognition_of_Kosovo" title="International recognition of Kosovo">recognised by 111</a> out of 193 <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">member states</a>, including the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ctvnorec_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-ctvnorec-63">[63]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Factual">Factual</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World_Factbook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Factual">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Factbook is full of usually minor errors, inaccuracies, and out-of-date information, which are often repeated elsewhere due to the <i>Factbook</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s widespread use as a reference. For example, <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a> was until recently, described in the <i>Factbook</i> as 70% <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, 20% <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox</a>, and 10% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>, which was based on a survey conducted in 1939, before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>; numerous surveys conducted since the fall of the Communist regime since 1990 have given quite different figures. Another example is <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, which the <i>Factbook</i> states has a <a href="/wiki/Total_fertility_rate" title="Total fertility rate">total fertility rate</a> of 0.78 children per woman, despite figures in Statistics Singapore which state that the rate has been about 1.2–1.3 children per woman for at least the past several years, and it is unclear when, or even whether, it ever dropped as low as 0.78.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> This low and inaccurate value then gets cited in news articles which state that Singapore has the world's lowest fertility, or at least use the figure for its shock value.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Another serious problem is that the Factbook never cites its sources, making verification of the information it presents difficult if not impossible.</p>
<p>In June 2009, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio" title="National Public Radio">National Public Radio</a> (NPR), relying on information obtained from the <i>CIA World Factbook</i>, put the number of Israeli Jews living in settlements in the <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">West Bank</a> and Israeli-annexed <a href="/wiki/East_Jerusalem" title="East Jerusalem">East Jerusalem</a> at 250,000. However, a better estimate, based on <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_State" title="United States Department of State">State Department</a> and Israeli sources put the figure at about 500,000. NPR then issued a correction. Chuck Holmes, foreign editor for NPR Digital, said, "I'm surprised and displeased, and it makes me wonder what other information is out-of-date or incorrect in the CIA <i>World Factbook</i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scholars have acknowledged that some entries in the <i>Factbook</i> are out of date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
