<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">High Desert (California)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><b>High Desert</b> is a name used to define geographic areas of <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a> deserts that are between 2,000 and 4,000 ft (609.9 and 1219.2 m) in <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevation</a>. The High Desert generally refers to areas of the <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave Desert</a>. The term "High Desert" is commonly used by local news media,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> especially in weather forecasts, because of the high desert's unique and moderate weather patterns compared to its low desert neighbors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> High Desert has also been incorporated into the names of businesses and organizations in these areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The term is used commonly to refer to <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Tree_National_Park" title="Joshua Tree National Park">Joshua Tree National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twentynine_Palms,_California" title="Twentynine Palms, California">Twentynine Palms</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Morongo_Basin" title="Morongo Basin">Morongo Basin</a>. However, the High Desert typically will be described as extending as far north as <a href="/wiki/Victorville,_California" title="Victorville, California">Victorville</a>, and Lancaster areas, and as far northwest as <a href="/wiki/Palmdale,_California" title="Palmdale, California">Palmdale</a>, and north to the <a href="/wiki/Barstow,_California" title="Barstow, California">Barstow</a> desert and beyond. The term "High Desert" serves to differentiate it from southern California's <a href="/wiki/Low_Desert" title="Low Desert">Low Desert</a>, which is defined by the differences in elevation, climate, animal life, and vegetation native to these regions. Comparison example: <a href="/wiki/Palm_Springs,_California" title="Palm Springs, California">Palm Springs</a> is considered "Low Desert", at 100 feet (30.48m) above sea level. In contrast, <a href="/wiki/Landers,_California" title="Landers, California">Landers</a> is considered "High Desert", at 3,100 feet (944.88m) above sea level.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_Desert_(California)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pale_Horizon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2280" data-file-width="4288" height="117" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Pale_Horizon.jpg/220px-Pale_Horizon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Pale_Horizon.jpg/330px-Pale_Horizon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Pale_Horizon.jpg/440px-Pale_Horizon.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pale_Horizon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Old Woman Springs Ridge in the high desert, Johnson Valley, California</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Depending on how the boundaries of the Mojave and the Colorado Desert region are defined, the High Desert either includes the entire California portion of the <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave Desert</a> (using a smaller geographic designation than its <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a>) or the northern portion of the California desert (using a larger geographic designation including the <a href="/wiki/Ecotope" title="Ecotope">ecotope</a> area of the lower and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Sonoran_Desert" title="Sonoran Desert">Sonoran Desert</a>).</p>
<p>The name of the region comes from its higher elevations and more northern latitude with associated climate and <a href="/wiki/Plant_community" title="Plant community">plant communities</a> distinct from the <a href="/wiki/Low_Desert" title="Low Desert">Low Desert</a>, which includes the Colorado Desert and the below sea level <a href="/wiki/Salton_Sea" title="Salton Sea">Salton Sea</a>. The High Desert is typically windier than the Low Desert, and averages between 12 degrees to 20 degrees Fahrenheit cooler in both the winter, and Summer seasons.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regions">Regions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_Desert_(California)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Regions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The High Desert is often divided into the following regions:</p>
<ul>
<li>The Los Angeles County portion, containing the <a href="/wiki/Antelope_Valley" title="Antelope Valley">Antelope Valley</a>, part of the Palmdale-Lancaster Urbanized Area, and in the greater <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_metropolitan_area" title="Los Angeles metropolitan area">Los Angeles metropolitan area</a>. This is the most populous area of the High Desert region, with close to 300,000 residents in the incorporated places alone.</li>
<li>The San Bernardino County portion, containing <a href="/wiki/Victor_Valley" title="Victor Valley">Victor Valley</a>, which is part of the <a href="/wiki/Inland_Empire" title="Inland Empire">Inland Empire</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a>, along with the Antelope Valley and the <a href="/wiki/Morongo_Basin" title="Morongo Basin">Morongo Basin</a>, where Yucca Valley and the Twentynine Palms Marine Base are located, are all considered to be part of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Los_Angeles_Area" title="Greater Los Angeles Area">Greater Los Angeles Area</a>.</li>
</ul>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Burnt_Horizon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Burnt_Horizon.jpg/220px-Burnt_Horizon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Burnt_Horizon.jpg/330px-Burnt_Horizon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Burnt_Horizon.jpg/440px-Burnt_Horizon.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Burnt_Horizon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Just after <a href="/wiki/Sunset" title="Sunset">sunset</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landers,_California" title="Landers, California">Landers, California</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Other parts of the San Bernardino County portion include the northeastern reaches of the High Desert, where the <a href="/wiki/Fort_Irwin_National_Training_Center" title="Fort Irwin National Training Center">Fort Irwin National Training Center</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Searles_Valley,_California" title="Searles Valley, California">Searles Valley</a> are located, and the far eastern edge of the <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">state</a> where places like Needles and Earp are located along the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_River" title="Colorado River">Colorado River</a>. San Bernardino County's portion of the High Desert region contains the most land mass of the four involved counties, making up approximately 70% of the total county's area.</p>
<ul>
<li>The Kern County portion, containing part of two valleys, with the southeastern part in the Antelope Valley, including Rosamond, California City, Boron, <a href="/wiki/Edwards_Air_Force_Base" title="Edwards Air Force Base">Edwards Air Force Base</a>, and Mojave, which are all a part of the Palmdale-Lancaster Urbanized area, and the northeastern part being in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Wells_Valley" title="Indian Wells Valley">Indian Wells Valley</a>, including the communities of Inyokern and Ridgecrest.</li>
<li>The Inyo County portion, north of Kern County and containing the northern end of the Indian Wells Valley, <a href="/wiki/Panamint_Valley" title="Panamint Valley">Panamint Valley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saline_Valley,_California" title="Saline Valley, California">Saline Valley</a>. This is the most sparsely populated area of the High Desert, with a single major community, <a href="/wiki/Lone_Pine,_California" title="Lone Pine, California">Lone Pine</a> in the southern <a href="/wiki/Owens_Valley" title="Owens Valley">Owens Valley</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cities_and_communities">Cities and communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_Desert_(California)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cities and communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sunset_over_the_Mojave_in_January.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1520" data-file-width="2688" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Sunset_over_the_Mojave_in_January.jpg/220px-Sunset_over_the_Mojave_in_January.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Sunset_over_the_Mojave_in_January.jpg/330px-Sunset_over_the_Mojave_in_January.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Sunset_over_the_Mojave_in_January.jpg/440px-Sunset_over_the_Mojave_in_January.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The major metropolitan centers in the region are primarily centered on the cities of <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_California" title="Lancaster, California">Lancaster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victorville,_California" title="Victorville, California">Victorville</a>. Lancaster, the largest city in the High Desert, is located in the Antelope Valley, with <a href="/wiki/Palmdale,_California" title="Palmdale, California">Palmdale</a>, and anchors the area's largest and most populous region with a <a href="/wiki/List_of_California_urban_areas" title="List of California urban areas">metro area</a> of just over 500,000. The <a href="/wiki/Victor_Valley" title="Victor Valley">Victor Valley</a> area, which includes such areas as Victorville, Hesperia, Adelanto, Apple Valley, and <a href="/wiki/Lucerne_Valley,_California" title="Lucerne Valley, California">Lucerne Valley</a>, boasts a population around 335,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The Barstow area, to the north of Victor Valley, and the Morongo Basin near the <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Tree_National_Park" title="Joshua Tree National Park">Joshua Tree National Park</a> each have populations of around 60,000.</p>

<dt>Popular filming sites</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
