<p><b>Skiing</b> can be a means of transport, a recreational activity or a competitive <a href="/wiki/Winter_sport" title="Winter sport">winter sport</a> in which the participant uses <a href="/wiki/Ski" title="Ski">skis</a> to glide on <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">snow</a>. Many types of competitive skiing events are recognized by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> (IOC), and the <a href="/wiki/International_Ski_Federation" title="International Ski Federation">International Ski Federation</a> (FIS).</p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_skiing" title="History of skiing">History of skiing</a></div>
<p>Skiing has a history of almost five millennia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Although modern skiing has evolved from beginnings in <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>, it may have been practiced more than 100 centuries ago in what is now <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, according to an interpretation of ancient paintings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The word "ski" is one of a handful of words Norway has exported to the international community. It comes from the <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> word "skíð" which means "split piece of wood or firewood".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-norway.org_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-norway.org-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Asymmetrical skis were used at least in northern Finland and Sweden until the late 19th century. On one leg the skier wore a long straight non-arching ski for sliding, and on the other a shorter ski for kicking. The bottom of the short ski was either plain or covered with animal skin to aid this use, while the long ski supporting the weight of the skier was treated with animal fat in similar manner to modern <a href="/wiki/Ski_wax" title="Ski wax">ski waxing</a>.</p>
<p>Early skiers used one long pole or spear. The first depiction of a skier with two <a href="/wiki/Ski_pole" title="Ski pole">ski poles</a> dates to 1741.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until the mid-19th century skiing was primarily used for transport, and since then has become a recreation and sport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saur_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saur-7">[7]</a></sup> Military ski races were held in Norway during the 18th century,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Ski_warfare" title="Ski warfare">ski warfare</a> was studied in the late 18th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> As equipment evolved and <a href="/wiki/Ski_lift" title="Ski lift">ski lifts</a> were developed skiing evolved into two main genres during the late 19th and early 20th century, <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing" title="Alpine skiing">Alpine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nordic_skiing" title="Nordic skiing">Nordic</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skiing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alpine">Alpine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skiing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alpine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing" title="Alpine skiing">Alpine skiing</a></div>
<p>Also called downhill skiing, alpine skiing typically takes place on a <a href="/wiki/Piste" title="Piste">piste</a> at a <a href="/wiki/Ski_resort" title="Ski resort">ski resort</a>. It is characterized by fixed-heel bindings that attach at both the toe and the heel of the skier's boot. Because alpine equipment is somewhat difficult to walk in, <a href="/wiki/Ski_lift" title="Ski lift">ski lifts</a>, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chairlifts" title="Chairlifts">chairlifts</a>, bring skiers up the slope. <a href="/wiki/Backcountry_skiing" title="Backcountry skiing">Backcountry skiing</a> can be accessed by <a href="/wiki/Heliskiing" title="Heliskiing">helicopter</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snowcat_skiing" title="Snowcat skiing">snowcat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hiking</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snowmobile" title="Snowmobile">snowmobile</a>. Facilities at resorts can include <a href="/wiki/Night_skiing" title="Night skiing">night skiing</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apr%C3%A8s-ski" title="Après-ski">après-ski</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Glade_skiing" title="Glade skiing">glade skiing</a> under the supervision of the <a href="/wiki/Ski_patrol" title="Ski patrol">ski patrol</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ski_school" title="Ski school">ski school</a>. Alpine skiing branched off from the older Nordic skiing around the 1920s, when the advent of <a href="/wiki/Ski_lift" title="Ski lift">ski lifts</a> meant that it was not necessary to walk any longer. Alpine equipment specialized to where it can only be used with the help of lifts.</p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nordic_skiing" title="Nordic skiing">Nordic skiing</a></div>
<p>The Nordic disciplines include <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ski_jumping" title="Ski jumping">ski jumping</a>, which both use bindings that attach at the toes of the skier's boots but not at the heels. Cross-country skiing may be practiced on groomed trails or in undeveloped <a href="/wiki/Backcountry" title="Backcountry">backcountry</a> areas. Ski jumping skiing is practiced at certain areas that are deemed for ski jumping only.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Telemark">Telemark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skiing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Telemark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Telemark_skiing" title="Telemark skiing">Telemark skiing</a></div>
<p>Telemark skiing is a ski turning technique and FIS-sanctioned discipline. It is named after the Telemark region of Norway. Using equipment similar to nordic skiing, the ski bindings having the ski boot attached only at the toe. This allows the skier to raise his/her heel throughout the turn.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competition">Competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skiing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Competition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following disciplines are sanctioned by the FIS and USSA. Many have their own world cups and are in the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympic_Games" title="Winter Olympic Games">Winter Olympic Games</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing_(sport)" title="Cross-country skiing (sport)">Cross-country</a>: The sport encompasses a variety of formats for <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiing</a> races over courses of varying lengths. Such races occur over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homologated" title="Homologated">homologated</a>, groomed courses designed to support classic (in-track) and free-style events, where the skiers may employ skate skiing. It also encompasses cross-country <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ski_marathon" title="Ski marathon">ski marathon</a> events, sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/Worldloppet_Ski_Federation" title="Worldloppet Ski Federation">Worldloppet Ski Federation</a>, and cross-country <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ski_orienteering" title="Ski orienteering">ski orienteering</a> events, sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/International_Orienteering_Federation" title="International Orienteering Federation">International Orienteering Federation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Biathlon" title="Biathlon">biathlon</a> a combination of cross-country and <a href="/wiki/Shooting_sports" title="Shooting sports">shooting</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ski_jumping" title="Ski jumping">Ski jumping</a>: contested at the Olympics, the <a href="/wiki/FIS_Ski_Jumping_World_Cup" title="FIS Ski Jumping World Cup">FIS Ski Jumping World Cup</a>, the summer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIS_Grand_Prix_Ski_Jumping" title="FIS Grand Prix Ski Jumping">FIS Grand Prix Ski Jumping</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIS_Ski-Flying_World_Championships" title="FIS Ski-Flying World Championships">FIS Ski-Flying World Championships</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nordic_combined" title="Nordic combined">Nordic combined</a>: contested at the Olympics and at the <a href="/wiki/FIS_Nordic_Combined_World_Cup" title="FIS Nordic Combined World Cup">FIS Nordic Combined World Cup</a>, it is a combination of cross-country skiing and ski jumping.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing" title="Alpine skiing">Alpine skiing</a> disciplines include <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing_combined" title="Alpine skiing combined">combined</a>, <a href="/wiki/Downhill_(ski_competition)" title="Downhill (ski competition)">downhill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slalom_skiing" title="Slalom skiing">slalom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giant_slalom" title="Giant slalom">giant slalom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Super-G" title="Super-G">Super-G</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Para-alpine_skiing" title="Para-alpine skiing">Para-alpine</a> there are also combined events that include two event. One run of each even like one run of <a href="/wiki/Super-G" title="Super-G">Super-G</a> and one run of <a href="/wiki/Slalom_skiing" title="Slalom skiing">Slalom skiing</a> this is called a Super Combined.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Speed_skiing" title="Speed skiing">Speed skiing</a> dates from 1898 with official records being set as of 1932 with an 89-mile-per-hour (143 km/h) run by Leo Gasperi. It became an FIS sport in the 1960s and a demonstration Olympic sport at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Winter_Olympics" title="1992 Winter Olympics">1992 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Albertville" title="Albertville">Albertville</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Freestyle_skiing" title="Freestyle skiing">Freestyle skiing</a>: includes <a href="/wiki/Mogul_skiing" title="Mogul skiing">mogul skiing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_skiing#Aerial_skiing" title="Freestyle skiing">aerials</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ski_cross" title="Ski cross">ski cross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Half-pipe" title="Half-pipe">half-pipe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Slopestyle" title="Slopestyle">slopestyle</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Snowboard" title="Snowboard">Snowboard</a> competition includes slopestyle, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snowboard_cross" title="Snowboard cross">cross</a>, half-pipe, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alpine_snowboarding" title="Alpine snowboarding">alpine</a>, parallel slalom, and parallel giant slalom.</li>
</ul>
<p>Other competition includes <a href="/wiki/Grass_skiing" title="Grass skiing">grass skiing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Telemark_skiing" title="Telemark skiing">Telemark</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Skiboarding" title="Skiboarding">Skiboarding</a>: consists of a combination of skiing and snowboarding. It uses ski boots with a snow board.</li>
</ul>

<p>Equipment used in skiing includes:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ski" title="Ski">Skis</a>, which may have <a href="/wiki/Ski_skins" title="Ski skins">skins</a> applied or be textured for uphill traction or <a href="/wiki/Ski_wax" title="Ski wax">wax</a> applied for minimizing sliding friction. <a href="/wiki/Twin-tip_ski" title="Twin-tip ski">Twin-tip skis</a> are designed to move forwards or backwards.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technique">Technique</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skiing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Technique">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Technique has evolved along with ski technology and <a href="/wiki/Ski_geometry" title="Ski geometry">ski geometry</a>. Early techniques included the <a href="/wiki/Telemark" title="Telemark">telemark</a> turn, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stem_(skiing)" title="Stem (skiing)">stem</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stem_Christie" title="Stem Christie">stem Christie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snowplough_turn" title="Snowplough turn">snowplough</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Parallel_turn" title="Parallel turn">parallel turn</a>.</p>
<p>New parabolic designs like the <a href="/wiki/Elan_(company)" title="Elan (company)">Elan</a> <a href="/wiki/Elan_SCX" title="Elan SCX">SCX</a> have enabled the more modern <a href="/wiki/Carve_turn" title="Carve turn">carve turn</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Skiing_without_snow">Skiing without snow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skiing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Skiing without snow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally and primarily a <a href="/wiki/Winter_sport" title="Winter sport">winter sport</a>, skiing can also be practiced <a href="/wiki/Indoor_skiing" title="Indoor skiing">indoors</a> without snow or outdoors <a href="/wiki/Grass_skiing" title="Grass skiing">on grass</a>, on <a href="/wiki/Dry_ski_slope" title="Dry ski slope">dry ski slopes</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ski_simulator" title="Ski simulator">ski simulators</a>, or with <a href="/wiki/Roller_skiing" title="Roller skiing">roller skis</a>.</p>
