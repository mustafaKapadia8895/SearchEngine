<p><b>Christopher William "Chris" Landsea</b> (born February 4, 1965)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorologist" title="Meteorologist">meteorologist</a>, formerly a research meteorologist with Hurricane Research Division of Atlantic Oceanographic &amp; Meteorological Laboratory at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NOAA" title="NOAA">NOAA</a>, and now the Science and Operations Officer at the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a>. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/American_Geophysical_Union" title="American Geophysical Union">American Geophysical Union</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Meteorological_Society" title="American Meteorological Society">American Meteorological Society</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_achievements">Research and achievements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christopher_Landsea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Research and achievements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Landsea earned his doctoral degree in Atmospheric Science at <a href="/wiki/Colorado_State_University" title="Colorado State University">Colorado State University</a>. He served as chair of the American Meteorological Society's Committee on Tropical Meteorology and Tropical Cyclone. Landsea was recognized with the American Meteorological Society's Banner I. Miller award for "best contribution to the science of hurricane and tropical weather forecasting."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deniers_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-deniers-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over the years Landsea's work has involved the general hurricane FAQ currently on the Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory website and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis" title="Atlantic hurricane reanalysis">Atlantic hurricane reanalysis</a>. Landsea has contributed to <a href="/wiki/Science_(journal)" title="Science (journal)"><i>Science</i></a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bulletin_of_the_American_Meteorological_Society" title="Bulletin of the American Meteorological Society">Bulletin of the American Meteorological Society</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_Climate" title="Journal of Climate">Journal of Climate</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Nature_(journal)" title="Nature (journal)"><i>Nature</i></a>. He has been vocal on the lack of a link between <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> and current hurricane intensity change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deniers_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-deniers-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Landsea has published a number of research papers on <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclones</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricanes</a>. He is the author of <i>Hurricanes, Typhoons, and Tropical Cyclones: FAQ</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He also has been the lead scientist in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis" title="Atlantic hurricane reanalysis">Atlantic hurricane reanalysis</a> since 1997.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="On_global_warming_and_hurricanes">On global warming and hurricanes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christopher_Landsea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: On global warming and hurricanes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January, 2005, Landsea withdrew from his participation in the <a href="/wiki/IPCC_Fourth_Assessment_Report" title="IPCC Fourth Assessment Report">IPCC Fourth Assessment Report</a>, criticizing it for using "a process that I view as both being motivated by pre-conceived agendas and being scientifically unsound." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-landsea-leaves-IPCC_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-landsea-leaves-IPCC-5">[5]</a></sup> Landsea claimed the IPCC had become politicized and the leadership ignored his concerns. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Colorado_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Colorado-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Landsea does not believe that <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> has a strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_warming_and_hurricanes" title="Global warming and hurricanes">influence on hurricanes</a>: "global warming might be enhancing hurricane winds but only by 1 percent or 2 percent". According to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salon.com" title="Salon.com">Salon</a></i> magazine, <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_George_W._Bush" title="Presidency of George W. Bush">Bush administration</a> personnel chose Landsea over another scientist at NOAA to speak to the news media about the link between hurricanes and climate change after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a> devastated <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an interview on PBS, Christopher Landsea said "we certainly see substantial warming in the ocean and atmosphere over the last several decades on the order of a degree Fahrenheit and I have no doubt a portion of that, at least, is due to greenhouse warming. The question is whether we're seeing any real increases in the hurricane activity." He went on to say "with the Atlantic hurricanes in particular, they're due to changes both in the ocean as well as the atmosphere. Just changing the ocean where it's a little bit warmer isn't sufficient." As for climate change affecting hurricane strength, Landsea said that global warming theories and numerical modeling suggest only that "hurricanes like Katrina and Rita may have been stronger due to global warming but maybe by one or two miles per hour." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christopher_Landsea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>2007 <a href="/wiki/NOAA_Administrator%27s_Award" title="NOAA Administrator's Award">NOAA Administrator's Award</a> for "establishing and administrating the Joint Hurricane Testbed, NOAA's first U.S. Weather Research Program testbed, accelerating research into operations, greatly improving forecasts."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>United States <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Commerce_Bronze_Medal" title="Department of Commerce Bronze Medal">Department of Commerce Bronze Medal</a> Award for Superior Federal Service (Oct 2000)(co-recipient) for "issuing the accurate and first official physically based Atlantic Seasonal Hurricane Outlooks for the 1998/1999 seasons, based upon new research"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>American Meteorological Society's Banner I. Miller Award (May 1993)(co-recipient) for the "best contribution to the science of hurricane and tropical weather forecasting published during the years 1990 - 1992."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
