<p>The <b>Sonoran Desert</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_American_deserts" title="North American deserts">North American desert</a> which covers large parts of the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">Southwestern United States</a> in <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a> and <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and of Northwestern Mexico in <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baja_California_(state)" title="Baja California (state)">Baja California</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a>. It is the hottest desert in Mexico. It has an area of 260,000 square kilometers (100,000 sq mi). The western portion of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States%E2%80%93Mexico_border" title="United States–Mexico border">United States–Mexico border</a> passes through the Sonoran Desert.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Phytogeography" title="Phytogeography">phytogeography</a>, the Sonoran Desert is within the Sonoran <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Floristic_province" title="Floristic province">Floristic Province</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Madrean_Region" title="Madrean Region">Madrean Region</a> in southwestern North America, part of the <a href="/wiki/Holarctic" title="Holarctic">Holarctic Kingdom</a> of the northern <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>. The desert contains a variety of unique and <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plants</a> and <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">animals</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Saguaro" title="Saguaro">saguaro</a> (<i>Carnegiea gigantea</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Stenocereus_thurberi" title="Stenocereus thurberi">organ pipe cactus</a> (<i>Stenocereus thurberi</i>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonoran_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Sonoran desert wraps around the northern end of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_California" title="Gulf of California">Gulf of California</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a> (<a href="/wiki/El_Vizca%C3%ADno_Biosphere_Reserve" title="El Vizcaíno Biosphere Reserve">El Vizcaíno Biosphere Reserve</a> in central and Pacific west coast, Central Gulf Coast subregion on east to southern tip), north through much of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baja_California_(state)" title="Baja California (state)">Baja California</a>, excluding the central northwest mountains and Pacific west coast, through southeastern California and southwestern and southern Arizona to western and central parts of <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-museum_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-museum-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is bounded on the west by the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Ranges" title="Peninsular Ranges">Peninsular Ranges</a>, which separate it from the <a href="/wiki/California_chaparral_and_woodlands" title="California chaparral and woodlands">California chaparral and woodlands</a> (northwest) and <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Desert" title="Baja California Desert">Baja California Desert</a> (Vizcaino subregion, central and southeast) ecoregions of the Pacific slope. To the north in California and northwest Arizona, the Sonoran Desert transitions to the colder-winter, higher-elevation <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Basin" title="Great Basin">Great Basin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Plateau" title="Colorado Plateau">Colorado Plateau</a> deserts.</p>
<p>To the east and southeast, the deserts transition to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate_coniferous_forests" title="Temperate coniferous forests">coniferous</a> <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Mountains_forests" title="Arizona Mountains forests">Arizona Mountains forests</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Sierra Madre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental_pine-oak_forests" title="Sierra Madre Occidental pine-oak forests">Sierra Madre Occidental pine–oak forests</a> at higher elevations. To the south the <a href="/wiki/Sonoran-Sinaloan_transition_subtropical_dry_forest" title="Sonoran-Sinaloan transition subtropical dry forest">Sonoran–Sinaloan transition subtropical dry forest</a> is the transition zone from the Sonoran Desert to the tropical dry forests of the Mexican state of <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-museum_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-museum-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sub-region_deserts">Sub-region deserts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonoran_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sub-region deserts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The desert's sub-regions include the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Desert" title="Colorado Desert">Colorado Desert</a> of southeastern California; and the <a href="/wiki/Yuma_Desert" title="Yuma Desert">Yuma Desert</a> east of the north-to-south section of the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_River" title="Colorado River">Colorado River</a> in southwest Arizona. In the 1957 publication, <i>Vegetation of the Sonoran Desert</i>, Forrest Shreve divided the Sonoran Desert into seven regions according to characteristic vegetation: Lower Colorado Valley, Arizona Upland, Plains of Sonora, Foothills of Sonora, Central Gulf Coast, Vizcaíno Region, and Magdalena Region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Many ecologists now consider Shreve's Vizcaíno and Magdalena regions, which lie on the western side of the <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Peninsula" title="Baja California Peninsula">Baja California Peninsula</a>, to be a separate <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Desert" title="Baja California Desert">Baja California Desert</a>.</p>
<p>Within the southern Sonoran Desert in Mexico is found the <a href="/wiki/Gran_Desierto_de_Altar" title="Gran Desierto de Altar">Gran Desierto de Altar</a>, with the <i><a href="/wiki/Pinacate_Peaks" title="Pinacate Peaks">Reserva de la Biosfera el Pinacate y Gran Desierto de Altar</a></i> ('Pinacate National Park' in Mexico), extending 2,000 square kilometers (770 sq mi) of desert and mountainous regions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Pinacate National Park includes the only active <a href="/wiki/Erg_(landform)" title="Erg (landform)">Erg</a> dune region in North America. The nearest city to the <i>Reserva de la Biosfera el Pinacate y Gran Desierto de Altar</i> is <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Pe%C3%B1asco" title="Puerto Peñasco">Puerto Peñasco</a> (a.k.a. 'Rocky Point') in the state of <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonoran_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Sonoran Desert includes 60 mammal species, 350 bird species, 20 amphibian species, over 100 reptile species, 30 native fish species, over 1000 native bee species, and more than 2,000 native plant species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The Sonoran Desert area southeast of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tucson" title="Tucson">Tucson</a> and near the Mexican border is vital habitat for the only population of <a href="/wiki/Jaguar" title="Jaguar">jaguars</a> living within the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Colorado_River_Delta" title="Colorado River Delta">Colorado River Delta</a> was once an ecological hotspot within the Sonoran desert, fueled by the flow of fresh water through the Colorado river in this otherwise dry area, but the delta has been greatly reduced in extent due to the damming and use of the river upstream.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora">Flora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonoran_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Flora_of_the_Sonoran_Desert" title="Flora of the Sonoran Desert">Flora of the Sonoran Desert</a></div>

<p>Many plants not only survive, but thrive in the harsh conditions of the Sonoran Desert. Many have evolved to have specialized adaptations to the desert climate. The Sonoran Desert's biseasonal rainfall pattern results in more plant species than any other desert in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The Sonoran Desert includes plant genera and species from the <a href="/wiki/Agave" title="Agave">agave</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Arecaceae" title="Arecaceae">palm</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Cactus" title="Cactus">cactus</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Legume" title="Legume">legume</a> family, and numerous others.</p>
<p>The Sonoran is the only place in the world where the famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saguaro_cactus" title="Saguaro cactus">saguaro cactus</a> (<i>Carnegiea gigantea</i>) grows in the wild.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cylindropuntia" title="Cylindropuntia">Cholla</a> (<i>Cylindropuntia</i> spp.), <a href="/wiki/Opuntia_basilaris" title="Opuntia basilaris">beavertail</a> (<i>Opuntia basilaris</i>), <a href="/wiki/Echinocereus" title="Echinocereus">hedgehog</a> (<i>Echinocereus</i> spp.), <a href="/wiki/Ferocactus_wislizeni" title="Ferocactus wislizeni">fishhook</a> (<i>Ferocactus wislizeni</i>), <a href="/wiki/Opuntia" title="Opuntia">prickly pear</a> (<i>Opuntia</i> spp.), <a href="/wiki/Peniocereus" title="Peniocereus">nightblooming cereus</a> (<i>Peniocereus</i> spp.), and <a href="/wiki/Stenocereus_thurberi" title="Stenocereus thurberi">organ pipe</a> (<i>Stenocereus thurberi</i>) are other taxa of cacti found here. Cactus provides food and homes to many desert mammals and birds, with showy flowers in reds, pinks, yellows, and whites, blooming most commonly from late March through June, depending on the species and seasonal temperatures.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Larrea_tridentata" title="Larrea tridentata">Creosote bush</a> (<i>Larrea tridentata</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Ambrosia_dumosa" title="Ambrosia dumosa">bur sage</a> (<i>Ambrosia dumosa</i>) dominate valley floors. <a href="/wiki/Psorothamnus_fremontii" title="Psorothamnus fremontii">Indigo bush</a> (<i>Psorothamnus fremontii</i>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mormon_tea" title="Mormon tea">Mormon tea</a> are other shrubs that may be found. <a href="/wiki/List_of_Sonoran_Desert_wildflowers" title="List of Sonoran Desert wildflowers">Wildflowers of the Sonoran Desert</a> include <a href="/wiki/Abronia_villosa" title="Abronia villosa">desert sand verbena</a> (<i>Abronia villosa</i>), <a href="/wiki/Geraea_canescens" title="Geraea canescens">desert sunflower</a> (<i>Geraea canescens</i>), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evening_primrose" title="Evening primrose">evening primroses</a>.</p>

<p>Ascending from the valley up <a href="/wiki/Bajada_(geography)" title="Bajada (geography)">bajadas</a>, various subtrees such as <a href="/wiki/Prosopis_velutina" title="Prosopis velutina">velvet mesquite</a> (<i>Prosopis velutina</i>), <a href="/wiki/Parkinsonia_florida" title="Parkinsonia florida">palo verde</a> (<i>Parkinsonia florida</i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olneya_tesota" title="Olneya tesota">desert ironwood</a> (<i>Olneya tesota</i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chilopsis_linearis" title="Chilopsis linearis">desert willow</a> (<i>Chilopsis linearis</i> ssp. <i>arcuata</i>), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canotia_holacantha" title="Canotia holacantha">crucifixion thorn</a> (<i>Canotia holacantha</i>) are common, as well as multi-stemmed <a href="/wiki/Fouquieria_splendens" title="Fouquieria splendens">ocotillo</a> (<i>Fouquieria splendens</i>). Shrubs found at higher elevations include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acacia_constricta" title="Acacia constricta">whitethorn acacia</a> (<i>Acacia constricta</i>), fairy duster, and <a href="/wiki/Jojoba" title="Jojoba">jojoba</a>. In the desert subdivisions found on Baja California, cardon cactus, elephant tree, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boojum_tree" title="Boojum tree">boojum tree</a> occur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Washingtonia_filifera" title="Washingtonia filifera">California fan palm</a> (<i>Washingtonia filifera</i>) is found in the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Desert" title="Colorado Desert">Colorado Desert</a> section of the Sonoran Desert, the only <a href="/wiki/Native_plant" title="Native plant">native</a> palm in California, among many other introduced <a href="/wiki/Arecaceae" title="Arecaceae">Arecaceae</a> genera and species. It is found at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spring_(hydrosphere)" title="Spring (hydrosphere)">spring</a>-fed <a href="/wiki/Oasis" title="Oasis">oases</a>, such as in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anza_Borrego_Desert_State_Park" title="Anza Borrego Desert State Park">Anza Borrego Desert State Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Tree_National_Park" title="Joshua Tree National Park">Joshua Tree National Park</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Kofa_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Kofa National Wildlife Refuge">Kofa National Wildlife Refuge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_population">Human population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonoran_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Human population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main page: <a href="/wiki/Category:History_of_indigenous_peoples_of_North_America" title="Category:History of indigenous peoples of North America">Category:History of indigenous peoples of North America</a></div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Category:Native_American_history_of_California" title="Category:Native American history of California">Category:Native American history of California</a></div>
<p>The Sonoran Desert is home to the cultures of over<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="This term requires quantification. (October 2017)">quantify</span></a></i>]</sup> seventeen contemporary <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> tribes, with settlements at <a href="/wiki/Category:American_Indian_reservations_in_California" title="Category:American Indian reservations in California">American Indian reservations in California</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:American_Indian_reservations_in_Arizona" title="Category:American Indian reservations in Arizona">Arizona</a>, as well as populations in Mexico.</p>
<p>The largest city in the Sonoran Desert is <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>, with a 2013 metropolitan population of about 4.3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-census-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (March 2014)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup> Located on the <a href="/wiki/Salt_River_(Arizona)" title="Salt River (Arizona)">Salt River</a> in central Arizona, it is one of the fastest-growing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_metropolitan_area" title="United States metropolitan area">metropolitan areas</a> in the United States. In 2007 in the Phoenix area, desert was losing ground to <a href="/wiki/Urban_sprawl" title="Urban sprawl">urban sprawl</a> at a rate of approximately 4,000 square meters (0.99 acres) per hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next largest cities are <a href="/wiki/Tucson,_Arizona" title="Tucson, Arizona">Tucson</a>, in southern Arizona, with a metro area population of around 1 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-census-11">[11]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Mexicali" title="Mexicali">Mexicali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baja_California" title="Baja California">Baja California</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Mexico" title="Municipalities of Mexico">municipality</a> also has a population of around 900,000. The municipality of <a href="/wiki/Hermosillo" title="Hermosillo">Hermosillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a>, has a population of around 700,000. <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Obreg%C3%B3n" title="Ciudad Obregón">Ciudad Obregón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a>, in the southern part of the desert, has a population of 375,800.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="California">California</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonoran_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: California">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley" title="Coachella Valley">Coachella Valley</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Desert" title="Colorado Desert">Colorado Desert</a> section of the Sonoran Desert, has a population of 365,000. Many famous <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a> desert resort cities such as <a href="/wiki/Palm_Springs,_California" title="Palm Springs, California">Palm Springs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palm_Desert,_California" title="Palm Desert, California">Palm Desert</a> are located here.</p>

The entrance to <a href="/wiki/Palm_Springs,_California" title="Palm Springs, California">Palm Springs, California</a> via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highway_62" title="Highway 62">Highway 62</a></div>

<p>During the winter months, from November to April, the daytime temperatures in the Coachella Valley range from 70°F (21°C) to 90°F (32°C) and corresponding nighttime lows range from 46°F (8°C) to 68°F (20°C) making it a popular winter resort destination. Due to its warm year-round climate <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a> and subtropical fruits such as mangoes, figs, and dates are grown in the Coachella Valley and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Valley" title="Imperial Valley">Imperial Valley</a>. Imperial Valley has a total population of about 188,000 and has a similar climate to that of the Coachella Valley. Other cities include <a href="/wiki/Indio,_California" title="Indio, California">Indio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coachella,_California" title="Coachella, California">Coachella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calexico,_California" title="Calexico, California">Calexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Centro,_California" title="El Centro, California">El Centro,</a> <a href="/wiki/Imperial,_California" title="Imperial, California">Imperial</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palm_Desert,_California" title="Palm Desert, California">Palm Desert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blythe,_California" title="Blythe, California">Blythe</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Undocumented_border_crossing">Undocumented border crossing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonoran_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Undocumented border crossing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Straddling as it does the <a href="/wiki/Mexico%E2%80%93United_States_border" title="Mexico–United States border">US-Mexican border</a>, with low levels of human-installed security, the Sonoran desert is a popular route to attempt illegal entry into the United States. The harsh conditions mean that the 3-5-day march, usually moving at night to minimize exposure to the heat, often ends in death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
