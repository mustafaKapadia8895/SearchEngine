<p>A <b>dike swarm</b> or <b>dyke swarm</b> is a large <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geological</a> structure consisting of a major group of parallel, linear, or radially oriented <a href="/wiki/Dike_(geology)" title="Dike (geology)">dikes</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intrusion" title="Intrusion">intruded</a> within <a href="/wiki/Continental_crust" title="Continental crust">continental crust</a>. They consist of several to hundreds of dikes emplaced more or less contemporaneously during a single intrusive event, and are magmatic and stratigraphic. Such dike swarms may form a <a href="/wiki/Large_igneous_province" title="Large igneous province">large igneous province</a> and are the roots of a volcanic province.</p>
<p>The occurrence of <a href="/wiki/Mafic" title="Mafic">mafic</a> dike swarms in <a href="/wiki/Archean" title="Archean">Archean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paleoproterozoic" title="Paleoproterozoic">Paleoproterozoic</a> terrains is often cited as evidence for <a href="/wiki/Mantle_plume" title="Mantle plume">mantle plume</a> activity associated with abnormally high <a href="/wiki/Mantle_(geology)" title="Mantle (geology)">mantle</a> potential temperatures.</p>
<p>Dike swarms may extend over 400 km (250 mi) in width and length. The largest dike swarm known on Earth is the <a href="/wiki/Mackenzie_dike_swarm" title="Mackenzie dike swarm">Mackenzie dike swarm</a> in the western half of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Shield" title="Canadian Shield">Canadian Shield</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, which is more than 500 km (310 mi) wide and 3,000 km (1,900 mi) long.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The number of known giant dike swarms on <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> is small, only about 25. However, the primary geometry of most giant dike swarms is poorly known due to their age and subsequent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tectonic" title="Tectonic">tectonic</a> activity.</p>
<p>Dike swarms have also been found on <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">Venus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GalganaEtAl2013_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GalganaEtAl2013-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ErnstAtAl2001_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ErnstAtAl2001-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>
