<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Rear admiral (Royal Navy)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Rear-Admiral_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Rear-Admiral of the United Kingdom">Rear-Admiral of the United Kingdom</a>.</div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For other uses of "rear admiral", see <a href="/wiki/Rear_admiral" title="Rear admiral">rear admiral</a>.</div>
<p><b>Rear admiral</b> (<b>RAdm</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Flag_officer" title="Flag officer">flag officer</a> rank of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>. It is immediately superior to <a href="/wiki/Commodore_(Royal_Navy)" title="Commodore (Royal Navy)">commodore</a> and is subordinate to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice_Admiral_(Royal_Navy)" title="Vice Admiral (Royal Navy)">vice admiral</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Two-star_rank" title="Two-star rank">two-star rank</a> and has a NATO ranking code of OF-7.</p>
<p>The rank originated in the days of naval sailing squadrons and each naval squadron would be assigned an <a href="/wiki/Admiral_(Royal_Navy)" title="Admiral (Royal Navy)">admiral</a> as its head. He would command from the centre vessel and direct the activities of the squadron. The admiral would in turn be assisted by a vice admiral, who commanded the lead ships which would bear the brunt of a naval battle. In the rear of the naval squadron, a third admiral would command the remaining ships and, as this section of the squadron was considered to be in the least danger, the admiral in command of the rear would typically be the most junior of the squadron admirals. This has survived into the modern age, with the rank of rear admiral the most-junior of the admiralty ranks of many navies.</p>
<p>The Royal Navy rank of rear admiral should be distinguished from the office of <a href="/wiki/Rear-Admiral_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Rear-Admiral of the United Kingdom">Rear-Admiral of the United Kingdom</a>, which is an Admiralty position usually held by a senior (and possibly retired) "full" admiral.</p>
