<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sand eel</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><b>Sand eel</b> or <b>sandeel</b> is the common name used for a considerable number of species of <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>. Most of them are sea fish of the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hyperoplus" title="Hyperoplus">Hyperoplus</a></i> (greater sand eels), <i><a href="/wiki/Gymnammodytes" title="Gymnammodytes">Gymnammodytes</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Ammodytes" title="Ammodytes">Ammodytes</a></i>. Many species are found off the western coasts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> to <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Seas</a>.</p>
<p>The three genera listed above all fall within the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ammodytidae" title="Ammodytidae">Ammodytidae</a></b>, the <a href="/wiki/Sand_lance" title="Sand lance">sand lances</a>. Members of these genera found in other <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a> are not usually called sand eels, and species from other parts of the world are known as sand eels are usually less closely related. None of the sand eels is related to the true <a href="/wiki/Eel" title="Eel">eels</a>.</p>
<p>Sand eels are an important food source for seabirds, including <a href="/wiki/Puffin" title="Puffin">puffins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black-legged_kittiwake" title="Black-legged kittiwake">kittiwakes</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sand_eel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A preferential habitat is a seabed floor, with a relatively smooth bottom of gravelly sand; an example of this prime habitat is the floor of the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_the_Hebrides" title="Sea of the Hebrides">Sea of the Hebrides</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Sand eels typically burrow in the sand to escape from predators.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_fishing">Commercial fishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sand_eel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Commercial fishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditionally, they have been little exploited for human food, but are a major target of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_fishing" title="Industrial fishing">industrial fishing</a> for animal feed and fertilizer. Increasing fishing for them is thought to be causing problems for some of their natural predators, especially the <a href="/wiki/Auk" title="Auk">auks</a> which take them in deeper water. They are also tied as flies to catch fish.</p>
<p>An instance of this was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RSPB" title="RSPB">RSPB</a> report linking a <a href="/wiki/Population_dynamics" title="Population dynamics">population crash</a> of seabirds in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> to fishing for sand eels. This led to political pressure for the closure of this fishery; the seabird populations subsequently improved. However, a second population crash of seabirds which occurred with no effect on the stocks of sand eel, cast doubt on this link.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
