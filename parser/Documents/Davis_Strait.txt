<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Davis Strait</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Davis Strait</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Détroit de Davis</i></span>) is a northern arm of the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Sea" title="Labrador Sea">Labrador Sea</a>. It lies between mid-western <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nunavut" title="Nunavut">Nunavut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Baffin_Island" title="Baffin Island">Baffin Island</a>. To the north is <a href="/wiki/Baffin_Bay" title="Baffin Bay">Baffin Bay</a>. The strait was named for the English explorer <a href="/wiki/John_Davis_(English_explorer)" title="John Davis (English explorer)">John Davis</a> (1550–1605), who explored the area while seeking a <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Passage" title="Northwest Passage">Northwest Passage</a>. By the 1650s it was used for <a href="/wiki/Whaling" title="Whaling">whale hunting</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extent">Extent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davis_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Extent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Hydrographic_Organization" title="International Hydrographic Organization">International Hydrographic Organization</a> defines the limits of the Davis Strait as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>On the North.</i> The Southern limit of <a href="/wiki/Baffin_Bay" title="Baffin Bay">Baffin Bay</a> [The parallel of <a href="/wiki/70th_parallel_north" title="70th parallel north">70° North</a> between <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baffin_Island" title="Baffin Island">Baffin Land</a>].</p>
<p><i>On the East.</i> The Southwest coast of Greenland.</p>
<p><i>On the South.</i> The parallel of <a href="/wiki/60th_parallel_north" title="60th parallel north">60° North</a> between Greenland and <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a>.</p>
<p><i>On the West.</i> The Eastern limit of the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Passage" title="Northwest Passage">Northwestern Passages</a> South of 70° North [the East coast of Baffin Island to East Bluff, its Southeastern extremity] and of <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Strait" title="Hudson Strait">Hudson Strait</a> [A line from East Bluff, the Southeast extreme of Baffin Island (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Davis_Strait&amp;params=61_53_N_65_57_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">61°53′N</span> <span class="longitude">65°57′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">61.883°N 65.950°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">61.883; -65.950</span></span></span></a></span>), to Point Meridian, the Western extreme of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Savage_Islands" title="Lower Savage Islands">Lower Savage Islands</a>, along the coast to its Southwestern extreme and thence a line across to the Western extreme of <a href="/wiki/Resolution_Island_(Nunavut)" title="Resolution Island (Nunavut)">Resolution Island</a>, through its Southwestern shore to Hatton Headland, its Southern point, thence a line to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Chidley" title="Cape Chidley">Cape Chidley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a> (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Davis_Strait&amp;params=60_24_N_64_26_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">60°24′N</span> <span class="longitude">64°26′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">60.400°N 64.433°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">60.400; <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davis_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2176" data-file-width="3872" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg/220px-Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg/330px-Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg/440px-Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sisimiut-nasaasaaq-from-palasip-qaqqaa.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The coast of Davis Strait in western Greenland</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Davis Strait is underlain by complex geological features of buried <a href="/wiki/Graben" title="Graben">grabens</a> (basins) and <a href="/wiki/Ridge" title="Ridge">ridges</a>, probably formed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geologic_fault#Strike-slip_faults" title="Geologic fault">strike-slip faulting</a> during <a href="/wiki/Paleogene" title="Paleogene">Paleogene</a> times about 45 million to 62 million years ago. The strike-slip faulting transferred <a href="/wiki/Plate_tectonics" title="Plate tectonics">plate-tectonic motions</a> in the Labrador Sea to Baffin Bay. It is the world's broadest strait.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Depth">Depth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davis_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Depth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With a water depth of between one and two thousand meters the <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> is substantially shallower than the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Sea" title="Labrador Sea">Labrador Sea</a> to the south or <a href="/wiki/Baffin_Bay" title="Baffin Bay">Baffin Bay</a> to the north.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tides">Tides</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davis_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tides">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The strait is famous for its fierce tides that can range from 30 to 60 ft (9.1 to 18.3 m), which discouraged many earlier explorers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oil_and_gas">Oil and gas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davis_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Oil and gas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_exploration_in_the_Arctic" title="Petroleum exploration in the Arctic">Petroleum exploration in the Arctic</a></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Geological_Survey" title="US Geological Survey">US Geological Survey</a> has estimated that at least 13% of the worlds undiscovered oil deposits and 30% of the worlds undiscovered gas pockets are located in the Arctic, with the seas around Greenland potentially holding large amounts of <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> and lesser amounts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crude_oil" title="Crude oil">crude oil</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_gas_liquids" title="Natural gas liquids">natural gas liquids</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This has led the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Greenland" title="Parliament of Greenland">Greenland's minister and provincial council</a> to offer a large number of off-shore concessions to potential hydrocarbon (oil and gas) extraction. The largest concessions areas are located in seas west of Greenland; primarily the Davis Strait and <a href="/wiki/Baffin_Bay" title="Baffin Bay">Baffin Bay</a>, but with several smaller concessions in the <a href="/wiki/Greenland_Sea" title="Greenland Sea">Greenland Sea</a> in the east also.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
